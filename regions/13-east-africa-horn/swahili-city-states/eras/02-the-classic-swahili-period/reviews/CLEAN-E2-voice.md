### Section III — The Mosque and the Meeting
Voice holds through the mosque construction and Friday prayer passages. The Islam passage ("Islam had come to the coast the way the monsoon brought everything else: gradually, carried by people who came and stayed") is the voice at its best: structural insight delivered as natural observation.

One area of concern:

**Lines:** "A man's inheritance followed Islamic law for his tradable wealth and Bantu custom for his clan lands. A woman's bride-price was negotiated in terms that both traditions recognized."
**Nature:** These sentences are accurate and important, but they shift slightly toward encyclopedic register: "followed Islamic law for," "was negotiated in terms that both traditions recognized." The rhythm is more textbook than maritime.
**Severity:** P3 — the sentences are embedded in a stronger passage and the voice recovers immediately.
**Recommendation:** Consider rewriting to embed in scene: perhaps a specific inheritance or bride-price moment rather than general description.

The dispute-resolution scene is excellent: the voice handles legal specificity without breaking register.

### Section IV — The Gold Corridor
This section sustains the voice through the densest analytical material in the chapter. The gold trade passage, the information asymmetry, the merchant's calculation are all delivered in the accumulative maritime register. The passage about the merchant calculating across monsoon seasons ("Between the weighing of the gold in the storehouse and the sale of the gold in Aden, six months would pass...") is a genuine achievement: economic analysis that reads as narrative.

One shift:

**Lines:** "This was the structural fact of the coast's wealth: it depended on distances that the wind bridged but did not eliminate."
**Nature:** "Structural fact" is analytical language. The baseline voice would not use this phrase. It belongs to a historian's summary, not to the coast's own voice.
**Severity:** P2 — the phrase introduces a register the chapter has otherwise avoided.
**Recommendation:** Rewrite: "This was how the coast's wealth worked" or simply cut the framing sentence and let the following sentences carry the argument.

### Section V — The Coral and the Tide
Voice is strong throughout the closing. The generational arc (grandson completing the grandfather's work) maintains the register. The coral metaphor delivers structural argument in the voice's own terms. The final passage is the voice at full extension: "The wind had not yet shifted. The harbor waited. The reef waited. The coast, at the height of its power, waited for the season to turn." Short, declarative, weighted. **No breaks detected.**
