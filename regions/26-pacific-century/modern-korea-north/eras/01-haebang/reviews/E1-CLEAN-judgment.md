# CLEAN Editorial — E1: Judgment Auditor

**Pass:** Judgment & Outside-Lens Violations  
**Auditor:** E-1 (Judgment Auditor)  
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

## Charge

Hunt for violations of the 13 principles from the Style Guide. Forbidden words (primitive, advanced, tribe, discovered by, the New World). Anachronistic morality. Romanticization or demonization. Any voice that steps outside the culture's own worldview.

---

## Findings

**Forbidden words audit:** PASS

- No instances of "primitive," "advanced," "civilization" (as compliment), "discovered by," "the New World," "tribe"
- No anachronistic framing ("While Europe was," "Before contact with the West," "remarkably sophisticated for")
- No demonization language ("brutal," "savage," "barbaric," "tragic inevitability")

**Anachronistic morality audit:** PASS

- The chapter does not judge the mythology as immoral or cynical
- The state's intolerance for doubt is presented as structural necessity, not as evil
- The imprisonment of Im Hae-jo is tragic but intelligible, not condemned from outside
- No sentence reads as modern judgment imported backward

**Romanticization audit:** PASS

- The chapter is clear-eyed about the cost of mythology (erasure of Im Hae-jo, erasure of Park Hon-yong, the mother having to teach a mythology she did not create)
- No sentiment that this was noble or beautiful or worth the cost
- No language that prettifies the machinery of control
- The landing is clear: mythology was necessary, not therefore beautiful

**Voice integrity audit:** PASS

- No sentence steps outside the North Korean frame to comment
- No "regime language" — no use of the word "regime," no framing as a failed state, no Cold War outsider commentary
- The voice never judges. It explains. It shows consequences. It does not stand apart.

**The Achebe Test audit:** PASS

- A thoughtful North Korean reading this could feel their story was told with dignity
- The founding fiction is presented as the state's own self-understanding
- The mythology is not mocked from outside
- The cost is acknowledged (doubt suppressed, people disappeared) without judgment
- A reader could disagree with every detail and still feel the story was their story, told from inside

---

## Detailed Notes

**Part 1 (Arrival):** 
The presentation of Im Hae-jo's doubt is particularly careful. He is not a hero resisting evil. He is a man noticing something that frightens him, then choosing silence because belief is necessary. This is honest. Not judged.

Line to affirm: "Later, when Im Hae-jo would speak these doubts aloud, he would be arrested for them. He would spend the next five years in prison for the crime of noticing that the emperor was wearing someone else's face." — This is critical of the system's intolerance but not from outside. It is diagnostic.

**Part 2 (What Was Lost):**
The Japanese occupation section is rendered through bodies, not through concepts. A child beaten for speaking Korean. A mother teaching in whispers. Starvation. This is not romantic suffering — it is real suffering with consequences. Good.

No romanticization of the liberation moment ("Koreans poured into the streets"). The celebration is authentic. The subsequent recognition ("freedom is not the same as sovereignty") is not condescending; it is structural analysis.

**Part 3 (Soviet Installation):**
The handling of the land reform and study groups is particularly strong. The peasant receives land and feels hope. The mother understands the land as a test. The study group learns the framework. None of this is mocked. All of it is shown as serious, as work, as real.

The phrase "not as indoctrination — not yet. As education." — This is careful. The machinery of belief is being installed, but the text does not presume to judge whether it is indoctrination or education. It lets the reader decide by showing it.

**Part 4 (The Name):**
"The legend said: a guerrilla fighter who had resisted the Japanese for years..."

This presentation of the legend is honest without dismissing it. The legend existed. The legend was real. Whether the man matched it is secondary to what the legend could do.

**Part 5 (The Bargain):**
"By 1950, five years of repetition had shaped a population."

This is not "propaganda shaped" or "indoctrinated." It is "repetition shaped." The voice is precise about mechanism without judging the outcome.

---

## Verdict

**Status: PASS — No revisions required**

The chapter maintains judgment-voice discipline throughout. It presents the founding mythology, the machinery of its installation, and the consequences of living inside it without stepping outside to judge. It is critical (the system suppressed doubt, people disappeared) while remaining inside (because this is what the system required for survival).

The style is consistent with LUCY's house rules: **Present from within. No historical judgment. Story-first.**

Ready for E2 (Voice Keeper) pass.
