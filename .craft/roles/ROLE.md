# LUCIA Review Organization

The Chronicle's quality system uses three review tiers, each with distinct
intellectual perspectives organized around productive tensions.

## Tier Structure

```
PANEL (5 voices)          ── Stages 3 & 5: PANEL-1, PANEL-2
  Every opening and notes pass through all five.
  They are the permanent reviewers — culture-agnostic,
  craft-focused, present for every chapter.

EDITORIAL (3 lenses)      ── Stage 9: CLEAN
  The cleanup pass. Catches what the panel isn't
  looking for: judgment leaks, voice drift, mechanical
  violations, re-explanation.

BOARD (per-culture)       ── Stage 10: BOARD
  2-3 domain specialists for the specific culture
  under review. Selected per chapter. Not permanent —
  assembled from a growing roster as regions are built.
```

## Panel: Five Productive Tensions

The panel is designed so that the five voices create natural friction:

```
Tuchman (craft) ──────── Ibn Khaldun (systems)
  "Does it pull?"    vs    "Does it explain WHY?"

Kapuściński (sense) ──── Ibn Khaldun (systems)
  "Can you feel it?"  vs  "Does it see the pattern?"

Achebe (voice) ──────── Tuchman (craft)
  "Whose voice?"     vs   "Does the reader follow?"

Davis (wonder) ──────── Ibn Khaldun (systems)
  "Is it honest awe?" vs  "Is it rigorous?"

Kapuściński (sense) ──── Achebe (voice)
  "Does it feel real?" vs "Does it feel THEIRS?"
```

These tensions prevent the manuscript from collapsing into any single mode:
pure storytelling without depth, pure analysis without feeling, pure immersion
without cultural ownership, or pure wonder without rigor.
