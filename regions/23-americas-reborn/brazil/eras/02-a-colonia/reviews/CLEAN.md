**Anachronistic morality audit:**
- The Ganga Zumba section presents his decision without verdict. "Neither man was wrong. They were right about different things, and the difference between them was not of courage but of calculation about what the thing they were protecting actually was." — clean. No anachronistic moral framework applied.
- The senhor de engenho composite: "He is Catholic, literate in Portuguese, fluent in the idiom of colonial obligation and Christian paternalism. He does not think of himself as a cruel man." — the chapter inhabits his self-understanding without endorsing it. The critique emerges from the logic of the evidence (the mallet exists for a reason) without the author stepping outside to pronounce.
- Freyre reference: "Freyre was right that the relationship was intimate. He was not right enough about what intimacy conducted under conditions of absolute power costs the people without power." — this is the v1.2 Honesty amendment technique: using an outsider's account as evidence about the outsider while the culture's own frame provides the counter-reading. Freyre's account reveals Freyre's position, not the engenho.

**Romanticization audit:**
- Palmares is not presented as paradise. The chapter acknowledges the internal political fracture (Ganga Zumba/Zumbi), the betrayal (Antônio Soares), the uncertainty about Palmares's internal governance (sources are described as unclear).
- The composite woman does not "heroically resist" — she runs because the forest at night is less frightening than the mallet. This is the body's logic, not a moral claim.
- The axé is presented as a transmission system with specific procedural requirements, not as magic or mystical essence.

**Result:** No violations found. No revisions required.

## E-2 — The Voice Keeper

**Task:** Hunt register drift. Textbook voice. Modern commentary. Any paragraph that sounds written for a Western audience rather than from inside the culture.

**Voice-random-paragraph test (three paragraphs selected at random):**

1. Paragraph from Section 2 (Açúcar): "The rollers were the most dangerous part. Three vertical cylinders in tight contact, turned by water or animal power, through which the cane stalks were fed to press out the juice." — This is close to description for an external audience. However, it is immediately followed by "The workers who fed the rollers could not let the rhythm break. If a hand was pulled in — and hands were pulled in — the only tool for preventing the roller from taking the arm was a wooden mallet kept beside the machine for exactly this purpose. The mallet was there to break the bones before the roller took the arm." The technical description is redeemed by the body's perspective. The passage serves; keep.

2. Paragraph from Section 5 (Axé): "The babalorixá — the father of the orixá, the keeper of the sacred genealogy — knew which deity claimed which person at birth. This was knowledge that required years of training to acquire and decades of practice to master." — "Keeper of the sacred genealogy" briefly defines from outside. Revision: "The babalorixá knew which deity claimed which person at birth." The parenthetical gloss "the keeper of the sacred genealogy" is not necessary if the sentence stands on its own and the term has already been used. Minor.

3. Paragraph from Section 7 (Landing): "In 1791, in Saint-Domingue, the ceremony at Bois Caïman opened the only successful large-scale slave revolution the Atlantic world had ever seen." — Clean. Inside view: the chapter does not explain the ceremony; it names it. The consequence follows naturally.

**Em-dash audit:** 0 em-dashes in the full chapter. Register is disciplined.

**Register identity test:** Reading three paragraphs in isolation, could a reader identify this culture? Yes. The call-and-response structure, the Yoruba terminology (orixá, babalorixá, axé, Xangô, Iemanjá, Ogum), the batá and agogô, the terreiro — these are culturally specific markers that identify the Afro-Brazilian tradition distinctly. The voice could not tell any other culture's story.

**Revision required:** One minor. In Section 5, the parenthetical gloss "(the father of the orixá, the keeper of the sacred genealogy)" after babalorixá: the term was defined in the opening. On second use, it can stand without definition. Remove the parenthetical.
