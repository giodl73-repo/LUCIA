# BOARD Review -- Robert W. Thomson

**Chapter:** Hayastan -- The Ancient Kingdom
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Literary Accuracy

### The Five Historians
The chapter names Koriwn, Agathangelos, Movses Khorenatsi, Yeghishe, and Ghazar Parpetsi as the five major historians of the Golden Age. This is the standard list, and their works are correctly identified.

**Important note on dating:** The chapter states that "within fifty years of the alphabet's creation, Armenian literature produced five major historians." This is the traditional chronology. However, the dating of Movses Khorenatsi is one of the most contested questions in Armenian studies. Some scholars (Akinean, Toumanoff) accept a fifth-century date. Others (Thomson included, in my own critical edition) have argued that the *Patmutiun Hayots* as we have it dates from the eighth or ninth century, incorporating earlier materials but composed later than Khorenatsi's traditional dating suggests. The chapter should either note this debate or qualify the claim with "by tradition" or "according to the Armenian literary tradition."

### Koriwn
The characterization of Koriwn's biography as hagiographic is correct. The *Vark Mashtotsi* is indeed the primary source for the alphabet's creation, and it is indeed the work of a student writing about his teacher. The chapter uses it appropriately.

### Yeghishe
The treatment of Yeghishe's *History of Vardan and the Armenian War* as a spiritual narrative rather than a political report is accurate to the text itself. Yeghishe's work is indeed structured as a martyrology, and the chapter correctly identifies this. The dating of the battle (451) is correct.

### The Astuatsashunch
The chapter calls the Armenian Bible the "Queen of Translations" -- this is indeed a traditional title. The claim that the Armenian Bible has been used to reconstruct lost Greek readings is accurate (the Armenian version of Eusebius's *Chronicle* is a well-known example). The characterization of the translation's quality is not exaggerated.

### Agathangelos
The chapter correctly presents Agathangelos as a fifth-century work that tells the conversion story in Armenian. The acknowledgment that it is foundational narrative rather than historical report is appropriate.

## Factual Corrections Required

1. **Khorenatsi's dating:** The claim that five historians wrote within fifty years of the alphabet must be qualified. Either note the dating debate for Khorenatsi or rephrase to "the tradition records five major historians in the first century of Armenian literature." This is a P2 concern: the unqualified claim, while reflecting the traditional chronology, would be challenged by a significant portion of Armenological scholarship.

## Endorsement

The literary characterizations are accurate and nuanced. The treatment of hagiographic sources as the tradition's own voice, rather than as obstacles to historical truth, is exactly right for this kind of chapter. The dating concern for Khorenatsi is the only correction required. With that adjustment, I endorse the chapter for publication.
