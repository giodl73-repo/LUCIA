# BOARD-B-inca-1 — Gary Urton — Board Review of Manco Cápac's Foundation

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Quipu encoding systems, the relationship between quipu and quipucamayoc, the status of current quipu scholarship.

## Historical Accuracy

- **Correct:** The description of the quipu's decimal system with three knot types (simple, long, figure-eight) is accurate. The figure of approximately 900 surviving quipus is current.
- **Correct:** The description of the quipucamayoc as trained specialists whose knowledge supplemented the quipu (and vice versa) correctly captures the inseparability of record and reader.
- **Correct:** The framing of the scholarly debate about narrative capacity ("the debate among scholars is not whether the quipu could hold narrative but how") is an accurate summary of the current state of the field.
- **Minor correction needed:** The chapter mentions "ply direction" in passing but does not specify that the binary choice between S-twist and Z-twist is a fundamental encoding dimension. This is not essential for the narrative but would strengthen the passage's precision if space allows.

## Cultural Authenticity

The chapter treats the quipu as a technology, not a deficit. This is exactly right. The sentence "the records did not burn. They went silent, which is a different kind of loss and in some ways a deeper one" captures the epistemological catastrophe precisely. The quipu was not destroyed by fire (as the Mayan codices were); it was rendered unreadable by the death of the people who could read it. The distinction matters.

## What the Chapter Gets Right

- The quipu is never compared to writing. It is described on its own terms as a different technology with its own logic.
- The quipucamayoc's physical practice (fingers moving along cords, pausing at knots) makes the technology embodied rather than abstract.
- The honest composite disclosure does not undermine the scene's effectiveness.
- The colonial source problem is acknowledged: "Killing the quipucamayoc destroyed both the record and the only people who could read it."

## What Needs Correction

1. **Garcilaso's account:** The chapter cites Garcilaso on quipucamayoc reciting genealogies and histories. This is accurate but should note that Garcilaso's account is itself mediated — he was writing from Spain decades later. His reliability on quipu practice is debated. A single qualifying phrase would suffice.

2. **Color significance:** The chapter mentions cord colors (brown, red, yellow) but does not specify that color in quipus appears to have carried categorical rather than merely aesthetic meaning. The cochineal red of a particular cord may have designated a particular category of information, not simply "red." This is worth a clarifying phrase.

## Verdict: APPROVED WITH CONDITIONS

The conditions are minor: qualify Garcilaso slightly, add a note on color as categorical information. The chapter's treatment of the quipu is the best I have seen in a narrative work — it gets the technology right while making it vivid.
