# Production Notes — The Age of the Great Pyramids

## Era
Fourth Dynasty (~2613-2494 BCE). Sneferu, Khufu, Khafre, Menkaure.

## Structural Engine
**Ma'at-as-maintenance**: The pyramid is not a monument but a cosmological instrument. The structural engine is the feedback loop: pharaoh maintains ma'at -> ma'at ensures the flood -> the flood feeds the people -> the people build the pyramid -> the pyramid ensures the pharaoh's solar ascent -> the ascended pharaoh maintains ma'at. This is a self-reinforcing cycle, and the chapter should trace it through the dynasty as both the logic of the state and the logic of its eventual limits.

The engine's terminal question: what happens when the cost of maintaining the instrument exceeds the capacity of the system? Sneferu builds three pyramids (Meidum, Bent, Red). Khufu builds the Great Pyramid. After Khufu, each pyramid is smaller. The engine is not breaking — it is being recalibrated as the state learns the limits of its own theology.

## Key Figures
- **Merer** — work crew foreman; historical figure from the Wadi al-Jarf papyri. The everyman, the inside voice.
- **Khufu** (Cheops in Greek) — pharaoh of the Great Pyramid. Known almost entirely through his pyramid and later, hostile traditions (Herodotus). The chapter should note the irony: the man who built the largest structure on earth left almost no other record.
- **Imhotep** — architect of Djoser's step pyramid, mentioned in opening context. The first named architect in history.
- **Sneferu** — Khufu's father. Built three pyramids. Remembered as a good king. The experimentalist.
- **Hemiunu** — Khufu's vizier and likely architect of the Great Pyramid. His statue survives.
- **Khafre** — Khufu's successor. Builder of the second Giza pyramid and the Great Sphinx.
- **Menkaure** — builder of the smallest Giza pyramid. "Drunkards of Menkaure" work gang.
- **The work crews** — the collective protagonist. Named gangs, organized labor, the graffiti culture.

## Key Corrections / Panel Feedback Anticipated
1. **NOT slaves.** This is the central factual point. Lehner/Hawass excavations prove organized labor camps, medical care, good nutrition, rotation schedules.
2. **Ma'at is not a "concept."** It is the operating system. The chapter must never frame it as an abstract idea Egyptians "believed in."
3. **The Nile flood is not "agriculture."** It is the annual proof that ma'at holds. The flood is theological before it is agricultural.
4. **Pyramid Texts** — mentioned but belong primarily to Dynasty 5-6. The Fourth Dynasty pyramids are uninscribed inside. This is important and should be noted.
5. **Alignment precision** — the Great Pyramid's base is aligned to true north within 3 arc-minutes. This is specific and verifiable.
6. **Sneferu's progression** — Meidum (possibly his), Bent Pyramid (angle changed mid-construction), Red Pyramid (the first true pyramid). This progression from step to bent to true is the engineering story that makes Khufu's achievement possible.

## Chapter Arc
1. **Opening** — Merer's crew at dawn. The inside voice of the work.
2. **The Precursor: Imhotep and Djoser** — the step pyramid. The first stone mountain. Brief.
3. **Sneferu's Experiments** — three pyramids, the engineering progression, the resources required.
4. **Khufu and the Great Pyramid** — the logistics, the labor, the theology. Hemiunu the architect.
5. **The Plateau of Rostau** — Khafre, the Sphinx, Menkaure. The three pyramids together.
6. **The Workers' World** — the village, the gangs, the graffiti, the food, the medicine. The inside voice.
7. **Ma'at and Its Limits** — after Menkaure, the pyramids shrink. The Fifth Dynasty shifts to sun temples. The engine recalibrates.
8. **Landing** — the flood comes. Ma'at holds. The stone endures.

## Voice Register
Monumental. Rhythmic. Cyclical like the Nile — the prose returns to the flood, the rising, the building, the returning. Weight-bearing sentences. Not the spare steppe voice or the rolling maritime voice. This is STONE voice: heavy, placed, precise, each sentence set like a block in a course. The rhythm should accumulate rather than rush.

## Em-dash Budget
Maximum 8 for the full chapter. Use sparingly — the stone voice uses periods and commas, not parenthetical insertions.

## Achebe Test Notes
- The workers are not "surprisingly" organized. They are simply organized.
- The pyramids are not "remarkable feats of engineering." They are the work that holds the world together.
- Herodotus's later account (slaves, 100,000 workers) should be noted as outsider error if mentioned.
- The inside voice: these people understood themselves as maintaining the cosmos. Not building tombs. Not serving an ego. Maintaining ma'at.
