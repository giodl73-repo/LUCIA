## Innovation Flag

**Innovation: Border-State Voice Register**

The chapter has confirmed a new voice register not previously catalogued in the v1.4 Voice Spectrum list: the border-state voice. This is distinct from all existing registers:

- Not steppe (which is sparse, forward, relentless — no qualification)
- Not court (which is layered, observational, lingering on gesture)
- Not legal-public (which is declarative and closes like pronouncements)
- Not sedimentary (which accumulates like account entries)

The border-state register is: advance-by-retreating. Each paragraph gives a proposition, then qualifies or redirects it, so the analytical point arrives from an unexpected angle after the narrative has already moved past the obvious approach. The em-dash is structurally impossible in this register because the register's pause mechanism is the full stop followed by a new sentence from a different angle — the interruption of the dash would break the rhythm's retreat. The voice is lapidary but not spare; precise but not cold; forward-moving but through apparent withdrawal. It mirrors the Parthian shot as rhetoric.

This innovation should be logged to scoring/INNOVATIONS.md for potential rubric amendment if 2+ similar instances appear.
