# Sources — People's Republic of China

## Key Academic Works
- Frank Dikötter, *Mao's Great Famine* (2010, Walker & Company) — the most thorough account of the Great Leap Forward famine
- Frank Dikötter, *The Cultural Revolution: A People's History, 1962–1976* (2016)
- Yang Jisheng, *Tombstone: The Great Chinese Famine, 1958–1962* (2012, Farrar, Straus) — the Chinese journalist's account; written in secret
- Ezra Vogel, *Deng Xiaoping and the Transformation of China* (2011, Harvard) — the reform era biography
- Perry Link — Chinese intellectual life and censorship; multiple works
- Ian Johnson, *The Souls of China: The Return of Religion After Mao* (2017)
- Elizabeth Economy, *The Third Revolution: Xi Jinping and the New Chinese State* (2018)

## Primary Sources
- Mao Zedong's selected works — carefully read; the official texts and their gaps are both sources
- The Xinhua News Agency — official PRC narrative; essential for understanding what the party wants known
- The Xinjiang Papers (leaked government documents, 2019) — authenticated by multiple independent analysts
- CCP Party Congress resolutions (especially 1981 resolution on Party history, 2021 historical resolution)

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- CIA and State Department declassified files on Cultural Revolution period
- Tiananmen Papers (2001 publication) — authenticity debated; available but contested
- Contemporary oral history of the reform era — Harvard Fairbank Center archive
- PRC State Archives (access extremely restricted; some through Hong Kong before 2020)
