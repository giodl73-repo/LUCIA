# Sources — Aboriginal Australians

## Primary & Oral Tradition
- Yolŋu clan songs and sacred narratives (oral; custodians hold; access through community agreement)
- Arrernte tjukurpa (Dreaming) oral records, as documented in collaboration with Central Land Council
- Palawa kani language revival materials — Tasmanian Aboriginal Centre

## Key Academic Works
- Clive Gamble et al. — settlement dating and the 65,000 BCE Madjedbebe site evidence
- Bill Gammage, *The Biggest Estate on Earth: How Aborigines Made Australia* (2011, Allen & Unwin) — the fire-stick farming thesis; essential
- Bruce Pascoe, *Dark Emu: Black Seeds: Agriculture or Accident?* (2014, Magabala Books) — aquaculture, grain harvesting, village life
- Deborah Bird Rose, *Nourishing Terrains: Australian Aboriginal Views of Landscape and Wilderness* (1996, Australian Heritage Commission)
- W.E.H. Stanner, *White Man Got No Dreaming* (1979) — the foundational anthropological statement on the Dreaming
- Marcia Langton — country, fire management, and land rights; multiple works
- David Trigger — kinship systems and social structure

## Archaeological Sources
- Madjedbebe rock shelter (Northern Territory) — 65,000 BP dating (Clarkson et al., *Nature*, 2017)
- Budj Bim Cultural Landscape (Victoria) — eel aquaculture, 6,600 BP (UNESCO World Heritage documentation)
- Kimberley rock art — Gwion Gwion and Wandjina traditions; dating c. 17,000 BP

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Nation-level oral history recordings (requires community partnership and protocols)
- Contemporary land rights and native title legal documentation
- AIATSIS (Australian Institute of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Studies) archive holdings
- First-hand accounts from Stolen Generations survivors — Bringing Them Home report (1997)
