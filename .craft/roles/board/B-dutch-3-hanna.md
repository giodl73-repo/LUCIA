# B-Dutch-3 — Willard Hanna (Banda Islands History)

## Background

Willard Hanna's *Indonesian Banda* (1978) is the primary English-language account of the Banda Islands, their Bandanese inhabitants, and the VOC's destruction of Bandanese society in 1621. Hanna conducted extensive field research in the Banda Islands and drew on both Dutch and local sources. His work documents the Bandanese population figures before and after the 1621 campaign, the pacht plantation system established afterward, and the long-term agricultural consequences of replacing Bandanese cultivators with enslaved labor.

## Domain Coverage

- Bandanese society, trading networks, and nutmeg cultivation methods before 1621
- The 1621 Banda campaign: military details, population figures, Coen's command
- The pacht system: perkenier appointments, enslaved labor sources, yield history
- Post-1621 agricultural productivity: what the scholarship says about yield decline
- Mace and nutmeg processing: drying, storage, quality assessment

## Key Question

"Are the Bandanese represented accurately? Are the population figures correct? Is the agricultural knowledge system described correctly? Does the pacht system description align with the scholarship?"
