# Sources — Teke / Bateke

## Primary

- Stanley, Henry Morton. *The Congo and the Founding of its Free State*. 2 vols. 1885. — Stanley's account of the Makoko treaty
- Brazza's expedition reports — French colonial archives; de Brazza's accounts of the Pool region
- Colonial administrative records — Archives nationales d'outre-mer; Belgian Congo records

## Academic

- Vansina, Jan. *The Tio Kingdom of the Middle Congo 1880–1892*. 1973. Oxford University Press. — the most thorough study of Teke political culture
- Vansina, Jan. *Paths in the Rainforests: Toward a History of Political Tradition in Equatorial Africa*. 1990. University of Wisconsin Press. — broad regional context
- Martin, Phyllis M. *The External Trade of the Loango Coast 1576–1870*. 1972. Oxford University Press. — the Loango coast trade system; Teke context
- Harms, Robert. *River of Wealth, River of Sorrow: The Central Zaire Basin in the Era of the Slave and Ivory Trade, 1500–1891*. 1981. Yale University Press.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on Teke political theology — the onkani concept and the Makoko's ritual authority
- Archaeological work on the Teke plateau settlements and trade sites
- Analysis of the Makoko-Stanley treaty in its Teke as well as colonial-legal context
- Recent ethnographic work on Teke communities in the Republic of Congo
