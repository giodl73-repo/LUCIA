## Manual Pass — Implicit Judgment, Hidden Hierarchy, Anachronistic Morality

**Finding 1 — P3 (borderline, author decides):**
Chapter, Section 2: "What followed was harder than Valencia's arrangement. The island's Muslim population had no acequia system the conquerors needed, no agricultural expertise that was irreplaceable. A significant portion was enslaved or expelled."

The sentence structure ("had no... that was irreplaceable") could be read as implying the Majorcan Muslims' fate was justified by their economic dispensability. The chapter's intent is to render James's calculation, not to endorse it. The current phrasing is accurate — James's reasoning was explicitly economic — but the construction "had no X that was irreplaceable" carries a passive implication that their fate was therefore appropriate.

Suggested approach: Add one phrase that registers the Majorcan Muslim community's existence on its own terms, separate from James's calculation. Currently they exist only as an object of James's reasoning. Even one clause — "The Majorcan Muslims, who had governed an island for generations, found no such compact waiting for them" — would restore their agency as subjects rather than objects. Author decides.

**Finding 2 — CLEAR:**
The chapter explicitly frames the mudéjar arrangement as "not tolerance" and "arrangement" — this is correct and prevents the romanticization failure mode. No implicit ranking of the arrangement as morally superior to what it replaced.

**Finding 3 — CLEAR:**
Abu Zayd's conversion is rendered without judgment ("Was it sincere? The sources do not answer"). Correct.

**Finding 4 — CLEAR:**
The "conditions changed. They always do." closing note names the future without moralizing it. No verdict on whether the eventual change was inevitable, just, or unjust.

**Finding 5 — P3 (borderline):**
Figures.md, *Justicia* section: "The chapter renders it at the moment of its formation: not yet the famous institution it would become, but already a real constraint on the king who had just ridden into Valencia."

"Famous institution" is slightly forward-looking — a framing from outside the era's own understanding. The *Justicia* in 1238 would not have been called "famous" by anyone present. The sentence is in figures.md rather than chapter.md, which is a reference section; this softens the concern. Author decides whether to revise.
