# Panel Review — R1-P5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski

**Chapter:** Ghana Empire — Era 2: The Age of Gold (opening.md)
**Voice:** Ryszard Kapuscinski — Sensory immersion, heat, smell, sound, texture
**Stage:** PANEL-1

---

The opening earns its place. The weight leaving the spine — not as metaphor but as physical fact — is the right entry. Three months in a saddle do something to a human back, and this chapter begins with that specific thing. The clay-water taste, the drums traveling "in the chest rather than the ears" — these are correct sensory choices. Not decorative. Not generic Saharan texture. Specific to the body receiving them.

The merchant's gratitude for bad water is the detail that convinced me. A man who has crossed forty days of sand is grateful for brackish clay water. He does not say it is bad. He drinks it and is glad. This is embodied knowledge, the kind that comes from having been somewhere or from having imagined it truly.

**The question I'd push on:** The full chapter must maintain this standard throughout. The gold road, the desert crossing, the Taghaza salt mines — these must have heat and weight and specific sensory character. Not atmosphere. Character. The body of a man cutting salt from a Saharan mine knows things that a general description of Taghaza cannot convey. The chapter must give that body its due.

The quiet just before the negotiation — "The merchant's back ached. He had slept on the mat in the merchant quarter and woken already calculating" — this is good. The physical exhaustion is not resolved; it continues into the next day's commerce. The body persists. Keep this.
