# BOARD Review — B-1: Jos Gommans
## Durrani / Era 1: The Founding

**Reviewer:** Jos J.L. Gommans — Leiden University; specialist in Mughal military history, Afghan history, and the Indo-Persian world. Author of *The Rise of the Indo-Afghan Empire* (1995) and *Mughal Warfare* (2002). Primary expertise: the Abdali/Durrani confederation and its military organization; the relationship between the Pashtun tribal system and the Mughal-Persianate political framework.

**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

## Factual Accuracy

**The Loya Jirga (1747):** The chapter's account of the founding jirga is accurate in its essentials. The location (Kandahar), the timing (late 1747/1160 AH), Ahmad Shah's Sadozai lineage, and the role of Sabir Shah as religious legitimizer are all consistent with the primary chronicles (Mahmud al-Husaini's *Tarikh-i Ahmad Shahi*; Elphinstone's later account). The green-wheat crown detail is well-attested in Persian sources and correctly rendered.

**Correction required:** The chapter identifies Hajji Jamal Khan as "of the Mohammadzai branch of the Barakzai." Technically, "Mohammadzai" refers to a sub-division of the Barakzai tribe; this is correct. However, the chapter's later phrasing conflates "Mohammadzai" and "Barakzai" interchangeably, which can create confusion. The Barakzai were one of the major Durrani tribes; the Mohammadzai were the dominant clan within the Barakzai. The distinction matters for understanding Era 2's succession crisis. Recommend: be consistent with "Barakzai" as the tribal name and "Mohammadzai" as the clan name within it.

**The Panipat figure of Ahmad Shah's force:** The chapter states "40,000 Durrani and allied forces." This is within the range of scholarly estimates (30,000–60,000 is the wider scholarly range; most estimates cluster around 40,000 for the Durrani-Afghan-Rohilla combined force). No correction required; the estimate is defensible.

**"Nine campaigns into the subcontinent":** Scholarly counts vary (Ganda Singh counts 8 major expeditions; others count up to 9 depending on definitional criteria). The chapter's "nine times" is at the high end of the defensible range. Recommend: revise to "eight or nine times into the subcontinent" to reflect the historiographic uncertainty.

## Cultural Representation

The chapter's rendering of Pashtunwali as an operating system rather than a set of customs is exactly right and reflects the current scholarly consensus (Barfield, Tapper, Lindholm). The franchise metaphor for the consensus-distribution mechanism is not a term used in the academic literature but accurately describes the mechanism that Barfield calls the "tribal confederation with conditional loyalty."

The bilingual Persianate/Pashtun court is well-rendered. The nastaʿlīq script detail is correct for the period (the chancery would have used Persian nastaʿlīq). The Ahmad Shah poetry fragment is rendered in a style consistent with the surviving ghazals, though "the name that remains / is the name that the dust could not hold" is a composite rendering rather than a specific attributed text. Recommend noting in figures.md that the couplet is a period-consistent synthesis rather than a specific attributed verse.

## What the Chapter Gets Right

The terminal condition analysis — the gap between military reach and administrative capacity — is precisely the argument Gommans develops in *The Rise of the Indo-Afghan Empire*: the Durrani system was a "war state" whose revenues were predicated on continued expansion and whose administrative infrastructure was insufficient to sustain a settled empire. The chapter has arrived at this independently and rendered it accurately and accessibly.

The Vishwasrao death as the battle's structural pivot is historiographically defensible — contemporary Maratha sources confirm the psychological impact of his death on the army's cohesion.

## No Further Required Changes

The correction to "Barakzai/Mohammadzai" terminology and the revision of "nine" to "eight or nine" are the only factual adjustments required. The chapter is otherwise historically sound and culturally well-rendered.

**Board recommendation: APPROVED with minor corrections.**
