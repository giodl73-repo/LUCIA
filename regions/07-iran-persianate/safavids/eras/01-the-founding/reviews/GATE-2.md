## Innovations Noted

**Potential innovation — Dual-register sourcing as structural technique:** The chapter uses two primary sources from within the same figure's voice at different moments of his life: the early *Divan-i Khatai* poem ("I am the living Khidr") and the later *Divan-i Khatai* poem ("My heart is like the bird that has fallen from the nest"). The contrast between the same source — same author, same pen name, same tradition — in two registers (divine claim vs. mourning) does analytical work without authorial commentary. The source asymmetry across time within a single figure becomes evidence of the chapter's terminal condition. This extends the "source asymmetry as technique" principle (v1.5 amendment, Evidence dimension) into the temporal dimension: not just asymmetry between sources, but asymmetry within a single source across time.
