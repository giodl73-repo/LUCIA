## Re-explanation Scan

**Finding 1 — P3 (minor):** In the Danelaw section, after the detailed account of the baptism, the chapter writes:

> "That is the hinge. Not the battle but the baptism. Not the defeat but the naming."

This sentence explicitly names what the narrative has already shown. The preceding account of the baptism and the naming has demonstrated the hinge without assistance. The sentence is rhetorical — it functions as a structural signal for the reader — but it edges toward telling what the scene already showed. However, in the Saga-Spare register, such plain declarative structural turns are a legitimate feature of the voice (the Chronicle itself uses this form). P3 — author decides. The sentence earns its place if read as part of the voice's deliberate plainness rather than as re-explanation.

**Finding 2 — P1:** In the "Shape of the Era" section, the chapter includes:

> "This matters because language is not merely communication. A shared written language is a shared legal medium, a shared administrative medium, a shared literary tradition."

The preceding paragraph has already demonstrated exactly this — the West Saxon dialect becoming the prestige written form, texts circulating, the administrative and legal standardization. The sentences named here (legal medium, administrative medium, literary tradition) have been shown in concrete detail in Section 4. This passage explains what the evidence already demonstrated. Cut: "This matters because language is not merely communication. A shared written language is a shared legal medium, a shared administrative medium, a shared literary tradition." The following sentence ("When Alfred insisted...") can follow directly from the preceding paragraph on West Saxon as prestige dialect, and the argument stands without the re-explanation.
