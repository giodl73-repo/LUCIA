# R2-P5 — Kapuscinski — Panel-2 Review of Notes

**Chapter:** Arameans — Era 2: The Age of the Aramean States

The plan addresses my concerns from Panel-1. Sections II and V are designated for sensory caravan-road content. The landing will close with a specific image.

**What the plan still needs:**

The camel. Section II names it but the notes do not describe it. I want the animal. The broad flat feet that do not sink into the steppe dust. The load on its back: bolts of cloth, copper ingots, sealed jars of oil. The pace, slower than a horse but relentless, covering distance the horse cannot cover because the camel carries its own water. The sound of the caravan: the bells, the creaking of the pack frames, the grunting of the animals at the water stop. This is not decoration. The camel IS the Aramean network. Without the animal, the network is abstract.

The Ghouta needs to be inhabited when the chapter visits it. The orchards should have workers. The irrigation channels should have water moving in them. The Barada should have a sound: the rush of the river emerging from the Anti-Lebanon, slowing as it spreads across the irrigated plain.

The metalworkers of Dammasq need to be present. The iron that fitted the chariots was worked somewhere. The hammering, the heat of the forge, the charcoal smoke. A chariot city has a sound and a smell, and both are iron.

**Specific sensory targets for the chapter:**
- Section I: Sound of Dammasq (market, metalwork, livestock)
- Section II: The camel, the road, the dust, the water stops
- Section III: Qarqar battlefield (the Orontes valley, the chariot lines, the noise)
- Section IV: The Tel Dan stone: the physical object, the basalt, the carved letters
- Section V: The merchant writing: the leather, the ink, the Aramaic letters forming under the reed
- Section VI: The road at dusk, the caravan moving east, the language traveling in the merchant's mouth
