## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Romani Era 1 Opening

**Overall:** The opening identifies a structural condition — a people whose origin is recorded not in chronicles but in vocabulary — and makes that condition its analytical engine. This is promising. But I want more from the causal reasoning. Why did these particular people leave? The opening says the question is unanswerable, which is honest, but it does not yet push on the structural conditions that made northwestern India a place of departure in the eleventh century.

**What works:**
- The reframing of the source absence is intellectually precise: "They were not the kind of people for whom chronicles were kept." This is structural analysis, not sentiment. It identifies the social position that produces silence in the record.
- The three hypotheses for departure (Dom caste, Ghazni's armies, general upheaval) are correctly presented as alternatives without false resolution. This is scholarly discipline applied with narrative skill.
- The linguistic stratigraphy is genuinely analytical: loanword layers as evidence of movement duration and direction. This treats linguistics as a system of evidence, not as decoration.

**What doesn't:**
- The opening presents the departure as a mystery but does not place it within the larger system of population movement in the eleventh-century subcontinent. Mahmud of Ghazni's raids are mentioned but not their structural effect: the disruption of the northwestern Indian economy, the destruction of temple networks that organized labor and craft, the displacement of communities whose social functions were tied to courts that no longer existed. The departure needs context not to explain it away but to make the mystery sharper.
- The concept of asabiyyah — group solidarity — is relevant here but unstated. What held these people together during a thousand-year journey? The language did, clearly. But language alone does not sustain a people. The kinship system, the craft specialization, the social code that later became the kris — these are the structural mechanisms of cohesion. The opening hints at this but does not name it.

**The question I'd push on:**
What is the structural engine of this chapter? The road? The language? The social position of a people outside the structures that produce written records? The chapter needs to decide. Language-as-witness is a technique, not a mechanism. What is the mechanism that allows a people to walk for three centuries across half a continent and arrive still themselves?
