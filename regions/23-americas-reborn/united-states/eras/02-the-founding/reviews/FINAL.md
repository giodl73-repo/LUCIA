## Innovation Log Entry

**Innovation candidate:** Founding-Paradox Hybrid (Legal-Public register variant)

**Category:** Voice
**Chapter:** United States, Era 2: The Founding

**Description:** The Legal-Public register, previously validated for Roman eras, was adapted for a culture whose founding act was simultaneously its founding paradox. The key technique: stating the paradox (founding principles + slavery) in the same declarative register as the constitutional mechanism, without tonal rupture. Where the Roman Legal-Public voice transforms across eras, this variant holds contradictory elements in the same sentence in the same voice: "Jefferson wrote this in the second week of June 1776, working in rented rooms in Philadelphia, revising a draft he had completed in seventeen days. He was thirty-three years old and had brought 175 people to Virginia in chains who had no say in whether he went or stayed."

This is the founding paradox stated as legal fact, in the Legal-Public register, without a shift to moral or sentimental register. The technique is distinct from: (1) Roman Legal-Public (which transforms; does not hold contradiction simultaneously); (2) Epistolary-Legal (which argues from shared premises; here the premises themselves are the contradiction). A second instance in a culture with a written founding document and an structural founding contradiction (Haitian Republic, South African constitutional transition) would be needed for full promotion.
