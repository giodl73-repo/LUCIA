**Scorer:** SCRIBE
**Date:** 2026-04-16

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with a scribe pressing reed into wet clay, the smell of damp earth and lamp oil, the wind off the plateau. A specific physical moment in the room where the edict is being dictated. Wholly anchored. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Hittite world emerges through its own logic: treaties as divine covenants, gods as punishers of oath-breakers, the court assembly as constitutional body, the Labarna title. No outside frame. The reader learns the treaty-culture from inside, never explained. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | Zero outside judgment. The edict is presented in its own theological terms. No "remarkable" or "sophisticated." The culture's reasoning (divine punishment for royal murder) is presented as cause-and-effect, not belief system. Inside-voice honesty. |
| Texture   | 4/5   | Damp earth, lamp oil, wind off the plateau, the Lion Gate, the Storm God's temple. Strong sensory grounding in the opening lines. Could use slightly more material detail in the middle sections (the clay tablet itself, the act of distribution). |
| Voice     | 5/5   | Treaty-speak, proclamation weight. Declarative sentences that close like legal pronouncements. The direct quotation from the edict anchors the register. Colons and periods do the pause work. Zero em-dashes. The voice carries both narrative and analytical weight in the same register. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | "Whether it worked is the question the edict itself could not answer." The entire opening is a question posed: can a written document stop succession murder? The final image — the tablet on the wall vs. the knife in the dark — creates genuine, culture-specific pull. |
| **Total** | **29/30** | |

**Notes:** Exceptionally strong opening. The voice finds a register that belongs to the Hittite treaty tradition: declarative, covenant-weighted, forensic. The structural engine (constitutional drama) is already visible. The one area for development is additional sensory texture in the middle paragraphs where the analytical argument about divine punishment could be further grounded in physical detail. The direct quotation from the edict is well deployed as the centerpiece.
