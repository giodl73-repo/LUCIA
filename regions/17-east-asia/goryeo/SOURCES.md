# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *Goryeo-sa* (History of Goryeo) — the official history
- *Samguk Yusa* (Iryeon) — compiled under Goryeo
- Haeinsa Tripitaka — the woodblock canon; catalogues and studies
- Goryeo celadon — museum collections and archaeological studies

## Key Academic Works

- Ki-baik Lee, *A New History of Korea* (1984)
- Robert Buswell — Korean Buddhist studies
- John Frankl — Goryeo political history
- Edward Willett Wagner — Goryeo aristocracy
- Henny Savenije — Goryeo celadon studies

## Needed

- Studies on the Goryeo military regime and the Choe family
- Goryeo-Mongol relations studies — the dual monarchy arrangement
- Recent Haeinsa preservation and Tripitaka scholarship
