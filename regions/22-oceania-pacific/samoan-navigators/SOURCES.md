# Sources — Samoan Navigators

## Key Academic Works
- Epeli Hau'ofa, *We Are the Ocean* (2008, University of Hawai'i Press)
- Margaret Mead, *Coming of Age in Samoa* (1928) — foundational but contested; read alongside Derek Freeman's critique and the subsequent debate
- Derek Freeman, *Margaret Mead and Samoa* (1983) — the controversy; context for the anthropological record
- Bradd Shore, *Sala'ilua: A Samoan Mystery* (1982, Columbia) — fa'asamoa, the matai system, and conflict
- Malama Meleisea, *The Making of Modern Samoa* (1987, USP) — the definitive history of Western Samoa
- Robert Louis Stevenson — *A Footnote to History* (1892) — the 1888–89 colonial crisis; unusually perceptive contemporary account
- Penelope Schoeffel — women's roles and the fine mat exchange system

## Primary Sources
- Samoan oral traditions (tala fa'asolopito) — held by matai families; require community protocols for access
- London Missionary Society records (1830–1900) — contact and conversion; housed at SOAS, London
- German colonial records — Samoan administration 1900–1914; Bundesarchiv, Berlin
- New Zealand National Archives — Mau movement records; League of Nations mandate

## Archaeological Sources
- Pulemelei Mound, Savai'i — largest ancient structure in Polynesia; dating and purpose still debated
- Tonga-Samoa corridor Lapita sites

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
