# R2-P1 — Barbara Tuchman
## Panel Review — Notes: La Violencia

**Round:** 2 (PANEL-2)
**Reviewer:** Barbara Tuchman (P-1)
**Chapter:** Colombia, Era 3: La Violencia — notes.md

---

The notes answer my question from PANEL-1. There are now two figures to carry us into the interior: the bandolero (El Cóndor or equivalent) and the composite campesino. The section plan has structure and arc. I am satisfied with the framework.

My pressure points at this stage are craft:

**The "La Máquina" section (Section 2) is the most dangerous.** The pájaros system, the municipality apparatus, the landlord-party boss-credit controller — this is structural analysis at its most necessary and most lethal to narrative. The notes correctly identify Líbano, Tolima, as the anchor. I need the chapter to open Section 2 in a specific moment in Líbano, not in an abstract description of the machinery. A specific alcalde making a specific decision. A specific pájaro arriving in a specific town. The machinery must be shown operating, not described as a system.

**The "La Gaitanada" section (Section 5) worries me.** Five hundred words on what Gaitán proposed — his economic program, his vision — risks becoming the chapter's sentimental pause. The "what might have been" is always a temptation and almost always a trap. I want this section to show us what was destroyed through the absence, not through description of what was planned. One specific scene: a village where Gaitán had spoken six months before the assassination, and what that village looked like in 1950. The absence as physical reality, not as lost program.

**The landing is correct in concept.** García Márquez's colonel waiting for the letter that never comes is the right image — the culture's own persistence as waiting. I want the chapter to land there cleanly. Do not overexplain. The colonel waits. The FARC is coming, but we do not name it. Name the future, do not tell it. The reader will know.

**The section on women** (Open Question 3) must be present. Not as a separate section but woven into Section 3 or Section 2. The CNMH documentation is thorough. One specific incident, named, from the testimonial record. The right register is not horror — it is the matter-of-fact accumulation of the discover voice. What happened, stated. No adverbs.

Overall: the notes are a sound production bible. The chapter is ready to write.
