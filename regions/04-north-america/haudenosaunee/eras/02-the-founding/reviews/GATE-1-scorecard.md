# GATE-1 Scorecard

**Chapter:** Haudenosaunee — The Founding (Kaianere'kó:wa)
**Scorer:** LUCIA Pipeline
**Date:** 2026-04-14

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with Atotarho sitting in the smoke of his fire, serpents moving in his hair. Specific person, specific place, specific moment. The reader is dropped into the scene without preamble. |
| World     | 5/5   | The culture's own logic governs entirely. The Mourning War cycle is explained from inside — the empty seats in the longhouse, the obligation to fill them. The condolence ceremony's function is presented as self-evident, not as a curiosity. The longhouse-as-political-philosophy emerges naturally. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | No forbidden words. No outside judgment. The serpents in Atotarho's hair are not treated as "myth" or "legend" — they are presented as the tradition presents them, which is as a description of what grief does to a person. No romanticization, no condescension. |
| Texture   | 4/5   | The fire, the smoke, the serpents, the lake, the forest, the combing — sensory grounding is strong. Could use one more non-visual sensory detail (sound of the clearing, weight of the wampum). The texture is present but not yet dense. |
| Voice     | 5/5   | Deliberative, patient, building through repetition. "They went to the Oneida, who listened. They went to the Cayuga, who listened. They went to the Seneca, who listened." This is oral-tradition rhythm, the cadence of a story told around a fire. The prose never breaks into textbook mode. |
| Pull      | 4/5   | The Atotarho scene creates genuine tension. The resolution (the combing of the serpents, the offer of the central fire) is earned. The closing image of the tree and the weapons is strong. Pull is real but could be slightly stronger if the stakes were made more visceral before the resolution. |
| **Total** | **28/30** | |

**Notes:** The opening passes the Gate comfortably. Its greatest strength is voice — the deliberative, oral-tradition rhythm is consistent and belongs to this story. The choice to open with Atotarho rather than the Peacemaker is effective: it begins with the problem (a mind twisted by grief, the cycle of violence) and builds to the solution (condolence, consensus, the Great Law). The single em-dash is well within the limit of 5. Texture is the area with most room for growth in the full chapter — more sound, smell, and tactile detail from the northeastern woodlands would ground the political philosophy in physical reality.
