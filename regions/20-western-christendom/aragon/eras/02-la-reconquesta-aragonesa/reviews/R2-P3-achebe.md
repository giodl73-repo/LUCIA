# PANEL-2 — P-3 Chinua Achebe
## R2-P3-achebe.md

**Chapter:** Aragon Era 2 — La Reconquesta Aragonesa
**Stage:** PANEL-2 (notes review — targeted)
**Reviewer:** Chinua Achebe

---

The notes have heard me.

Zayyan ibn Mardanish has a history now — not just a name. The notes record that he came from a family that had governed Valencia before the Almohads, that his grandfather was the Wolf King, that he spent the rest of his life in exile still calling himself king of Valencia. That is a man. That is a man with a loss that is specific and biographical, not merely geopolitical. The chapter must render that.

The acequia amin as composite figure is correctly handled. The notes disclose the composite nature in advance, which is honest — and the disclosure will go into figures.md where it belongs. The composite is legitimate when the institution is real and documented, as this one is. The Tribunal de las Aguas as a living institution with roots in this moment is exactly the kind of cultural persistence detail that the landing section should carry.

The landing as written in the notes is strong. The amin checking the channel in late autumn, the Catalan notary, the record made without shared language — that is a specific image that does the work of multiple paragraphs of analysis without performing analysis. It shows that the knowledge system continued. It shows the awkwardness of the transition. It shows that the water still ran. This is the right way to close.

Two remaining concerns:

First — the section on Majorca (Section 4, "The Island Logic") needs to include at least one line about what happened to the Muslim population of Majorca after 1229. If the chapter is going to use Majorca as the template for the Valencia strategy, the reader needs to know that the Majorcan arrangement was less generous — a significant portion of the Muslim population was enslaved or expelled — and that Valencia represented a different calculus. Without this, the chapter implies that pactisme was the default, when in fact it was a choice James made differently in different contexts, for different reasons. That specificity is more honest and more interesting.

Second — the mudéjar community's decision to stay needs more interiority. The notes say "mostly stay. Why. The orchards are their orchards." That is correct and important. But the chapter needs to let one representative of that community articulate the decision from inside their own understanding — not as James's calculation, but as their own assessment of the risk. Staying under Aragonese rule was a bet. They were betting that the compact would hold, that the protections James promised were real, that the Aragonese needed them enough to protect them. That is a calculation from inside a very specific political experience. The chapter should find it.

The production bible is ready.
