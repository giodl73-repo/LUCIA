# Sources — Argentina

## Key Academic Works
- Tulio Halperin Donghi, *Politics, Economics and Society in Argentina in the Revolutionary Period* (1975, Cambridge)
- Daniel James, *Resistance and Integration: Peronism and the Argentine Working Class* (1988, Cambridge) — the definitive study of Peronism and labor
- Jacobo Timerman, *Prisoner Without a Name, Cell Without a Number* (1981) — the Proceso from inside a journalist's experience; essential
- CONADEP (National Commission on the Disappearance of Persons), *Nunca Más* (1984) — the definitive documentation of the military dictatorship
- Victor Bulmer-Thomas, *The Economic History of Latin America since Independence* (3rd ed., 2014) — Argentina's economic cycles in regional context
- Jorge Luis Borges — the Argentine intellectual tradition; *Labyrinths* and others (not history, but the texture of the world)

## Primary Sources
- Juan Perón's speeches and writings
- Eva Perón, *La Razón de Mi Vida (My Mission in Life)* (1952)
- Testimonios from the ESMA — the memory site archive
- *Nunca Más* (CONADEP, 1984) — the truth commission report
