# BOARD-B1 — Denise Schaan (model) — Review of The Teso Florescence

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Social archaeology of the Marajoara Florescence period; Camutins chiefdom; ceramic typology and burial differentiation.

---

## Historical Accuracy

- **Chronology:** The chapter's date range (c. 700–1000 CE) is consistent with current radiocarbon dating. The overview's claim of c. 700 as the beginning of the Florescence aligns with the phase transitions visible in the Camutins stratigraphic sequence.

- **Camutins complex:** The chapter's description of "more than thirty individual mounds, the largest covering several hectares" is accurate to the published settlement survey. The phrase "five meters above the floodplain" for the teso height is within the documented range.

- **Female specialist burials:** The chapter's claim that "the specialist-level burials at Camutins are disproportionately female" is accurately grounded in the burial evidence. My analysis of the Camutins mortuary data does show that the most elaborate urns are associated with female burials. The chapter does not overclaim: it says "disproportionately female" and "probable," which is the correct level of certainty.

- **Secondary burial:** The description of the secondary burial practice — decomposition followed by painting of cleaned bones with red pigment, then placement in the urn — is accurate to the osteological and ceramic evidence from Teso dos Bichos.

- **Tanga:** The figures.md treatment of the tanga is careful. The chapter's brief mention in the notes (not developing it in the chapter itself) is a defensible editorial decision. The tanga's association with female ceremonial life and its presence in both use and burial contexts is accurately represented.

- **Visual language description:** The characterization of the visual language elements (spiral, paired-eye motif, zoomorphic figures) is consistent with my typology. The chapter correctly identifies these as elements of a coherent system rather than decorative variety.

## Cultural Authenticity

The chapter's central interpretive claim — that the ceramic knowledge was a knowledge system transmitting cosmological authority through female lineages — goes slightly beyond what the evidence strictly demonstrates but remains within the range of defensible inference. My own work argues for the chiefdom model with female elites at the apex; the chapter's "lineage system" framing is compatible with this interpretation.

The honest acknowledgment that the chiefdom vs. heterarchy debate exists (in the notes) while not engaging it in the chapter itself is the correct editorial choice.

## What the Chapter Gets Right

- The structural principle: the teso as accumulated community, not as a monument to a leader. This is the most important interpretive insight in recent Marajoara scholarship and the chapter deploys it correctly.
- The urn-before-death as a cultural fact rather than a morbid curiosity. This is authentic to what the burial record tells us about preparation and anticipation.
- The treatment of the anonymous figures: the chapter does not invent names or invent interior lives. It derives the figures' presence from the logic of what the objects require.

## What Needs Correction

1. **Teso count at Camutins:** The chapter says "more than thirty individual mounds." The current site survey puts the number at approximately forty to fifty discrete mound features, depending on how mound boundaries are defined. The chapter's "more than thirty" is accurate as a lower bound but understates the complex's extent. Suggest: "more than forty individual mounds" or "dozens of individual mounds."

2. **"A grown woman could fit inside it":** This evocative description is accurate for the largest Florescence-period urns (the largest documented reach approximately 80–90 cm in height and 50–60 cm in diameter). However, not all Florescence urns are this large; many are considerably smaller. The chapter should be read as describing the largest examples, which is accurate, but could be clearer: "the largest standing nearly a meter tall."

3. **Slip preparation sequence:** The chapter's description of the slip preparation (red, black, white) is generally accurate, but the claim that white kaolin slip "must be applied at the right moment in the drying sequence" is an inference from ceramic behavior rather than a documented Marajoara practice. This should be flagged as inference in figures.md.

## Verdict

**APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS**

Conditions: (1) correct the teso count; (2) clarify that the urn size description refers to the largest examples; (3) flag the kaolin slip timing claim as inference. All three are minor. The chapter is ready for FINAL after these adjustments.
