## B-3 — Barjamovic (model) — Board Review of The Merchants of Ashur

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Trade route geography, caravan logistics, Anatolian political geography of the Old Assyrian colony period.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The route description (northeast from Ashur, across the steppe, into the Anatolian highlands) is consistent with the itinerary evidence. The general trajectory is correct.
- The journey time of six to eight weeks is a reasonable estimate for the Ashur-to-Kanesh route, consistent with the distance (roughly 1,000 to 1,200 km by road) and donkey travel speed.
- The description of donkeys as the primary transport animal is correct. Donkey load capacity of 60-90 kg is within the range estimated from the tablet evidence.
- The mountain pass seasonality and the risk of wintering on the wrong side are consistent with the geographic evidence. The Taurus passes are snow-closed in winter months.
- The description of pastoralist groups along the route and the need to negotiate passage is consistent with the letters mentioning delays and negotiations.
- Kanesh's location in a broad valley with a volcanic peak visible to the south (Erciyes Dag / Mount Argaeus) is geographically correct.

**What needs attention:**
- The chapter describes the route as running "northeast from Ashur." The primary route actually ran more northward initially, crossing the Tigris and ascending through the upper Tigris region before turning northwest toward the Taurus passes. The northeast direction is an oversimplification. Consider "north from Ashur, crossing the Tigris and climbing through the upper river country before turning toward the Taurus passes."
- The steppe between Ashur and the Taurus is described but the chapter does not mention the intermediate stops that are well attested in the tablets: Hahhum on the Euphrates crossing, and the way-station towns where the caravans could resupply and rest. A brief mention would strengthen the sense of the route as a known, structured itinerary rather than a wilderness crossing.
- The description of terrain change at the highlands is generally correct but could note that the Anatolian plateau itself is a high, dry landscape, not solely a mountainous one. The valley around Kanesh is approximately 1,000 meters in elevation and has a continental climate quite different from the Mesopotamian lowlands.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The road section treats the caravan journey with appropriate seriousness. The knowledge of the route as earned, embodied knowledge is well presented.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS. Correct the route direction and consider adding intermediate stopping points.
