# Sources

## Primary Sources

- I Jing, *A Record of the Buddhist Religion as Practised in India and the Malay Archipelago* (671–695 CE)
- Kedukan Bukit inscription (683 CE) — earliest Srivijayan inscription in Old Malay
- Telaga Batu inscription — Srivijayan oath of loyalty
- Arab geographical sources (al-Masudi, Ibn Khordadbeh) on Zabaj/Srivijaya

## Key Academic Works

- O.W. Wolters, *Early Indonesian Commerce* (1967) and *History, Culture, and Region in Southeast Asian Perspectives* (1982)
- Kenneth Hall, *Maritime Trade and State Development in Early Southeast Asia* (1985)
- Pierre-Yves Manguin — Srivijayan archaeology and port sites
- Jan Wisseman Christie — Srivijayan political economy
- Hermann Kulke — Srivijayan state formation and mandala model

## Needed

- Recent archaeological surveys of Palembang and Jambi sites
- Studies on the transition from Srivijaya to Majapahit maritime dominance
- Analysis of Srivijayan Buddhist scholarship networks
