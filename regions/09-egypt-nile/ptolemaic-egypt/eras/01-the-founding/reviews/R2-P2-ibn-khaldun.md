## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Targeted Review of Notes (PANEL-2)

**Focus:** The economic base; the terminal condition.

The structural engine (symbol-accumulation as legitimacy) is correctly identified. The mechanism (self-reinforcing accumulation) is sound. But the notes must address two things the chapter needs.

**The economic base:**
Ptolemy's legitimacy construction was funded by real wealth. Egypt under the Ptolemies was the richest single territory in the Mediterranean, and the wealth was specific: grain from the Delta and the Fayum, papyrus (an Egyptian monopoly), linen, glass, gold from Nubian sources still partially accessible. The tax system Ptolemy inherited from the Persians (and reformed) was extractive and efficient. The chapter must show that the symbols (Library, temples, festivals) were built on material surplus, not on cleverness alone. A section of the chapter, likely Section 3 (Building Alexandria), should ground the construction in economic reality.

**The terminal condition:**
The notes correctly defer the terminal condition to later eras. But the first era must plant it. I see three structural vulnerabilities:
1. The dual-legitimacy system requires a ruler who can operate in both registers. A ruler who fails in either one (neglects the temples or alienates the Greek settlers) loses half his base. This is a competence requirement, and competence is not hereditary.
2. The accumulation engine requires ongoing investment. Temples must be maintained. The Library must be funded. The festivals must continue. Each generation must do more than the last, or the legitimacy stalls. This is a resource escalation trap.
3. The Egyptian priestly establishment cooperates because cooperation serves its interests (temple restoration, land grants, tax exemptions). If the state can no longer afford those concessions, the cooperation ends.

The chapter should plant all three vulnerabilities in the narrative without resolving them. They are seeds for Eras 2-4.

**Verdict:** The notes are analytically sound. The chapter must add economic grounding and plant the terminal conditions.
