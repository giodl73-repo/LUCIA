# PANEL-1 Review — P1: Barbara Tuchman
## Mexico Era 4 — El Porfiriato y La Revolución

**Lens:** Narrative craft — pacing, scene construction, arc

---

The opening earns attention. The Plan de Ayala as the scene's first object is a craft choice of real intelligence: a document that is simultaneously the scene, the argument, and the era's entire problem. The prose does not explain the Plan — it lets the Plan demonstrate itself through what Zapata did with the village records.

What works: the pacing from the iron box in the church floor through the courtroom defeats to the planted fields is exactly right — each step in the sequence adds pressure, so that when Zapata plants the contested land and waits, the reader waits with him. The Díaz exile on the Ypiranga has precisely the right weight of irony without editorializing. The final three lines compress the chapter's entire arc into an image that the chapter will be measured against.

What I would push on: the opening spans 1810 and 1917 and 1920 in rapid succession near the end. For readers coming to Mexico fresh, the revolution's multiple stages — Madero, Huerta, Villa, Carranza, Obregón — are listed rather than dramatized. This is appropriate for an opening, but the chapter will need to give each movement its own human moment: not just its political function, but a body in a specific place. The risk in the chapter is that the Revolution's complexity becomes a list of names rather than a progression of lived experience.

The corrido-narrative voice is a strong find. The period-as-cadence-stop is working. Watch for whether it can sustain through dense political analysis without slipping into the journalistic neutral.

**The question I'd push on:** The opening centers Zapata, which is right. But Villa — who operated from the north with entirely different politics and a different relationship to land — is named once in passing. If the chapter's engine is the covenant between the Revolution and the villages, Villa's northern populism (which was not primarily about ejido land) will either complicate or test that engine. How does the chapter hold both?
