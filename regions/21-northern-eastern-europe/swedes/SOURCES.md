# Sources — Swedes

## Primary Sources

- Adam of Bremen, *Gesta Hammaburgensis Ecclesiae Pontificum* — c. 1075; the description of the Uppsala temple; the key outside source
- *Västgötalagen* (Law of Västergötland) — c. 1220; the oldest Swedish provincial law code
- *Upplandlagen* (Law of Uppland) — 1296; the most important Swedish law code
- *Erikskrönikan* (Erik's Chronicle) — c. 1320; the first Swedish language chronicle
- Runestones — especially the Rök stone (c. 800) and the Varangian runestones commemorating men who died in the east
- Birka excavation records — the archaeological foundation

## Secondary Sources

- Ambrosiani, Björn and Helen Clarke. *Towns in the Viking Age*. Leicester University Press, 1991.
- Sawyer, Birgit and Peter Sawyer. *Medieval Scandinavia: From Conversion to Reformation*. University of Minnesota Press, 1993.
- Lindkvist, Thomas. *Plundring, skatter och den feodala statens framväxt*. 1988.
- Duczko, Władysław. *Viking Rus: Studies on the Presence of Scandinavians in Eastern Europe*. Brill, 2004.
- Larsson, Lars-Olof. *Kalmarunionens tid*. Prisma, 1997.
