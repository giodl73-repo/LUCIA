# PANEL-2 — P-2 Ibn Khaldun

**Chapter:** Aragon Era 2 — La Reconquesta Aragonesa
**Stage:** PANEL-2 (notes review — targeted)
**Reviewer:** Ibn Khaldun

---

The notes have answered my primary question from PANEL-1. The structural engine is now named clearly: pactisme as covenant-machine, expanding through multiplying compact obligations rather than through imposing uniformity. This is analytically precise. The terminal condition — that the machine cannot centralize without violating its own logic — is planted in the correct place.

What the notes have done well: the *why* of the Furs de València is now present and correct. James gave Valencia its own laws not from constitutional principle alone but because he already had two incompatible law codes and a third was cheaper than war. That is the kind of systemic reasoning Ibn Khaldun values. The chapter must state this with the same plainness as the notes.

The Abu Zayd figure is important and underused in the notes. A Muslim governor who converted and allied with the Aragonese is not an anomaly — he is the evidence that the Muslim political world of Valencia in 1238 was not unified, that Zayyan's position was undermined from within as well as from without. This is the structural context for why Valencia fell: not Aragonese military superiority alone but the fragmentation of Almohad legitimacy after Las Navas de Tolosa (1212). The chapter should name this fragmentation. The Almohad empire was already in terminal decline before James arrived at the walls. The conquest was the final act of a structural collapse that had been underway for decades.

The one gap I still push on: the notes identify the terminal condition (pactisme cannot centralize) but do not yet locate the specific moment in this era's arc where the stress appears. The Valencian noble resistance to the *Furs* — Aragonese nobles wanting Aragonese law over their new tenants — is the right place, and the notes gesture at it. The chapter must show this tension operating in a specific case, not just assert it existed. A named noble. A specific demand. James's response. The compact system under stress from the inside is more revealing than the compact system performing smoothly.

One structural observation: the chapter plan has five sections, which is correct for ~5000 words. The distribution of weight is reasonable. I would watch Section 3 (the machine of compact) — it risks becoming explanatory rather than narrative. The *Justicia* confrontation scene must carry the analytical weight of the section without becoming a civics lecture. Show the *Justicia* holding. Let the reader understand the mechanism from the holding.

The production bible is ready to execute.
