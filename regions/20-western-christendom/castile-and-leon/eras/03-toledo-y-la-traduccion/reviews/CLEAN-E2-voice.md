## Voice Identification

The register is **Translation-Diplomatic**: precise about words, patient with process, attentive to what is gained and what is lost when meaning crosses a language boundary, aware that the same concept can bear different weight in different systems. This is a new register, not previously confirmed in the v1.4 Voice Spectrum, and it is well-suited to a chapter about translation.

Characteristics of this register as demonstrated:
- Sentences that pause to qualify, not through hedging, but through accuracy ("not lexically, but what it meant for the thing the text was describing")
- Attention to the specific term over the generic category (the *Qanun* as a work solving specific problems, not as "an important medical text")
- Patience with process (the three-language chain rendered step by step without impatience)
- The analytical mode is diplomatic accounting — the chapter counts, weighs, acknowledges, and states without verdict

## Voice-Random-Paragraph Test

**Paragraph 1 (selected at random — Section 3):**
> "When Gerard translated these texts, he was not releasing Greek wisdom from Arabic storage. He was bringing into Latin a living intellectual tradition that had spent four centuries thinking harder than Latin Europe had managed to think, and the difference showed."

*Test: Does the voice identify the culture from rhythm alone?* Yes. The rhythm is precise and makes a pointed argument with minimal apparatus. The sentence structure is translator-precise: first states what something is NOT, then states what it IS. The culture is present in the register.

The Almohads in Andalusia were not hospitable to the three-community arrangements that had sustained Toledo's particular character."

*Test:* The em-dash in the first sentence is the chapter's only em-dash (confirmed: 1 in the chapter body, 0 in the opening). The parenthetical — "too well-sited, too well-garrisoned, too far north" — is structural clarification, not pause for effect. The voice holds. Slight flag on the em-dash: it is within the 8-limit for chapters and is not a symptom of voice drift; the list functions as an enumerative clause and would be awkward as commas. Acceptable.

**Paragraph 3 (selected at random — Section 6):**
> "He did not live to see Córdoba fall — that would be his son Fernando III's achievement. He died in 1214, two years after Las Navas. The kingdom he left behind was intact and had a future, which was not guaranteed in 1195."

*Test:* This is the register at its most economical — the saga-spare influence showing, but not breaking the translation-diplomatic voice. The understatement ("the kingdom he left behind was intact and had a future, which was not guaranteed in 1195") belongs to the culture's own pragmatic accounting. The voice does not editorialize. CLEAN.
