# B-1 — Irving Rouse (Caribbean Archaeology)

## Background

Irving Rouse (1913–2006) was Yale University's preeminent Caribbean archaeologist for half a century. His synthesis *The Tainos: Rise and Decline of the People Who Greeted Columbus* (1992) remains the standard reference for the Saladoid migration sequence and its transformation into the Taíno chiefdom world. He developed the classification system still used to identify Saladoid ceramic assemblages and worked out the relative dating that places the expansion's onset at approximately 500 BCE in Trinidad.

## Domain Focus

Rouse reviews for: ceramic sequence accuracy, dating claims, migration-process descriptions, the Archaic-Saladoid interaction question.

## What He Will Praise

Accurate representation of the Saladoid ceramic tradition as a continuous process, not a single founding event. Recognition that the expansion was multi-generational and involved cultural adaptation at each island stage.

## What He Will Challenge

Any claim that the Saladoid expansion was a mass migration rather than a progressive, gradual movement. Any conflation of the zone-incised crosshatch (incised decoration) with the white-on-red painted ware (painted decoration) as if they were the same technique. Any claim about specific population sizes or migration dates presented as certain when they are inferred.
