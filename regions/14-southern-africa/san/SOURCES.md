# Sources — San

## Primary / Oral / Archive

- Bleek, Wilhelm & Lloyd, Lucy. *Specimens of Bushman Folklore*. 1911. — the ||Xam oral tradition archive; the foundational collection
- Lloyd, Lucy. *A Short Account of Further Bushman Material Collected*. 1889.
- Katz, Richard. *Boiling Energy: Community Healing Among the Kalahari Kung*. 1982. Harvard University Press. — the trance dance documented from inside
- Biesele, Megan. *Women Like Meat: The Folklore and Foraging Ideology of the Kalahari Ju/'hoan*. 1993. Witwatersrand University Press.

## Academic

- Lewis-Williams, J. David. *The Mind in the Cave: Consciousness and the Origins of Art*. 2002. Thames & Hudson. — the neuropsychological theory of rock art
- Lewis-Williams, J. David & Dowson, T.A. *Images of Power: Understanding Bushman Rock Art*. 1989. Southern Book Publishers.
- Lee, Richard B. *The !Kung San: Men, Women, and Work in a Foraging Society*. 1979. Cambridge University Press. — the classic ethnographic study
- Liebenberg, Louis. *The Art of Tracking: The Origin of Science*. 1990. David Philip. — tracking as epistemology
- Deacon, Janette & Dowson, T.A. (eds.). *Voices from the Past: |Xam Bushmen and the Bleek and Lloyd Collection*. 1996. Witwatersrand University Press.
- Marshall, Lorna. *The !Kung of Nyae Nyae*. 1976. Harvard University Press.
