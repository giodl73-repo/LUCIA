## Overall Assessment

An exceptionally strong chapter that validates the philosophical-devotional voice register at chapter length. The voice is the chapter's greatest achievement: it is new to the manuscript, consistent throughout, and inseparable from the culture being depicted. The prose moves at the speed of careful thought, mirroring the translator's own method, and this creates an immersive effect unique in the project so far.

The structural engine (translation-as-civilizational-recovery) works as narrative plot, not as background context. The Yeshe-O ransom story provides genuine structural depth: a system that consumes its own patron to complete itself, told from inside a worldview where this consumption is fulfillment rather than tragedy. The translation method (Section IV) achieves the v1.3 Knowledge-System-as-Evidence standard through the worked example of "bodhicitta."

The main areas for development are: (1) slightly more sensory grounding in Section III's political analysis, and (2) the slight outward gesture in the landing toward the Gelug tradition. Both are minor within a chapter that otherwise sustains its world from first word to last.

## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation 1: Translation-as-Structural-Engine.** The chapter uses an intellectual practice (translation) as the narrative's driving mechanism in the way that v1.1 described for political/economic mechanisms (Mali's redistribution, the Mourning War cycle, the eland pivot). This is a new mode: the culture's own defining act is intellectual-devotional rather than political or ecological, and the chapter makes it DRIVE the arc rather than serving as context. If a second culture's chapter achieves this (the Abbasid translation movement, the Greek-Arabic-Latin chain), the Progression dimension should be amended to include intellectual-practice-as-engine.

**Innovation 2: Philosophical-Devotional Voice Register.** This voice is new to the manuscript and has now been validated at chapter length. It is neither court nor steppe nor oral-tradition nor maritime. It is the register of a culture where the careful placement of a word is simultaneously scholarship and devotion, where the speed of thought IS the speed of the prose. The Style Guide's Voice Spectrum section should be expanded to include this register, with the Tholing translation room as its example.

**Innovation 3: Self-Consuming Patron Logic.** The Yeshe-O ransom story presents a variant of the Mali self-consuming covenant logic: a system where the patron himself becomes the price of the system's completion. The difference from Mali (where self-consumption was unintended) is that here the consumption is deliberate and understood by the culture as the system performing correctly. This could inform the Progression dimension's Structural Engine note: self-consuming systems can be intentional (Guge) or emergent (Mali).
