# B-inca-1 — Gary Urton

## Background

Gary Urton is the Dumbarton Oaks Professor of Pre-Columbian Studies at Harvard.
His work on the quipu has fundamentally changed scholarly understanding of what
the knotted-string records could encode. His analysis of binary coding in cord
construction (spin direction, ply direction, attachment method) has opened the
possibility that quipus encoded not just numerical data but a form of narrative
or categorical information.

## Key Question

**"Is the quipu treated as what it was — a technology — or as what it was not — writing?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Accurate description of quipu physical structure
- Treatment of the quipucamayoc as specialists with rigorous training
- Acknowledgment that our inability to read most quipus is our failure, not theirs
- Honest statement of the scholarly debate about narrative capacity

## What He Will Challenge

- Any characterization of the quipu as "simple" or "limited"
- Any suggestion that the Inca lacked a recording system (they had one; it was different)
- Overstatement of what we can currently read from surviving quipus
- Understatement of the quipu's potential capacity
