# B-amarna-1 — Donald Redford

## Background

Donald Redford is Professor of Classics and Ancient Mediterranean Studies
at Penn State. His *Akhenaten: The Heretic King* is the standard political
reading of the Amarna period. He approaches Akhenaten not as a mystic or
visionary but as a ruler whose theological program served political ends.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter understand the Aten revolution as a political act
with theological instruments, not merely as a spiritual crisis?"**

## What He Will Praise

- The economic dimension (temple wealth, priesthood strangulation)
- The phased timeline (co-worship to escalation to proscription)
- Honest treatment of the Amarna Letters as evidence of diplomatic neglect
- Avoidance of romanticizing Akhenaten

## What He Will Challenge

- Any residual treatment of Akhenaten as a spiritual visionary
- Oversimplification of the revolution's timeline
- Insufficient attention to the Amun priesthood as a political actor
- Any suggestion that the Aten theology was "ahead of its time"
