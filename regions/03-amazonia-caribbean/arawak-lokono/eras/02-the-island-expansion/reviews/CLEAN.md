## E-2 — Voice Keeper

**Keeper lens:** Register drift. Textbook voice. Modern commentary. Passages written TO a Western audience rather than FROM the culture.

**Random paragraph test (three paragraphs selected):**

Paragraph 1 (opening of Section 2): "On the mainland, the same morning, Aiyana's hands were already working. She had been at the manioc since before light — the digging the evening before, the grating in the pre-dawn dark, and now the matapi: a cylinder of woven tirite-palm fiber, the weave tight enough to hold the grated mash but open enough to let the liquid through."
— Voice: Maritime-accumulative with a craft-rhythm modulation for the processing section. Identifiable as belonging to a river-coastal culture. Passes the random paragraph test.

Paragraph 2 (from Section 4): "The network had no center in geography. Its center was always the community whose lonko knew the most people."
— Voice: Declarative, structurally precise. Short sentences. The rhythm belongs to the chapter's analytical register — this is the chapter's most compressed analytical moment, and the compression is appropriate to the insight. Does not break into textbook voice because it makes no comparative claims and uses no external categories.

Paragraph 3 (from Section 5): "When mainland Lokono visited island communities and encountered the batey, they recognized the game. What they did not recognize was the weight."
— Voice: The contrast between "recognized the game" and "did not recognize the weight" is stylistically precise and maintains the inside-perspective throughout. Neither the mainland Lokono nor the island community is evaluated from outside. Passes.

**Drift flags:**
- The linamarin explanation in Section 2: "The bitter manioc her family grew produced its defense — linamarin, which broke down in damaged cells to release what the plant used to discourage animals from eating it — not in the fiber but in the juice." The parenthetical about the plant's evolutionary defense mechanism is the most externally-framed sentence in the chapter. It risks sounding like a biology textbook. However, the sentence is rendered as Aiyana's understanding — what she knows about why the plant does this — not as a scientist's observation. The clause "what the plant used to discourage animals from eating it" is a folk-biological framing, not a laboratory one. Borderline; acceptable as written. If the scorer were to revise, the revision would be: "The liquid was the danger, not because of what it looked like or smelled like but because the plant had always defended itself in the juice, and the generations before her had learned this through attention so careful it had become knowledge in the hands." — But the current sentence is within acceptable range.

**E-2 verdict: CLEAN with one borderline flag noted.** No required revisions. The linamarin sentence is within range; a future revision could naturalize it further.
