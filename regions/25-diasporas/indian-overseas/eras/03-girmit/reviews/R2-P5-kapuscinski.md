## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Focus:** Sensory plan for the ship and plantation sections.

**On Section 2 (The Ship):** The notes name the right elements: the hold, the salt, the horizon. But I want specifics at the method level. What did the ship smell like? The hold carried hundreds of people for ninety days with minimal ventilation. Vomit, bilge water, bodies, cooking smoke from the galley that reached below decks as a thin acrid layer. What did the ocean sound like to a person from Bihar who had never seen more water than a monsoon-swollen river? The constant sound of water against the hull, day and night, for three months. The absence of silence. The absence of land sounds: no birds (until approaching land, and the first bird was an event), no insects, no wind through trees. The sensory world of the ship is a subtraction from everything the girmitiya knew.

**On Section 3 (The Plantation):** Sugarcane. The chapter must render the cane itself. The height of it: two meters, three meters, forming walls on either side of the cutter. The leaves that cut skin. The machete's weight after ten hours. The heat inside a cane field where the air does not move. The syrup that attracts insects. The overseer's whistle. These are not decorations. They are the physical reality of the contract's promise of "agricultural work." The gap between the arkatti's description and this reality is the gap the chapter must make the reader feel in their body.

**On Section 4 (The Mandali):** The sensory shift here must be dramatic. From the cane field to the evening gathering. The cooking: mustard oil in a pan, the smell of dal tempering, the flat roti on an iron tava. The sound of Dulari's voice singing the Sundarkand to people who have spent the day cutting cane. The contrast between the plantation's sensory world (heat, exhaustion, the overseer) and the mandali's sensory world (food, song, the evening air) IS the chapter's argument about cultural reconstitution. Do not explain the argument. Let the sensory contrast carry it.

**Recommendation:** Build a sensory map for each section. Bihar (dust, neem, mango, dung-fire smoke). Calcutta depot (crowds, Hooghly stench, the medical examiner's cold hands). Ship (salt, vomit, water-sound, confined bodies). Plantation (cane, machete, heat, whistle). Mandali (mustard oil, song, evening air, brass lota). Each world has its own sensory signature.
