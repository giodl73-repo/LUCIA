# CLEAN — E-3: Compression Editor
## Haiti, Era 3: Fè Sa — The Revolution

**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

**Audit scope:** Re-explanation, filler, redundancy, scenes followed by explanations of what they showed.

---

### Re-explanation Check — CLEAR with one flag

Most scenes are trusted to land without commentary. The massacre section handles its own weight: "He knew what he was ordering." No explanation follows. The ceremony at Bois Caïman is rendered and then the chapter moves. "One week later, the north plain was burning." No explanation of what this means. The reader is trusted.

**One flag:** In the hemispheric response section: "Slaveholders from Virginia to Cuba to Brazil read the news and did what slaveholders do when confronted with proof that their system can be overturned: they tightened." The phrase "which is what slaveholders do when confronted with proof that their system can be overturned" is mildly re-explanatory — the tightening itself communicates this. 

**Recommendation:** "Slaveholders from Virginia to Cuba to Brazil read the news and tightened." Let the verb carry it. The reader knows what the news was. The reader knows what tightening means.

### Redundancy Check — ONE MINOR

The phrase "the first Black republic in the history of the world" appears twice: once in the declaration section and once in the final paragraphs. The first instance is Dessalines's proclamation — it belongs there as the historical claim. The second instance ("Born from the only successful large-scale slave revolution in the history of the Atlantic world") is close but distinct: it refers to the revolution, not the republic. These are technically different claims, so the redundancy is light. But the closing paragraph could be tightened by trusting that the reader remembers the republic claim from two pages earlier.

**Recommendation:** The closing paragraph's second sentence ("Named for the land the Taíno had named. Declared on New Year's Day, 1804, by a man who carried the marks of the world he had just ended on his skin.") is the stronger close of the two. Consider cutting "Born from the only successful large-scale slave revolution in the history of the Atlantic world" since this is implicit in everything that precedes it.

### Transitions Check — CLEAR

Section transitions are not explained. The chapter moves from ceremony to colonial context to structure to military campaign to constitution to Dessalines to hemispheric response without marking the transitions as transitions. The section breaks (three dashes) carry the movement without explanation. The reader follows the logic of the engine.

### Length Audit — APPROPRIATE

At ~5,100 words, this is within the extended range for a chapter covering distributed events across 13 years and multiple theaters of operation. No section is padded. The Three Wars at Once section is the densest; it earns its density by showing the structural logic of Toussaint's strategic switching, which is the most analytically complex moment in the chapter.

---

**CLEAN status (E-3):** Two minor revisions recommended (the "which is what slaveholders do" re-explanation; the redundant claim in closing paragraph). Overall compression is strong.
