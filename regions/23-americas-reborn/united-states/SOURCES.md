# Sources — United States

## Key Academic Works
- Gordon Wood, *The Creation of the American Republic, 1776–1787* (1969, UNC Press) — the founding political thought
- Edmund Morgan, *American Slavery, American Freedom* (1975, Norton) — the Virginia paradox; slavery and liberty as inseparable
- Eric Foner, *Reconstruction: America's Unfinished Revolution, 1863–1877* (1988, Harper) — the definitive account
- C. Vann Woodward, *The Strange Career of Jim Crow* (1955, Oxford) — the legal history of racial segregation
- Taylor Branch, *Parting the Waters / Pillar of Fire / At Canaan's Edge* (trilogy, 1988–2006) — the Civil Rights Movement
- Dee Brown, *Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee* (1970) — the western expansion from indigenous perspectives
- Robert Caro, *The Years of Lyndon Johnson* (4 vols., 1982–2012) — political power and the Great Society
- Ta-Nehisi Coates, *Between the World and Me* (2015) — the Black American experience in the twenty-first century

## Primary Sources
- The Declaration of Independence (1776)
- The Federalist Papers (Hamilton, Madison, Jay, 1787–1788)
- The Constitution of the United States (1787) and its Amendments
- Frederick Douglass's autobiographies (1845, 1855, 1881)
- The 14th and 15th Amendments (1868, 1870)
- Martin Luther King Jr.'s "Letter from Birmingham Jail" (1963) and "I Have a Dream" speech (1963)
