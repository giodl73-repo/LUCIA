## Innovations

**Garden-Town Voice (new):** A register for settled forest-agriculture cultures that does not map onto any existing Voice Spectrum entry. Characteristics: patient, labor-attentive, thinking-through-maintenance, matter-of-fact about ceremony, attentive to small environmental shifts. Distinct from: river (directional/institutional), maritime (rolling/tidal), steppe (spare/forward), oral-tradition (cadenced/repetitive). Closest to: Monument/Stone in its groundedness in material work, but warmer and more attentive to the living landscape's daily pressure. Propose logging in INNOVATIONS.md.

**Non-Chronological via Ceremony-as-Center (confirms v1.5):** The Quarup as organizing center rather than chronology works cleanly. Each chapter section corresponds to a phase of the ceremony or its material infrastructure, and the chapter covers the era's defining features without needing a timeline. Confirms the v1.5 amendment's application to ceremonial cultures.

**Infrastructure-as-Commitment (log):** The road as diplomatic communication — the maintained corridor as the announcement of intention to belong to the network. This is a specific technique for showing political relationship through physical object, distinct from the v1.5 Material Object as Structural Principle amendment (which focuses on how the object's properties determine the narrative's logic) — here the object's existence rather than its properties carries the meaning. Possible addition to the material-object amendment.
