# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *The Secret History of the Mongols* (Monggol-un Niucha Tobchiyan, c. 1240) — the foundational Mongol narrative source
- Rashid al-Din, *Compendium of Chronicles* (Jami' al-Tawarikh, c. 1307) — the most comprehensive Islamic account
- William of Rubruck, *Itinerary* (1255) — Flemish friar's eyewitness account of Karakorum
- Marco Polo, *Travels* — account of the Yuan dynasty Mongol empire
- Ibn Battuta, *Rihla* — observations on the Golden Horde and Chagatai khanate

## Key Academic Works

- Timothy May, *The Mongol Conquests in World History* (2012)
- David Morgan, *The Mongols* (2nd ed., 2007)
- Thomas Allsen, *Culture and Conquest in Mongol Eurasia* (2001)
- Christopher Atwood, *Encyclopedia of Mongolia and the Mongol Empire* (2004) — essential reference
- Peter Jackson, *The Mongols and the Islamic World* (2017)

## Needed

- Archaeological work at Karakorum and other Mongol capital sites
- Studies on the Black Death's movement through the Mongol trade network
- Analysis of the Pax Mongolica's actual commercial and cultural transfers
