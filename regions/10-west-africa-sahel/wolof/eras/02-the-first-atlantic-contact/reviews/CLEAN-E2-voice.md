# CLEAN-E2 — Voice Keeper — The First Atlantic Contact

**Chapter:** Wolof / Era 2: The First Atlantic Contact
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Baseline Voice

Established in the opening: the discover register. Measured, diplomatic, calculating. Short declarative sentences for conclusions. Longer, layered sentences for the political and commercial assessments that precede them. The prose weighs and evaluates, matching the Wolof political culture it describes. Not the accumulative oral-tradition cadence of the Mali chapters. Not textbook. Not lyrical. Observational, precise, and confident.

**Key baseline passages:**
- "The Wolof knew more." (three words, total confidence)
- "They knew the horses were good." (assessment, not admiration)
- "He sent men to see." (diplomatic restraint in five words)
- "each time with the terms a little different, each time with the balance a little harder to hold" (the discover voice at its most characteristic)

## Rhythm Assessment

The chapter's rhythm matches the discover voice throughout. Sections I and V are the strongest rhythmically: the opening builds from observation to calculation to pivot, and the landing closes with the gewel's accumulative cadence. Section II sustains the rhythm through ecological detail by anchoring the analysis in the stable, the flies, the dying horses. Section III is the most rhythmically varied (Lisbon's stone and cold introduce new textures), but the variation serves the content: Bemoim is in a foreign place, and the prose registers that foreignness without abandoning the Wolof observer's register.
