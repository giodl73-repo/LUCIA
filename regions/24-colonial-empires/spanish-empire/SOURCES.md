# Sources — Spanish Empire

## Key Academic Works
- John Hemming, *The Conquest of the Incas* (1970, Harcourt) and *The Conquest of Brazil* — definitive accounts
- Hugh Thomas, *Conquest: Montezuma, Cortés, and the Fall of Old Mexico* (1993, Touchstone)
- J.H. Elliott, *Imperial Spain, 1469–1716* (1963, Edward Arnold) and *Empires of the Atlantic World: Britain and Spain in America* (2006)
- Inga Clendinnen — the Aztec world and the Spanish encounter; *Aztecs* (1991)
- Lewis Hanke, *The Spanish Struggle for Justice in the Conquest of America* (1949) — Las Casas and the theological debates
- Enrique Tandeter — Potosí and the colonial labor system
- Angel Rama, *The Lettered City* (1984; trans. 1996) — the intellectual culture of colonial Latin America

## Primary Sources
- Bartolomé de las Casas, *A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies* (1542)
- Bernal Díaz del Castillo, *The True History of the Conquest of New Spain* (written c. 1568) — Cortés's campaign from a soldier's perspective
- Felipe Guaman Poma de Ayala, *Nueva Corónica* (c. 1615)
- Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz, *Respuesta a Sor Filotea* (1691) — the defense of women's intellectual life
- The New Laws of the Indies (1542)

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Archivo General de Indias (Seville) — the most complete colonial archive in the world
- Inquisition trial records (Archivo Histórico Nacional, Madrid)
- Potosí mita labor records and casualty documentation
- Contemporary indigenous-language sources on the conquest period
