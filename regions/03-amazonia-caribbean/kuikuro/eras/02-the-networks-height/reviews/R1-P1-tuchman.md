# Panel Review — R1-P1 — Barbara Tuchman
## The Network's Height | Kuikuro Era 2

---

I read this straight through twice, as a reader, without stopping to take notes.

The first time, I found I had arrived at the end before I expected to. That is the test.

The opening lands exactly where it should: in a body, at a task, with time pressure (three moons) and a specific occasion (the egitsü) already loaded and waiting. Iauatá is not a specimen. He is a man who is thinking about a ceremony while doing work that needs doing, in the way people always think about coming occasions while doing present tasks. The universality is achieved by specificity — not by abstraction.

What works structurally: the road is an elegant narrative vehicle because it carries multiple freight simultaneously. It is a physical object requiring maintenance. It is a diplomatic communication. It is a representation of the network's logic. The chapter does not explain any of this — it dramatizes it, and the reader deduces it, which is the correct order. The dropped Inga branch section is the best passage: specific, physical, with the understory growing up around it in the weeks since it fell. This is what scenes should do — make the abstract (the network requires maintenance) feel like the particular (this branch, these vines, this hour of heavy work).

The closing works. "He picks up his blade and moves to the next section." No summary, no announcement of significance. The road goes on. The work goes on. The Quarup is coming. The reader holds this.

**The question I would push on:**

The chapter gives us the external view of the road but not the internal structure of what Iauatá is approaching — the Quarup. I want to feel the scale of Kuhikugu, the convergence of all those arriving communities. The opening hints at it (the wrestling, the mourning songs, the dancing, the gift exchange between anetü) but doesn't put us there. The chapter needs to arrive at what the opening only gestures toward. We are being held at the edge of the ceremony, not inside it.

The production bible should make the Quarup its structural center — show it operating, show the gift exchange in motion, show the anetü negotiating the network's obligations through the ceremony's protocols. The opening earns this. Now the chapter must deliver it.

— Tuchman
