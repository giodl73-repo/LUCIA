## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation 1: Corrido-Narrative Register**
- Dimension: Voice
- Description: A new voice register discovered for Mexico's revolutionary era, derived from the corrido tradition — Mexico's oral-journalistic ballad form. Characteristics: declarative forward motion, named figures as refrain anchors, third-person facticity building to refrain-weight, period as pause mechanism, zero em-dashes as emergent property. Analytically distinct from Griot-Cadence (first-person authority), Oral-Tradition (proverb-weight, deep time), and Prophetic-Migratory (eschatological horizon). The corrido reports rather than performs; it records rather than inscribes; it builds to the refrain that is collective knowledge. Sustained at chapter length (~4,500 words) with zero em-dashes. Recommend logging as new register candidate.

**Innovation 2: Domestic Covenant Gap as Terminal Condition**
- Dimension: Progression
- Description: A terminal-condition type not captured by the current v1.6 framework of 10 types. In the Mexican case, the state that made the revolutionary covenant (Constitution of 1917, Article 27) is the same state that systematically fails to implement it. This is distinct from Premise non-recognition (v1.6 Type 4, where the colonial party does not recognize the premises as binding at all) because the Mexican state fully recognizes the ejido's legal status — it wrote Article 27 — and then fails to implement it for class-interest reasons. The terminal condition is domestic, recognized, and deliberately deferred. The covenant exists in writing; the gap between the text and the delivery IS the terminal condition. Potential name: "Recognized-but-deferred covenant" or "Written covenant gap." Candidate for v1.6 extension.
