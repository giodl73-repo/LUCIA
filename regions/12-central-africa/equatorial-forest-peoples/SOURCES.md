# Sources — Equatorial Forest Peoples

## Primary / Ethnographic

- Turnbull, Colin. *The Forest People*. 1961. Simon & Schuster. — the defining account of the Mbuti; now critically reassessed but still foundational
- Turnbull, Colin. *The Mbuti Pygmies: Change and Adaptation*. 1983. Holt, Rinehart and Winston. — his later, more analytical work
- Bahuchet, Serge — extensive fieldwork with Aka and Baka from the 1970s onward; the definitive French-language scholarship

## Academic

- Bahuchet, Serge. *Les Pygmées Aka et la forêt centrafricaine*. 1985. SELAF. — the Aka economy and social organization
- Hewlett, Barry S. *Intimate Fathers: The Nature and Context of Aka Pygmy Paternal Infant Care*. 1991. University of Michigan Press.
- Lewis, Jerome. "Ekila: Blood, Bodies and Social Order Among the Mbendjele BaYaka Forest People." PhD thesis, UCL, 2002.
- Arom, Simha. *African Polyphony and Polyrhythm: Musical Structure and Methodology*. 1991. Cambridge University Press. — the Aka musical tradition
- Hewlett, Barry S. & Cavalli-Sforza, L.L. "Cultural Transmission Among Aka Pygmies." *American Anthropologist* 88:4 (1986).

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on the honeyguide-human mutualism (Spottiswoode et al., 2016 science paper is key)
- Studies on Baka and Aka land rights in current legal frameworks
- Work on forest peoples' responses to logging and conservation in the DRC and Cameroon
- Updated genetic studies on the deep history of forest specialist populations in Africa
