**Combined score: GATE-1 (27/30) + GATE-2 (50/60) = 77/90**

**Notes:** Passes GATE-2 comfortably. The chapter's distinctive contribution is the covenant-economics structural engine: the logic of intermediary power sustained by sacred contract, and the cyclical historiography of the Dausi that frames rise and fall as covenant-honored and covenant-broken. The figures dimension is the weakest (7/10) due to the thinness of the record for named individuals, but the chapter handles this honestly and the v1.1 amendment on honest limits applies.

**Innovations flagged:**
1. **Intermediary Economics as Structural Engine** (Progression): The "wealth from position, not production" logic, sustained through the Bida covenant, represents a novel engine type. Previous engines have been based on internal social mechanisms (covenant logic in Mali, grief-violence cycle in Haudenosaunee, ecological integration in San). This is the first engine driven by trade-position economics explained through the culture's own sacred framework. Potential applicability to other intermediary cultures (Sogdians, Swahili, Phoenicians).
2. **Twin-source narration** (Evidence): The systematic use of an outside written source (Al-Bakri) for material detail and an inside oral source (the Dausi) for interpretive framework, with the tension between them acknowledged and productive, may represent a reusable technique for cultures where the primary written record is external.
