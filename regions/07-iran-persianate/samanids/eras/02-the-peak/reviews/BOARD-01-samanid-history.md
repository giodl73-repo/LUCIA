# Board Review — Domain Expert 1: Samanid Political and Administrative History
## Samanids Era 2: The Peak

**Expertise:** Medieval Islamic history, Khorasan and Transoxiana, Samanid dynasty, Abbasid caliphate relations

---

## Factual Accuracy Assessment

**Ismail Samani's dates and achievements:** Accurate. The traditional date for his birth is given as 849 CE in some sources and approximately 842 CE in others; the chapter's "c. 842" is within acceptable range. His death in 907 CE is well-attested. The Battle of the Oxus (900 CE) and capture of Amr ibn al-Layth are historically documented.

**The Abbasid confirmation:** The chapter correctly frames the caliphal confirmation as politically significant for both parties. One clarification: the caliph who confirmed Ismail's authority was al-Mu'tadid (r. 892–902 CE), not al-Muktafi, and the formal grant of authority over Khorasan came in stages rather than in a single moment after the Oxus battle. The chapter's handling ("the caliph confirmed him") is narratively simplified but not inaccurate in its essential claim.

**Nasr ibn Ahmad's reign dates:** 914–943 CE. Correct.

**The mausoleum:** Dating to "approximately 943 CE" is consistent with scholarly consensus, though some sources suggest it may have been completed slightly earlier. The architectural description — basket weave, engaged pilasters, lantern dome, fired brick — is accurate and reflects current scholarship on the building.

**Bal'ami and the Tabari translation:** The commission is well-attested. The characterization of Bal'ami as adapting and expanding rather than simply translating is accurate and reflects Richard Frye's and other scholars' assessment of the work.

---

## Cultural Authenticity Concerns

**One concern:** The chapter identifies Ismail Samani as having "been raised in a world where governance was written in Arabic." This is partially true but slightly simplified. The Samanids at the local administrative level likely used both Persian and Arabic from early in their governance. The claim is defensible as characterizing the high-culture prestige hierarchy (Arabic above Persian in formal scholarly discourse) rather than asserting that Persian was absent from administration. Recommend the chapter stand as written — the nuance is present in the "Two Languages, One World" section.

**The *farr* concept:** Accurately described. The theological nuance — that *farr* departs from rulers who lose it, visible in their failures — is correctly rendered. The connection to Zoroastrian theology is appropriate without being overstated.

---

## Sources Assessment

The chapter draws on primary and tier-2 sources appropriately. The "Mother of Wine" attribution to Rudaki is hedged correctly ("attributable to him with reasonable confidence"). The Ibn Sina library account is accurately sourced from the autobiography as mediated through al-Juzjani.

**One addition recommended:** The chapter would benefit from an explicit reference to Narshakhi's *Tarikh-i Bukhara* for Bukhara's physical description. Narshakhi is the closest thing to an eyewitness account of the Samanid capital and should appear in SOURCES.md.

---

## Verdict

Historically accurate. Culturally authentic. The chapter correctly represents the current state of Samanid scholarship. No factual errors requiring revision.

**Board endorsement: APPROVED.**
