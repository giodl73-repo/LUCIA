# R1-P5 — Ryszard Kapuściński

**Opening reviewed:** Brazil, Era 2 — A Colônia — Açúcar, Ouro, e Escravos
**Round:** Panel-1

---

I have been in places that felt like Palmares. Not in Brazil — in Angola, in the mato, the deep bush, where the war had been for fifteen years and people had built their lives around the permanent expectation of attack. The economy of survival. The way a community learns to move through landscape that is simultaneously home and danger. You read the land differently when the land is the only protection you have.

The Serra da Barriga opening gives me the sensation of that landscape without announcing it. The silence the forest gave back. The Portuguese columns that came in from the wrong direction because they did not know how to read the terrain. This is not metaphor — this is how the quilombo actually worked. Palmares survived not because its warriors were more numerous than Portuguese soldiers, but because Palmares knew the land and the Portuguese did not. That is a form of knowledge. The chapter opens inside that knowledge, and this is exactly right.

The sensory texture is strong where it is present: the red soil, the drum at dusk, the smell of the engenho that the great house stood at a distance from. But I want more. What does Palmares smell like? What do you hear at dawn in the quilombo? What does it feel like to walk into a place that has organized its entire existence around the premise that you are free? I have never been in such a place and I believe I would feel it in my body before I understood it with my mind. The chapter should give me this.

**The question I'd push on:** The engenho section establishes the terror of the sugar mill economically but not sensorially. The cane, the rollers, the heat — these are mentioned but not inhabited. I want the smell of burning cane. I want the sound the rollers make. I want the moment when someone decides — in their body, before their mind — that the risk of running is less than the certainty of staying. This is not background. This is the condition that makes Palmares comprehensible. The chapter must give me a person in the senzala before it gives me the forest.

The final two sentences are perfect. I would not change one syllable. The whole chapter should be earning those two sentences from the first paragraph.
