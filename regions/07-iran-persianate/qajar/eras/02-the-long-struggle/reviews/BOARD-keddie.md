## Historical Accuracy Assessment

**On the Tobacco Protest mechanism:**

The chapter correctly identifies the key nodes of the fatwa's transmission: the marja issues the ruling, students carry it, Friday mosque preachers amplify it, bazaar guilds enforce it, households comply. This matches the primary source evidence from 1891 and aligns with the account in *Religion and Rebellion in Iran*.

One scholarly note: the chapter says "Mirza Hasan Shirazi issued his ruling in the winter of 1891." The dating should be corrected. The fatwa was issued in March 1891 (Rajab 1308 AH), which is early spring by the Iranian calendar. The tobacco ban's most visible effects built through the summer and autumn of 1891. The concession was cancelled in January 1892. The chapter conflates "winter of 1891" for the fatwa with "winter of 1892" for the cancellation; these are different events separated by approximately nine months. This confusion should be corrected.

**On Mirza Hasan Shirazi:**

The chapter's description of Shirazi as living in Samarra at the shrine of the tenth and eleventh Imams, as financially independent through *khums*, and as ungovernable from the Iranian state's perspective is accurate. The characterization of his ruling as a legal opinion within the *ijtihad* framework rather than a political statement is nuanced and correct: Shirazi himself framed it as a *hukm shar'i* (religious ruling), not as a political intervention, even though its political consequences were enormous.

The chapter says Shirazi was "in his eighties" at the time of the fatwa. He was born in 1815 and issued the fatwa in 1891, making him 75 or 76, not "in his eighties." This should be corrected to "in his mid-seventies."

**On the *marjaiyyat* and *taqlid*:**

The chapter correctly distinguishes the *marja* system from state authority and correctly explains *khums* as the material basis of clerical independence. The description of how authority "accrued through reputation" rather than through appointment is accurate.

**On Jamal al-Din al-Afghani:**

The chapter describes al-Afghani as "of uncertain Iranian origins" — this is a recognized scholarly debate (Keddie, *Sayyid Jamal ad-Din "al-Afghani"*, 1972, argues he was Iranian despite his Afghan self-presentation). The chapter's handling of this is correct.

## Cultural Authenticity Assessment

The chapter handles the clerical-state relationship accurately: it shows neither the clergy as disinterested guardians of the public good nor as purely political actors. The merchant coalition's independent economic grievances are correctly noted. The chapter does not reduce the Tobacco Protest to a single motivation.

---

## Required Corrections (Before FINAL)

1. **Fatwa timing:** "winter of 1891" should be "spring of 1891" for the fatwa. The nine-month gap between the fatwa (spring 1891) and the cancellation (January 1892) is historically significant and should be preserved.

2. **Shirazi's age:** "in his eighties" should be corrected to "in his mid-seventies." Shirazi was born in 1815, issued the fatwa in 1891: age 75-76.

---

## Overall Assessment

The chapter passes historical accuracy review with two required corrections. The Tobacco Protest is rendered with accuracy and with appropriate analytical depth. The transmission mechanism is shown at method level, which is the chapter's most important historiographic contribution.

**Board verdict: PASS with two required corrections.**
