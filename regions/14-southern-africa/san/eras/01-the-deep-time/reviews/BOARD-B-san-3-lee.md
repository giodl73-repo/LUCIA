## B-san-3 — Richard Lee — Board Review of San, The Deep Time

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Ju/'hoan ecology and social organization; the Dobe area ethnography; the sharing ethic and hxaro exchange; nutritional and demographic data on foraging populations; the ethnographic-present problem.

**Historical accuracy:**
- "Insulting the meat" is accurately described. The quote from the Ju/'hoan man is a faithful rendering of the explanation given to me at Dobe (Lee 1979:244). The arrow-ownership distribution rule is correctly described.
- The hxaro exchange network is accurately presented as a system of reciprocal obligation extending across communities over hundreds of kilometers. The characterization as "insurance against local environmental failure" is the correct structural interpretation.
- The sharing ethic's functional logic (variable environment + mobile communities = distributed risk through obligatory sharing) is correctly understood and well presented.
- The tracking descriptions are consistent with the ethnographic record. The cognitive complexity described matches Liebenberg's analysis and my own observations.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The chapter correctly presents the sharing ethic as a structural feature of the economy, not as a sentimental preference. "Cool his heart and make him gentle" is the right framing.
- The chapter avoids the "affluent society" romanticization that has sometimes been applied to my work. The San's economy is presented as effective and knowledge-intensive, not as an idyll.
- The egalitarian mechanisms are presented as deliberate social technology, not as the absence of hierarchy. This is the correct framing — the San actively produce equality; it does not just happen.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The integration of plant knowledge and water-finding into the tracking section. The San's ecological knowledge is not limited to animal tracking — it encompasses the entire biotic environment. The bi! bulbs, tsamma melons, and sip-wells are all accurate details.
- The refusal to use "hunter-gatherer" as an identity label. The people hunt and gather, among many other things. Good.
- The temporal anchoring of my fieldwork to "the 1960s at Dobe" rather than presenting it as eternal. Important.

**What needs correction:**
- The chapter's use of present tense for ongoing practices ("the trackers still read the sand," "the old women still tell the story") is appropriate for practices that do continue, but should acknowledge that the Ju/'hoansi of the Dobe area have undergone significant social and economic changes since the 1960s. The trance dance and tracking continue, but within a very different economic and political context. A sentence noting this would prevent the "timeless people" misreading.
- The tsamma melon's role as water source is slightly overstated. Tsamma is important, but the San use a wide variety of water-bearing roots and tubers (particularly the bi! root). The chapter already mentions bi! bulbs but could make clearer that the water-source knowledge encompasses dozens of species, not primarily tsamma.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (two minor notes above)
