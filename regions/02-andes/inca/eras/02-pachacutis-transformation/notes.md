# Production Notes — Pachacuti's Transformation (Era 2)

## Structural Engine

**Reciprocity scaled to empire.** The structural engine is the mit'a: the transformation of ayllu-level reciprocal labor (ayni/minka) into a state-level system that builds roads, terraces, storehouses, and temples across a continent. Pachacuti does not invent reciprocity. He scales it. The chapter's arc follows the scaling: from valley defense to systematic conquest, from local exchange to imperial redistribution, from the ayllu's qollqa to the state's 2,000 storehouses.

**Book-level arc:** This is the pivot chapter. Era 1 planted the seeds (ayllu, quipu, vertical archipelago). Era 2 shows the scaling. The three threads escalate:
1. **Mit'a** — transforms from communal labor to state labor tax; the chicha still flows, the reciprocity is still invoked, but the scale has changed
2. **Vertical archipelago** — transforms from ayllu-level ecology to state-level territorial strategy; Pachacuti's conquests follow ecological logic
3. **Quipu** — transforms from local record to imperial administration; the quipucamayoc become state officials

## Chapter Arc

1. **The Battle** (opening material) — Chanka attack, Viracocha flees, Cusi Yupanqui stays, purunaucas, victory, the name Pachacuti
2. **The Rebuilding of Qusqu** — Pachacuti tears down and rebuilds the capital; the Coricancha; the ceque system formalized; Qusqu as the navel
3. **The Road** — The qhapaq ñan begins; the mit'a mobilized for road-building; the chasqui relay system; the road as the empire's nervous system
4. **The Storehouses** — The qollqa system scaled from ayllu level to imperial level; redistribution as the proof of legitimate rule; Huanuco Pampa
5. **Machu Picchu** — The royal estate as a microcosm of the system; terraces, astronomy, the intimate scale of imperial planning
6. **The System** — The decimal administration; the mit'a formalized; the aqllawasi; the empire as a reciprocal machine; the closing: the earth-shaker's transformation is complete, and the road runs in both directions

## Key Figures

- **Pachacuti (Cusi Yupanqui)** — The historical pivot; the earth-shaker; the one who transforms a valley polity into Tawantinsuyu
- **Viracocha Inca** — The father who ran; the foil; the old order that Pachacuti replaces
- **The mit'a laborers** — Composite; the people who built the roads and terraces and storehouses; their experience of the reciprocal state
- **A chasqui** — Composite; the relay runner; the human nervous system of the empire

## Sensory Palette

- **Sight:** The megalithic walls of Sacsayhuaman; the gold-plated Coricancha; the road stretching across the altiplano; Machu Picchu in morning cloud
- **Sound:** Chanka war drums; the slap of runners' feet on stone road; the llamada (conch shell trumpet) calling the mit'a assembly
- **Smell:** Battle dust and blood; wet stone of new construction; the fermentation of chicha in ceramic vessels
- **Touch:** The impossible precision of Inca stonework (no mortar, blade-thin joints); the runner's bare feet on cold stone; the weight of a stone being moved into position
- **Taste:** Chicha at the mit'a feast; the blood taste of altitude exertion
