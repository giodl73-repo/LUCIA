# Production Notes — The Conquest of Italy and the Punic Wars (Era 2)

## Structural Engine

**The alliance system as the Republic's expansion mechanism.** The structural engine is the Roman alliance system: the network of treaties (foedera) that bound Italian communities to Roma with varying degrees of autonomy, all requiring military manpower contributions. The system converted conquered peoples into allies who contributed soldiers, which gave Roma the reserve depth that survived Cannae and won the Punic Wars. The engine is self-reinforcing: conquest produces allies, allies produce soldiers, soldiers enable further conquest. The chapter traces this logic from the Latin War through the Samnite Wars through the Punic Wars to the destruction of Carthago.

**Book-level arc continuation:** 
- Res publica tested at scale: can city machinery govern a peninsula? A sea?
- Libertas vs. imperium: the proconsular command stretches the annual rotation
- Mos maiorum: the tradition absorbs Greek influence, military innovation, overseas empire
- The law: extends to allies through the ius Latinum, creating tiers of citizenship

## Chapter Arc

1. **Cannae** (opening material, expanded) — The battle as test of the system; the aftermath; the Senate's response; the system that could absorb catastrophe
2. **The Italian Conquest** — The Latin War; the Samnite Wars; Pyrrhus; how the alliance system grew; the manipular legion
3. **Carthago** — The First Punic War; Roma builds a navy; Sicily; the twenty-three-year war
4. **Hannibal** — The Second Punic War; the Alps crossing; Trebia, Trasimene, Cannae; Fabius Maximus and the strategy of delay; Scipio takes the war to Africa
5. **Zama and After** — Scipio Africanus at Zama; the settlement; the Mediterranean as Mare Nostrum; the Third Punic War and the destruction of Carthago; delenda est
6. **The Cost** — What empire cost the Republic; the provinciae; the wealth that changed Roma; the seeds of the crisis Era 3 will tell

## Key Figures

- **Hannibal Barca** — Carthago's greatest general; from inside his world: a Carthaginian serving his city's interests, not a villain in Roma's story
- **Scipio Africanus** — The general who defeated Hannibal; the first Roman to be known by a cognomen earned in war; the tension between individual glory and senatorial collective
- **Fabius Maximus** — The Delayer; the man who refused to fight Hannibal and was mocked for it; the strategy of patience
- **Cato the Elder** — "Delenda est Carthago"; the voice of the old Republic confronting the wealth of the new; the censor who tried to hold the line
- **The Italian ally (composite)** — A soldier from a Latin or Italian allied community, serving in the Roman army under Roman command, not yet a Roman citizen

## Voice Notes

Continue the legal-public register from Era 1, but with military weight. The era is defined by war, and the prose should carry the sound of the march, the logistics of the campaign, the Senate debating in the shadow of military crisis. Not steppe-spare (the Republic was too verbal for that) but weighted with the urgency of a state at war.
