## Innovations Logged

**Innovation 1 — Knowledge System as Structural Principle**

The chapter shows sakoku not as primitive isolation but as a sophisticated knowledge system. The system worked because everyone accepted the boundary. When one actor violated the assumption, the system broke through revelation of conditionality rather than through military defeat.

**Category:** Evidence / Honesty / Progression  
**Technique:** Showing a system's fragility by revealing its dependence on external acceptance (knowledge-system-level analysis, v1.3 amendment applied)  
**Source:** Bakumatsu chapter, Sections 1–2

**Innovation 2 — Trapped Choice as Institutional Engine**

The chapter implies that the shogunate faced a genuine trapped choice: fighting risks foreign war; negotiating reveals weakness. The institution breaks not from external force but from attempting to choose between two unacceptable options.

**Category:** Progression / Voice  
**Technique:** Showing institutional collapse through the logic of impossible choice (rather than military defeat or external conquest)  
**Source:** Bakumatsu chapter, throughout (implicit in structure)
