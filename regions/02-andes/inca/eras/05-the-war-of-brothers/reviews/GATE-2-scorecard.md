**Notes:** The chapter's central achievement is telling the conquest from inside the Inca world without offering a verdict. The book-to-ear moment is the chapter's most important scene: it captures the encounter between two information technologies (the book and the quipu) in a way that makes the Inca framework the lens through which the reader understands the encounter. The closing completes the book-level arc by returning to the opening images of Era 1 (the lake, the staff, the puna, the wool) and finding continuity in the culture's own persistence: the ayni still functioning, the quipus still holding, the thread still tied. The five-era arc (seeds → scaling → limits → peak/crack → destruction/persistence) is complete.

**Innovation candidate:** The book-to-ear moment — using the Inca's information-technology framework to render the encounter with a rival information technology (the written book) — may constitute a new technique for conquest chapters: making the colonized culture's own epistemological categories the lens through which the reader experiences the encounter.
