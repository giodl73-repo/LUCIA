# Sources — Wolof / Jolof Confederacy

## Primary / Oral

- Wolof oral traditions — maintained by gewel families; versions collected by various scholars
- Portuguese chronicles — accounts of Senegambian contact from the 1440s onward
- French colonial administrative records — Senegal, nineteenth century

## Academic

- Boulègue, Jean. *Le Grand Jolof (XIIIe–XVIe siècle)*. 1987. Façades. — the foundational study of the confederacy
- Klein, Martin A. *Islam and Imperialism in Senegal: Sine-Saloum, 1847–1914*. 1968. Edinburgh University Press.
- Diouf, Mamadou. *Histoire du Sénégal: Le modèle islamo-wolof et ses périphéries*. 1990. Maisonneuve & Larose.
- Searing, James F. *West African Slavery and Atlantic Commerce: The Senegal River Valley, 1700–1860*. 1993. Cambridge University Press.
- Bouche, Denise. *L'enseignement dans les territoires français de l'Afrique occidentale de 1817 à 1920*. 1975. — educational context
- Diouf, Sylviane. *Servants of Allah: African Muslims Enslaved in the Americas*. 1998. NYU Press. — Wolof and other Senegambian Muslims in the Atlantic world

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on the Wolof gewel tradition specifically (distinct from Mande jeli)
- Archaeological work on the Jolof heartland
- Scholarship on Lat Dior's political career and his religious transformation
- Analysis of the horse-for-captive trade and its specific political effects on the confederacy
