# Board Review — Weildler Guerra Araújo
## Wayúu, Era 3: The Great Cattle Era

**Reviewer:** Weildler Guerra Araújo (Wayúu scholar, pütchipü'üi jurisprudence, Wayuunaiki language)
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The pütchipü'üi is rendered correctly in its essential logic and most of its operational detail. The water hospitality protocol (the palavrero drinking carefully, neither refusing nor draining the vessel) is ethnographically accurate. The core distinction the chapter draws, payment vs. acknowledgment of relationship, is the correct analytical distinction and one that most outside scholarship misses.

**Required refinement:** The sentence "That was the palavrero's art: to find the resolution that both parties could accept as a victory, because only victories held" implies the pre-consultation model is the universal practice. Some palabreros in the documented tradition arrive without a pre-determined settlement. Revise to: "That, for Jayariyuu, was the palabrero's art." This is a small change but matters for cultural accuracy.

**Minor notation:** Pattern names described in "The Pattern Travels" (serpentine double-line, diagonal interlocking grid, stepped rhombus) are plausible but not attributed to specific eirruku. This is correct; the chapter should not attribute specific named patterns without more precise documentation. The approach is appropriate.

The use of *sawa* for both grass and abundance is linguistically correct. The Jepira dream reference is cosmologically accurate and correctly underplayed.

**Verdict:** APPROVED with one required refinement.
