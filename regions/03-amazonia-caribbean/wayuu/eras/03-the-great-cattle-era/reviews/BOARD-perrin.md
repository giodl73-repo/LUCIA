# Board Review — Michel Perrin
## Wayúu, Era 3: The Great Cattle Era

**Reviewer:** Michel Perrin (Wayúu cosmology, oral tradition, ecological knowledge, yoluha/Jepira cycle)
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The ecological knowledge sections are this chapter's strongest contribution from a cultural documentation perspective. The behavioral observation of cattle vs. goats, the sky-reading, and Seyuuya's accumulated pastoral intelligence are consistent with documented Wayúu pastoral knowledge as recorded in fieldwork studies of the Guajira region. The chapter correctly treats this knowledge as empirical and sophisticated without needing to say so explicitly.

The Jepira reference in the mochila section is cosmologically accurate and correctly handled: the dream as a source of valid pattern knowledge is not an exotic claim in Wayúu culture but a normative one. The chapter's choice not to explain this is correct; it allows the reference to stand as the Wayúu world's own logic.

The dry well as political fact is exactly right. In fieldwork, inter-eirruku conflicts over water access were consistently more significant than any other single source of dispute.

**Minor ecological notation:** The Guajira has two wet seasons: the minor rains (April-May) and the major rains (September-November). The opening's reference to "the wet season" as singular is a slight simplification. This does not affect the chapter's argument and requires no revision, but a note in figures.md or sources that the Guajira's bimodal rainfall pattern was the ecological context for the pastoral migrations would add precision for readers who study the region.

**Verdict:** APPROVED. No revisions required; one minor notation for sources context.
