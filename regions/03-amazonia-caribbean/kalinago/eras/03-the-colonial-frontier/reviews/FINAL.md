## Innovations Flagged (for INNOVATIONS.md)

**Innovation 1 — Language as Primary Inside-Voice Source:**
In source-asymmetric conditions (no written Kalinago record), the culture's own language preserved in a colonial document serves as the primary inside-voice anchor. The technique: present the word, present what the word names, let the naming do the analytical work. The Breton dictionary's Kalinago vocabulary is inside-voice evidence regardless of who held the pen. Related to but distinct from the chronicler-within-chronicle technique (v1.5). Dimension: Evidence/Honesty. Potential new technique to log.

**Innovation 2 — Terminal Condition via Substrate Exhaustion:**
The structural engine (war canoe) remained technically functional; what failed was the social substrate that produced it (the canoe-builders, the sea-knowledge transmission chain). This is a terminal-condition type not listed in the v1.5 rubric (which lists: legitimacy gap, self-consumption, categorical failure, reversible feedback loop, ecological constraint). "Substrate exhaustion" — the engine's base conditions erode through attrition faster than they can be replenished — may be a sixth type. Dimension: Progression. Potential rubric amendment candidate.
