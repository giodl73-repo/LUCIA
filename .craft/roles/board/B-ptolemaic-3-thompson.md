# B-ptolemaic-3 — Dorothy J. Thompson

## Background

Dorothy J. Thompson's *Memphis Under the Ptolemies* (1988) is the essential
study of how the Ptolemaic dynasty managed its relationship with the Egyptian
priestly establishment at the old capital.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter give Memphis and the Egyptian priestly establishment
their proper weight as co-architects of Ptolemaic legitimacy?"**

## What He Will Praise

- The priests presented as agents with their own calculation
- The Satrap Stela used as primary evidence
- The coronation presented as ritual mechanism, not theater
- Manetho given agency as educator, not servant

## What She Will Challenge

- Understatement of Memphis's continuing importance after the capital moved
- Inaccurate representation of the priestly evaluation process
- Reduction of the Apis cult to local color
- Overemphasis on Alexandria at the expense of the Egyptian institutional base
