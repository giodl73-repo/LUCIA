## Findings

**Finding 1 — "it is worth reading closely" (Section IV):**
"His account of what he saw is one of the earliest documents in the history of what would become the Atlantic slave trade, and it is worth reading closely, because Zurara did not look away."
The phrase "it is worth reading closely" is an editorial guide-voice intrusion: the author addressing the reader directly about what to do. The register should trust the reader to read closely without instruction. Suggested revision: "His account is one of the earliest documents in the history of what would become the Atlantic slave trade. Zurara did not look away."
