## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — PANEL-2 Review (Notes)

**Focus:** Sensory plan for the full chapter. The opening has fire and bodies. The chapter needs the Kalahari.

**The sensory gaps I see in the section plan:**

- **Section 1 (revised opening):** Needs the night sky, the cold, the sound of feet on sand. The plan acknowledges this. Good.
- **Section 2 (tracking):** This is the most sensory-rich section available in the entire chapter. The tracker is on the ground. He is looking at sand, at broken grass, at the angle of a hoof print. The light matters — morning light shows spoor differently than noon light. The heat matters — the tracker starts at dawn because the sand is cool and the prints are sharp. The chapter must put the reader's eyes at ground level.
- **Section 3 (/Kaggen stories):** The storytelling scene needs a physical setting. Night. Fire. The teller's voice. The children's faces in firelight. The pauses. The laughter.
- **Section 4 (sharing):** The meat itself. The butchering. The distribution. Who gets which cut. The fat, which is the most valued part — the texture and taste of eland fat, which the San prize above all other foods.
- **Section 6 (rock art):** I want to be inside the rock shelter. The temperature change as you step out of the sun. The smell of dry stone. The way the paintings appear as your eyes adjust to the half-light. The texture of the rock surface under your hand.
- **Section 7 (landing):** Return to the fire. The cold is deeper now. The stars have moved. The physical passage of time during the trance dance.

**What I would add to the plan:**
One moment of absolute Kalahari stillness. The silence between songs. The desert at night when nothing is moving except the stars. This silence is not empty — it is the acoustic environment in which the San's extraordinary hearing operates (they can hear game at distances that seem impossible to outsiders). The silence is full of information, and the chapter should have one moment where the reader hears it.
