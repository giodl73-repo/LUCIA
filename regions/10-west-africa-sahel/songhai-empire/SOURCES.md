# Sources — Songhai Empire

## Primary

- Mahmud Kati, *Tarikh al-Fattash*, c. 1519 (completed later by descendants) — history of the western Sudan from a Timbuktu scholar
- Abd al-Rahman al-Sadi, *Tarikh al-Sudan*, c. 1655 — the most important primary source for Songhai and its fall
- Ahmad Baba al-Timbukti, *Nayl al-Ibtihaj* — biographical dictionary of Maliki scholars; intellectual history of the western Sudan
- Leo Africanus (al-Hasan ibn Muhammad al-Wazzan), *Description of Africa*, c. 1526 — account of Timbuktu and the Songhai state from a Moroccan-Andalusian traveler

## Academic

- Hunwick, John O. *Timbuktu and the Songhay Empire: Al-Sadi's Tarikh al-Sudan down to 1613*. 1999. Brill.
- Levtzion, Nehemia. *Ancient Ghana and Mali*. 1973. Methuen.
- Cissoko, Sékéné Mody. *Tombouctou et l'Empire Songhay*. 1975. Les Nouvelles Editions Africaines.
- Hall, Bruce S. *A History of Race in Muslim West Africa, 1600–1960*. 2011. Cambridge University Press.
- Gomez, Michael A. *African Dominion*. 2018. Princeton University Press.
- Abitbol, Michel. *Tombouctou et les Arma*. 1979. Maisonneuve & Larose. — the post-conquest Moroccan pashalik
