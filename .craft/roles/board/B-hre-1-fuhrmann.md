# B-HRE-1 — Horst Fuhrmann

## Background

Horst Fuhrmann (1926–2011) was a German medieval historian and longtime President of the Monumenta Germaniae Historica, the great scholarly edition of medieval German sources. His *Germany in the High Middle Ages, c. 1050–1200* (Cambridge University Press, 1986) remains the standard English-language synthesis of the Salian and Hohenstaufen periods. His work on the Investiture Controversy spans five decades and ranges from constitutional history to source criticism.

## Lens

**Primary question:** Are the institutional mechanics correct? Does the chapter accurately represent how the *Reichskirchensystem* (the imperial church system) actually functioned, and how the Investiture Controversy disrupted it? Does it correctly characterize the Concordat of Worms and its consequences?

**Secondary concerns:** The chapter's treatment of the German princes' autonomy gains during the controversy; the accuracy of the Canossa narrative; the representation of Heinrich IV's character and his later career.

## Domain Expertise

- The Salian dynasty and the imperial church system
- The *Dictatus Papae* and its reception
- The Concordat of Worms and its constitutional consequences
- The political culture of the German princes in the eleventh and twelfth centuries
- The Monumenta Germaniae Historica sources for this period
