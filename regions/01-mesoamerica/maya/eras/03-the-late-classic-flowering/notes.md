# Production Notes — Maya, The Late Classic Flowering (Era 3)

## Structural Engine

**Inscription-as-reality.** The Maya Late Classic's structural engine is the identity between the carved record and the event itself. A stela is not a memorial; it IS the act. The Long Count date is not a timestamp; it IS the event's position in cosmic order. The chapter's arc follows this engine from its flowering through its rupture: when the inscriptions stop, the political system they constitute also stops.

This engine is a new type: not a covenant (Mali), not a psychological mechanism (Haudenosaunee), not a knowledge system (Tang/San), not a material medium (Benin), not a system-as-machine (Mongol), not a prestige-exchange (Tongan). It is an **inscription-system** where the record and the event are ontologically identical.

## Arc

1. **Opening** — Lady K'abal Xook's bloodletting at Yaxchilan (681 CE). Establishes inscription-as-act.
2. **Pakal's Lakamha** — The tomb, the inscriptions, the dynasty text. The Long Count as the framework of legitimacy.
3. **The Rival Cities** — Tikal vs. Calakmul (the "superpower" rivalry). Named kings, specific battles, the star-war glyph. Shield Jaguar of Yaxchilan. Lady Six Sky of Naranjo.
4. **The Ballgame and Xibalba** — The Hero Twins, the cosmological logic. The ballcourt as Underworld portal. The game as enacted cosmology, not sport.
5. **The Scribes and the Codices** — Painted ceramics, the Aj Tz'iib (the scribe). The written word as living act.
6. **The Transformation** — The last dated monuments (9.19.0.0.0 at some sites, later at Tonina). What stopped and what did not. No "collapse" framing — the Maya reorganize, move northward, continue. 6 million today.

## Key Figures

- **K'inich Janaab Pakal** (Palenque/Lakamha) — acceded 9.9.2.4.8 (615 CE), died 9.12.11.5.18 (683 CE). 68-year reign. The sarcophagus lid. The Temple of the Inscriptions.
- **Lady K'abal Xook** (Yaxchilan) — wife of Itzamnaaj Bahlam III ("Shield Jaguar"). Lintel 24, 25, 26. Bloodletting scene.
- **Yik'in Chan K'awiil** (Tikal/Mutul) — defeated Calakmul's vassal Wak Kab'nal of Naranjo. Tikal's resurgence.
- **Yuknoom Ch'een II "the Great"** (Calakmul/Kaan) — Tikal's great rival. Orchestrated a network of vassal alliances.
- **Lady Six Sky** (Naranjo) — arrived from Dos Pilas, restored Naranjo's dynasty. Depicted on stelae standing on a captive.
- **Ukit Kan Lek Tok'** — painter of the Bonampak murals or patron.

## Calendar Rules

Long Count dates LEAD. CE in parentheses. Format: 9.15.0.0.0 (22 August 731 CE).

## Voice

**Inscribed. Architectural. Precise.** The Maya voice is layered, accumulative, and visual. The prose should feel carved. Sentences build like stone courses. The rhythm is deliberate, measured, like a hieroglyphic text read column by column. NOT steppe (too spare), NOT maritime (too rolling), NOT oral-tradition (too spoken). This is a culture of sculptors and scribes. The prose should feel like inscription.

## Achebe Test Watchpoints

- Bloodletting is not "barbaric" — it is work, communication, obligation.
- The ballgame is not "ritual violence" — it is enacted cosmology.
- The "collapse" is not a mystery or tragedy — it is a transformation, and the Maya continue.
- 6 million Maya alive today. End with continuity, not loss.
- No "lost civilization" framing anywhere.

## Honest Uncertainty

- The collapse: present what is known (drought evidence, political fragmentation, warfare increase, Long Count cessation) and what is not. No single-cause explanation.
- Calakmul inscriptions less well preserved than Tikal's — acknowledge the evidentiary asymmetry.
- The codices: most were burned after contact. Acknowledge the destruction honestly.
