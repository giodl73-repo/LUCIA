The production bible correctly identifies that the machinery of belief must be shown at method level. I'm satisfied that Section 3 (The Soviet Installation) is the right place to show this. The question is: what specific lever of institutional practice do you show?

The notes suggest "A school curriculum? A party rally? Show the machinery at method level." I want to be more precise.

**The most powerful single mechanism you could show is this: the study session.**

The study session (study group, organized learning of Kim Il-sung's thought) is the foundational institutional technology of the North Korean state. It is how belief is installed, maintained, and verified. It is simultaneously:
- Intellectual exercise (studying a text)
- Political demonstration (showing loyalty through participation)
- Surveillance mechanism (anyone who asks the wrong question reveals doubt)
- Ritual reinforcement (repetition becomes belief)

**Here's what I recommend:** In Section 3, include a scene (maybe 300 words) of a first study session. The content could be:
- A Korean Communist leading a study group
- The text they're studying: maybe one of Kim Il-sung's early proclamations about land reform or resistance
- The discussion: people offering their understanding of the text
- The moment of confirmation: when the leader corrects a misinterpretation, everyone adjusts
- The structural revelation: this group will meet every week, and attendance will be mandatory, and what you say in this room will be noted

One scene like this would do the work that your summary is trying to do, and it would do it with texture, with bodies, with the sensory experience of how power is installed through knowledge production.

**Secondary point:** The machinery also works through **commemoration**. By 1950, there will be statues of Kim Il-sung. There will be holidays named for him. There will be photographs in every room. These material objects are not decoration. They are the physical embodiment of the mythology. You don't need to explain this. Just show a room where a portrait appears for the first time, and show how people respond to it.

**Verdict:** Good structural instinct. Sharpen it by choosing study sessions as your methodology anchor. One scene of a study session would illuminate the entire machinery.
