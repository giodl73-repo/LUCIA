# Sources — Kongo Kingdom

## Primary

- Afonso I's letters — preserved in the Portuguese national archives (ANTT); translated and published by multiple scholars
- Italian capuchin missionary accounts — seventeenth and early eighteenth century; Cuvelier, Jadin collections
- Dutch records — West India Company archives; seventeenth century Kongo commercial contacts
- Thornton, John — the primary modern translator/analyst of Kongo diplomatic correspondence

## Academic

- Thornton, John K. *The Kingdom of Kongo: Civil War and Transition, 1641–1718*. 1983. University of Wisconsin Press.
- Thornton, John K. *Africa and Africans in the Making of the Atlantic World, 1400–1800*. 2nd ed. 1998. Cambridge University Press.
- Hilton, Anne. *The Kingdom of Kongo*. 1985. Oxford University Press.
- MacGaffey, Wyatt. *Religion and Society in Central Africa: The BaKongo of Lower Zaire*. 1986. University of Chicago Press.
- Fromont, Cécile. *The Art of Conversion: Christian Visual Culture in the Kingdom of Kongo*. 2014. University of North Carolina Press.
- Sweet, James H. *Recreating Africa: Culture, Kinship, and Religion in the African-Portuguese World, 1441–1770*. 2003. University of North Carolina Press.
