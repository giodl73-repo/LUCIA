# Sources — Czechs / Bohemia

## Primary Sources

- *Chronica Boemorum* by Cosmas of Prague — c. 1119–1125; the foundational Czech chronicle
- Charles IV, *Vita Caroli* — the emperor's autobiography; in Latin and Czech
- *Golden Bull* (1356) — Charles IV's codification of the imperial electoral system
- Jan Hus, sermons and letters — especially the letter from Constance before his execution
- *Husitská kronika* by Vavřinec z Březové — contemporary chronicle of the Hussite revolution
- The Kutná Hora Decree (1409) — the university reform that Czech students gained majority votes; precipitated the German exodus and radicalized the reformers
- *Kronica česká* by Václav Hájek — later 16th century; the popular chronicle tradition

## Secondary Sources

- Spěváček, Jiří. *Karel IV. Život a dílo (1316–1378)*. Prague, 1979.
- Šmahel, František. *Die Hussitische Revolution*. 3 vols. Hannover, 2002.
- Fudge, Thomas A. *The Magnificent Ride: The First Reformation in Hussite Bohemia*. Ashgate, 1998.
- Bradley, J.F.N. *Czechoslovakia: A Short History*. Edinburgh University Press, 1971.
- Klassen, John M. *The Nobility and the Making of the Hussite Revolution*. Columbia University Press, 1978.
- Bartoš, F.M. *The Hussite Revolution 1424–1437*. East European Monographs, 1986.
- Heymann, Frederick G. *John Žižka and the Hussite Revolution*. Princeton University Press, 1955.
