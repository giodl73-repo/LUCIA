# Sources — Romani People

## Key Academic Works
- Ian Hancock, *We Are the Romani People* (2002, University of Hertfordshire Press) — the definitive overview by a leading Romani scholar
- Donald Kenrick & Grattan Puxon, *The Destiny of Europe's Gypsies* (1972) — foundational account including the Porajmos
- Yaron Matras, *Romani: A Linguistic Introduction* (2002, Cambridge) — the language and its Indian origins
- Michael Burleigh & Wolfgang Wippermann, *The Racial State: Germany 1933–1945* (1991) — the Nazi policies toward Roma
- Isabel Fonseca, *Bury Me Standing: The Gypsies and Their Journey* (1995, Vintage) — journalism and ethnography; widely read
- Wim Willems, *In Search of the True Gypsy* (1997) — the scholarly construction of "the Gypsy" and its effects

## Primary Sources
- Romani oral traditions — family histories, songs, oral law (require community access)
- The Nuremberg Laws documentation on Roma (1935–1938)
- Auschwitz-Birkenau survivor testimonies — Zentralrat Deutscher Sinti und Roma archive
- European Court of Human Rights case law on Roma discrimination (searchable at ECHR database)
