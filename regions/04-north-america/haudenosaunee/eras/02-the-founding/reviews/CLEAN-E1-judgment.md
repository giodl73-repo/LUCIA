## E-1 — The Judgment Auditor — CLEAN pass on The Founding (Kaianere'kó:wa)

**Violations found:** 3

1. Line 9: "It was an agricultural system that fed the longhouse communities" — **P3 (borderline):** The phrase "agricultural system" leans toward a modern analytical frame. The three sisters were already well described through their physical reality (cornstalks, beans, squash leaves). Consider rephrasing to "It was how the longhouse fed itself" or similar.

2. Line 65: "When European observers later asked to see the Haudenosaunee 'constitution'" — **P2 (implicit hierarchy):** This sentence introduces a European lens ("constitution" in scare quotes, European observers as a reference point). The comparison serves the point about wampum-and-voice being a unified knowledge system, but the framing momentarily centers a European expectation. Consider reframing: the wampum and voice were the law, and those who could not read beads could not read the law. This makes the same point without importing the European perspective.

3. Line 75: "The sachem's legitimacy came not from election by the governed, and not from inheritance, and not from conquest." — **P3 (borderline):** The negations define the Haudenosaunee system by what it is NOT (not election, not inheritance, not conquest). These are categories from other political traditions. The sentence that follows, about the clan mother's judgment, is the positive description and works well. The negation series is borderline — it could be read as contrast within the culture's own logic or as measuring against external systems. Author decides.

**Verdict:** 3 fixes needed (1 P2, 2 P3)
