## Voice Random-Paragraph Test

Three paragraphs selected at random:

**Paragraph A (knowledge section):** "The tepui hekura moved differently in the chest — slower, heavier, with a quality of endurance that the smaller forest hekura did not have. The knowledge they carried was correspondingly older. When a tepui hekura showed the landscape, it showed a landscape of longer cycles: the flood histories of the rivers, the movements of animal populations across generations, patterns of plant distribution that had settled over centuries."

Identity from rhythm alone: The prose moves at the speed of geological time, slow and heavy. Comparative observation (tepui vs. forest hekura) without analytical scaffolding. Sensory-cosmological. Identifiably Shamanic-Observational. Would not fit any other culture in the register.

**Paragraph B (feast section):** "The feast's second day was when Aramo and Towaho worked. The two shapori sat at the plaza's edge, the yakoana prepared, and the calling began in sequence. Aramo first called the waterfall's hekura and showed Towaho what it had revealed about the child's fever. Towaho listened in the way shapori listened to each other — not hearing words but attending to the quality of the hekura's presence, the accuracy of the calling, the information coming through."

Identity from rhythm alone: Short, deliberate, present-tense observation. Technical description ("the way shapori listened to each other") rendered from inside the practice, not from outside it. The analytical content (how inter-community shamanic collaboration works) is carried entirely by the description of the action. Identifiably Shamanic-Observational.

**Paragraph C (shabono section):** "The gaps were the point. Entry happened through invitation, through relationship, through the acknowledgment that passing through another family's section required the tacit permission of that family. Identity from rhythm alone: The period-stop after "The gaps were the point" — the register's characteristic pause mechanism. Then the elaboration through specific practice rather than abstraction. The stranger who blunders is rendered from inside (identified as someone who doesn't understand where they are, not as an outsider described with anthropological distance). Identifiably inside-voice, maintenance-register.

**Random-paragraph test result: PASS.** All three paragraphs identify as Yanomami through rhythm and perspective.

## Register Drift Check

**Potential drift points identified:**

1. **"The political economy of the feast was straightforward in its structure and demanding in its execution"** — "political economy" is a social-science term. However, it is used here in a way that is immediately grounded in the culture's own logic (the next sentence explains what "straightforward" and "demanding" mean in practice). Marginal. The phrase "political economy" is the one moment where an academic term floats briefly before being grounded. Consider: "The feast's structure was clear and its demands were not." — simpler, stays inside.

2. **"This was not a limitation. It was the design."** — E-1 has flagged this as an outside-anticipating frame. Voice analysis confirms: this is the voice stepping outside momentarily to address a reader who might have read the preceding paragraph as describing a deficiency. The inside-voice version simply continues: the design was this, and here is how the community lived with it.

3. **"The overall pattern was uneven: not a smooth transmission of a fixed body of knowledge but a living practice that grew and contracted..."** — "overall pattern," "smooth transmission," "fixed body of knowledge" — three abstractions in sequence. This paragraph is the chapter's most at-risk passage for textbook drift. The analytical content is correct and necessary, but the vehicle is slightly wrong. The same content is reachable through a specific generation or specific shapori who was exceptional or who failed to transmit. The chapter has earned the reader's trust; use a specific figure to carry this abstract point.
