## Assessment: APPROVE with minor note

The chapter's treatment of the Makoko's onkani authority is accurate in its main lines. The commercial class characterization — the transmitted route knowledge, the professional ethic of not revealing information, the intelligence gathering during hospitality exchanges — is consistent with what the Teke commercial culture as documented in the 1880s period and extrapolable backward with appropriate caution.

**Finding 1 (minor):** The commodity authentication sequence (weight, color, sound of the copper ingot) presents a reconstructed practice based on general knowledge of how pre-literate commercial authentication works. This is methodologically defensible. Recommend adding a brief note in figures.md acknowledging that the specific authentication details are reconstructed inference from general commercial culture knowledge rather than specifically documented Teke practice.

**Historical accuracy:** The onkani concept is rendered without over- or under-spiritualizing. The Pool physical description is accurate. The Portuguese 1483 date is accurate. The 1560s Kongo wars as a marker for the slave trade's entry is consistent with the documentary record.

**Conclusion:** I would recognize the Teke commercial culture in these pages. The chapter honors the scholarly record while telling the story the record itself cannot tell — the interior experience of the broker position across three centuries.
