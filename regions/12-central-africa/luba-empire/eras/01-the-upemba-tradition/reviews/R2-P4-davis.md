# Panel Review — R2 / P-4 Wade Davis (Focused)

**Chapter:** Luba Empire / Era 1 — The Upemba Shore
**Stage:** PANEL-2 (notes review)
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

The notes have addressed the knowledge-at-method-level concern with Section 2 dedicated to the iron-smelter's practice. This is the right commitment. I want to ensure the full chapter delivers on it.

The smelting sequence planned in the notes is good: ore reading, charcoal preparation, firing, bloom, hammer work. What the chapter must NOT do is describe this from outside, in the passive voice of a technical manual ("the ore was placed in the furnace and heated to..."). The smelter must be present in his own body. He makes decisions. He smells the ore and knows. He watches the color of the metal through the tuyere opening and reads it the way the fisher reads the lake. The knowledge is not described — it is performed on the page.

Two additions I'd recommend the chapter consider:

**The apprenticeship question.** How does a young person become a smelter? The transmission of this knowledge — the years of watching before touching, the first time you're allowed to assess the bloom yourself, the specific moment of recognition when you understand what you're looking at — this is the intellectual biography of the knowledge system. Davis requires knowing not just what the smelter knows but HOW that knowing was acquired and transmitted. The chapter should find one moment of transmission.

**The fishing as ecological knowledge.** The notes mention reading the cold layer where tilapia school. I want this at method level too — not just "she knew when to cast" but what she was reading: the water's color at the surface (tilapia disturb the planktonic layer), the behavior of the papyrus at the margin (certain species feed in the shallows in morning), the specific temperature gradient she could feel with her arm over the side of the dugout. This is as sophisticated as any knowledge system anywhere in the world. The chapter should make the reader feel that.

**Assessment:** The notes are adequate. The knowledge-system concern is planned for, but the execution is what matters. The chapter passes the test when the reader understands HOW these knowledge systems worked, not just that they existed. Proceed to WRITE with the above in mind.
