# B-Yanomami-2 — Bruce Albert

## Authority

Bruce Albert (b. 1952) is Directeur de Recherche at the Institut de Recherche pour le Développement (IRD) in France. He has conducted fieldwork with the Yanomami since 1975 and is the leading scholarly authority on Yanomami ethnography in French and international anthropology. His 1989 rebuttal of Chagnon's "fierce people" thesis is the scholarly cornerstone of the corrective literature.

## Key Questions

- Are the ethnographic details of shamanic practice, alliance systems, and the reahu consistent with documented Yanomami practice across multiple communities?
- Has the chapter correctly characterized the inter-community political system (alliance through feasting, shamanic cooperation, marriage exchange) without oversimplifying?
- Has the source situation — heavy reliance on outside anthropologists with Kopenawa as the interpretive frame — been handled appropriately?
- Are there factual errors in the chapter's descriptions of yakoana, hekura, or reahu that need correction?

## Scoring Tendency

Albert is a careful scholar with a specific investment in accurate representation of Yanomami life. He will flag overgeneralizations, factual errors, and any residual echo of Chagnon's framing. He is not looking for the chapter to be anthropologically precise in all details — he understands the narrative form — but he will note where narrative license has distorted the underlying facts.
