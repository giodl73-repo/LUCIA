## P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of San, The Deep Time (Opening)

**Overall:** This is one of the strongest openings I have seen in the project. It does the hardest thing an opening can do: it makes the reader care about a practice that could easily have been exoticized, and it does so by making the practice intelligible from the inside.

**What works:**
- The structural choice to open with the community, not the healer. The first line is the fire and the women singing. /Twi!kxao does not appear until the third paragraph. This is exactly right — the trance dance is a communal act, and by the time the healer enters, the reader understands the instrument he is playing within.
- The "He said" sequence (paragraph 4). Reported speech keeps the trance experience at one honest remove — the healer's own account — while making it vivid. This is a narrative solution to the epistemological problem of writing about altered states of consciousness.
- The closing image. Children watching and learning. The knowledge transfer is shown in a single sentence. No commentary. Perfect craft.

**What doesn't:**
- The transition from trance dance to rock art (paragraph 6) is slightly abrupt. We move from /Twi!kxao's specific healing to the Drakensberg paintings in a single sentence. A beat of physical return — the healer coming back to his body, the circle relaxing — would make the pivot to the paintings feel earned rather than editorial.
- The final knowledge paragraph ("We do not know when the trance dance began") shifts register slightly. The "we" voice is honest, but it is also the one moment where a narrator appears who is not inside the circle. Consider whether the honest uncertainty can be delivered without that shift.

**The question I'd push on:**
Can the chapter sustain this level of inside-voice for 5,000 words? The opening succeeds because it stays in the circle. The full chapter will need to cover tracking, social structure, rock art across millennia, and deep-time uncertainty. How will the narrative move across those topics without becoming a survey? What is the arc?
