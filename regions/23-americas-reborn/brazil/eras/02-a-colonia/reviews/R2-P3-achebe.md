# R2-P3 — Chinua Achebe (PANEL-2 — Notes Review)

**Notes reviewed:** Brazil, Era 2 — A Colônia — Production Bible
**Round:** Panel-2

---

The production bible addresses my two central concerns: the women of Palmares (the composite quilombola woman in figures.md and the ialorixá tradition in Section 5), and the internal diversity of African cultures in the diaspora (the Bantu/Nagô distinction held throughout).

I want to press on one thing further. The composite figure technique (v1.1, Figures amendment) is a valid method when the record cannot support a named individual. But the production bible should be explicit about what the composite is drawn from: which ethnographic and historical accounts are being synthesized? "A woman, born in Africa, arrived in Pernambuco on a slave ship" is a starting point; the figure needs the specificity of her origin (Dahomey? Angola? The Mina coast?), her approximate period, and her particular role in the transmission — was she an initiate? A daughter of an initiate? Was she carrying specific knowledge of a specific deity's ceremonies? The more specific the composite, the more honestly it can be disclosed as composite.

This is not pedantry. The history of the African diaspora in Brazil has suffered from exactly the kind of flattening I am warning against — the reduction of dozens of distinct peoples, languages, and religious traditions to "the Africans." The chapter's most important contribution may be to hold this specificity.

The section on Ganga Zumba is the one I will read most carefully. The judgment question is correctly framed: he was not wrong. But I want the chapter to give him the same depth it gives Zumbi — not as villain, not as accommodationist, but as a man who had thought carefully about what the quilombo was and what it owed its people. If the chapter gives Zumbi interiority and gives Ganga Zumba only a treaty decision, it has already passed judgment.
