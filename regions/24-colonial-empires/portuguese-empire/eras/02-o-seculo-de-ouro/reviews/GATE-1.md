## Notes

The opening achieves full marks. Key strengths:

1. **The Indian Ocean world as protagonist.** Calicut is not the backdrop for Portugal's arrival; it is a functioning world into which Portugal inserts itself. The Zamorin's customs system, the Arab merchants' wariness, the Chinese junks sharing the anchorage — these establish the empire's context from within the Indian Ocean's own logic.

2. **Saudade grounded in material conditions, not nostalgia.** The passage on saudade earns its abstraction because it is preceded by: specific sailors, specific deaths, specific economics, specific return ratios. The feeling is not announced; it arrives through its conditions.

3. **The feitoria as structural engine planted in the opening.** "Not a territory. A point on a network." The structural engine of the chapter — the feitoria chain as the empire's organizing principle, with its specific maintenance logic and terminal condition — is named and demonstrated before the chapter begins.

4. **Voice consistent with Region 20 Portugal but distinct from it.** The Research-Program (Maritime-Scientific) voice of Era 4 (Henrique) is deliberate, accumulative, hypothesis-and-result. The saudade-maritime voice here is also accumulative, but where Region 20's voice is directed (toward the proved hypothesis), this voice circles around absence. The departing ship, not the arriving chart. Distinct, appropriate to the empire rather than the nation.

5. **Em-dash count: 0.** Under the mechanical limit of 5.
