**Finding 2 — P3 (Borderline — author decides)**
Location: Section on Afonso's letters
Quoted text: "in the language of the people who would later claim that Africa had no written history worth recording"
Violation: Borderline. The chapter is making a rhetorical point about the archive's irony — that the letters are preserved by the same system that would later deny African literacy. This is a valid and historically accurate observation. But "would later claim" steps slightly outside 1526 — it is the author's commentary on the archive's historical irony, not Afonso's perspective. The sentence works, but it is the chapter speaking, not Afonso.
Author decision: The sentence can remain if the author judges the archive-irony point worth the slight register break. Alternatively: end on "he put the argument on record" and let the fact of the archive's existence carry the irony without naming it.

**Finding 3 — P3 (Borderline — author decides)**

However, in figures.md (not the chapter itself), editorial commentary is somewhat more permissible, as the figures section operates at one remove from the narrative voice.
Author decision: Consider removing "This is not an exoneration. It is a structural account." The No Verdicts note that follows ("The chapter's No Verdicts discipline applies here") is itself editorial meta-commentary that belongs in the figures rather than the chapter proper.
