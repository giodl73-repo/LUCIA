**Implicit verdict scan:**

One candidate phrase: "What the French colonial administration could not account for, in its dispatches and its analyses and its subsequent meetings of the colonial assembly, was the coordination. The insurrection had moved across the plain with a precision that the administration's categories had not anticipated."

This is not an implicit verdict — it is an inside-voice description of the administration's own surprise. "Could not account for" is the administration's limitation registered through the administration's own epistemological framework. Cleared.

Second candidate: "Napoleon received the news at Saint-Cloud and is recorded to have said: 'That man is dead, and France is well rid of him.'"

This is a primary source citation (the phrase appears in multiple contemporary accounts, though its precise provenance is debated). It is Napoleon's own words, not the author's verdict. The author does not comment on the phrase. Cleared.

Third candidate: "The system was very good at clean documents."

This is a sharp line, and I want to examine it. It is delivered at the end of the section on the *ordonnance royale* of 1825. It does not say "the system was unjust" or "the system was cruel." It says the system was good at clean documents. This is a description of the system's capabilities, made from inside the logic that produced those capabilities. The irony is present but it is the irony of the system's own premises — the same irony that runs through the whole chapter. This is not the author stepping outside to comment. This is the register working as it should. Cleared.

**One flag for revision:**

Section II, paragraph 3: "Condorcet had laid out the arithmetic: the colonial economy produced profit for approximately 10,000 French citizens and misery for 500,000 Africans and their descendants."

"Misery" is an outside verdict. The chapter is using the word to describe the condition of the enslaved, which is accurate, but "misery" carries an emotional weight that is the author's, not the system's. The system did not use the word "misery" about its own administration. It used words like "labor," "production," "the colonial order." Suggested revision: "the colonial economy produced profit for approximately 10,000 French citizens and the productive labor of 500,000 Africans and their descendants, at costs the ledger did not record." This stays inside the administrative logic while conveying the same weight.

**Verdict:** One revision required (Section II, "misery" → in-register phrasing). Otherwise clean.
