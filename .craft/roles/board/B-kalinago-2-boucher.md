# B-Kalinago-2 — Philip Boucher

Philip Boucher is an historian of the French Caribbean whose *Cannibal
Encounters* (1992) systematically reassessed the European construction of
Kalinago identity in colonial-period sources. His work on the French Lesser
Antilles — including Guadeloupe, Martinique, and Dominica — provides the
most detailed scholarly account of French-Kalinago relations in the period
this chapter covers (1600–1650 specifically). He is the primary authority
on the Breton sources and their limitations.
