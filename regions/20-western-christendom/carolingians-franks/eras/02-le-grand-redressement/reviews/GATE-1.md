## Gate Verdict

**29/30 — PASSES GATE-1**

Target: 24+/30. This opening passes comfortably.

**Strongest dimensions:** Scene, World, Texture, Voice, Pull — all at 5. The
*correctio* concept does the heavy lifting for World, connecting the corrupted
manuscript directly to the corrupted empire and establishing the era's engine
before the chapter proper begins.

**One note (Honesty, 4/5):** The phrase "not education as the modern world means
it" is a ghost of an outside voice. It risks positioning the modern reader's
assumption as the default. In the chapter, this distinction should be made
through the culture's own terms (the capitularies' own language of *correctio*)
rather than via negation of a modern assumption. PANEL-1 should assess whether
this is a craft risk or a craft choice.

**Voice flag:** The "Capitulary" register is new — not present in the v1.4
Voice Spectrum taxonomy. It belongs between Court (too layered, too slow) and
Edict-Inscriptional (too carved, too outward-facing). Capitulary moves at the
speed of deliberate reform: declarative main clause, brief qualification,
back to the directive. If the chapter sustains this at full length, log for
innovation consideration.

**Em-dash count:** 0. Voice discipline confirmed.
