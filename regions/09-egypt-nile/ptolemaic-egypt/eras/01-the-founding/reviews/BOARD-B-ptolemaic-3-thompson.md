## BOARD — Dorothy J. Thompson — Review of Ptolemaic Egypt, Era 1: The Founding

**Overall assessment:** I am pleased with the treatment of Memphis and the priestly establishment. The chapter gives the priests genuine agency. The Satrap Stela is correctly used. The crowning ceremony is presented as mechanism, not theater. Manetho is given his proper role as educator, not translator. These are the right choices.

**Memphis and the priestly class:**

1. **The Satrap Stela** is correctly dated (311 BCE) and its significance accurately described. The stela was indeed erected at Buto. The interpretation (the priests composed the rhetoric, Ptolemy approved it) is consistent with how these documents functioned.

2. **The coronation** is described at the right level of detail. The kyphi incense is a good sensory choice and historically attested as an evening offering compound. The fivefold titulary is correctly identified as the mechanism of legitimation.

3. **The Apis bull cult** is mentioned in the opening scene and in the expanded passage. This is correct: the Apis cult at Memphis was one of the most important sacred animal cults, and Ptolemaic maintenance of it was a key demonstration of legitimacy.

4. **The priestly evaluation criteria** (temple maintenance, daily offerings, sacred animal cults, estate protection) are correctly identified. The detail about offerings being distributed among priests as salary after spiritual consumption by the god is accurate and important. This is how the Egyptian temple economy functioned.

**Areas for attention:**

5. **Memphis after the capital moved.** The chapter correctly notes that Ptolemy moved the capital to Alexandria, but Memphis's continuing importance as the site of Egyptian legitimation could be stronger. The Ptolemaic coronation at Memphis continued even after Alexandria became the administrative capital. The chapter implies this but could state it more explicitly.

6. **Manetho's temple affiliation.** The chapter says "probably from the temple of Ra at Sebennytos." This is uncertain. Sebennytos is correct as his origin, but his specific temple affiliation is not firmly established. The "probably" is appropriate.

**Overall verdict:** Sound. The Egyptian dimension of Ptolemaic rule is given its proper weight. One suggestion: strengthen the statement about Memphis's continuing role after the capital transfer. Otherwise, this chapter passes the Achebe Test from the Egyptian priestly perspective.
