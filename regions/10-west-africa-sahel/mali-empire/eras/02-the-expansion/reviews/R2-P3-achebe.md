# P-3 — Chinua Achebe — Review of "When the Empire Reached the Sea" Notes

**Overall:** The notes maintain the inside voice. The structural plan is sound. One concern.

**What works:**
- Khalifa is framed as "test case," not villain. The notes explicitly state: "Not a villain — a ruler who broke the covenant's terms and was removed by the system designed to remove him." This is correct. Keep this discipline in the draft.
- Sakura's status as jon is handled within the Kurukan Fuga's framework. The notes do not apologize for the social category, do not euphemize it, and do not exoticize it. They present it as the covenant presents it: a social order with defined roles and relationships.
- The composite Dyula merchant is disclosed honestly, per v1.1 Honest Limits.

**What I'd push on:**
- The section on the goldfields (Section 4) must handle the Wangara secret-keeping as institutional intelligence, not as exotica. The Wangara communities' refusal to reveal gold sources to outsiders was a rational economic strategy that protected their bargaining position. It must be presented from inside their logic, not as a "fascinating" cultural practice.
