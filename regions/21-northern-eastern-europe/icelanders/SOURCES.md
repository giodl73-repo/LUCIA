# Sources — Icelanders

## Primary Sources

- *Íslendingabók* by Ari Þorgilsson — c. 1122; the earliest history of Iceland
- *Landnámabók* — the Book of Settlements; records of the first settlers; multiple versions
- *Njáls saga* — the longest and most complex saga; the fullest picture of the Commonwealth
- *Egils saga* — biography of the skald-warrior Egill Skallagrímsson; attributed to Snorri Sturluson
- *Laxdæla saga* — the family saga; famous for Guðrún Ósvífrsdóttir
- *Grœnlendinga saga* and *Eiríks saga rauða* — the Vinland sagas
- *Sturlunga saga* — the contemporary account of the Sturlung age civil wars
- Snorri Sturluson, *Prose Edda* and *Heimskringla* — the greatest works of medieval Norse literature, written at Reykholt

- Byock, Jesse L. *Viking Age Iceland*. Penguin, 2001.

- Vésteinsson, Orri. *The Christianization of Iceland*. Oxford University Press, 2000.
