# R1-P3 — Chinua Achebe

**Chapter:** Naqada I — The Amratian
**Stage:** PANEL-1
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## First Read

The voice is nearly right. The opening tells this story from within the craftsman's world, from inside his hands. The copper is not an archaeological artifact. It is a tool someone made. The pottery is not Naqada I White Cross-Lined Ware. It is a vessel with walls so thin a thumb shows pink through them. This is the correct instinct. The culture is the subject, not the specimen.

## What Works

The people of Nubt are not explained. They are presented through what they make. The opening never says "Predynastic Egyptians believed" or "this culture practiced." It says: they shaped, they painted, they knapped, they laid their dead facing west. The verbs are active and the people are the subjects of them. This is ownership.

The refusal to impose modern categories is well executed. "Did not have a word for revolution" is good because it frames the absence as the culture's own fact, not as a lack. "Neither would have called this a network, because it had no name" does the same work. The opening respects the culture's own categories by refusing to apply ours.

The dead are treated with full seriousness. "They were somewhere else, and they would need what the living could provide." This is the culture's own understanding, stated without quotation marks, without hedging, without the word "believed." It is simply true in the world of the opening. This is how you write from inside.

## What Needs Attention

One moment gives me pause. "It was not the first copper tool anyone in the valley had made." This sentence steps slightly outside the craftsman's moment to make a historical observation. He would not think this. He would think about the taper, the weight, the ore. The sentence is the author's knowledge, not the character's world. It is a small violation but it is there.

"The people of Nubt did not have a word for revolution" is effective but carries a faint whiff of the author comparing this culture against a framework where "revolution" is a relevant concept. The sentence works because the next one ("They had copper") reclaims the interior perspective immediately. But the framing implies that revolution is the concept we should be looking for and are not finding. A subtlety.

## The Question I'd Push On

Who is this craftsman? Not his name, which the record cannot give us. But his place in his community. Is he working alone? Does he have an apprentice watching? Is the workshop one of several or the only one? The chapter will need to render this figure as a person, not merely as a pair of hands. The opening grounds him in material practice. The chapter must give him a world of people.
