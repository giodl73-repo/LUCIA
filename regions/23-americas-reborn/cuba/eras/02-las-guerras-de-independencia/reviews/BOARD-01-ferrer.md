# BOARD Review — Ada Ferrer
**Domain:** Cuban history, Afro-Cuban culture, independence wars, Caribbean historiography
**Primary Works:** *Insurgent Cuba: Race, Nation, and Revolution* (1999); *Cuba: An American History* (2021)
**Chapter:** Cuba Era 2 — Las Guerras de Independencia — La Manigua
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

## Historical Accuracy Assessment

The chapter's account of the independence wars is historically sound. The dates are correct: Ten Years' War 1868–1878, Little War 1879–1880 (correctly placed in Maceo's exile period rather than elaborated), the 1895 landing, Dos Ríos, Maceo's western invasion October 1895, his death December 1896, the USS Maine February 1898, the Treaty of Paris December 1898, the Platt Amendment 1901, the republic declared 1902.

The figure of 1,500 soldiers for Maceo's western invasion is on the conservative end of estimates but defensible; some sources put the initial force higher and accounting for losses en route the figure varies. No correction required.

The reconcentration death toll estimate (170,000–300,000) is appropriately presented as a range, and the language ("the most careful historical accounting") is honest about the difficulty of the figures. Correct.

The assertion that when Santiago surrendered, the Cuban Liberation Army was not permitted to enter the city is historically accurate and important. This is one of the most telling moments of the independence wars' outcome and the chapter is right to include it.

## Specific Commendation

The Protest of Baraguá is correctly characterized: Maceo's refusal was not merely military stubbornness but a precise political position about what the war had been for. The chapter gets this right. Too many accounts treat the Protest as an episode of Maceo's intransigence rather than as the single clearest statement of the Afro-Cuban independence tradition's terms. The chapter holds the distinction.

The Ortiz tobacco/sugar counterpoint is correctly positioned as a framework for understanding the social geography of the independence movement. This is exactly the right lens.

## Concerns

The chapter correctly states that the USS Maine's explosion "has never been definitively established" and cites the 1976 inquiry, but does not mention the 1998 National Geographic investigation (which reached similar conclusions: internal rather than external explosion). This is a minor gap that can be addressed with a parenthetical addition.

The chapter states that Maceo led "1,500 soldiers" on the western invasion. The more historically precise framing is that he left with roughly that force; he was joined by additional forces along the route and the western invasion ultimately mobilized more. For a chapter at this level, the framing is defensible but could note that the force grew as it moved.

The chapter does not mention Rafael Montoro or the Cuban autonomists, who represented the alternative political current arguing for reform rather than independence. Their presence in the period (they continued to advocate for autonomy even into 1897) is the background against which the independence argument was made. This is an omission rather than an error, and the chapter cannot include everything, but the autonomists' position is the political context that makes the independence movement's choice meaningful.

## Cultural Authenticity

The Lucumí and orixá passage is accurate in its framework. Ogún as the orixá of iron and warriors is correct. The Abakuá brotherhoods and Palo Monte are correctly named as distinct traditions present in eastern Cuba. The chapter does not conflate these three religious-cultural systems, which is important: they are related but not identical, and the chapter treats them with appropriate specificity.

## Verdict

Factually sound. Historically competent. Culturally accurate where the religious traditions are concerned. The autonomist gap is an editorial choice rather than an error. Minor corrections: cite the 1998 investigation alongside the 1976 one; consider clarifying the western invasion force as a growing rather than fixed number.

**Recommended for FINAL with minor corrections.**
