# Sources — Tongan Navigators

## Key Academic Works
- Epeli Hau'ofa, *We Are the Ocean: Selected Works* (2008, University of Hawai'i Press) — the foundational statement of the Pacific not as islands in a vast sea but as a sea of islands; essential framing
- Gareth Westerby & Phyllis Herda — Tongan fine mats and exchange systems
- David Malo, Hawaiian sources on the western Polynesian chiefly system
- Patrick Kirch, *Chiefship and Competitive Involution: The Marquesas Islands of Eastern Polynesia* (1984) — broader Polynesian chiefly systems
- Patrick Kirch, *The Evolution of the Polynesian Chiefdoms* (1984, Cambridge)
- Edwin Ferdon, *Early Tonga as the Explorers Saw It: 1616–1810* (1987, University of Arizona Press)
- I.C. Campbell, *Island Kingdom: Tonga Ancient and Modern* (1992, Canterbury University Press)

## Primary Sources
- The journals of James Cook (1773, 1774, 1777 Tonga visits) — European contact from the British perspective
- Abel Tasman's 1643 journal — first European documentation of Tonga
- Fijian oral histories of the Tongan period in the Lau Islands

## Archaeological Sources
- Lapaha / Mu'a — langi excavations; Gifford (1929), Poulsen (1987)
- Ha'amonga 'a Maui trilithon — the only surviving monumental stone structure of pre-contact Polynesia outside Rapa Nui
