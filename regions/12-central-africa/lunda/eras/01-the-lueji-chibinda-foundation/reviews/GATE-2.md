## Assessment

**Passes GATE-2. Target: 48+/60. Score: 51/60. Combined: 78/90 (above the 72+ target and above the 75+ manuscript mean target).**

### Strengths

The Progression (9/10) and Voice (9/10) are the chapter's high points. The structural engine — the lukanu as constitutive object — is deployed correctly per v1.5 requirements: the bracelet's actual properties (portability, accumulatability, transferability, the requirement of legitimate holding) determine the narrative logic, not symbolic imposition. The six-section arc builds toward the landing with purpose.

The v1.5 source asymmetry technique is operating correctly: the oral tradition's multiple reasons for the brothers' departure is presented as cultural epistemological practice, not as the author's hedge. The composite figure disclosure in figures.md is correct handling of thin records.

The landing is the chapter's finest moment: the return to the opening's images, grounded in the same sensory language (smell of copper and skin, worn smooth, not large) but now carrying the full accumulated weight of what the bracelet has constituted.

### Flags

**Immersion (8/10) — two brief lapses:**
- Section III, when explaining the franchise logic, moves slightly outside the world into analytical summary: "These departures were not, from the Lunda perspective, a loss. They were the first evidence that the political culture was transmissible." This is correct analysis but the register shifts briefly toward the author speaking to the reader rather than the tradition speaking about itself. Could be embedded more fully in the world's own logic — perhaps in Lueji's or Chibinda Ilunga's understanding rather than as the author's observation.
- Section V, the handa passage, is excellent (the sensory rendering of the copper ingot is chapter-quality work) but the transition into "The copper underwrote the network" is slightly sudden.

**Evidence (8/10):** Katema as composite is correctly handled. If a future revision can identify a named historical figure from the tradition for one of the departing headmen (Jan Vansina's Kingdoms of the Savanna lists some named figures from the founding generation), the Evidence score could push to 9.

## Innovation Flag

**Innovation candidate:** The lukanu is given its own entry in figures.md — an object, not a person, with its own historical narrative, political theology, and continuing survival into the modern era. This is not a standard figures.md technique. It treats the material object as an agent in the story equal to the human figures, which is consistent with the v1.5 Material Object amendment but extends it into the figures section format. This may be worth logging in INNOVATIONS.md after FINAL is reached.
