# Sources — British Empire

## Key Academic Works
- C.A. Bayly, *Imperial Meridian: The British Empire and the World 1780–1830* (1989, Longman)
- Ronald Hyam, *Britain's Imperial Century, 1815–1914* (3rd ed., 2002, Palgrave)
- Niall Ferguson, *Empire: The Rise and Demise of the British World Order* (2003, Basic Books) — the favorable account; read alongside its critics
- Mike Davis, *Late Victorian Holocausts: El Niño Famines and the Making of the Third World* (2001, Verso) — the famines; essential counterweight to Ferguson
- Caroline Elkins, *Imperial Reckoning: The Untold Story of Britain's Gulag in Kenya* (2005, Henry Holt) — the Mau Mau suppression; won Pulitzer Prize
- John Darwin, *The Empire Project: The Rise and Fall of the British World-System* (2009, Cambridge)
- Pankaj Mishra, *From the Ruins of Empire* (2012) — the non-Western intellectual response to British imperialism

## Primary Sources
- Rudyard Kipling, "The White Man's Burden" (1899) — the imperial ideology's most honest expression
- Lord Lugard, *The Dual Mandate in British Tropical Africa* (1922) — the indirect rule theory
- The Balfour Declaration (1917)
- The Montagu–Chelmsford Reforms (1919)
- Winston Churchill's correspondence on Bengal famine (1943) — available in Public Record Office
