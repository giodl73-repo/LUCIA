# Panel Review R1 — P-5 Ryszard Kapuściński
## The High Wagadu — Opening

**Voice:** Ryszard Kapuściński — Sensory immersion, heat, smell, sound, texture

---

The cold. This is what the opening gives me that most chapters do not: it is cold. The harmattan air carrying the Sahara's chill, the cold of the sand through the leather soles, the grey light separating sky from plain before the sun comes — I can feel where I am. The Sahel in the dry season before dawn is a specific physical experience, and the chapter has found the right specific detail: cold, not heat. People think of Africa and think of heat. They are wrong about the Sahel before dawn in the dry season, and the chapter corrects them without saying they are wrong. It simply shows the cold.

The cloth tight across the girl's back — yes. This is how you begin a chapter about obligation. Not the abstract idea of sacrifice but the physical fact of the cotton wound tight. She is aware of it "the way you are aware of anything that is both familiar and final." That sentence carries more feeling than a paragraph of explanation.

The sounds build correctly through the opening. The drums not yet speaking — this is the important thing. The muffled drums. You hear their potential, their waiting. Then the call to prayer six miles across the flat country. Then, at the end: "The drum speaks." The whole opening is a crescendo held back until the last possible moment. This is the right use of sound in a chapter about a world organized around a drum and a well.

What I want more of in the chapter: the specific smell of the market. Salt blocks from the Saharan crossing have a smell — the mineral bitterness of the desert on them, the dust of weeks of travel in their pores. Gold dust in small brass scales has a smell. Kola nuts have a smell. The chapter must give me the market through every sense, not sight and sound alone. And the sweat of the caravan animals — that is a smell that announces the world is working, the crossing is open, the covenant is holding.

**The question I'd push on:** Where is Bida's presence in the physical world? Not as theological proposition but as sensation? The sound from the well, or its absence. The temperature at the stone lip. What does the girl's body know that her mind translates into covenant? The chapter must find Bida's physical correlate.
