## Dimension Notes

**Immersion (9/10):** The chapter sustains the inside perspective across all six sections. The Carrera Séptima → Líbano, Tolima → the mountains → the Plaza de Bolívar → Bogotá/Sitges → the colonel's courtyard. Each location is physically established before the analysis begins. The one dip: in "La Máquina," two sentences describe the pájaros system in summary before showing it operate — reversible in CLEAN.

**Evidence (9/10):** The v1.3 amendment standard — knowledge system at method level — is met through the Silent March: the chapter shows the methodology of gaitanismo (not just that it was a mass movement, but HOW the silent march worked as political argument, what its specific logic was). The pájaros machinery is shown operating in Líbano, not described in the abstract. Rosa's testimony demonstrates the displacement at the level of specific objects (the cast-iron pot, the saint). El Cóndor's codes are evidenced through Sánchez and Meertens' fieldwork.

**Progression (9/10):** The structural engine (partisan inheritance + state absence) is planted in Section 2, its mechanism explored in Sections 3 and 4, its exhaustion named in Section 6, and its incomplete resolution acknowledged in the landing. The terminal condition (legitimacy gap → National Front) follows from the engine's own logic. The chapter arrives where it must.

**Figures (9/10):** The composite figure disclosure in figures.md is handled correctly. Gaitán is present in his own words (the Oración para la Paz) rather than in paraphrase. El Cóndor is named and documented with appropriate acknowledgment of the historical record's limits. The colonel is not García Márquez's colonel (it is a fictional character being deployed as a cultural persistence mechanism, which the chapter handles without confusion about this). One potential flag: the chapter could develop Rosa's moment of departure with one additional sensory detail — the rooster, perhaps — before she looks back.

**Voice (9/10):** The discover register is sustained. The García Márquez close achieves full register — "patiently, without hope, with dignity intact, for a letter that was never going to come" — the discovers-as-realism voice at its finest. Zero em-dashes. The pause mechanism (period) is consistent throughout. No academic register slippage.

**Landing (10/10):** The colonel's Friday walk, the gold fish, the wars finishing with the men rather than the men finishing the wars — this is the culture's own persistence mechanism as final note. The chapter does not state that the FARC was the consequence of the National Front's failure; it names the founding, the location (Marquetalia, Tolima — same Tolima, same mountains), and the founding figures (men who "had never come down"), and leaves the reader to complete the connection. Name the future, do not tell it.

## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation candidate — Testimonial Composite as Named Technique:** The Rosa figure — explicitly disclosed as composite in figures.md, assembled from the CNMH archive, with the archival basis named — achieves something the rubric has not yet fully codified. It is distinct from the "anonymous builder" technique (Swahili) and from the "composite figure" mentioned in the Figures guidance as valid for oral/archaeological cultures. Rosa is not anonymous — she is named and given an arc — but her composite nature is disclosed explicitly and her archival basis is named. This may be a new technique for cultures where the testimonial record is extensive but the individual record is partial. Log for review after second instance.
