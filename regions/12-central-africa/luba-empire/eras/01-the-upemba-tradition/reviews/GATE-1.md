## Notes

The opening passes GATE-1 comfortably. The lacustrine voice is distinctive and earned — it belongs to a lakeshore culture, not to any general "African" register. The v1.5 material-object principle is present in embryonic form: the net is both literal and structural. The "discover" engine is correctly positioned — this is not a chapter about the Luba kingdom but about the accumulation of preconditions for it, and the chapter holds that frame from beginning to end.

The Pull score of 4 rather than 5 reflects the absence of a specific dramatic tension for the unnamed fisher-woman. She is vivid and embodied, but the story's question ("what will accumulate?") is more intellectual than dramatic at the individual level. This is appropriate to Era 1 (there is no kingdom yet, no named figure in the record), but it slightly softens the pull. The chapter earns its momentum through accumulation rather than through a burning individual question — which is correct for the era, but worth noting as a potential target for Panel-1 to probe.

**Gate-1 result: PASS (29/30). Proceed to PANEL-1.**
