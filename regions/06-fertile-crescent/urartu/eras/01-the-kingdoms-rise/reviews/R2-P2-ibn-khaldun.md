# R2 — P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (The Kingdom's Rise)

**Focus:** Structural engine and causal logic.

The engine is now named: inscription-as-state-making. The mechanism is generational accumulation: each king inscribes more, in a more Urartian voice, claiming more territory. The terminal condition is the completion of the founding project: language, god, canal, fortress network.

This is adequate but must be tested in the writing. The risk is that "inscription-as-state-making" becomes a framing device rather than a true engine. A true engine DRIVES the narrative. The question for the chapter is whether the act of inscribing actually caused the kingdom to coalesce, or whether it merely recorded a coalescence that had other causes.

The notes gesture at the ecological-military explanation (Assyrian raids forced cooperation; Tushpa provided the node). This is the deeper engine. The inscription is the visible expression, but the fortress-and-canal system is the mechanism. I would frame the engine as: **the fortress-canal cycle**. Build fortresses to defend the highland; build canals to feed the fortress garrisons; the agricultural surplus from the canals enables more fortresses; the fortress network enables more canals. Each generation extends both systems. The inscription records and claims what the fortress and canal have made real.

The terminal condition then becomes: the cycle sustains as long as the highland has space to fill. When the fortresses reach the edge of the defensible plateau and the canals reach the limit of the available water, the founding phase is over and the expansion phase begins.

**Verdict:** The engine should be refined to fortress-canal cycle with inscription as its voice. The writing will test whether this holds.
