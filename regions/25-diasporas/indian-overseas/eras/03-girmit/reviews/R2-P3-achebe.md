## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Focus:** Voice ownership in the system-description sections.

**On Section 3 (The Plantation):** The plan names the risk correctly: plantation conditions and women's experience could slip into historian's voice. The solution is anchoring. Every systemic observation must be attached to a person's experience. Do not write "the overseer system imposed penalties for absence." Write it through Ramnaresh or Kunti experiencing the penalty. The system is revealed through what it does to specific people, not through what a historian knows about its design.

**On the "wife-murder" crisis:** This is the most dangerous moment in the chapter. The colonial records documented a high rate of violence against women on the plantations, attributed by colonial observers to jealousy caused by the gender imbalance (roughly 40 women per 100 men). The colonial framing blamed Indian men. The girmitiya's reality was that women were subject to coercion from overseers and other men with power, and the violence was a symptom of a system that had deliberately disrupted kinship structures. The chapter must refuse the colonial framing while not erasing the violence. Show the conditions. Show the specific women who navigated them. Let the reader understand the structural cause without the text pronouncing a verdict.

**On Totaram Sanadhya's voice:** When his testimony is quoted or paraphrased, it must remain his testimony. Do not reprocess his words into analytical language. He spoke in Hindi. He described what happened to him. Let his voice carry his evidence.

**Recommendation:** For each section, ask: whose eyes? If the answer is "the historian's," revise until the answer is a named person's.
