# Sources — Portugal

## Primary Sources

- Fernão Lopes, *Crónica de D. João I* — the great chronicle; literary masterpiece; primary source for Aljubarrota and the Aviz dynasty
- Gomes Eanes de Zurara, *Crónica de Guiné* — chronicle of Henry the Navigator's African explorations; the first account of the Atlantic slave trade
- Duarte Pacheco Pereira, *Esmeraldo de Situ Orbis* — c. 1505–1508; practical navigation manual; the accumulated knowledge of Portuguese exploration
- João de Barros, *Décadas da Ásia* — the major chronicle of Portuguese Asian expansion
- Luís de Camões, *Os Lusíadas* — 1572; the national epic; retrospective but the definitive statement of Portuguese providential nationalism
- *Ordenaçoens Afonsinas* (1446) — the first Portuguese law code; Afonso V's legal compilation
- Maps of the Cantino planisphere (1502) — earliest surviving map showing Portuguese discoveries

## Secondary Sources

- Russell, Peter. *Prince Henry 'the Navigator': A Life*. Yale University Press, 2000.
- Diffie, Bailey W. and George D. Winius. *Foundations of the Portuguese Empire, 1415–1580*. University of Minnesota Press, 1977.
- Newitt, Malyn. *A History of Portuguese Overseas Expansion, 1400–1668*. Routledge, 2005.
- Mattoso, José, ed. *História de Portugal*. 8 vols. Círculo de Leitores, 1992–1994.
- Blackmore, Josiah. *Moorings: Portuguese Expansion and the Writing of Africa*. University of Minnesota Press, 2009.
- Saunders, A.C. de C.M. *A Social History of Black Slaves and Freedmen in Portugal 1441–1555*. Cambridge University Press, 1982.
