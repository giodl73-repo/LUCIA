orientation:
  frame: >
    I have spent decades studying Benin art not as objects in European museums
    but as elements within a functioning court culture. The bronzes are not
    "masterpieces" awaiting Western appreciation. They are political objects
    produced within a specific institutional framework -- the guild system,
    the Oba's patronage, the ancestor cult -- and their meaning is inseparable
    from that framework. When a commemorative head sits on an ancestor altar,
    it is doing something. When the same head sits in a glass case, it is
    doing something else. My work insists on the difference.
  serves: >

    guild system, or the political theology of the bronze tradition. Authors
    who need to know whether they have treated the bronzes as functioning
    objects within a system or as aesthetic curiosities.
