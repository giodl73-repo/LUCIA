# BOARD-B2 — Anna Roosevelt (model) — Review of The Teso Florescence

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Marajoara archaeology; Teso dos Bichos excavations; Amazonian paleodemography and environmental reconstruction; post-Meggers revisionism.

---

## Historical Accuracy

- **Population claims:** The chapter's phrasing — "the population of the island is at its greatest density" with estimates ranging "from a few thousand to tens of thousands" — correctly represents the uncertainty in the paleodemographic literature. My own Teso dos Bichos work supports a substantial population; the range is accurate. The chapter does not understate complexity.

- **Environmental description:** The várzea ecology section is accurate. The flood timing described (December through June, waters to depths of 1–2 meters in the flat interior) is consistent with the paleohydrological record for Marajó. The account of the seasonal resource pattern (catfish following grass edges, turtles nesting on mound tops) is accurate to the ethnobiological record for seasonally flooded Amazonian environments.

- **Teso construction:** The claim that the mounds were not built in single construction episodes but accumulated over generations is foundational to my own analysis and is fully supported by the stratigraphic evidence. The characterization of the teso as "a library of the community's physical past" is a narrative interpretation consistent with the stratigraphic record.

- **Amazon mouth geography:** "Three hundred and twenty kilometers wide" for the Amazon mouth is accurate.

- **Florescence-period ceramic output:** The claim that "more urns are being made at Camutins during the Florescence than at any other time in the Marajoara sequence" is consistent with the density of ceramics in Florescence-period strata vs. earlier and later periods.

## Cultural Authenticity

The chapter does not reproduce the Meggers-Evans environmental determinism that dominated earlier Marajoara scholarship. It treats the várzea as an environment the Marajoara read and worked with, not as a constraint that limited them. This is correct. The chapter's implicit argument — that the Marajoara built a complex society adapted to, not despite, the seasonal flood — is the post-1991 consensus.

## What the Chapter Gets Right

- The flood-as-calendar framing is the single most important ecological insight in recent Amazonian scholarship, and the chapter applies it at the right depth: not as background but as organizing principle.
- The absence of the Meggers-Evans population minimalism. The chapter treats the Florescence as a genuine florescence, consistent with the excavation record.
- The seasonal labor pattern: the chapter correctly identifies the dry season as the period of construction and ceramic production, the flood season as the period of concentrated residence and fishing.

## What Needs Correction

1. **Island size:** The chapter describes Marajó Island as "forty-eight thousand square kilometers — the size of a small country." The commonly cited figure is approximately 48,000 km², which is accurate. However, the comparison "the size of a small country" could mislead — it is closer to the size of Denmark or Switzerland. The overview already uses "the size of Switzerland," which is more precise. The chapter should align with the overview.

2. **Flood duration:** "Between December and June" describes the core flood season accurately, but the onset and recession vary considerably across years. The chapter's own acknowledgment — "The flood's timing varied across years and across decades" — mitigates this. No correction required, but the chapter's own note about variability should be read as primary.

3. **Catfish and turtle behavioral ecology:** The specific claims about catfish following grass edges and turtles nesting on mound tops are accurate to the general Amazonian seasonal pattern but are not specifically documented for Marajó Island in the Florescence period. These are inferences from the general ecology. The chapter should acknowledge this in notes or figures.

## Verdict

**APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS**

Conditions: (1) align island-size comparison with overview (Switzerland); (2) acknowledge the catfish/turtle behavioral claims as ecological inference. Minor adjustments. Chapter is accurate to the post-1991 scholarly consensus.
