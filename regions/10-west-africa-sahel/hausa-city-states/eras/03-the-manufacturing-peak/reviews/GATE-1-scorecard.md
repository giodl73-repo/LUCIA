## Notes

The new opening solves the original's structural problem. The original opened in the dye pit — immersive but static, showing the system from inside production. The new opening shows the system from the point of failure: a merchant who knows the difference between good and second-quality cloth, and whose knowledge makes the competition's stakes immediately visible. When Ibrahim refuses full price, the reader understands why Kano guilds guard their recipes. The competition is dramatized, not described.

The Bori drums are in the opening now — present but not entered, heard from a distance, Ibrahim noting them and filing them under "not his business." This plants the structural tension (the two systems operating in the same city) from line one.

The brass weight image ("the weight of things was the beginning of trust") is a precise pre-echo of the landing this chapter needs to earn.
