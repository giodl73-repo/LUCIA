# Sources — Muscovy / Russia

## Primary Sources

- *Moskovskiy letopis* (Moscow Chronicle) and other Russian chronicles — the primary narrative record
- *Epistles of Philotheus of Pskov* — c. 1510–1524; the Third Rome doctrine articulated
- Ivan III's correspondence and decrees
- *Domostroi* — c. 1550s; household management manual; the most detailed picture of Muscovite domestic life
- Ivan IV's correspondence with Prince Kurbsky — an extraordinary primary source for Ivan's self-justification
- *Kazan Chronicle* — the account of the conquest of Kazan 1552
- Sigismund von Herberstein, *Rerum Moscoviticarum Commentarii* — 1549; the most important outside observer of Muscovy

## Secondary Sources

- Fennell, J.L.I. *Ivan the Great of Moscow*. Macmillan, 1961.
- Halperin, Charles J. *Russia and the Golden Horde: The Mongol Impact on Medieval Russian History*. Indiana University Press, 1985.
- Ostrowski, Donald. *Muscovy and the Mongols: Cross-Cultural Influences on the Steppe Frontier*. Cambridge University Press, 1998.
- Crummey, Robert O. *The Formation of Muscovy 1304–1613*. Longman, 1987.
- Kollmann, Nancy Shields. *By Honor Bound: State and Society in Early Modern Russia*. Cornell University Press, 1999.
- Martin, Janet. *Medieval Russia 980–1584*. Cambridge University Press, 1995.
- Platonov, S.F. *Ivan the Terrible*. Academic International Press, 1974.
