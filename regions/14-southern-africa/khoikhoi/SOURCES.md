# Sources — Khoikhoi

## Primary

- VOC records — Dutch East India Company archives; van Riebeeck's journal; the documentary record of early contact
- Shell, Robert C-H. — colonial Cape sources on Khoikhoi and enslaved peoples
- Schoeman, Karel — scholarly work on the Cape colonial period

## Academic

- Elphick, Richard. *Khoikhoi and the Founding of White South Africa*. 1985. Ravan Press. — the foundational study
- Penn, Nigel. *The Forgotten Frontier: Colonist and Khoisan on the Cape's Northern Frontier in the 18th and 19th Centuries*. 2005. Ohio University Press.
- Worden, Nigel. *The Making of Modern South Africa: Conquest, Apartheid, Democracy*. 5th ed. 2012. Wiley-Blackwell.
- Ross, Robert. *Cape of Torments: Slavery and Resistance in South Africa*. 1983. Routledge.
- Malherbe, V.C. "Krotoa, Called 'Eva': A Woman Between." *Communications* No. 19, University of Cape Town. 1990.
- Schrire, Carmel & Deacon, Janette. "The Mills of Memory: The Making of Khoekhoe History." *South African Archaeological Bulletin* 44 (1989).

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on the Griqua states and their political history
- Studies on the 1713 smallpox epidemic and its demographic impact
- Analysis of Khoikhoi-San interactions and the boundary between the two communities
- Scholarship on Eva / Krotoa — her life and its symbolic weight in Cape colonial history
