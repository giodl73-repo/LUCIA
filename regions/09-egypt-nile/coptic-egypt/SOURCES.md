# Sources — Coptic Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Nag Hammadi Library — fourth century CE Coptic texts; Gnostic and early Christian documents
- Pachomian Rules — the founding documents of cenobitic monasticism
- Letters of Athanasius — the theological position of the Alexandrian church
- Shenouda of Atripe's homilies — the voice of the great Coptic monastic reformer
- Coptic liturgical texts — ongoing critical editions

## Academic

- Meinardus, Otto. *Two Thousand Years of Coptic Christianity*. 1999. American University in Cairo Press.
- Pearson, Birger A. *Gnosticism and Christianity in Roman and Coptic Egypt*. 2004. Continuum.
- Gabra, Gawdat (ed.). *Be Thou There: The Holy Family's Journey in Egypt*. 2001. American University in Cairo Press.
- Wipszycka, Ewa. *The Second Gift of the Nile: Monks and Monasteries in Late Antique Egypt*. 2018. Journal of Juristic Papyrology Supplements.
- Lyster, William. *The Cave Church of Paul the Hermit at the Monastery of St. Paul, Egypt*. 2008. Yale University Press / AUC Press.
- Atiya, Aziz S. (ed.). *The Coptic Encyclopedia*. 8 vols. 1991. Macmillan. — comprehensive reference
