## B-portugal-3 — Bailey Diffie (with George Winius) — Board Review of O Infante e o Atlântico

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Co-author of *Foundations of the Portuguese Empire, 1415–1580* (University of Minnesota Press, 1977), the standard structural history of Portuguese overseas expansion. Our work provided the institutional, economic, and political analysis of how Portugal organized and sustained the exploration program across the fifteenth century.

**Historical accuracy:**

- **CORRECT:** The structural analysis in Section II is accurate and consistent with the framework we developed. The Reconquista's completion, the Aviz dynasty's legitimacy needs, the English alliance, and the Order of Christ as revenue source — these are the correct structural conditions for Portugal's overseas program.
- **CORRECT:** The Treaty of Windsor in 1386 between João I and John of Gaunt. Correct parties, correct date.
- **CORRECT:** The Order of Christ: Portuguese successors to the Templars, suppressed elsewhere in 1312. Henry became Grand Master in 1420. All correct.
- **CORRECT:** The Fernão Gomes contract: 1469, subcontracted from Afonso V, 100 leagues per year requirement. Our work documented this contract; the chapter's account is accurate.
- **CORRECT:** São Jorge da Mina begun 1482. Correct.
- **CORRECT:** Bartolomeu Dias 1488 at the Cape. Correct.
- **CORRECT:** The volta do mar is correctly described. This is the key technical innovation of the period.
- **CORRECT:** The chapter correctly identifies the padrão as legal possession under canon law, not merely a navigation marker. This is the correct Portuguese legal theory.

**On the Cadamosto claim:**

The chapter notes that Cadamosto "reached the Gambia River" and "probably the Cape Verde Islands" during his two voyages c. 1455–1456. The attribution of the Cape Verde Islands discovery to Cadamosto is contested. Diogo Gomes also claims discovery of the islands, and the two accounts are contradictory. The chapter's use of "probably" is appropriately cautious. No correction required, but worth noting that this is contested in the sources.

**On the structural engine:**

The chapter's identification of the caravel as the material form of the structural engine is analytically correct and represents good historical thinking. The lateen rig's ability to beat into the wind was genuinely the enabling technology for the return route. The chapter does not overstate Henry's personal genius at the expense of the structural conditions, which is the more sophisticated analysis.

**On the post-1460 transition:**

The chapter correctly characterizes the Fernão Gomes contract as "an inversion of the original program" (exploration as franchise condition rather than the purpose). This is accurate. The program did transform from a patronage-organized research enterprise into a commercially licensed extraction operation after Henry's death. The chapter handles this transition correctly.

**Cultural authenticity:**

The chapter correctly presents the Portuguese providential nationalism (pequena nação, grande destino) as a real cultural formation, not an anachronistic imposition. The chapter notes correctly that Fernão Lopes's chronicles developed this ideology — though the specific note about Lopes in the SOURCES.md is more accurately about the Aviz dynasty and Aljubarrota than the overseas program, which is primarily Zurara's domain.

**What the chapter gets right:**

- The institutional analysis of how the program sustained itself financially
- The transition from Henry's household operation to the licensed-trade franchise
- The padrão as legal act, not merely navigational marker
- The book-level arc: the program outlives its founder because it achieves institutional independence before it achieves its navigational goal

**What needs correction:**

No factual errors. One note: the chapter refers to "Henry's Guinea trade license and operations passed to his nephew Afonso V." For precision: Henry did not hold a personal "license" in the modern sense; he held the contractual right as Grand Master and governor to organize and license the trade. The transition was the passing of this organizational authority, not a formal license transfer. This is a minor precision issue, not an error.

**Verdict:** APPROVED
