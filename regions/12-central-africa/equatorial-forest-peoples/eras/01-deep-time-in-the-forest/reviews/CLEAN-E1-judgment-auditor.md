## Anachronistic Morality Check

Any outside moral verdict imposed on the culture or its practices?

**Result: CLEAR.** The chapter presents the ekila restrictions as the culture's own relational ethics, without authorial endorsement or critique. The farming exchange asymmetry is described structurally — "the structural advantages that were hidden in the beginning would become visible when the forest was smaller" — without calling it exploitation, and without attributing moral weight to either party's position. The chapter shows what happened and why, without verdicting it.

---

## Romanticization Check

Any tendency to present the forest peoples as noble, pristine, or idealized?

**Two items flagged:**

1. "What the Ituri contains, and the forests of the Central African Republic, and the Baka territories of Cameroon, is not primitive survival. It is something harder to describe and rarer than anything a state produces." — The phrase "rarer than anything a state produces" comes close to a comparative judgment that elevates the forest peoples above state-organized societies. The intent is to assert the knowledge system's sophistication without the usual condescension, but the comparison to states could read as inverse condescension: romanticizing by contrast. **Recommend revising** to remove the comparative. Something like: "It is something harder to describe and not reducible to the categories states use to organize their own knowledge."

2. "The forest provides because the relationship is maintained." — This is presented as the forest peoples' own understanding, which is correct. It is not authorial endorsement. CLEAR.

---

## Inside-Voice Honesty Check (v1.2 amendment)

Does the honesty feel like it belongs to the culture, not to the project's rules?

**Result: PASSES.** The chapter's refusal to judge comes from staying inside the relational worldview, not from editorial restraint. When the farming exchange asymmetry is described without verdict, this is because the Baka at that moment do not see it as exploitation — and the chapter honors that perspective without correcting it toward the reader's expected modern framework. The future tense at the end of Section 5 ("What would change, in ways invisible to any person living at any single moment, was the margin itself") names the future honestly without verdicting it.

---

## Required Revision

Line in final "What the Ituri contains" paragraph: revise to remove "rarer than anything a state produces." Replace with neutral descriptor of uniqueness that does not rank the knowledge system against state-produced knowledge by making states the reference class.

---

## Judgment: PASSES WITH ONE REVISION

Apply the revision above, then the chapter clears E-1.
