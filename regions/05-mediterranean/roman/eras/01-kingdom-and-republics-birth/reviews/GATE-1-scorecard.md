**Scorer:** Chronicle Pipeline

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with Brutus's funeral procession through the Forum. Specific moment, specific person, specific action. The body being carried, the women mourning, the senators walking. The scene is immediate and anchored. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Roman world emerges through its own categories: mos maiorum, res publica, dignitas, pietas. The Forum is described not as a monument but as a drained marsh becoming something. The relationship between the origin stories (Romulus, Numa, Servius) and the constitutional order is shown from inside. The toga as civic identity marker emerges naturally. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | Livy is handled critically from the start: "whether Brutus himself existed as Livy drew him, these are questions the sources cannot answer and the tradition does not ask." The Republic is not presented as superior to what came before. |
| Texture   | 4/5   | Strong sensory grounding: river mud and woodsmoke, hides curing near the Tiber, terracotta roof decorations, the soft ground of the drained marsh. The toga is physically described (heavy undyed wool, folds that require practice). Slightly less sensory in the final paragraphs where the constitutional machinery is described. |
| Voice     | 5/5   | Legal, precise, public. The prose has the Forum's acoustics: declarative, weighted, built for persuasion. Short sentences carry verdicts. The rhythm belongs to this culture. "The argument was circular, and the circularity was the point." The voice is Roman. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | "The space would hold until the machinery itself became the weapon that tore it apart." The closing creates specific tension: the reader understands the Republic's machinery and knows it will eventually fail. The pull is not generic curiosity but specific structural tension. |

**Notes:** Passes Gate-1 (24+ required). The opening establishes the Republic's constitutional logic as the structural engine: the oath (never again a king) and the machinery (how to keep the oath). The voice is distinctly Roman: legal, public, weighted with the Forum's gravity. The handling of Livy as both source and tradition-bearer sets up the epistemological approach for the entire book. The toga passage echoes the Inca cumbi cloth connection without forcing the parallel. Strong pull toward the Republic's internal contradictions.
