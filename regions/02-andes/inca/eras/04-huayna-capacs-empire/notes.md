# Production Notes — Huayna Cápac's Empire (Era 4)

## Structural Engine

**The quipu's blind spot.** The structural engine is the gap between what the system can record and what is actually happening. The quipucamayoc manage the empire at its peak, but the epidemic arrives from outside the system's categories. The quipu recorded labor obligations, tribute, census — it could not record an epidemiological catastrophe that moved faster than the chasqui. The chapter's tension: the most information-rich state in the Americas encounters an event its information system cannot process.

**Book-level arc:** Era 1 planted the seeds. Era 2 scaled them. Era 3 tested the limits. Era 4 is the empire at its peak AND the moment the first crack appears — not from external invasion but from internal vulnerability (the succession crisis triggered by epidemic death). The three threads:
1. **Mit'a** — strained by the prolonged northern campaigns; the labor tax system at maximum demand
2. **Vertical archipelago** — at its greatest extent but also at its most fragile; the epidemic disrupts the labor that maintains the system
3. **Quipu** — at its most sophisticated but encountering its categorical limit; the system records what the system was designed to record, and the epidemic is not one of those things

## Chapter Arc

1. **The Counting** (opening material) — The quipucamayoc and the peak; the numbers have never been larger
2. **Huayna Capac in the North** — The campaigns; Tomebamba; the court's shift northward; the tension between two capitals
3. **The Empire at Its Height** — The daily functioning of the system; a provincial center in operation; the mit'a at work; the feasts; the qollqa full
4. **The Epidemic** — Smallpox (probably) moves south ahead of any European the Inca have seen; Huayna Capac dies; the succession crisis
5. **Two Sons** — Huascar in Qusqu, Atahualpa in the north; the split that will become the civil war
6. **The Knots Go Silent** — The quipucamayoc record the death of the Sapa Inca but cannot record the full scope of the loss; the closing: the system at its most perfect is the system at its most blind

## Key Figures

- **Huayna Capac** — Eleventh Sapa Inca; the last ruler of a unified Tawantinsuyu; dies of epidemic disease in Tomebamba
- **Huascar** — Son designated to rule from Qusqu; the legitimate heir in the southern faction's view
- **Atahualpa** — Son born in the north; Huayna Capac's preferred successor according to some accounts; will fight Huascar for the throne
- **A provincial quipucamayoc** — Composite; recording the empire's daily operations at the moment the system begins to fail
