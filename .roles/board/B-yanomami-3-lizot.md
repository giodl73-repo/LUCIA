# B-Yanomami-3 — Jacques Lizot

## Authority

Jacques Lizot (b. 1938) is a French anthropologist who studied under Claude Lévi-Strauss and spent approximately 1968–1990 living in Venezuelan Yanomami communities. His *Tales of the Yanomami* (1985) is the most sustained daily-life account of Yanomami life before intensive disruption. His work provides the granular ethnographic detail most directly relevant to the Parima World era chapter.

**Note:** Lizot's work is not without controversy (accusations of misconduct led to his expulsion from Venezuela). His ethnographic observations are generally accepted as accurate; his personal conduct is not the chapter's business.
