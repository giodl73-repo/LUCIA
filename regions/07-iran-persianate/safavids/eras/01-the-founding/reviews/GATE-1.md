## Notes

GATE-1 passed at full score. The opening achieves several things simultaneously:

1. **The structural engine is seeded:** The tension between Qizilbash charismatic loyalty (divine kingship) and the institutional requirements of a Shia state (clerics from Bahrain, legal frameworks, endowments) is planted in the opening without being named. Ibrahim sailing to fill an institutional gap that doesn't yet exist IS the engine.

2. **The terminal condition is implied:** Chaldiran is named and withheld — a deliberate structural signal that the Qizilbash cavalry enthusiasm has a reckoning coming.

3. **Voice register identified:** Ceremonial-proclamatory. Neither steppe nor court in the classic sense — it carries the weight of a Sufi order declaring its moment while knowing the Ottoman response is being prepared. The voice will sustain through the full chapter.
