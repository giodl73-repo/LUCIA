## B-roman-1 — Mary Beard — Board Review of Roman Era 1

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Roman Republican political culture, the construction of Roman identity, the relationship between Roman self-narrative and Roman practice.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The dating of the Republic's founding to the late 6th century BCE aligns with current consensus, though the traditional date of 509 BCE should be treated with caution.
- The Servian Wall dating problem is correctly identified: the surviving wall dates from the early 4th century, not from Servius's time. Good.
- The sacred chickens episode (Claudius Pulcher, 249 BCE) is accurately placed and correctly used as evidence of Roman attitudes toward divination.
- The Twelve Tables provisions cited (bone-breaking fine, tree-branch rule, debt-chain weight) are verifiable from the surviving fragments. Accurate.
- The Gallic sack is correctly placed at 390 BCE (Varronian dating).

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The treatment of the mos maiorum as a constitutional principle rather than mere conservatism is correct and important. This is how the Romani understood it.
- The integration of religion into the political system is handled well. The augural system is presented as political technology, which is accurate.
- The treatment of the toga as social grammar is effective and historically grounded.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The Lucretia section is the best I've seen outside scholarship. It gives her the space the tradition demands without treating the story as simple history.
- The patrician-plebeian struggle is presented as structural, not as a morality play. Correct.
- The acknowledgment that Roma assembled itself from multiple peoples (Latini, Sabini, Etrusci) is essential and well handled.

**What needs correction:**
- The chapter says the comitia centuriata elected "senior magistrates." More precisely, it elected consuls and praetors. The censors were also elected by this assembly. Specify.
- The claim that plebeian tribunes could "stop any magistrate's act" needs qualification: the tribune's veto operated within the pomerium. Outside the city boundary, the consul's imperium was unchecked. This distinction matters for the book's arc.
- The Lex Hortensia of 287 BCE is mentioned but its significance could be sharpened: it effectively ended the formal struggle of the orders. After 287, the distinction between patrician and plebeian was social, not legal. This should be stated.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (minor corrections above)
