# Sources — Italian City-States (Venice, Florence, Genoa)

## Primary Sources

- *Villani Chronicles* (Giovanni, Matteo, Filippo Villani) — 14th-century Florentine chronicles; detailed economic and political records
- *Libro di mercatanzie et usanze de' paesi* (Francesco Pegolotti) — c. 1340; merchant handbook; trade routes and commercial practice
- Doge Andrea Dandolo, *Chronica per extensum descripta* — Venetian chronicle; constitutional history
- *Statuta communis Bononie* and similar commune statutes — the legal texts of Italian self-governance
- Giovanni Boccaccio, *Decameron* — 1353; the cultural response to the Black Death; the bourgeois world on display
- Leonardo Bruni, *Laudatio Florentinae Urbis* — 1403; the humanist celebration of republican Florence
- Venetian *Senate Deliberations* — the most complete medieval administrative record in Europe
