**Innovation candidate:** The chapter develops a new technique for handling historical figures with multiple ancient accounts that systematically contradict each other (Cambyses): rather than choosing one account or averaging them, the chapter shows what each tradition's institutional interest was in the portrait it gave. "Which account is true? The scholars disagree." Then: here is what the Saqqara stelae actually show; here is what Herodotus says; here is why both accounts exist. This is an extension of the v1.3 "Source Asymmetry as Narrative Technique" into character rendering — the source asymmetry becomes the figure's characterization.
