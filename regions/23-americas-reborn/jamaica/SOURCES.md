# Sources — Jamaica

## Key Academic Works
- Orlando Patterson, *The Sociology of Slavery* (1967, MacGibbon) — the Jamaican plantation system
- Carolyn Cooper, *Noises in the Blood: Orality, Gender and the 'Vulgar' Body of Jamaican Popular Culture* (1993) — reggae and popular culture
- Horace Campbell, *Rasta and Resistance: From Marcus Garvey to Walter Rodney* (1987)
- Colin Palmer — Marcus Garvey and the UNIA; multiple works
- Gareth Higgins — Jamaican political violence and garrison communities
- Kari Levitt & Lloyd Best — Caribbean political economy
- Amy Jacques Garvey, *Garvey and Garveyism* (1963)

## Primary Sources
- Marcus Garvey's speeches and writings (collected in *Philosophy and Opinions of Marcus Garvey*, ed. Amy Jacques Garvey, 1923–1925)
- Bob Marley's lyrics and interviews (comprehensive collection)
- Samuel Sharpe's testimony before execution (1832)
- The Maroon Treaties (1739, 1740) — Public Record Office, London
