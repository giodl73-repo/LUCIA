# R1-P1 — Barbara Tuchman — Panel Review of Opening

**Chapter:** Marajoara — Era 2: The Teso Florescence
**Round:** Panel-1
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The opening earns attention. I say this without qualification: the paragraph that stands alone — "The person who will be buried in this urn is still alive." — is the kind of sentence that a reader underlines and cannot explain why. It is the right length. It arrives at the right moment. It does what scene-construction must do: it shifts the ground.

The scene itself works because it respects a principle I spent a career arguing for: begin with an action, not a description. The woman is not "a Marajoara ceramic artist." She is applying a slip layer with a fish-rib tool, and the specificity of that tool makes her real in a way that cultural description never can. I notice the fish-rib: it is not accidental. It tells us that the ocean and the ceramics are part of the same material world, that the instrument for marking the living comes from the dead.

What I would push on: the fifth paragraph — the one describing the Camutins complex at its peak, with the statistics on mounds and the visual language — reads like a survey rather than a scene. It is the one place where the author loses confidence in the opening strategy and falls back on telling us what we should be seeing rather than showing it through the woman's hands. The information is correct and important. The placement is wrong. Save it for the chapter. The opening should hold the woman.

The ending is strong but could be stronger. "It will be ready before anything changes" is an excellent penultimate note. But the final line — "It will be ready before anything changes" — is followed by nothing, and I wonder if the silence after it is doing the work the prose cannot. In my experience, when the prose is relying on white space to land, the prose has not quite finished its job. The chapter should trust the line more: let it stand and then perhaps one sentence — not of explanation, but of material weight — to give the silence somewhere to land.

**The question I'd push on:** When the woman finishes the urn, what happens next? Where does it go? The production bible should answer this — where the urn lived between completion and use — because that spatial journey is the chapter's emotional throughline.
