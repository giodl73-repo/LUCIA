## Paragraph 1 Test: Part 3 (Soviet Installation)

> "By late 1945, the institutions were in place. Land reform was complete. The Party was organized. A provisional government had been established, with Soviet approval. The boundaries between what was happening and what the Soviets wanted were becoming increasingly blurred. The Soviets gave an order; a Korean communist would implement it, and afterward it would be presented as a Korean decision. The Soviets announced policy; Korean media would carry it, framed as Kim Il-sung's thought. The machinery was being built at speed, with a clarity of purpose, and with the consent of Koreans who understood that this was the price of remaining in the Soviet sphere and remaining protected from American dominance."

**Voice identification:** Yes. This is recognizable as the founding-machinery register. Short declarative sentences establishing facts. Longer accretions showing how power operates. The rhythm is the prose of a state understanding its own construction. Not textbook (which would be "The Soviet occupation resulted in the establishment of communist institutions"), not Western commentary ("The Soviets cynically manipulated Korean communists"), but the voice of the machinery itself, explaining how it works from inside.

**Register consistency:** Yes. This matches the opening and the landing. No break.

**Textbook markers:** None. No "was based on," no passive voice evasion, no "the economy was characterized by." Active voice throughout. Direct observation of mechanism.

**Verdict:** PASS

## Paragraph 2 Test: Part 5 (The Bargain)

> "By 1950, five years of repetition had shaped a population. The study groups met every week. The lessons were constant. The photographs, the statues, the stories, the songs — they formed a dense mythology that was woven into every institution. To learn to read was to learn to read about Kim Il-sung. To join the Communist Party was to study his thought. To be a good Korean was to serve the revolution that he led."

**Voice identification:** Yes. Recognizable immediately. The voice that has been sustained: the machinery register carried forward. Short sentences and longer accretions. The repetition of structure ("To learn... To join... To be...") is not textbook enumeration; it is the rhythmic repetition of a state that functions through repetition.

**Register consistency:** Yes. Matches opening, middle, and elsewhere. No register break.

**Textbook markers:** None. No "was characterized by," no "served to," no passive evasion. The action of repetition is shown through rhythm, not described in analytical language.

**Verdict:** PASS

## Paragraph 3 Test: Figures.md (Kim Il-sung)

> "The fundamental mystery of North Korean history begins with this man. On September 19, 1945, he stepped off a Soviet ship and was presented to North Korean communists as Kim Il-sung — a name that carried the weight of resistance, of legend, of hope. Whether he was the actual Kim Il-sung who had fought the Japanese in Manchuria or a carefully selected Soviet proxy remains unknowable. Soviet records are inaccessible. Korean memory is filtered through mythology."

**Voice identification:** Yes, but in a different register. This is the figures.md register — more analytical, more explicit about historical uncertainty. But still consistent with the voice of the chapter. The rhythm is heavier, more philosophical ("The fundamental mystery"). But it is not a break into textbook. It is not Western commentary. It is the voice of a culture confronting the uncertainty of its own foundation.

**Register consistency:** Yes. Different from the narrative-machinery register of the chapter proper, but consistent with the figures section throughout. The voice announces itself as the space where historical uncertainty is acknowledged while the narrative sections maintain the mythology from inside.

**Textbook markers:** None. No "has been characterized," no "served as a mechanism." Direct historical observation.

**Verdict:** PASS
