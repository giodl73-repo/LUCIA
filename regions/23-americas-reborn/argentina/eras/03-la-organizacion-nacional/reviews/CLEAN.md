## E-1 — Judgment Auditor

**Purpose:** Hunt outside-lens violations. Forbidden words, anachronistic morality, romanticization, condescension.

**Forbidden word audit:** No instances of "primitive," "advanced," "tribe," "Dark Ages," "discovered by," "the New World," "remarkably sophisticated," "before contact with." Clean.

**Anachronistic morality audit:**

Flag 1: "He was not cruel in his intentions and not gentle in his methods." (Figures, Roca section.) This reads as editorial characterization. It is balanced, but "not gentle in his methods" is a judgment applied from outside. Suggest revision: render this through what the chapter has already shown — the fourteen thousand prisoners, the thousands who died — without the authorial summation of his character.

Flag 2: "He was not a hypocrite. He was a man who had completed the one possible journey and then defined its end-point as the beginning of the only legitimate society." (Section II, Pellegrini.) This is close to the No Verdicts line. It characterizes Pellegrini's psychology in terms of what he "could not see." Rendered sympathetically but reads as authorial interpretation of interior state. Suggest retaining — it is an inside-the-loop characterization, not an outside-the-loop judgment — but the CLEAN note is recorded.

Flag 3: "This distinction mattered enormously to the Generación del 80 and not at all to the people removed." (Section II, Roca/Campaña.) Flagged by GATE-2. This is not a forbidden framing but it is a No Verdict near-miss: the two-clause construction creates an implicit judgment (the distinction was self-serving). Suggest revision: separate the two observations. "The Generación del 80 understood this as civilization. For the Mapuche and Tehuelche, it was removal." Two perspectives, no verdict implied.

**Romanticization audit:** The milonga is rendered without romanticization. The tango is not romanticized — it is described as a "social technology" and a "grammar," which is analytical rather than sentimental. The Afro-Argentine erasure is handled as mechanism rather than tragedy (no "heartbreaking," no "devastating"). Clean.

**Required revision:**

In Section II, replace "This distinction mattered enormously to the Generación del 80 and not at all to the people removed" with:

"The Generación del 80 understood this as civilization. For the Mapuche and Tehuelche confederacies, it was their land and then it was not."

## E-2 — Voice Keeper

**Purpose:** Hunt register drift. Textbook voice, modern commentary, paragraphs written for a Western audience rather than from inside the culture.

**Voice-random-paragraph test:** Selecting paragraphs from three points in the chapter:

*Paragraph selected from Section I:* "The name 'tango' is already in use by the time these families are crowded into Defensa 179. Where the word comes from is contested: some say it is African, a word for the gathering place where enslaved people danced; others say it comes from the Portuguese; others trace it to the Latin tangere, to touch. Nobody in the conventillo is worrying about etymology. The music is being played."

Register: discover. Accumulative ("some say... others say... others trace"), broken by the short declarative ("Nobody in the conventillo is worrying about etymology"). Voice intact.

Register: discover. Short declaratives. "Technology" is slightly analytic-cool but consistent with the register. Clean.

*Paragraph selected from Section V:* "He was wrong about the immigrants' second generation."

Register: discover, short declarative at maximum weight. The Yrigoyen section's voice is correct throughout. Clean.

**Textbook audit:** No instances of "The economy was characterized by," "The society was organized around," "This period saw the development of." Clean.

## E-3 — Compression Editor

**Purpose:** Hunt re-explanation and filler. "This shows that..." Scenes followed by explanation. Redundancies.

**Re-explanation audit:** Section I closes with "The tango is a compression of the whole thing, three minutes long. A country that wanted to be Europe so badly that it needed Europe's permission to recognize what it was." This appears in both the revised opening and the closing landing. The repetition is intentional — a structural return — not re-explanation. Clean.

Section III's final paragraph ("This is what the European-validation loop produced when it was not paying attention") appears in Section VI, not Section III. No forward re-explanation.

**Filler audit:** Section IV has one near-filler sentence: "What none of these European observers understood, and what was not their concern to understand, was where the tango had come from." The second clause ("and what was not their concern to understand") adds no new information. Suggest trimming to: "What none of these European observers understood was where the tango had come from."

**Scene-explanation redundancy audit:** Section III shows the tango as "a technology for sharing space" and does not then explain what this means — the explanation IS the description. Clean.

**Required revision:**

In Section IV, paragraph beginning "What none of these European observers understood," trim to:

"What none of these European observers understood was where the tango had come from."
