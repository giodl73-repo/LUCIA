## Notes

This opening achieves something rare: the cosmological weight of Yvy Maraey is present without being explained. The reader understands it is real — not metaphorically real, not symbolically real, but real in the way the river behind Arapoty is real — without being told this. The voice maintains its register from first line to last.

The one dimension at 4 rather than 5 is Voice: the "tooth-in-the-belly" passage is slightly over-extended, and one sentence in the karai's address to the village uses a parallel structure that briefly feels more literary than prophetic. These are revision candidates for FIXES if the panel presses.
