# GATE-1 Scorecard

**Chapter:** Arameans — Era 2: The Age of the Aramean States
**Scorer:** GATE-1 Scorer
**Date:** 2026-04-16

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with a specific messenger arriving at a specific gate with dust on his sandals and leather in his hand. The negotiation between Ben-Hadad and Ahab is immediate, physical, placed. The reader is at the gate. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Aramean world emerges through its own logic: commerce as diplomacy, bazaar concessions as treaty terms, the caravan road as organizing structure, water as toll gate. The culture's priorities (trade, practical language, network over empire) shape every sentence without being explained. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | No outside judgment. No forbidden words. The Aramean states are presented on their own terms: the fragmented polity as functional form, the language spreading by utility not prestige. No comparison to Western equivalents. The culture's own sources (1 Kings commercial language, Assyrian annals) provide the evidence. |
| Texture   | 5/5   | Dust on sandals. Dry hill country. The Ghouta's apricot and almond orchards. The Barada spilling from the Anti-Lebanon. The brown steppe running east toward the Euphrates. Iron-fitted chariots. Horses bred on the grasslands. Water channels cut from the Barada's flow. Sensory throughout. |
| Voice     | 4/5   | Strong merchant-diplomatic register: declarative, practical, grounded in the vocabulary of exchange and negotiation. The prose rhythm is steady and directional, carrying goods and information the way a caravan road does. Occasional slight elevation in the closing paragraph leans more literary than the established register, though it recovers. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | The opening negotiation creates immediate dramatic tension (two kings balancing war and commerce with Assyria looming). The closing paragraph opens the meta-question: the language will outlast the states. The reader wants to see how. |
| **Total** | **29/30** | |

**Notes:** Passes GATE-1 decisively. The voice register is merchant-diplomatic-discover, grounded in the caravan road and the market transaction. The slight elevation in the final paragraph is a Pull-serving choice rather than a voice break, but a scorer could reasonably hold it at 4. The opening's use of 1 Kings 20:34 as a direct commercial quotation is strong evidence work. Zero em-dashes. The Aramean world's logic (trade network as infrastructure, language as the durable product) emerges without lecture.
