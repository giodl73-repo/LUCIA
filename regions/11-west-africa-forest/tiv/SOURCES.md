# Sources — Tiv

## Primary / Oral

- Tiv oral traditions — genealogical recitation maintained by elders; the Swem migration cycle
- Akiga Sai, *Akiga's Story: The Tiv Tribe as Seen by One of Its Members* (trans. Rupert East, 1939) — the essential Tiv self-account; an insider perspective from the colonial period

## Academic

- Bohannan, Paul. *Tiv Farm and Settlement*. 1954. HMSO (Colonial Research Studies).
- Bohannan, Paul & Laura. *The Tiv of Central Nigeria*. 1953. International African Institute.
- Bohannan, Paul. "Some Principles of Exchange and Investment Among the Tiv." *American Anthropologist* 57:1 (1955). — the spheres of exchange
- East, Rupert. *Akiga's Story*. 1939. Oxford University Press. — Akiga's account translated
- Byanrus, T.O. *Tiv Traditional Political Structure*. 1992. Ahmadu Bello University Press.
- Wegh, Shagbaor F. *Between Continuity and Change: Tiv Concept of Tradition and Modernity*. 1998. Vantage. — contemporary Tiv identity

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent historical work on Tiv expansion in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries
- Studies on Tiv-Jukun relations and the pre-colonial Benue Valley political landscape
- Analysis of the "Tiv riots" of 1929, 1939, 1960 in their political context
- Anthropological work on akombo in contemporary Tiv practice
