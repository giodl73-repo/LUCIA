## Findings

**Finding 1 — P3 (Minor repetition)**
Location: Body at Caen, penultimate block
Text: "England was reorganized from the ground up in twenty years, by a man who had stood on a field of ravens and understood that the battle was the easy part."

The phrase "reorganized from the ground up in twenty years" restates what the chapter has shown in four sections. The "ravens" callback is correct and earns its place. The rest of the sentence is the author summarizing before the final movement. The sentence could be removed entirely and the landing would be stronger. But the callback to "the battle was the easy part" (echoing the opening's "winning a battle and winning a country are not the same thing") is a valid structural choice. Author should consider: does the sentence add to the landing, or does it explain what the landing is doing? If the latter, cut it.

**Finding 2 — P3 (Slight hedge)**
Location: Domesday section, final paragraph
Text: "The book was presented to him, according to later sources, at Salisbury..."

"According to later sources" is a correct scholarly hedge and honest uncertainty per Style Principle 13. In this case, the uncertainty is real: the source for the Salisbury presentation is not contemporary. Retain.

**Finding 3 — P2 (Transition restatement)**

Text: "The Norman lord learned to hold his English court alongside the inherited shire court. He sat where the English had sat, administered the pleas the English had administered, collected the taxes the English had owed. The result was something that was neither fully Norman nor fully English."

The last sentence ("The result was something...") announces a conclusion that the preceding two sentences have already demonstrated. Cut: "The result was something that was neither fully Norman nor fully English." The two sentences before it have shown this without saying it.

**Finding 4 — PASS (not a re-explanation)**

This could be read as announcing what follows, but the paragraph continues with distinctions (replacement, village knowledge vs. documentary record) that the preceding scene does not fully make. It functions as a pivot, not a re-explanation. Retain.
