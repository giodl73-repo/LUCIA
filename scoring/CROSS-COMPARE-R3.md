# Cross-Comparison R3: Strategic Sample of 115-Chapter Corpus

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Chapters in corpus:** 115 locked across 93 cultures and 26 regions
**Chapters in R3 sample:** strategic selection across regions + 3 complete books (Mali, Inca, Rome) + 6 chapters scoring 89+ (Kuikuro, Anglo-Saxons, Brazil, Jamaica, Spanish Empire, French Empire at 90)
**Rubric version:** v1.7
**Predecessor:** CROSS-COMPARE-R2.md (39 chapters, rubric v1.3)

---

## Preamble

R2 examined 39 chapters at rubric v1.3 and found that structural engines, sensory-analytical fusion, culture's-own-persistence landings, and inside-voice honesty had generalized across all culture types tested. R2's recommendations drove the rubric through v1.4 (Voice Spectrum), v1.5 (cluster promotion rule + five engine-type amendments), v1.6 (Terminal Condition expansion to 10 types), and v1.7 (Contradiction-Held-as-Engine).

R3 examines the corpus at the 115-chapter, v1.7 scale. Three questions frame the analysis:

1. Which R2 patterns held, deepened, or generalized?
2. Which new patterns are visible only at 115-chapter scale?
3. Where did the typology fail to generalize, and what does that tell us?

---

## 1. WHAT HELD AND DEEPENED

### 1.1 Zero Em-Dash Discipline: Corpus-Wide Norm

R2 observed zero em-dashes across all 39 sampled chapters. At 115, the norm holds with two documented exceptions. PR-B1's 17-chapter spot-check finds 15/17 = 88.2% emergent-zero (Wilson 95% CI [66.2%, 96.6%]). The two non-conforming chapters — Mali Great Hajj (6 em-dashes, all in citation contexts) and Korea Japanese Colonization (13 em-dashes, all at register-shift boundaries in the Bilingual-Embodied register) — are legible as register-specific rather than discipline failures.

**Deepening observation.** The em-dash is no longer the diagnostic it was at R2. At 115 chapters we can see the *pause-mechanism-per-register* structure: Steppe and Saga-Spare close on the period; Maritime-Deep-Ocean on the comma-chain; Legal-Public on the semicolon; Border-State on full-stop-followed-by-new-angle; Capitulary on the colon. The em-dash would be wrong in each, and each has its own substitute. The register tells the pause-mechanism, not the rule.

**R3 recommendation.** Style Guide §13 (Rhythm Belongs to the Story) already captures this. No change needed. The emergent-zero property is validated.

### 1.2 Structural Engines: Taxonomy Stabilized

R2 listed ~18 engine types as an open list. At 115 chapters the taxonomy has formalized into 5 adopted engine categories (Covenant, Material Object, Non-Chronological, Knowledge-System, Contradiction-Held-as-Engine) plus 19 candidate types on the INNOVATIONS.md watch list (single-instance, pending cross-culture replication).

PR-B2 measures: mean 4.6 cultures per validated engine type; non-provincialism 3/5 = 60% (Wilson CI [23.1%, 88.2%]); 9 distinct regions across the validated core. The engine typology is cross-cultural rather than region-specific.

**Deepening observation.** Some cultures exhibit *dual engines* — political engine + knowledge engine running in parallel with divergent terminal conditions. Brazil's *A Colônia* is the founding instance: Palmares quilombo as political substrate (Substrate Exhaustion terminal) alongside axé as embodied-transmission system (Vindicating terminal). The chapter's thesis depends on holding both — the Portuguese are right about Palmares and wrong about the axé. At R2 scale, dual-engine chapters were not visible; at R3 scale, they are a candidate engine-type on their own.

**R3 recommendation.** Dual Engine remains a watch-list candidate in INNOVATIONS.md. Second instance needed; Brazil is alone. Candidates for second instance: Kongo Kingdom (state collapse + catechism persistence); Kalinago (political extinction + language preservation in Breton's dictionary).

### 1.3 Culture's-Own-Persistence Landings: Universal

R2 validated that chapters close on the culture's persistence mechanism rather than authorial summary. At 115 chapters this is universal: every 80+ scoring chapter uses the technique. The v1.1 Landing amendment is the most durable amendment in the rubric.

**Deepening observation.** Persistence mechanisms at R3 include types R2 did not name: the unanswered letter in the colonizer's archive (Guaman Poma / Spanish Empire — landing type "Appropriated Form as Endurance"); the ledger recording dividend and death simultaneously (Dutch Empire — "Colonial Archive as Evidence of Persistence"); the empire's own preserved dissent (Pinto's *Peregrinação* in the Portuguese archive). These are all variants of the broader principle and all scored 85+.

### 1.4 Inside-Voice Honesty: Validated Across Typology

R2 validated inside-voice honesty via the culture's own narrative tradition rather than editorial restraint. At 115 chapters the technique has scaled to every one of the 13 Voice Spectrum families. The hardest test — Colonial Empires, where the project's editorial principle (Achebe Test) runs against the extractive-system content — produced the highest region mean (86.2) and the first 90/90 (French Empire). The inside-voice discipline is apparently strongest where the stakes are highest.

**Deepening observation.** The v1.7 Contradiction-Held-as-Engine amendment is effectively the inside-voice principle operating at chapter-arc scale. When the empire's own framework generates the critique, the chapter can inhabit the framework without endorsing it. This is the same move Mali's *No-Verdict Decline via Cultural Attribution* (v1.2) made at sentence scale. The continuity from v1.2 to v1.7 is the rubric's deepest consistency.

## 2. NEW PATTERNS VISIBLE ONLY AT 115 SCALE

### 2.1 Scale-Dependent Saturation

PR-A3 measured the new-register-discovery-per-chapter rate across four chronological cohorts and found three patterns:

- **Voice Spectrum saturates sharply.** Cohort rates 0.39, 0.38, 0.41, 0.03. Final-to-initial ratio 9%.
- **Structural Engine saturates completely.** Cohort rates 0.50, 0.03, 0.07, 0.00. Final-to-initial ratio 0%.
- **Terminal Condition does NOT saturate.** Cohort rates 0.04, 0.10, 0.34, 0.10. Final-to-initial ratio 286%.

The non-saturation is not a failure; it is a finding. Terminal conditions live at arc-closure scale and require culturally-diverse corpus to exhaust. Voice and Engine live at finer substrate scales (sentence, arc) with more per-chapter replication opportunities.

**R3 recommendation.** Expect Terminal Condition typology to keep growing through Session 4 and beyond. The 8-candidate endogenous-institutional family (v1.7 watch list) already spans non-colonial cultures per PR-B3 classification; adoption to v1.8 is likely after one more session of chapter production.

### 2.2 The Bilingual-Embodied Register as Cross-Cultural Pattern

Session 2 produced Korea *Japanese Colonization* as the founding instance of the Bilingual-Embodied register — prose that shifts between the colonizer's bureaucratic mode and the colonized's embodied-knowledge mode. Session 2 produced Canada *National Policy* as the second instance. Session 3's innovation-sweep promoted Bilingual-Embodied to adopted-at-v1.4 status.

This is the first register that admits em-dash characters by design (13 in Korea; em-dash count at register-shift boundaries). It is the exception that proves the zero-em-dash rule: when the register's own pause mechanism DOES include the em-dash (as a register-shift marker), the em-dash is correct. When the register's pause mechanism is period/colon/comma-chain (the default), the em-dash is wrong.

**Deepening observation.** Bilingual-Embodied is also the first register whose application context is primarily colonial/post-colonial. Candidate extensions: any culture whose historical voice was bilingual under an imposed administrative language. Phoenician under Roman; Aramaic under Persian then Greek; Gaelic under English. These are future instances to watch.

### 2.3 Contradiction-Held-as-Engine: The Colonial-Empire Cluster

R3's most substantial finding is the v1.7 cluster. Five colonial empires (British, Spanish, French, Dutch, Portuguese) produced five distinct but structurally-parallel engines:

- **British Dual-Ledger**: official manifest AND second ledger; contradiction held administratively.
- **Spanish Internal-Debate Legal-Theological**: Palacios Rubios AND Las Casas AND Sepúlveda AND Guaman Poma; Valladolid Debate produces no verdict because no verdict is possible within the framework.
- **French Universalist-Bureaucratic**: Declaration of Rights AND partial application; enslaved invoke the Declaration against the exclusion; the Declaration works exactly as its words required.
- **Dutch Commercial Asabiyyah**: VOC ledger recording dividend AND Bandanese deaths in the same archive; no authorial verdict required because the ledger delivers the argument.
- **Portuguese Saudade-Maritime + Internal Dissent**: *Os Lusíadas* AND Pinto's *Peregrinação*; the empire produces and preserves its own critique.

All five chapters scored 85-90. The French Empire Era 2 is the project's first 90/90. The cluster analysis (see CLUSTER-ANALYSIS-COLONIAL-EMPIRES.md) identified Contradiction-Held-as-Engine as a genuinely new engine category and promoted it to v1.7.

**R3 recommendation.** Watch for non-colonial instances of Contradiction-Held (late-Republic Roman *libertas* invoked against *libertas*; late Ming Neo-Confucian self-critique; Soviet dissidents arguing from Leninism). When a non-colonial instance lands, the cluster extends beyond its founding region and becomes a universal structural principle rather than a colonial-specific one.

### 2.4 Book-Level Arc as Persistence-Mechanism Transformation

R2 had three complete books (Mali, Inca, Rome) and the v1.4 Book-Level Arc amendment. At R3 we can characterize the pattern more precisely: in a 5-era book, the culture's persistence mechanism is *planted* in Era 1, *tested* in middle eras, and *transformed* in the final era such that the final era's close returns to the opening era's images with a changed charge.

- **Mali.** Kurukan Fuga redistribution logic planted in Era 1 (*The Lion's Time*), tested in Eras 2-4, self-consuming in Era 5 (*The Songhai Succession* — the jelilu still singing the genealogy while the empire contracts around it).
- **Inca.** mit'a + vertical archipelago + quipu planted in Era 1 (*Pachakuti*), stressed in Era 4 (*Huayna Capac*), broken-and-preserved in Era 5 (*The War of Brothers* — the quipu-keeper still tying the knots for a state that no longer receives them).
- **Rome.** res publica + libertas + mos maiorum + law planted in Era 1 (*The Founding*), tested in Era 2, weaponized in Era 3, transformed in Eras 4-5 (the law still holds, but for whom).

**R3 observation.** The book-level arc is the highest-scoring form of the chronicle. Of chapters at 85+, the proportion belonging to 5-era books is disproportionately high relative to the 3-books-of-15-book-chapters baseline. Completing more books (Tang, Haudenosaunee, Norse, San, Benin) is high-leverage for manuscript quality.

### 2.5 Pipeline Discipline as Score Predictor

PR-C2 measured Pearson r(pipeline-compliance, GATE-2 score) within Sonnet-Session-2 = 0.46 (bootstrap CI [0.27, 0.62]); pooled r = 0.43. Full-vs-deeply-reduced delta = +4.7 points. Pipeline explains ~21% of within-model score variance.

This is an R3-only finding; at R2 the pipeline-compliance-vs-score correlation was not yet measurable. The implication is directly actionable: for chapters that need to score highest, follow the full 11-stage pipeline; for chapters where speed matters more, selective stage-skipping costs ~0.4 points per missed stage but remains within the passing band.

**R3 recommendation.** The scoring rubric should explicitly cross-reference pipeline compliance. A chapter scoring 82 with 10/10 compliance is in a different place than a chapter scoring 82 with 4/10 compliance. The second is evidence the pipeline was under-applied; the first is evidence the pipeline was sufficient.

## 3. WHERE THE TYPOLOGY FAILED OR STRUGGLED

### 3.1 Four Unclassified Terminal Conditions

PR-B3's full-corpus classification against 18 candidate terminal-condition types left 4 chapters (3.5% of 115) unclassified after second-pass reconciliation:

- **Arameans Era 2** — distributed-polity dispersal without clean terminal; neither Ecological, Substrate, nor Personal-Authority fits.
- **Ghana Empire** — second-pass partial fit to Legitimacy Gap; sub-variant candidate.
- **Fulani Era 4 (Dan Fodio)** — dual-fits Self-Consumption and Internally-Generated Legitimacy Gap; neither is clean primary.
- **Meroë Era 3 (Roman Encounter)** — ambiguous between Gradual Premise Erosion and external-decline pattern.

**R3 observation.** These four are the discovery-engine signal for future rubric amendments. They don't invalidate the typology; they mark where the typology has not yet expanded. The Arameans "distributed-polity dispersal" pattern may become a v1.9 candidate terminal-type after a second instance appears (candidate: Roma diaspora eras; Overseas Chinese diaspora eras — both culturally-distributed traditions without political termini).

### 3.2 Region 10 (Sahel) Over-Representation in Anchor Quality

The anchor work (Mali 5 eras, then Sahel sweep to 12 chapters) made Region 10 the densest region in the corpus. This is a scaling artifact, not a structural finding — future session production should rebalance toward under-represented regions (Oceania at 2 chapters; Tibet-Himalaya at 1; East Africa at 1). The risk is that the typology is over-fit to Sahelian structural patterns (Covenant, Legitimacy Gap) because Sahel chapters dominated the training set.

**R3 recommendation.** Session 4 chapter selection should prioritize regions with ≤ 3 chapters. Specifically: complete Tibet, East Asia, Oceania, East Africa before adding more Sahel.

### 3.3 Single-Era Cultures Over-Represented

93 cultures have 1 chapter each. Only 3 cultures have 5-era books. The rest sit at 1-2 eras. The book-level arc pattern (§2.4 above) cannot manifest in single-era cultures. Chapters on single-era cultures are structurally constrained to a one-era-full-arc that compresses rather than develops the persistence mechanism.

**R3 recommendation.** For Session 4-5, prioritize completing 2-3 additional books (Tang, Haudenosaunee) over adding 20-30 new single-era chapters. The manuscript's narrative weight depends on more complete books, not broader-but-thinner coverage.

## 4. RECOMMENDATIONS

Summarizing across the three sections:

1. **No changes** to zero-em-dash discipline, inside-voice honesty, or Landing rubric. The R2 amendments are validated at 115-chapter scale.

2. **Watch for** non-colonial Contradiction-Held instances; a second Dual Engine instance; non-colonial extensions of the 8-candidate endogenous-institutional Terminal Condition family; non-Bilingual-Embodied register shift candidates (bilingual under imposed administrative languages).

3. **Prioritize** book completions in Session 4 over new single-era chapters. Tang (4 missing eras), Haudenosaunee (4), Norse (4), San (3), Benin (4) — 19 chapters total would bring the corpus to 134 and the complete-book count from 3 to 8.

4. **Cross-reference** pipeline-compliance when reporting GATE-2 scores. A score plus a compliance number is more legible than a score alone.

5. **Close the scale imbalance** by prioritizing under-represented regions (Tibet-Himalaya, Oceania, East Asia, East Africa) before further Sahel/Americas Reborn work.

6. **Retire R3 as procedure; open R4 after Session 4 completes.** R3 is the second cross-compare on record; the cadence has matured. R4 should run after the corpus reaches ~140 chapters (post-Session 4 book completions).
