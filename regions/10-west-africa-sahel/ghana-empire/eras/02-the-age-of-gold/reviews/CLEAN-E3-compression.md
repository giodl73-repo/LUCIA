## Findings

1. **Section II, paragraph 4:** "This was the foundation beneath the foundation." followed by a sentence that restates what the preceding paragraph already showed (that the Wangara's control of the source was what made the system work). The preceding paragraph's final sentences ("If the terms were wrong, the gold did not appear, and there was nothing the merchants or the Ghana could do about it") already established the Wangara's control. "This was the foundation beneath the foundation" restates the point. **P2 — throat-clearing. Recommend cut or merge.**

2. **Section IV, paragraph 2:** "The second pillar was commercial control. The dual-city arrangement allowed the Ghana to tax the trans-Saharan trade without alienating the Muslim merchants who conducted it. The merchants had their mosques, their qadis, their legal system. The Ghana had his grove, his priests, his ceremony. Neither encroached on the other. The separation was the mechanism." This restates Section III's discovery of the dual-city structure. The reader already knows this from having just read Section III. **P1 — re-explanation. Recommend cutting or reducing to a single transitional sentence.**

3. **Section IV, paragraph 3:** "This balance was not static. It was managed, generation after generation, by Ghana rulers who understood that the arrangement required active maintenance." This tells the reader what the subsequent examples will show. The examples (new merchant communities given space, Soninke nobles converting) are sufficient on their own. **P2 — throat-clearing. Recommend cut.**

4. **Section V, paragraph 3:** "The growth of Islam among the Soninke elite added an internal pressure. More courtiers worshipped in the merchant quarter's mosques. More of the Ghana's own counselors observed Ramadan and made the hajj." The first sentence states what the next two sentences show. **P2 — re-explanation. Recommend cutting the first sentence and starting directly with "More courtiers..."**

5. **Section VI:** "The arrangement held. It held because the gold still came and the salt still came and the Ghana still stood between them, maintaining the balance that made both possible. It held because the Wangara still chose to bring their gold and the Sanhaja still chose to bring their salt and the merchants of the Muslim world still chose to cross the desert for the profit the exchange offered. It held because the grove still stood and the covenant still governed and the drums still sounded at dawn." This is deliberate rhetorical repetition ("It held because...") that serves the landing's rhythmic purpose. It is not re-explanation. **NOT a finding — rhetorical structure.**
