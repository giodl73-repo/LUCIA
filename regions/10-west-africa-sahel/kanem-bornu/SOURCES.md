# Sources — Kanem-Bornu

## Primary

- Ahmad ibn Fartuwa, *History of the First Twelve Years of the Reign of Mai Idris Alooma of Bornu* — the most detailed contemporary chronicle of a Saifawa ruler
- *Diwan of the Sultans of Bornu* — the dynasty's official king list; key chronological document
- Al-Umari, *Masalik al-Absar* — account of Kanem in the fourteenth century
- Ibn Battuta, *Rihla* — mentions of the Lake Chad region

## Academic

- Lange, Dierk. *A Sudanic Chronicle: The Borno Expeditions of Idris Alauma (1564–1576)*. 1987. Steiner Verlag. — Ibn Fartuwa translated and analyzed
- Connah, Graham. *Three Thousand Years in Africa: Man and His Environment in the Lake Chad Region of Nigeria*. 1981. Cambridge University Press.
- Urvoy, Y. *Histoire de l'Empire du Bornou*. 1949. IFAN. — foundational study
- Brenner, Louis. *The Shehus of Kukawa: A History of the al-Kanemi Dynasty of Bornu*. 1973. Oxford University Press. — the nineteenth-century transition
- Lavers, John E. "Islam and the State in Kanem-Bornu: Some Thoughts on the Late Saifawa and the Early Kanemi Periods." In *Studies in the History of Pre-Colonial Borno*, ed. Bawuro Barkindo. 1983.
