> "He was not a man who mistook the running for permanence." (near end of Panipat section)

**Finding 2 — P3 (borderline)**
In the franchise section:
> "The arrangement was not a bureaucracy. It was a franchise."

And then later:
> "What made the franchise work was what made all such arrangements work: the expectation of returns."

The term "franchise" is introduced, then immediately re-explained in what it means. The introduction and the explanation are close together and the second sentence works better without the first sentence's framing already in place. However, this is a borderline case — the repetition is structural (the term introduced, then its mechanism named) rather than redundant. Author decides.

**Recommended action:** Read both sentences together; if the second sentence works without the first term having been established, consider merging: "It was a franchise — it ran on the expectation of returns." But do not shorten if the rhythm suffers.

## Restating Transitions

**Finding 1 — P2**
The opening of the Panipat section:
> "The Third Battle of Panipat was fought on the fourteenth of January, 1761, on the fifth of Jumada al-Awwal in the year 1174 of the Hijra, on the same ground outside Delhi where Babur had defeated Ibrahim Lodi in 1526 and where Akbar's forces had defeated Hemu in 1556."

This sentence is doing important work (the geography of Panipat as historically overdetermined) but the parenthetical historical references — 1526, 1556 — require the reader to have a reference for what those dates mean. The sentence assumes more background than the chapter has provided. This is not a compression issue but a context issue. The chapter should either provide the micro-context for why Panipat is historically overdetermined (which the next sentence partially does: "Panipat was not a coincidence but a geography") or cut the specific dates from this opening sentence.

**Recommended fix:** Cut the specific battle dates (1526 and 1556) from the opening sentence. Retain "the same ground outside Delhi where two armies before them had decided the shape of the subcontinent." Simpler, more evocative, no assumed background required.
