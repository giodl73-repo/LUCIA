# Sources — French Empire

## Key Academic Works
- Alice Conklin, *A Mission to Civilize: The Republican Idea of Empire in France and West Africa, 1895–1930* (1997, Stanford) — the mission civilisatrice analyzed
- Benjamin Stora, *Algeria, 1830–2000: A Short History* (2001, Cornell) — the standard account
- Alistair Horne, *A Savage War of Peace: Algeria 1954–1962* (1977, Macmillan) — the most thorough account of the Algerian War
- Martin Shipway, *Decolonization and its Impact: A Comparative Approach to the End of the Colonial Empires* (2008)
- Gary Wilder, *The French Imperial Nation-State: Negritude and Colonial Humanism between the Two World Wars* (2005, Chicago)
- Frantz Fanon, *The Wretched of the Earth* (1961) — the anti-colonial philosophy from within the French colonial experience

## Primary Sources
- Aimé Césaire, *Notebook of a Return to the Native Land* (1939) and *Discourse on Colonialism* (1950)
- Léopold Sédar Senghor — selected poems and essays
- Ho Chi Minh's writings and speeches
- The Évian Accords (1962)
- French colonial legal codes — Archives Nationales, Paris

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Archives d'Outre-Mer (Aix-en-Provence) — colonial administrative records
- Algerian National Archive — the independence movement and war
- Oral histories of pieds-noirs and Harkis
- Contemporary Françafrique documentation — Agir ici and Survie (NGO archives)
