### Manual Pass — Implicit Judgment, Hidden Hierarchy, Anachronistic Morality

**Finding 1 — P3 (Borderline)**
**Location:** chapter.md, "La Tierra" section
**Quoted text:** "Most of Latin America resolved its colonial land question through the hacienda: large estates worked by dependent peons, sharecroppers, or indigenous communities. This created poverty, but poverty has its own stability."

**Concern:** "poverty has its own stability" is a sociological observation offered without attribution to the culture's own framework. It reads slightly as authorial analysis from outside the campesino experience. Not a forbidden framing, but could be read as rationalizing structural inequality in a way that doesn't belong to the text's discover register.

**Recommendation:** Author decision. One option: replace with "The peon family's relationship to the land was defined by the landlord's interest, which was also the landlord's party's interest." This grounds the observation in the structural fact without the editorial gloss.

**Finding 2 — P3 (Borderline)**
**Location:** chapter.md, "El Bandolero" section
**Quoted text:** "What El Cóndor represented, by the time of his death, was the structural residue of La Violencia: the violence had found a way to sustain itself after the conditions that produced it had been nominally resolved."

**Concern:** "nominally resolved" implies a judgment that the National Front was inadequate — which is a historically defensible claim, but one the chapter makes twice (here and in the landing). The redundancy of the verdict ("nominally resolved" here, then the landing's fuller treatment) risks turning the chapter's No Verdicts discipline into repeated editorial observation. The landing handles this better; consider whether "nominally resolved" here adds value or anticipates the landing prematurely.

**Recommendation:** Author decision. Consider: "the conditions that produced it had been set aside rather than addressed."
