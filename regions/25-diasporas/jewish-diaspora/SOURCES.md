# Sources — Jewish Diaspora

## Key Academic Works
- Simon Dubnow, *World History of the Jewish People* (10 vols., 1925–1929) — the diaspora-centered account
- Salo W. Baron, *A Social and Religious History of the Jews* (18 vols., 1952–1983) — encyclopedic
- Paul Johnson, *A History of the Jews* (1987, Harper & Row) — readable comprehensive history
- Yuri Slezkine, *The Jewish Century* (2004, Princeton) — Jewish modernity and its transformations
- Deborah Lipstadt, *Denying the Holocaust* (1993) — Holocaust memory and its defense
- Amos Elon, *The Pity of It All: A Portrait of the German-Jewish Epoch* (2002) — the German-Jewish synthesis and its destruction
- Yosef Hayim Yerushalmi, *Zakhor: Jewish History and Jewish Memory* (1982) — the relationship between history and memory in Jewish tradition

## Primary Sources
- The Talmud (Babylonian and Jerusalem) — the foundational Jewish legal and intellectual record
- Maimonides, *Mishneh Torah* (1180) and *Guide for the Perplexed* (1190)
- The Passover Haggadah — the primary liturgical document of collective memory
- Testimony collections — USC Shoah Foundation; Yad Vashem; USHMM
- Sholem Aleichem — Yiddish literature; the shtetl world
