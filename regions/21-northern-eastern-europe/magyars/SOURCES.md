# Sources — Magyars

## Primary Sources

- *Gesta Hungarorum* by Anonymus — c. 1200; the earliest Hungarian chronicle; the founding narrative
- *Gesta Hunnorum et Hungarorum* by Simon of Kéza — c. 1285; the Hun-Magyar continuity theory
- *Tripartitum* by István Werbőczy — 1514; the codification of Hungarian noble constitutional law; the "Sacred Crown" doctrine
- Golden Bull of 1222 — the foundational charter of Hungarian noble rights
- Laws of Saint Stephen — 11th century; the foundational legislation
- *Chronica Hungarorum* — 1473; first printed book in Hungary; the medieval chronicle tradition consolidated

## Secondary Sources

- Engel, Pál. *The Realm of St. Stephen: A History of Medieval Hungary, 895–1526*. I.B. Tauris, 2001.
- Kontler, László. *A History of Hungary*. Palgrave Macmillan, 2002.
- Berend, Nora. *At the Gate of Christendom: Jews, Muslims and 'Pagans' in Medieval Hungary*. Cambridge University Press, 2001.
- Bak, János M., ed. *Coronations: Medieval and Early Modern Monarchic Ritual*. University of California Press, 1990.
- Sinor, Denis. *History of Hungary*. George Allen & Unwin, 1959.
- Teke, Zsuzsa. *Hunyadi János és kora*. Gondolat, 1980.
