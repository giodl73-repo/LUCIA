# Sources — Predynastic Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Naqada excavation records — Petrie, 1895; foundational sequence dating for Predynastic culture phases
- Hierakonpolis excavations — ongoing; Hoffman, Adams, et al.; elite burials, painted tomb, early urban evidence
- Abydos royal cemetery records — Dreyer, German Archaeological Institute; Dynasty 0 and Early Dynastic tombs
- Narmer Palette — Cairo Museum; canonical document of late Predynastic royal ideology

## Academic

- Wilkinson, Toby. *Genesis of the Pharaohs*. 2003. Thames & Hudson. — accessible synthesis of Predynastic origins
- Wengrow, David. *The Archaeology of Early Egypt*. 2006. Cambridge University Press. — social transformations of the Predynastic period
- Hendrickx, Stan & Vermeersch, Pierre. "Prehistory: From the Palaeolithic to the Badarian Culture." In *The Oxford History of Ancient Egypt*, ed. Ian Shaw. 2000. — period survey
- Köhler, E. Christiana. "The Formation of the Egyptian State." In *A Companion to Ancient Egypt*, ed. Alan Lloyd. 2010. Wiley-Blackwell.
- Patch, Diana Craig (ed.). *Dawn of Egyptian Art*. 2011. Metropolitan Museum of Art. — visual culture of the Predynastic

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent Hierakonpolis field reports (2010s–2020s) for updated urban evidence
- Specialist studies on Naqada II / Mesopotamian contact (Baines, Wengrow debate)
- Radiocarbon chronology refinements for Naqada sequence
- Studies on A-Group Nubian contemporaries and their relationship to Predynastic Egypt
