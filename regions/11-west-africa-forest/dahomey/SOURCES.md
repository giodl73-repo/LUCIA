# Sources — Dahomey / Fon Kingdom

## Primary / Oral

- Fon royal traditions — maintained at Abomey; the palace oral historians
- Alpern, Stanley B. — fieldwork with Agojie descendants
- French colonial records — Archives nationales d'outre-mer, Aix-en-Provence
- Forbes, Frederick E. *Dahomey and the Dahomans*. 2 vols. 1851. — first-hand British account

## Academic

- Bay, Edna G. *Wives of the Leopard: Gender, Politics, and Culture in the Kingdom of Dahomey*. 1998. University of Virginia Press.
- Law, Robin. *The Slave Coast of West Africa 1550–1750*. 1991. Oxford University Press.
- Alpern, Stanley B. *Amazons of Black Sparta: The Women Warriors of Dahomey*. 1998. NYU Press.
- Herskovits, Melville J. *Dahomey: An Ancient West African Kingdom*. 2 vols. 1938. J.J. Augustin. — foundational ethnographic study
- Blier, Suzanne Preston. *African Vodun: Art, Psychology, and Power*. 1995. University of Chicago Press.
- Monroe, J. Cameron. *The Precolonial State in West Africa: Building Power in Dahomey*. 2011. Cambridge University Press.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on the Agojie as a military institution (post-Alpern)
- Studies on vodun in its Dahomey form and its transformation in the diaspora
- Archaeological reports on the Abomey palace complex
- Scholarship on the repatriation of Dahomey royal treasures from the Musée du quai Branly
