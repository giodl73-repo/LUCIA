# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *Samguk Yusa* (Memorabilia of the Three Kingdoms, Iryeon, 1281) — contains the Dangun origin myth; trans. Ha Tae-hung and Grafton Mintz
- *Samguk Sagi* (History of the Three Kingdoms, Kim Busik, 1145) — earliest sections on Gojoseon
- Chinese sources: *Shiji*, *Hanshu* — accounts of Gojoseon and the Han conquest

## Key Academic Works

- Ki-baik Lee, *A New History of Korea* (1984) — foundational
- Gina Barnes, *State Formation in Korea: Historical and Archaeological Perspectives* (2001)
- Mark Byington — Gojoseon archaeological and historical studies
- Hyung Il Pai — Korean origins studies

## Needed

- Recent archaeological studies of the Gojoseon bronze culture distribution
