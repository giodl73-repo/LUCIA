## B-mitanni-1 — Amanda Podany — Board Review of The Age of Equals

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Bronze Age international relations, the Amarna diplomatic system, gift-exchange protocols, the Great Power concept.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The representation of the diplomatic system is accurate to the scholarly consensus. The "brotherhood" concept, the calibrated gift-exchange, the multilateral nature of the correspondence, and the role of marriage alliances are all correctly drawn.
- Tushratta's letters are paraphrased accurately. The gold complaints, the specification of quantities, the tone of the correspondence match the Moran translation.
- The five Great Powers are correctly identified. The inclusion of Alashiya (Cyprus) as a marginal participant is a nice touch and accurate.
- The Gilukhipa scarab and the 317 attendants are correctly attributed to Amenhotep III.
- Minor note: the chapter states Amenhotep III's dates as c. 1390-1353 BCE (in figures.md). The conventional dates are c. 1391-1353 or 1388-1351, depending on the chronological system. Either is acceptable, but the chapter should be internally consistent.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The diplomatic system is presented from within its own logic, not as a curiosity. The "fiction of equality" framing is analytically sharp without stepping outside the worldview.
- The gift-exchange is correctly presented as diplomatic rather than commercial, which is essential. The distinction between gift and payment is the system's central operating principle.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The structural engine (the fiction of equality maintained through calibrated reciprocity) is an excellent analytical framework that accurately captures how the system operated.
- Burnaburiash's gold-leaf complaint is correctly cited and effectively deployed as evidence of the system's operating logic.
- The Shaushka statue episode is correctly situated within the diplomatic correspondence.

**What needs correction:**
- No factual corrections required. The chapter is historically sound.

**Verdict:** APPROVED
