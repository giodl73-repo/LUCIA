# Sources — Danes

## Primary Sources

- Jelling runestones — c. 958–965; Gorm the Old and Harald Bluetooth; primary monument of early Danish history
- Saxo Grammaticus, *Gesta Danorum* — c. 1200; the major Danish chronicle; mythology and history combined
- *Jyske Lov* (Jutish Law, 1241) — the great Danish law code of the high medieval period
- Cnut's letter to the English people, 1027 — primary source for Cnut's self-presentation as European monarch
- *Anglo-Saxon Chronicle* — detailed Danish activity in England from the Great Heathen Army onward
- Adam of Bremen, *Gesta Hammaburgensis Ecclesiae Pontificum* — c. 1075; the ecclesiastical history of the Hamburg-Bremen archbishopric; primary source for Scandinavian Christianization

## Secondary Sources

- Sawyer, Peter H. *The Oxford Illustrated History of the Vikings*. Oxford University Press, 1997.
- Bolton, Timothy. *Cnut the Great*. Yale University Press, 2017.
- Roesdahl, Else. *Viking Age Denmark*. British Museum Publications, 1982.
- Lund, Niels. *Lid, leding og landeværn: Hær og samfund i Danmark i ældre middelalder*. 1996.
- Christiansen, Eric. *The Northern Crusades*. Penguin, 1997.
- Hermanson, Lars. *Bärande band: Vänskap, kärlek och brödraskap i det medeltida Nordeuropa*. 2009.
