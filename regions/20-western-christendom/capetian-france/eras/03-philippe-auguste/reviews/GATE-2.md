## Innovation Flags

**Voice Register: Sacred-Legal.** A new register, not in the current confirmed list. Definition: prose that moves with the simultaneous weight of legal pronouncement and sacred obligation, without rushing (steppe) or accumulating (maritime) or questioning (argumentative). Medium declaratives, colons for legal enumeration, the rhythm of the king's chancery. Zero em-dashes as an emergent property of the register. Analytically the voice performs legal-institutional argument in the same rhythm as narrative. Recommend logging to INNOVATIONS.md with this chapter as source. With prior confirmed legal-public register (Roman), this Sacred-Legal variant represents a distinct register that should be added to the Voice Spectrum in the style guide.

**Structural Engine: Three-Register Power (Legal + Sacred + Military in Concert).** The chapter demonstrates that Capetian power was not any single mechanism but the simultaneous operation of three: legal enforcement of feudal claims, sacred legitimacy that made challenges feel sacrilegious, and military confirmation that made legal and sacred claims irreversible. The chapter shows all three operating and their interaction. This engine type may be applicable to other theocratic-legal states and is worth flagging for the rubric's Progression amendment consideration.
