**The physical register of each section:**

*Section 1 (Herat campaign):* The road from Tehran to Herat runs east through Khorasan, across the plateau, through the desert edge at Nishapur and Mashhad, then across open country to the Afghan frontier. The army in winter on this road would have been cold, dust-heavy in the windy sections, moving at the pace of the slowest gun carriage. The British naval squadron at Bushire was in the Persian Gulf — humid, salt-bleached, nothing like the plateau. The chapter should put us briefly in both places: the army's eastward confidence, and then the news arriving from the south that the Gulf coast was no longer open. The reversal should be felt as a spatial disjunction — you march east, you are stopped from the south.

*Section 2 (Dar al-Funun):* The Dar al-Funun opened in Tehran in 1851, on what is now Nasir Khosrow Street. Its first building was modest — a courtyard school that would have smelled of fresh plaster and animal manure from the nearby stables. Austrian officers taught artillery in a language their Iranian students did not initially speak; a Georgian-born Russian-trained physician taught anatomy on a human cadaver while a translator rendered his words. What did those students feel? The chapter should put one student in that room.

*Section 3 (Tehran, 1873):* Naser al-Din Shah's first European trip. He kept detailed diaries. He was astonished by the Paris Opera, by the London docks, by the Belgian railway network. He compared everything to what Tehran had and did not have. The diary is primary evidence of what a Persian eye saw in Europe — use it directly.

*Section 4 (the fatwa and the boycott):* The sensory life here is the absence: the coffee-houses empty, the qalyan cold, the tobacco growers sitting on their unsold harvest. But also the positive presence of the clerical network's activity — the courier from Samarra arriving at the Tehran mosque, the cleric reading the letter before the Friday congregation, the silence in the bazaar the next morning. Show each step with a body in it.
