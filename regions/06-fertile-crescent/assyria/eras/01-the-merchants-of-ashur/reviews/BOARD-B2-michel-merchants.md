## B-2 — Michel (model) — Board Review of The Merchants of Ashur

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Women's correspondence in the Kanesh archive, textile production, family law, merchant household structure.

**Historical accuracy:**
- Lamassatum's role as described is consistent with her letters in the archive. She managed textile production, oversaw weavers, tracked debts, and corresponded with her husband about business matters. The chapter accurately presents her as a business partner, not merely a household manager.
- Pushu-ken's characterization as a merchant at Kanesh who managed the Anatolian end of the trade is consistent with the corpus.
- The description of textile production in merchant households as the foundation of the outbound trade is correct. The textiles were a major cargo alongside tin, and their production was supervised by the women in Ashur.
- The mention of local Anatolian marriages alongside the legally binding marriages in Ashur is correct. The legal status of these unions varied, and the chapter does not overstate the evidence.
- The instruction from Pushu-ken about fine-quality textiles for the palace at Kanesh is consistent with the type of instructions found in the merchant correspondence.

**What needs attention:**
- The chapter attributes specific letter content to Lamassatum and Pushu-ken without noting that the letters are composites reconstructed from fragmentary tablets. While the general content is accurate, the specific phrasing should be understood as representative of the corpus rather than direct quotation from a single complete letter. The figures.md handles this correctly with "the interior lives of these figures are not reconstructed," but the chapter itself should signal composite representation somewhere in the opening section.
- The chapter mentions "your daughter" in the opening letter but does not specify whether Pushu-ken and Lamassatum had a daughter attested in the archive. The inclusion is effective narratively but should be verified or disclosed as a composite detail.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The chapter avoids both minimizing and romanticizing women's roles. Lamassatum is presented through her own words and actions, not through modern commentary about gender empowerment. This is the correct approach.
- The textile production is correctly presented as structurally essential, not as a sidebar.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS. Add a signal in the opening that the letter content is composite/representative, and verify or disclose the daughter detail.
