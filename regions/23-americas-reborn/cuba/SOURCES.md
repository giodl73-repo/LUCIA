# Sources — Cuba

## Key Academic Works
- Louis A. Pérez Jr., *Cuba: Between Reform and Revolution* (4th ed., 2011, Oxford) — the standard scholarly history
- Ada Ferrer, *Cuba: An American History* (2021, Scribner) — the most recent comprehensive treatment; essential
- C.L.R. James — on Martí and the Caribbean revolutionary tradition
- Reinaldo Arenas, *Before Night Falls* (memoir, 1992) — the revolution from inside a dissident's experience
- Nicolás Guillén — Cuba's national poet; the Afro-Cuban literary tradition
- Fernando Ortiz, *Cuban Counterpoint: Tobacco and Sugar* (1940) — the foundational text of Cuban cultural anthropology

## Primary Sources
- José Martí, *Nuestra América* (1891) and selected essays and correspondence
- Fidel Castro, "History Will Absolve Me" (defense speech, 1953)
- The Cuban Constitution (1940, 1976, 2019)
- Ernesto "Che" Guevara, *Guerrilla Warfare* (1961) and *Bolivian Diary* (1968)

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Cuban state archive materials (access restricted; some available through National Archives of Cuba)
- CIA operational records on Bay of Pigs and assassination plots (partially declassified)
- Soviet archive materials on the Missile Crisis
- Contemporary oral history from both sides of the Straits of Florida
