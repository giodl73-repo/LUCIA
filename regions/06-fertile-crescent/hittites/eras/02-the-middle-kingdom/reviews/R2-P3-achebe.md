## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Focus:** Voice consistency; inside-voice risk areas

**Assessment:** The notes identify the correct voice register (treaty-speak, proclamation, witness-heavy) and the structural engine is grounded in the culture's own categories (covenant, divine witness, oath). Good. The panel pressure points section shows awareness of where the voice may slip into modern categories.

**Remaining concern:** Section 3 ("The Covenant Turned Inward") is the highest-risk section for voice drift. Analyzing the treaty form's components (historical preamble, obligations, divine witnesses, curse clauses) can easily become a scholar's anatomy lesson rather than a Hittite's experience of what a covenant IS. The chapter should present the treaty form from inside: not "the treaty contained these elements" but "the gods heard the words, and the words were binding, and the tablet held them." The analysis must be conducted in the treaty-voice, not in an academic register layered over it.

**Additional note:** The notes correctly plan to use direct quotation from the edict as the voice's anchor. This is the right approach for an inscriptional/edict culture. The primary source IS the voice. Let the edict speak whenever possible, and let the narrative voice calibrate itself to the edict's register.
