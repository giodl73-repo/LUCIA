# Sources — Portuguese Empire

## Key Academic Works
- C.R. Boxer, *The Portuguese Seaborne Empire 1415–1825* (1969, Hutchinson) — the standard scholarly history
- A.J.R. Russell-Wood, *The Portuguese Empire, 1415–1808* (1998, Johns Hopkins)
- Malyn Newitt, *A History of Portuguese Overseas Expansion, 1400–1668* (2005, Routledge)
- Gilberto Freyre, *Casa-Grande & Senzala* (1933) — the Lusotropicalism thesis; read critically
- Patrick Chabal, *A History of Postcolonial Lusophone Africa* (2002, Hurst)
- John Hemming — Brazil; indigenous peoples of the Amazon

## Primary Sources
- Luís Vaz de Camões, *Os Lusíadas* (1572) — the national epic; the Portuguese imperial project in its own highest literary form
- Fernão Lopes de Castanheda — chronicles of the India route
- Gaspar Correia — *Lendas da Índia* (written 1550s; published later) — the Estado da Índia from inside
- João de Barros — *Décadas da Ásia* — the official history of Portuguese expansion in Asia

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Arquivo Histórico Ultramarino (Lisbon) — the overseas archive; essential for colonial documentation
- Torre do Tombo National Archive — the full institutional record
- PIDE/DGS (secret police) records — the Estado Novo period
- African independence movement archives (MPLA, FRELIMO, PAIGC)
