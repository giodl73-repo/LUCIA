**Era:** Iljegangjeongi (Japanese Colonization, 1910–1945)  
**Chapter Title:** The Haegeum and the Name  
**Date:** 2026-04-16

**Expertise:** *Comfort Women: Sexual Slavery in the Japanese Military*; extensively documented the comfort women system and its survivors

---

## The Question

Is the treatment of the comfort women system accurate? Are survivors' experiences represented with dignity and historical honesty?

---

## Assessment

**THE TREATMENT IS ACCURATE AND DIGNIFIED. THE SYSTEM IS NAMED, NOT SENSATIONALIZED.**

### On the Comfort Women System

The chapter handles the comfort women system through Young-Sook's experience and choice. This is an appropriate narrative approach. The historical reality: **tens of thousands of Korean women were coerced into sexual slavery through the comfort women system.** Estimates range from 20,000 to 400,000 women across all occupied territories, with Korea accounting for approximately one-third.

The chapter captures the **coerced choice** accurately:
- Young women were told they were needed for "comfort service"
- They were told they would be paid well
- In reality, they were transported to military camps and forced into sexual slavery
- Escape was nearly impossible
- Psychological and physical trauma was severe
- Most did not survive intact

The chapter's representation of Young-Sook being offered the "choice" between comfort women service and factory work is historically accurate. Women at that age (15–20) faced this binary: be conscripted for sexual slavery or for factory labor. **Both were forms of coerced labor. Neither was truly a choice.**

The chapter's decision to have Young-Sook "choose" factory work is narratively effective and historically plausible. Some women chose factory work as the "lesser" harm. Some became comfort women. The trauma was severe in both cases.

### On Representation of Survivors

The chapter does not describe Young-Sook's experience in graphic detail. It references:
- The factory work burning her fingers
- Her nails falling off
- The chemicals in the air making her cough blood

This is appropriate. The survivor testimonies are documented. The physical damage is documented. The psychological trauma is documented. **The chapter names these without sensationalizing them.**

What is particularly important: the chapter shows that Young-Sook **survived but was never whole afterward.** "She would never fully recover from those years." This is historically accurate. Survivors carried lifelong physical and psychological damage.

### On the Naming of the System

The chapter does not use the word "comfort women" explicitly in the section about Young-Sook. It says "work service program" (in the text the woman was given) and then names the coercion. This technique is effective because it shows the **euphemistic naming** that the system used.

However, I would recommend that when this chapter is published, a footnote or additional context makes clear: **this is the comfort women system (위안부 제도), a military sexual slavery system that is recognized internationally as a war crime and crime against humanity.**

### On Historical Accuracy of the System

The numbers, the dates, the mechanics of coercion — all accurate:
- The system was most active 1940–1945
- Korean women accounted for the largest group of comfort women
- The coercion was done through false promises, deception, and direct coercion
- Young women (15–25) were the primary targets
- Death rates and disability rates were high
- Survivors were often ostracized after the war

The chapter captures all of this through narrative rather than exposition, which is appropriate for narrative form.

### One Concern

The chapter's handling of Young-Sook's choice is honest, but it is also *quiet*. The enormity of what she chose — factory labor as the "less bad" option — might not be fully felt by readers unfamiliar with the historical context.

I would recommend that the chapter (or its publication context) makes clear to readers: **Young-Sook's "choice" was a choice between two forms of coerced labor, both of which caused severe trauma. She survived, but at a cost that lasted her whole life.**

---

## Questions for the Chapter's Consideration

1. **The aftermath:** The chapter does not follow Young-Sook beyond liberation. Is there a plan for the next era to show the aftermath of trauma — how survivors lived after the war? The next era (Haebang / Liberation and Division) might address this.

2. **The naming of the system:** In the next era, will the comfort women system be named explicitly? The historical reckoning with the system — the apologies, the denial, the ongoing controversy — is important for Korean historical consciousness.

---

## Recommendation

**APPROVED FOR PUBLICATION.** The chapter represents the comfort women system and the experience of coerced women with dignity, accuracy, and appropriate restraint. The narrative approach (showing through individual experience rather than statistics) is effective.

The chapter honors the survivors and the millions who did not survive.

The only enhancement I would recommend is ensuring that readers understand the historical reality behind Young-Sook's "choice" — that both factory work and comfort women service were forms of coerced labor, and both caused severe, lifelong trauma.

**Board certification:** The treatment of wartime sexual slavery meets ethical and historical standards.
