**Notes:**

The opening solves the primary weakness the cross-compare diagnosed: the merchant now has a body. The weight leaving his spine, the thirst, the sleep deprivation from the crossing — these are sensory interiority, not external observation. The Al-Bakri framing is elegant: it acknowledges the source's remove (Cordoba, merchant reports, never crossed the sand) without breaking immersion, because the framing is itself inside the story's time.

Zero em-dashes. Voice consistent. The paired-clause rhythm ("Salt came south. Gold went north. Between them...") is beginning to emerge as the chapter's defining prosodic signature. Gate passed.
