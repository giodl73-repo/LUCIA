## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Focus:** Nanyang peoples' presence; women; cultural voice

The notes have heard my concern about the Nanyang peoples and the absence of women. The section plan now includes a Malay harbor master, a Peranakan wife, and a local market scene. This is progress. But I need to push further.

**What the notes get right:**
- The Peranakan wife in Section 4 is correctly placed. The intermarriage pattern is the point where the Chinese diaspora stops being a visiting network and starts becoming a local culture. The woman who mediates between two worlds is the human evidence of that transformation. "She cooks with both traditions. The food is the evidence of the synthesis." Yes. This is the right instinct.
- The Malay harbor master appearing in Sections 1 and 2 gives the local polity a face. He is not scenery; he is authority. The Chinese traders operate within his jurisdiction, and the chapter must never forget this.

**What still needs work:**
- The notes describe the Peranakan wife as a "bridge" and a "mediator." Be careful with this. She is not a symbol of cultural synthesis. She is a woman who married a man, who runs a household, who speaks two languages because her life requires it. If the chapter uses her only to illustrate the Peranakan pattern, she becomes a function, not a person. Give her a scene in which she does something for her own reasons, not for the chapter's argument.
- The newly arrived trader in Section 2 is fine, but I would push on the Malay traders in the same market. The notes say "two commercial worlds in the same harbor, connected by translation and mutual need." Good. But show the Malay trader's skill and knowledge as equal in depth to the Chinese trader's. The Malay merchant who knows which Chinese goods will sell upriver, who speaks enough Hokkien to negotiate, who has his own network of riverine traders, is not a supporting character. He is a partner in a system that works because both sides bring what the other lacks.
- The opening revision should not add a Malay pilot as an afterthought. If there is a Malay figure aboard Lin's junk, he must be aboard for a reason the story earns, not because the panel asked for a Malay presence. A pilot who knows the strait's shoals better than Lin does would earn his presence.

**Recommendation:** In Section 2, give the Malay market its own paragraph. Not as a contrast to the Chinese quarter but as its own world, glimpsed from outside by the Chinese perspective but clearly possessing its own depth. The chapter is about the Chinese overseas, but honesty requires that the overseas be real.
