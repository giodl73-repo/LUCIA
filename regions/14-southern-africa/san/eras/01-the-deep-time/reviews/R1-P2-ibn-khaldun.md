## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of San, The Deep Time (Opening)

**Overall:** The opening is vivid and immersive, but I want to see the structural logic beneath the practice. Why does the trance dance work as a social institution? The opening shows what it is. The chapter must show why it persists.

**What works:**
- The insight that the circle "is the instrument" (paragraph 5). This is not just a beautiful sentence — it is a structural claim about how the trance dance functions as a social technology. The healing requires the community; the community is constituted by the healing. This is the kind of self-reinforcing mechanism I look for in any enduring institution.
- The honest uncertainty passage. "Between those two numbers is a silence the archaeological record cannot fill." This is epistemologically rigorous and narratively powerful. The chapter must maintain this standard throughout — no false certainty about deep time.

**What doesn't:**
- The opening presents the trance dance as spiritual practice and medicine but does not yet address its social function. In a community without hierarchy, without coercive authority, the trance dance is one of the mechanisms that holds the group together — it redistributes social capital (the healer serves, does not rule), it creates shared experience, it reinforces the group's cosmological framework. These structural dimensions need to emerge in the chapter.
- The eland's significance is stated ("the animal closest to the spirit world") but the ecological and economic logic is not yet visible. The eland is sacred because it is also the most important food animal, the most difficult to hunt, and the one whose fat is used in medicine, paint, and ceremony. The sacred and the practical are not separate categories. The chapter must show this integration.

**The question I'd push on:**
What is the structural explanation for sixty-five thousand years of continuity? Other societies fragment, centralize, collapse, reform. The San persisted as small, mobile, egalitarian communities across an enormous time span. What are the mechanisms — ecological, social, cognitive — that made this possible? The chapter must address this, and it must do so without implying that continuity means stasis.
