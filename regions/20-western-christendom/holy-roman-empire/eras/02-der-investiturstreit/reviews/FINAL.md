## Innovations Logged

**Innovation flagged for INNOVATIONS.md:**

**Bells-as-Exclusion (Immersion/Texture):** Rendering institutional exclusion through the auditory experience of what the excluded party can hear but cannot enter. In Section III, the canonical hours bells ring in the Canossa chapel, audible in the outer courtyard where the excommunicate king stands — the prayer rising inside the walls that he is barred from joining is the sound of his exclusion made specific and physical. This technique extends the sensory-analytical fusion principle: the sensory detail does not merely ground the scene but renders the institutional condition (excommunication) as a bodily experience. Distinct from existing techniques because it uses auditory presence to establish institutional absence.
