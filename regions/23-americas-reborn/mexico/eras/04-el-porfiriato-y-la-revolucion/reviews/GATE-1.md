## Notes

**Voice discovery:** The corrido-narrative register is a genuine discovery candidate. Mexico's corrido tradition — narrative ballads circulating news of revolutionary figures, battles, and betrayals — has no prior counterpart in the Voice Spectrum. The prose rhythm is: declarative statement, accumulation of named evidence, period as cadence-break, refrain-weight at the close of each paragraph. Analytically identical in function to Griot-Cadence but texturally distinct — where the griot performs with first-person authority, the corrido reports with third-person facticity, building to the refrain that is everyone's knowledge ("Zapata never surrendered"). The register carries both narrative and analytical weight in the same voice. Recommend logging as innovation candidate after chapter-length validation.

**Structural engine visible in opening:** The "unfulfilled covenant" engine — the Constitution of 1917 as a promise that has not been kept — is planted in the final paragraph. The engine is the gap between constitutional text and lived reality, and the terminal condition is the one Zapata's assassination could not close: the villages still waiting. This is a "Premise non-recognition" variant terminal condition (the state acknowledges the covenant in writing but does not honor it in practice), related to v1.6 Type 4 but distinct in that the non-recognition is domestic rather than colonial.

**Innovations flagged:**
1. Corrido-narrative voice register — candidate for Voice section (Innovation Log)
2. "Domestic covenant gap" as terminal condition — potential extension of v1.6 framework
