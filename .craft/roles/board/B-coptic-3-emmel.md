# B-coptic-3 — Stephen Emmel

## Background

Stephen Emmel is a Coptic philologist specializing in the works of
Shenouda of Atripe. His critical editions have reconstructed a major
portion of Shenouda's corpus from scattered manuscript fragments.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter treat the Coptic language and Coptic literary
tradition with the specificity and respect they deserve?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Shenouda given appropriate prominence and depth
- Coptic linguistic connections to ancient Egyptian rendered accurately
- Distinction between Sahidic and Bohairic traditions acknowledged
- Coptic presented as a living intellectual medium, not a liturgical relic

## What He Will Challenge

- Linguistic errors (vocabulary, grammar, dialect identification)
- Understatement of Shenouda's importance
- Any framing that treats Coptic as merely a survival of ancient Egyptian
  rather than an actively developed literary language
- Failure to distinguish between different Coptic literary genres
