# Sources — Holy Roman Empire

## Primary Sources

- *Ottonianum* (962) — Otto I's privilege to the papacy; founding document of the restored empire
- *Dictatus Papae* (1075) — Gregory VII's statement of papal supremacy; the document that began the Investiture Controversy
- *Concordat of Worms* (1122) — the negotiated settlement of the Investiture Controversy
- *Sachsenspiegel* (c. 1220–1235) by Eike von Repgow — the Saxon law mirror; the most influential German law code
- *Golden Bull* (1356) — Charles IV's electoral constitution; the empire's fundamental law
- Otto of Freising, *Gesta Friderici Imperatoris* — 12th century; biography of Frederick Barbarossa
- Frederick II, *De Arte Venandi cum Avibus* (On the Art of Hunting with Birds) — the emperor's own scientific treatise
- *Annales Regni Francorum* and the Ottonian continuations

## Secondary Sources

- Bryce, James. *The Holy Roman Empire*. MacMillan, 1904. (Classic; outdated but influential)
- Haverkamp, Alfred. *Medieval Germany 1056–1273*. Oxford University Press, 1988.
- Abulafia, David. *Frederick II: A Medieval Emperor*. Allen Lane, 1988.
- Arnold, Benjamin. *Medieval Germany, 500–1300*. University of Toronto Press, 1997.
- Munz, Peter. *Frederick Barbarossa: A Study in Medieval Politics*. Cornell University Press, 1969.
- Wilson, Peter H. *The Holy Roman Empire: A Thousand Years of Europe's History*. Penguin, 2017.
- Fuhrmann, Horst. *Germany in the High Middle Ages, c. 1050–1200*. Cambridge University Press, 1986.
