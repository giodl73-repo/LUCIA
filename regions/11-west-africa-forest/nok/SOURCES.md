# Sources — Nok

## Primary / Archaeological

- Fagg, Bernard — Nok figures discovered in Jos tin mines, 1943–1944; first published descriptions
- Taruga excavation reports — Fagg, Sutton, Shaw; iron smelting furnace complexes
- Samun Dukiya excavation — Shaw; terracotta and iron together
- Jos Museum collection — the principal institutional repository for Nok materials

## Academic

- Fagg, Bernard. *Nok Terracottas*. 1977. Ethnographica / National Museum Lagos.
- Shaw, Thurstan. "Those Igbo-Ukwu Radiocarbon Dates: Facts, Fictions and Probabilities." *Journal of African History* 16:4 (1975).
- Breunig, Peter & Neumann, Katharina. "From Savanna Hunters to Early Farmers: West Africa Before and After 2000 BP." In *African Archaeological Review*. 2002.
- Breunig, Peter (ed.). *Nok: African Sculpture in Archaeological Context*. 2014. Africa Magna Verlag. — most comprehensive recent study; results of the Frankfurt University excavations
- Willett, Frank. *African Art*. 3rd ed. 2002. Thames & Hudson. — Nok in context of the broader West African artistic tradition

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Full results of Frankfurt University Nok Project (Breunig et al., ongoing)
- Radiocarbon date series for the complete Nok period (revised chronology)
- Studies on the spatial distribution of Nok sites and what it implies about political organization
- Scholarship on the Nok-Ife artistic connection (direct vs. parallel development debate)
