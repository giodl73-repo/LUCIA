### Finding 1 — Modern Intrusion in Final Section

**Location:** Final section "The Archive That Endured All of Them," paragraph beginning "The mound at Susa accumulated..."

**Offending passage:** "It was excavated by French archaeologists in the nineteenth century CE, the tablets carried to Paris, housed in the Louvre, photographed and catalogued and subjected to the sustained attention of a scholarly tradition that has not yet found the key."

**Problem:** This sentence introduces a modern observer (French archaeologists, the Louvre, a scholarly tradition) into the chapter's world. Until this moment, the chapter has been inside the Elamite world's own experience. The final section begins moving outward correctly — naming later empires that would come and go — but those empires are named as future events within the Elamite civilizational arc, not as a modern scholar's retrospective. The French archaeologists are outside the arc entirely. They are the modern world looking at the ancient one. This breaks the inside perspective.

**Recommended fix:** Cut the sentence. The paragraph that precedes it ("The archive at Susa that held the proto-Elamite tablets — in the dark and dry of its storeroom...") ends with "waiting for someone to read them" — which is the correct frame. The tablets are still waiting. We do not need to say where they are now or who has looked at them. The waiting is enough.

**Revised paragraph (final section):**

> The archive at Susa that held the proto-Elamite tablets — in the dark and dry of its storeroom, in the clay of a script that has not yet given up its words — was built on a mound that was already ancient when the first tablet was pressed. It was buried by later buildings when the city expanded. The tablets are still there, in the clay, waiting.

(Or keep the current text up to "photographed and catalogued" and simply end: "The tablets still wait." — either version recovers the voice.)
