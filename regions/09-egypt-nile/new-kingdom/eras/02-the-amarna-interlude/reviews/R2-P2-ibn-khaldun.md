## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (Panel-2)

**Focus:** Structural engine adequacy and economic dimension.

**Assessment:** The notes now carry a dual engine (chisel + isolation loop) and this is correct. The terminal condition is precisely stated: the sole mediator dies, the system has no fallback. This is strong.

**What needs attention:**

1. The section plan gives Section IV (The Letters They Did Not Answer) 700 words for the diplomatic dimension. This is thin. The Amarna Letters are one of the richest primary sources for international relations in the ancient Near East. Rib-Hadda alone wrote over 60 letters. The diplomatic collapse is not a subplot; it is one of the two consequences of the inward turn (the other being the domestic theological crisis). Consider expanding this section or weaving diplomatic evidence into Section V.

2. The economic dimension is identified as a pressure point but does not have its own section. Temple economics should be woven into Section II (the escalation) or Section V (the sole mediator). The Amun priesthood's control of land and labor is the material base of the theological conflict. Without it, the revolution looks purely ideological.

3. The terminal condition is clean for the Aten cult's collapse, but the notes should also name what survives. The chisel-loop (creation-destruction-re-creation) implies that the restoration is simply a reversal. But the restoration was also a transformation: the post-Amarna theology was different from the pre-Amarna theology. The experience of having the gods removed and returned changed the relationship between the state and the priesthood permanently. This belongs in the landing.

**Verdict:** The production bible is ready to write from, with the caveat that economic evidence must be more deeply integrated and the landing must acknowledge that the restoration was not a simple return.
