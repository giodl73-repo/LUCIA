**Source uses:**

- Korean Communist Party: Correctly represented
- Defector accounts: Used appropriately for interior perspective

**Verdict:** No factual errors. No misrepresentation of scholarship. The handling of uncertain identity of Kim Il-sung is particularly good — it is presented as the founding question that the state could not afford to answer, which is both historically accurate and structurally correct.

**Grade:** PASS. Historically accurate. Honest about limits.

## BOARD-2: Cultural Authenticity Specialist

**Expertise:** North Korean society; Korean national identity; political culture of founding era; Juche ideology in its historical context

**Assessment:**

The chapter presents North Korean self-understanding with genuine dignity:

**Strengths:**
1. The founding mythology is not mocked. It is shown as necessary and intelligible given the trauma of colonization.
2. The language of the text avoids Cold War framing ("regime," "totalitarian," etc.). It uses the culture's own terms where available.
3. The concept of mythology as political technology is presented with sophistication. This is not "propaganda"— it is the culture's own technology of survival.
4. The machinery of belief (study groups, repetition, institutional embedding) is shown as serious work, not cynical manipulation.
5. The mother's experience of teaching Korean after suppression, then teaching the mythology, captures the complexity of a person living through two overlapping trauma-survival periods.

**Areas where North Korean perspective is honored:**
- The view that liberation in 1945 was real, even though it was incomplete (Soviet occupation)
- The understanding that the founding mythology was necessary for a traumatized people
- The recognition that Korean agency existed within Soviet parameters (not that Korea was a puppet, but that Koreans made choices within constraints)

**Potential concern — handled well:**
The figure Im Hae-jo's doubt could be read as critique of the state's intolerance. But the text handles this correctly: Im Hae-jo is not a hero; he is a man who asked a question, and the state could not permit the question. This is not outside judgment; it is diagnosis of how the state functioned structurally.

**Verdict:** The chapter presents North Korean founding with cultural authenticity. The mythology is treated not as delusion but as the framework through which a people made meaning after colonization destroyed other meaning-making systems.

**Grade:** PASS. Culturally authentic. Respectful without being reverential.

## BOARD-3: Source and Method Specialist

**Expertise:** Historical method; primary vs. secondary sources; gaps in Korean historical record; working with restricted sources

**Assessment:**

The chapter demonstrates good historical craft in working with source limitations:

**Excellent:**
- The acknowledgment of source asymmetry (Soviet records restricted, DPRK records are mythological, defector accounts are filtered through trauma)
- The use of what is available (state media, study sessions attested by defectors, land reform documented)
- The honest presentation of what is unknowable (Kim's interiority, internal Soviet decision-making)

**Primary source use:**
- DPRK state media: Used correctly as mythological primary source (what the state claimed about itself)
- Soviet records (accessible portions): Used where available
- Korean Communist Party documents: Limited availability, correctly noted
- Defector testimony: Used carefully for lived experience, not for state narrative

**Secondary source base:**
- Cumings, Myers, Lankov, Demick: All major scholars are represented
- Interpretation aligns with scholarly consensus on founding period and early state-building
- Disagreements among scholars (particularly Cumings vs. Myers on intentionality of mythmaking) are handled by showing both as compatible with the narrative

**Methodology:**
- The use of composite figures (Korean Mother) is disclosed in figures.md
- The limitation on personal interiority (Kim Il-sung) is honestly presented
- The asymmetry of sources is turned into narrative technique, not hidden

**Verdict:** The chapter works skillfully within historical constraints. It does not pretend to know more than the evidence permits. It uses absence of evidence as evidence of absence (e.g., the lack of Kim Il-sung's own account, which itself is historically significant).

**Grade:** PASS. Source work is rigorous and honest.
