## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation 1 — Inscription-as-Structural-Complexity**
The Ka'ba-ye Zartosht section uses the primary inscription to hold two contradictory accounts (Shapur's tolerance and Kartir's suppression) in the same chapter without resolving them, treating the contradiction itself as the primary evidence of the founding's structural tension. This extends the v1.5 Source Asymmetry technique in a new direction: not "source gap as cultural honesty" but "source contradiction as structural argument." The stone preserves both stories; the chapter lets both stand. Candidate for INNOVATIONS.md log.

**Innovation 2 — Craftsman-at-Enemy-Work**
The Roman engineer building the Band-e Qaysar bridge is a figure at one remove from the Sassanid worldview — a Roman prisoner doing Roman work for a Sassanid king. The chapter uses him not to introduce an outside perspective but to demonstrate the Sassanid system's capacity to absorb and convert its enemies' competencies. The bridge is Roman engineering committed permanently to Sassanid imperial infrastructure. This extends the v1.4 "Archaeological Figures" amendment: a figure who does not share the culture's worldview but whose labor is entirely consumed by it. Candidate for INNOVATIONS.md log.
