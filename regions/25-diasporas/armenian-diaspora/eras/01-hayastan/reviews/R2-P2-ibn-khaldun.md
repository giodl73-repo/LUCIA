## Assessment of the Production Bible

The structural engine is now clearly named, and the terminal condition is specific: the fall of the Arsacid dynasty (428 CE) proves the engine. The alphabet was designed to outlast the kingdom. The kingdom falls. The alphabet endures. This is a clean, testable structural argument.

## What the Notes Addressed from My PANEL-1 Concerns

1. **The pre-alphabet gap** is now filled. Section II (The Highland Kingdom) covers Urartu, the Artaxiad dynasty, and Tigran. Section IV (Between Two Fires) covers the Arsacid partition. The 1,265 years before the alphabet are no longer backstory. They are structural context: the long vulnerability that made the alphabet necessary.

2. **The terminal condition** is explicit. Good. The fall of the Arsacid dynasty is not the engine's failure but its test. This is elegant: the terminal condition vindicates the engine rather than destroying it. This is a variant we have not seen before -- most terminal conditions (Songhai's legitimacy gap, Tibet's patron-system consuming the king) end the engine. Here, the terminal condition completes it. Worth flagging as a potential innovation.

3. **The 301 conversion** has structural explanation. The geopolitical logic (distinguishing from Zoroastrian Persia) is named. This is the causal depth I asked for.

## Remaining Concerns

1. **Section II needs a mechanism for Armenian persistence before the alphabet.** The notes list Urartu, Tigran, and the buffer-state position, but these are events, not a mechanism. What held Armenian identity together in the Artaxiad and early Arsacid periods? Was it the dynasty itself (asabiyyah of the royal house)? Was it the highland geography (defensive terrain creates persistence)? Was it the language (spoken Armenian as identity marker)? The chapter must propose an answer, even if the answer is "the highland geography created a natural fortress that sustained a people even when the dynasty changed hands."

2. **The source asymmetry for Tigran** is well-identified (Greek/Roman sources). But the notes should clarify what technique will be used. Will the chapter present the Roman account and then reveal the gap? Will it use twin-source narration? The technique should be named at the planning stage.

## Verdict

The production bible is ready. The structural argument is clear, the section plan is sound, and the panel concerns have been anticipated. Proceed to FIXES and WRITE.
