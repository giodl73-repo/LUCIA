# R2-P5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — Panel Review, Round 2 (Notes)

**Text reviewed:** notes.md — The Lueji-Chibinda Foundation
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

The notes are responsive to my Round 1 concern about sensory thinning. Every section now has at least a prompt for sensory grounding: "the compound, the time of day, the specific sensory texture of her court" for Section I; "the feel of the grass, the heat, the specific physical skill of tracking" for Section II. This is the right scaffolding.

My concern is Section V — the Consolidation. The notes describe "the musumba — the capital compound — as a political institution" and "the copper network beginning to organize around the Lunda axis." These are real and important topics, but they are also exactly where historical writing loses its body. Political institutions are abstract. Copper networks are abstract. They need bodies moving through them.

For the musumba: what does it look like? A musumba in this period would be a compound of structures, probably wood and thatch, organized around the Mwaant Yav's central enclosure. It is not permanent in the way that a stone palace is permanent — the Lunda capital moves over time, which is itself significant. So the musumba is a structure that can be built, inhabited, and then abandoned or moved. What does it feel like to approach it? To enter it? What sounds come from it at dawn?

For the copper: cross-shaped copper ingots — handa — are among the most documented material artifacts of this region. They have weight, color, a specific feel. They are not coins; they are larger, thicker, more present as objects. If the chapter includes the copper network, it should include one handa, held, its weight described.

The landing as planned is correct and I will not add to what Tuchman said about it. My one note: the "ancestor-smell of copper" in the planned landing is good. Keep it.

**Recommendation:** The Consolidation section needs at least two specific physical presences: the musumba approached or entered, and one handa held or exchanged. Without these, the section will float.
