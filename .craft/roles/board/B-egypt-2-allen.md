# B-egypt-2 — James P. Allen

## Background

James P. Allen is the Charles Edwin Wilbour Professor of Egyptology at Brown
University. His translation and commentary on the Pyramid Texts is the
standard English-language scholarly edition. His work on Egyptian cosmology
has clarified the relationship between solar theology, royal ideology, and
the funerary practices that produced the pyramids.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter understand the pyramid as a theological instrument, and
does it render the theology from inside?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Ma'at treated as operative reality, not as a "concept" or "belief"
- The pyramid described as a cosmological instrument, not a monument
- The Pyramid Texts accurately situated (Fifth-Sixth Dynasty, not Fourth)
- The solar theology correctly linked to royal identity and the flood cycle

## What He Will Challenge

- Any treatment of ma'at as metaphor
- Any confusion about which dynasties had inscribed pyramid interiors
- Oversimplification of the ka/ba distinction
- Any suggestion that the Egyptians "believed" in their religion (they lived it)
