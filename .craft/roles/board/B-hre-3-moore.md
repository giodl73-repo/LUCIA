## Background

R.I. Moore (b. 1941) is a British medieval historian and Emeritus Professor at Newcastle University. His *The First European Revolution, c. 970–1215* (Blackwell, 2000) argues that the period of the Investiture Controversy was not just a constitutional argument between popes and emperors but a social transformation of European society — a revolution in literacy, institutionalization, and the relationship between the literate class and the rest of the population. His earlier work *The Formation of a Persecuting Society* (1987) examines how the reform movement's drive for institutional purity created new categories of exclusion.
