## Assessment of Structural Engine

The engine is correctly identified: tumulus-as-political-statement, cattle-as-sovereignty. The self-escalating logic is sound: each ruler must exceed his predecessor's burial to demonstrate legitimate succession. The terminal condition (visibility as vulnerability) is adequate but could be sharper.

## Refinement Needed

**The terminal condition should be more specific.** "Visibility as vulnerability" is a general observation. The specific mechanism is: Kerma's power derived from controlling the trade chokepoint at the Third Cataract. As long as Egypt was fragmented (Second Intermediate Period), Kerma could exploit this position -- even raiding into Upper Egypt. But a reunified Egypt could concentrate military force sufficient to seize the chokepoint. The terminal condition is not visibility per se but **chokepoint dependency**: a kingdom whose wealth depends on controlling a single geographic bottleneck is fatally vulnerable to any power strong enough to take the bottleneck by force.

**The escalation logic needs a limit.** The self-escalating burial system (more cattle, more retainers, larger mound) requires an ever-growing surplus. Where does the surplus come from? Trade revenue and pastoral production. If either is disrupted, the escalation stalls and legitimacy weakens. The chapter should show this logic operating, not just state it.

## Section Plan Feedback

The six-section plan is sound. Section 5 (Egypt Watches) is the right structural move: use the Egyptian evidence as evidence about Egypt, not about Kerma. The Execration Texts are perfect for this. However, Section 6 (Landing) must resist the temptation to narrate the fall. The fall belongs to Era 2. This chapter's landing should be the kingdom at its height, with the shadow of what is coming visible only in the geographic logic that the reader already understands.
