# BOARD Review -- James R. Russell

**Chapter:** Hayastan -- The Ancient Kingdom
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Linguistic and Religious Accuracy

### The Alphabet
The phonological description is fundamentally correct. Armenian does distinguish between aspirated and unaspirated stops, and between voiceless, voiced, and ejective series. The chapter's claim that "each phoneme received its own character" is accurate for the basic system. The number 36 is correct for the original alphabet (38 with the later additions of "o" and "f," added in the 12th century).

**Minor note:** The chapter mentions "ejective k" in Section V. The ejective series is a real feature of Armenian phonology, but Mashtots likely did not conceptualize it in modern phonological terms. The chapter's description is accessible and not misleading, but the modern phonological framework should not be attributed to Mashtots's own understanding. The passage does not make this attribution explicitly, so no change is required.

### The Pre-Christian Religious World
The chapter mentions Aramazd, Anahit, and Vahagn as the pre-Christian deities. This is correct. The temple of Anahit at Erez is accurately named. The chapter does not go into depth about the pre-Christian religion, which is appropriate given the era's focus on the alphabet and the conversion. However, the transition from the old faith to the new is treated as cleaner than it was. The Armenian Church incorporated significant elements of the pre-Christian religious calendar, sacred sites, and even vocabulary. Etchmiadzin itself may have been a pre-Christian sacred site. This complexity is not necessary for the chapter's argument, but the absence should be noted.

### The First Words
The chapter cites the first words of the Armenian alphabet as "Chanaparh imastayn yev zkhrat" from Proverbs. This is the traditional attribution from Koriwn. The transliteration is serviceable for a non-Armenian reader. The translation "To know wisdom and instruction, to perceive the words of understanding" is accurate.

### Koriwn and the Hagiographic Tradition
The chapter correctly identifies Koriwn's biography as hagiographic and acknowledges the gap between the text and the historical figure. The figures.md section handles this with appropriate honesty. The claim that Mashtots "began with the sounds" is derived from Koriwn's account and is the tradition's own understanding of the method.

## Factual Corrections Required

None required. The linguistic and religious details are accurate within the chapter's scope.

## Notes for Accuracy

1. The "o" and "f" additions to the alphabet are attributed to the 12th century by most scholars. The chapter says "added later" without dating, which is acceptable.
2. The pre-Christian religious continuities into Christian Armenia are a significant scholarly topic. The chapter's silence on this is not an error but a deliberate scope choice.

## Endorsement

The chapter treats the alphabet with genuine technical care. The phonological detail meets the standard of showing the knowledge system at method level without overclaiming. The religious transition is handled with appropriate nuance. I endorse the chapter for publication.
