## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — PANEL-2 Review of Hammurabi's Babylon Notes

**Focus:** Cultural voice and the Amorite tension.

**The notes address my concern about the Amorite identity.** Section 2 (The Gate of the Gods) plans to develop how pastoral newcomers adopted the theological authority of the city they conquered. The framing is correct: they did not claim Marduk's authority; they submitted to it. Marduk absorbed his conquerors.

**One risk to watch in the writing:**
- The plan includes the Enuma Elish, the divine assembly, and Marduk's elevation. These are rich theological materials. The risk is that the chapter will present them as "myth" (a word the notes correctly forbid) or as political propaganda (a modern interpretation). The Enuma Elish was recited at the Akitu festival. It was performed. The recitation was itself the renewal of cosmic order. Present the epic as the Babylonians experienced it: not a story about the gods but a ritual act that maintained the world. The distinction matters.

**The naditu priestesses are a good choice.** Their legal documents show women exercising the rights the code describes. But be careful not to present them as "remarkably independent women" or any other framing that measures them against a modern expectation. They were priestesses of Shamash. Their celibacy and their commercial activity were aspects of their sacred role. The commerce served the god. Present it from inside.

**Recommendation:** The plan is sound. Write from within.
