# Panel Review — R1-P3 — Chinua Achebe
## The Network's Height | Kuikuro Era 2

---

This is Iauatá's story told from Iauatá's world. Not a story about Iauatá told from the outside. There is a difference, and this opening knows the difference.

What the opening does correctly: the road crew is "not ceremonial. There is no song for clearing roads, no ritual preparation. You wake up, you eat, you cut." This is the inside voice. An outsider writing about an indigenous people might have romanticized the road-clearing — given it a spiritual dimension, made it a ritual, turned it into the kind of thing that satisfies a reader's expectation of how indigenous life should look. This opening refuses that. The road is work. The road does not care about ceremony. That refusal is the opening's most important sentence.

The anetü is introduced correctly: not as a figure of romantic authority, but as someone whose authority has to be continuously earned through the giving of feasts. The complex mourning obligation — "a lineage connection complex enough that Iauatá has spent time thinking through exactly how he should mourn" — is given without explanation, because no explanation is needed. The complexity is simply there, as it would be for someone living inside it.

The final line: "not a word any of the people of the upper Xingu use, because they do not need to name what simply is." This is the opening's most sophisticated sentence and the one I trust most. It refuses the label the outside world would put on the network. The Kuikuro do not have a name for the network because the network is not a concept — it is a lived reality. The sentence is the inside voice at its most exact.

**The question I would push on:**

The chapter must not, at any point, become a demonstration of sophistication. The danger for chapters about peoples whose complexity is contested — and the upper Xingu network's complexity has historically been denied by outsiders — is that the text begins arguing with the denier. "Look how sophisticated this is!" That is an outsider's register. The chapter must simply be sophisticated without announcing that it is.

The opening threads this needle perfectly. The chapter must maintain it. Every time the temptation arises to pause and underscore the network's organizational achievement, the chapter must resist it. Let the Quarup operate. Let the anetü exchange gifts. Let the terra preta do what it does. The reader will understand.

— Achebe
