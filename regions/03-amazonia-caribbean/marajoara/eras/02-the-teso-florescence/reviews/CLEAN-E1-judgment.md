## Manual Pass — Implicit Judgment and Hidden Hierarchy

**Finding 1 — P3 (borderline)**
> "The burial urns of the Florescence period are the most complex material objects produced anywhere in the Amazon basin in this era."

The comparative ("most complex... anywhere in the Amazon basin") is factual and grounded in the archaeological record, but the word "complex" carries a latent hierarchy risk. The sentence intends to establish scale without judgment, but "complex" slides toward "advanced." Consider: "The burial urns of the Florescence period are among the largest and most densely decorated material objects produced on Marajó Island at any period, and among the most technically demanding ceramic works in the Amazon basin." This replaces the evaluative judgment with specific material description. Author decision: the current phrasing is defensible, but the revision is stronger.

**Finding 2 — P3 (borderline)**
> "...the most technically demanding ceramic works in the Amazon basin..."

Same category. "Technically demanding" is descriptive (it refers to the three-slip preparation, firing conditions, and coiling technique) rather than evaluative, but it still positions Marajoara ceramics relative to a regional baseline. Author should decide whether this framing serves the inside-voice logic or represents a subtle outside comparison.

**Finding 3 — CLEARED**
> "She is the chapter's center because the urns are the chapter's center, and the urns required her."

This line, in figures.md, is the author's meta-commentary — it is in the notes/figures register, not the narrative register, so it is not a violation. Cleared.

**Finding 4 — P2**
> "...the knowledge goes with it." (Section 3, specialist lineage passage)

The passage describing the disruption of the specialist lineage ends with a declarative that reads as authorial judgment about the value of what was lost. The sentence is not a verdict on the culture — it is a structural fact — but its placement after the description of the Contraction gives it a tonal weight that could be read as elegy. This is borderline: the author may intend the weight, and the weight is earned by the chapter's logic. No required fix. Author decision.
