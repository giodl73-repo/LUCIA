## Register Scan

**Finding 1 — P2 (must fix):**
Chapter, Section 3 (The Machine of Compact):
"The Crown of Aragon had been, since 1137, a federation produced by a marriage contract: Petronilla of Aragon and Ramón Berenguer IV of Barcelona, signed when Petronilla was an infant and Ramiro her monk-father was briefly recalled from his monastery to produce an heir before returning to his vows."

This sentence is accurate and necessary, but its register is slightly encyclopaedic — it reads as orientation for a new reader rather than as a continuation of the deliberate contractual voice. The baseline voice would render this as the Aragonese understood it, not as an outside summary. A small reconstruction: begin with what the Aragonese themselves understood the 1137 marriage contract to mean (the compact began there; a king who could be recalled from a monastery to produce an heir was already a king whose function was contractual) rather than with the neutral historical summary.

**Finding 2 — P2 (must fix):**
Chapter, Section 3: "Each tradition had its own assembly. The Aragonese *Corts* — the parliament of Aragon proper — and the Catalan *Corts* met separately, legislated separately, and taxed separately."

The parenthetical "the parliament of Aragon proper" is an explanatory gloss that breaks the voice. The baseline voice does not explain what things are — it shows them operating. "The Aragonese *Corts* met separately, legislated separately, and taxed separately" — without the parenthetical — maintains the register. The word "parliament" in particular carries an anachronistic resonance (it is the English term; the Aragonese term is *Corts*). Fix: remove the parenthetical gloss.

**Finding 3 — CLEAR:**
The Justicia passage ("The instrument that made the compact real was the *Justicia* of Aragon") maintains the register well. "The law moved first" is exactly the right construction for the compact-legal voice.

**Finding 4 — CLEAR:**
The island logic section (Section 4) shifts naturally from the constitutional voice to the commercial-maritime register — this is appropriate because the subject shifts from the *Corts* to the Catalan merchant world. The register shift is earned and recovers cleanly.

**Finding 5 — CLEAR:**
The landing section is perfect for the register. "The conditions changed. They always do." is the compact-legal voice's own ending: declarative, weighted, no qualification.
