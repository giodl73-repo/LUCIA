## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — Review of Notes (Round 2)

**Overall:** The notes correctly prioritize women's presence and Tongan terminology. Good. But I want to push harder on one point: the etak concept.

**What works:**
- Section IV (The Women Who Wove the Network) is correctly positioned as a full section, not a parenthetical. This is essential.
- The honest-limits section acknowledges that etak is documented primarily from Micronesian sources. This honesty is necessary.

**What needs attention:**
- If etak is Micronesian rather than specifically Tongan, the chapter should say so. Do not attribute a Micronesian concept to Tongans without disclosure. If there is a Tongan equivalent or a broader Polynesian framework, name it. If the concept is inferred, say it is inferred. The Achebe Test applies to intellectual honesty about knowledge systems as much as it applies to cultural voice. Attributing someone else's concept to this culture is a form of flattening.
- The landing section mentions Hau'ofa. Hau'ofa was Tongan-Fijian. His reframing of the Pacific is culturally appropriate here. But be careful not to let Hau'ofa's academic voice replace the chapter's inside voice. His insight ("we are the ocean") should arrive as the culture's own understanding, which Hau'ofa articulated, not as an academic thesis applied from outside.
