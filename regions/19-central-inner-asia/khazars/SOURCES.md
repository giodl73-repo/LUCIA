# Sources

## Primary Sources

- The Khazar Correspondence (Hasdai ibn Shaprut and King Joseph, c. 960 CE)
- Ibn Fadlan, *Risala* (921–922 CE) — account of the Volga Bulgars and observations on the Khazar world
- Arab geographers (al-Masudi, al-Istakhri, Ibn Hawqal) — descriptions of the Khazar state and Itil
- Constantine VII Porphyrogennetos, *De Administrando Imperio* — Byzantine diplomatic perspective on the Khazars

## Key Academic Works

- Peter Golden, *Khazar Studies* (1980) — foundational scholarly study
- Douglas Dunlop, *The History of the Jewish Khazars* (1954)
- Kevin Brook, *The Jews of Khazaria* (3rd ed., 2018)
- Roman Kovalev — Khazar archaeology and the Volga trade
- Thomas Noonan — the silver dirham economy of the early medieval north

## Needed

- Archaeological identification of Itil's location (the site has not been definitively found)
- Studies on the extent of Jewish practice beyond the ruling class
- Analysis of the Khazar role in transmitting Islamic commercial practices to northern Europe
