**Innovations to flag:**

1. **Source-asymmetry inversion as terminal structure** (Evidence/Progression): The chapter uses the victim's surviving voice (Lament for the Destruction of Ur) against the conqueror's permanent silence as a structural device — not just as an acknowledgment of the source situation but as the actual terminal condition of the figure section. Kindattu's characterization is built from what the Sumerians said about him, not from what he said about himself, because that doesn't exist. This extends the v1.5 "source gap as cultural honesty" technique into a structural figure principle: the figure is characterized by the gap itself. Log in INNOVATIONS.md.

2. **Multi-script undecipherment as engine** (Progression/Voice): A civilization with three distinct writing systems (two undeciphered) uses the silence of its own scripts as the chapter's structural backbone. Not one gap but two layered gaps (proto-Elamite, Linear Elamite), each serving a different purpose in the narrative arc. The engine is not "we can't read this" but "the civilization produced two distinct solutions to the writing problem, and both solutions are still silent in different ways." This is a new application of the material-object engine: the script-as-object, where the object's properties (readability vs. silence) determine the narrative's logic. Log in INNOVATIONS.md.
