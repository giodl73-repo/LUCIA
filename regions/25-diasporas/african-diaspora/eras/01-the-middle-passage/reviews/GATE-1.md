# GATE-1 — The Middle Passage (Opening)

**Chapter:** African Diaspora, Era 1 — The Trade: The Middle Passage
**Scorer:** SCRIBE/Gate
**Date:** 2026-04-16

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with a specific man on a specific beach (Ouidah, 1727), bought from a specific ship (*São João Baptista*), for a specific price (forty iron bars). The sensory detail is immediate: oak gall ink, the hold's dimensions, the shackle against skin. This is the "drop into cold water" the rubric describes. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Yoruba cosmological framework is not explained but inhabited. Shango is voice, not weather. The bata drums bring Shango into the dancer's body. The Ifa system maps fate in binary figures. The dead gather in compound soil. The reader is inside the worldview without being taught it. The cosmology's relationship to the Middle Passage (Olokun's domain, no precedent in the tradition) emerges from the culture's own categories. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | Zero outside judgment. The violence is present (the hold's dimensions, the shackles, the dysentery) but never spectacularized. The captives are not pitied; they are carrying civilizations. The traders' blindness ("purchasing labor / purchasing a library") is stated from inside, not editorialized. The Achebe Test: a descendant of the Middle Passage would recognize their ancestors' dignity here. No sentimentality, no voyeurism. |
| Texture   | 5/5   | Oak gall and vinegar ink. Seven hundred square feet. Plank space the width of a coffin. Iron against skin, skin against wood, wood against the Atlantic. The smell of flux. The bata drum rhythm called alujá. Palm nuts and cowrie shells. The texture is inseparable from the argument: what the body carried could not be manifested or shackled. |
| Voice     | 4/5   | The call-and-response structure is present and earned (the singing in the hold, the "And yet" repetition). The rhythm alternates between the ledger's cold precision and the warmth of what the ledger could not record. Occasional sentences run slightly long for the register. The voice is close to fully discovered but could sharpen further in the chapter. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | "He had a name. The ship's log would not record it." The opening line creates a pull that the entire opening sustains. The singing in the hold answers it. The final line ("the rhythm he carried crossed the water, and it is still playing") opens forward without closing. The reader needs to know what happened next. |

**Total: 29/30**

**Notes:** This is an exceptional opening. The decision to make the structural argument (what survives the crossing is what is carried in the body) emerge from one man's specific knowledge system is the right choice. The contrast between the ledger and the library is the chapter's engine announced in the first paragraphs. The singing in the hold is the proof. No forbidden words. No outside judgment. The violence is present but never the point; the survival is the point. The voice needs slight refinement in the chapter to fully sustain the call-and-response register across 5000 words. GATE-1 passed.
