# PANEL-2 — Chinua Achebe (targeted)
## The First Empire: Notes Review

The production bible addresses my main concern: Tacky's War appears in Section 4, and the Fante merchants at Anomabo appear in Section 2. Both are necessary. Both are correctly framed — not as parallel protagonists but as actors with their own knowledge inside the system the chapter is analyzing.

The engine is right: the dual-ledger system generating its own abolitionist critique. The terminal condition is right: legitimacy gap via the American Revolution. My remaining concern is the forty-six from the *Dobson*. The notes flag this as an open question — can we find a name in the Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade Database? I would look. If a name can be found, use it. If not, the notes propose to acknowledge the gap honestly, which is the correct approach under v1.2's Honesty amendment.

The Equiano passage must give him an action. Not just the *Narrative* as document. Equiano physically traveling to Liverpool in 1792, speaking at public meetings, addressing the men who had funded the voyages — that is the second ledger speaking directly into the first ledger's room. Check whether Equiano visited Liverpool; the Carretta biography should have the itinerary.

The Achebe Test for this chapter is not about sympathy for the enslaved (the chapter handles that through Equiano and Tacky's War). The Achebe Test is about the British themselves: could a thoughtful British person — not a contemporary apologist but a serious British historian in 1800 — read this chapter and feel their world was rendered accurately, from inside, without either whitewashing or anachronistic condemnation? The notes have this right. The chapter needs to hold that tone for 5,000 words without slipping.
