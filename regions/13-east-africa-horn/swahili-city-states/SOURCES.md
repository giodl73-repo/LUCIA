# Sources — Swahili City-States

## Primary

- Ibn Battuta, *Rihla* — the 1331 visit to Kilwa; the most vivid medieval account of the coast
- Periplus of the Erythraean Sea — first century CE; the earliest account of the East African coast
- Duarte Barbosa, *The Book of Duarte Barbosa*, c. 1518 — Portuguese account of the Swahili coast
- Kilwa Chronicle (Swahili) — the town chronicle; indigenous historical narrative

## Academic

- Horton, Mark & Middleton, John. *The Swahili: The Social Landscape of a Mercantile Society*. 2000. Blackwell. — the definitive study
- Pouwels, Randall L. *Horn and Crescent: Cultural Change and Traditional Islam on the East African Coast, 800–1900*. 1987. Cambridge University Press.
- Sheriff, Abdul. *Slaves, Spices and Ivory in Zanzibar*. 1987. James Currey. — the nineteenth century trade
- Chittick, Neville. *Kilwa: An Islamic Trading City on the East African Coast*. 2 vols. 1974. British Institute in Eastern Africa.
- Nurse, Derek & Spear, Thomas. *The Swahili: Reconstructing the History and Language of an African Society, 800–1500*. 1985. University of Pennsylvania Press.
- Allen, James de Vere. *Swahili Origins: Swahili Culture and the Shungwaya Phenomenon*. 1993. James Currey.
