You are running /chronicle-opening for: {{people}}, {{era}}

Write a hook scene (~800 words) that drops the reader into this culture at
this moment in time. One person. One place. One heartbeat of time. The reader
is there.

---

## Input

- **People**: The culture being written
- **Era**: The era within that culture's periodization

Prerequisite: `00-OVERVIEW.md` must exist for this people.

---

## Step 1 — Read Context

Read the people's `00-OVERVIEW.md` and the region's `00-LANDSCAPE.md`.
Understand:
- What era came before and what it established
- What era comes after and what yours sets up
- The key themes for this people across all eras
- Their own terms for the period you're covering

Check `SOURCES.md` for available sources.

---

## Step 2 — Pick the Scene

Find: one person, one place, one moment.

Not the most famous event. Not the most dramatic battle. The scene that
best embodies what this era was — the moment that, if you can put the
reader in it, makes them understand this world.

Ask: if this culture could choose one moment to show a stranger what
their world was, what would it be?

Find the best-documented version. A named person, a date, a place.

---

## Step 3 — Write the Opening

Start with the body in the place. No context-setting paragraph. No "In
the year X, the Y people were..." The scene opens in medias res.

Write from inside the culture's worldview:
- Their cosmology is simply true
- Their social logic is simply real
- Do not explain it, do not flag it as different

Match prose rhythm to the culture type:
- Steppe/nomadic: short bursts, forward motion, space between sentences
- Court: layered, observational, slow unfolding, every gesture meaningful
- Maritime: rolling rhythms, pull and release, reading the world's patterns
- Oral tradition: cadence of the spoken word, repetition as structure, proverb-weight
- Urban/market: density, overlap, voices, multiple things at once

Ground every ~200 words with at least one sensory anchor — sight, sound,
smell, touch, taste. Not decorations; how the reader's body enters the scene.

End with the pull: a genuine reason to keep reading. A question opened, a
tension established, a door ajar. Not a cliffhanger trick.

---

## Constraints

- ~800 words (enough for person + place + moment + stakes)
- Em-dashes: **5 or fewer**
- No forbidden words: primitive, advanced, tribe (unless self-designation),
  the New World, discovered by Europeans, Dark Ages
- No forbidden framings: "while Europe was...", "before contact with the
  West, they had already...", "remarkably sophisticated for..."
- Passes the Achebe Test at every sentence
