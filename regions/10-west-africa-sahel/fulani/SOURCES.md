# Sources — Fulani / Fulɓe

## Primary

- Usman dan Fodio, *Ihya al-Sunna wa-Ikhmad al-Bid'a* — the theological foundation of the jihad
- Dan Fodio, *Kitab al-Farq* — the distinction between Islamic and un-Islamic governance practices
- Muhammad Bello, *Infaq al-Maysur* — history of the Sokoto jihad by dan Fodio's son
- Abdullahi dan Fodio, *Tazyin al-Waraqat* — the brother's account of the movement

## Academic

- Last, Murray. *The Sokoto Caliphate*. 1967. Longman. — the foundational study
- Hiskett, Mervyn. *The Sword of Truth: The Life and Times of Shehu Usuman Dan Fodio*. 1973. Oxford University Press.
- Stenning, Derrick J. *Savannah Nomads: A Study of the Wodaabe Pastoral Fulani*. 1959. Oxford University Press. — pastoral Fulani economy
- Burnham, Philip. *Opportunity and Constraint in a Savanna Society: The Gbaya of Meiganga, Cameroon*. 1980. — Fulani expansion context
- Lovejoy, Paul E. *Transformations in Slavery: A History of Slavery in Africa*. 3rd ed. 2012. Cambridge University Press. — the Sokoto Caliphate's slave economy
- Robinson, David. *The Holy War of Umar Tal*. 1985. Oxford University Press. — the western Fulani jihad movements
