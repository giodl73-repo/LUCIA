## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of The Great Expansion (Opening)

**Overall:** The opening demonstrates a knowledge system with genuine intellectual depth, but I am waiting for the structural mechanism that drives this era's arc. The navigation is the technology. What is the political logic that makes the network expand?

**What works:**
- The moving-island reference frame ("the canoe was still, the islands moved") is presented not as a curiosity but as a navigational paradigm. This is the correct treatment. It is a different set of axioms, not a lesser version of European navigation. The opening treats the epistemology with the seriousness it demands.
- The connection between navigation and political network is implicit in the cargo description. Fine mats as biography, tabua as currency of prestige, exchange as obligation. The reader begins to see that the kalia is not a trade ship. It is a diplomatic instrument. This is strong.
- The songs-as-charts insight is important. An oral encoding system that carries navigational data across generations is an institutional achievement, and the opening correctly presents it as such.

**What doesn't:**
- I do not yet see the structural engine. WHAT drives the expansion? The opening shows HOW Tongans navigate. It does not yet show WHY the network expands. Is it the prestige logic of the Tu'i Tonga? Is it the exchange system's own internal momentum? What force compels the kalia to sail, and what force would make it stop? Without this, the chapter risks becoming a catalog of navigational techniques rather than a story with causation.
- The Tu'i Tonga appears only as a title attached to Tongatapu. The sacred chieftainship's relationship to the network needs to become visible. The mana of the Tu'i Tonga presumably radiates outward through these exchange voyages. That is the political mechanism. I want to see it.

**The question I'd push on:**
What is the structural engine? The navigation knowledge system is the technology, not the plot. The Kurukan Fuga's redistribution logic drove the Mali arc. What is the Tongan equivalent? I suspect it is the prestige-exchange system: that connection to Tongatapu confers legitimacy, and legitimacy is the currency, and the expansion is driven by the demand for legitimacy across the network. If so, the chapter must make this the engine, not the navigation itself.
