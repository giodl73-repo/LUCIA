## B-san-1 — David Lewis-Williams — Board Review of San, The Deep Time

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** San rock art interpretation through the neuropsychological model; the trance hypothesis; Drakensberg and Western Cape rock art sites; the relationship between ethnographic testimony about trance experience and the painted imagery.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The description of the trance dance and |kia is accurate. The n/um rising up the spine, the community's role in managing the trance, the "dying" posture, nasal bleeding — all consistent with the ethnographic record (Marshall, Katz, Biesele).
- The therianthropes are correctly identified as trance-transformation imagery, not as "animal spirits" or "totems."
- The claim of "twenty-seven thousand years" for the oldest rock art is within the range of published dates for the Apollo 11 Cave in Namibia (c. 25,000–27,000 BP), though this should ideally be noted as the oldest dated panels rather than a precise start date for the entire tradition.
- The connection between eland fat as paint binder and eland as the most sacred animal is correctly drawn. The material and the spiritual are genuinely integrated in the paint itself.
- The palimpsest description of rock panels (multiple generations painting on the same surface) is accurate and reflects current understanding.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The chapter treats rock art as theology recorded on stone, not as decoration or narrative illustration. This is correct.
- The entoptic patterns (now described as "patterns that dancers see in the first stage of trance") are handled appropriately — the visual phenomena are real and are the correct interpretation of the geometric imagery.
- The chapter correctly avoids treating the rock art as a "picture book of San life" — the older, now-discredited interpretation.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The integration of trance, rock art, and the eland into a single knowledge system. This is the core insight of the trance hypothesis, and the chapter renders it clearly.
- The honest uncertainty about dating. "The paintings begin at twenty-seven thousand" is more honest than a false precision claim.
- The rock shelter sensory description. You can feel the space. This matters — the shelters are not museums, they are living spiritual sites.

**What needs correction:**
- The chapter should note that not all scholars accept the trance hypothesis as the sole or complete explanation for rock art. Some panels may have functions beyond trance recording. The chapter need not give equal space to competing theories, but a sentence acknowledging scholarly debate would strengthen the honest-uncertainty principle.
- "The oldest Tsodilo paintings may date to twenty-seven thousand years ago" — the Tsodilo dating is less secure than the Apollo 11 dates. Consider specifying that the oldest securely dated southern African rock art comes from Apollo 11 Cave in Namibia, with Tsodilo and Drakensberg dates being less precisely established.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (two minor corrections above)
