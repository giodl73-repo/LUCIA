# Sources — Micronesian Peoples

## Key Academic Works
- David Lewis, *We, the Navigators: The Ancient Art of Landfinding in the Pacific* (1972, University of Hawaii Press) — the foundational study of traditional Pacific navigation; essential
- Thomas Gladwin, *East Is a Big Bird: Navigation and Logic on Puluwat Atoll* (1970, Harvard) — the definitive study of the etak system; includes extended direct observation
- Ben Finney, *Voyage of Rediscovery: A Cultural Odyssey through Polynesia* (1994) — the Hokule'a voyages and the navigation revival
- Nainoa Thompson — contemporary master navigator; multiple interviews and documented voyages
- Patrick Kirch, *On the Road of the Winds* (2000) — Micronesian settlement history
- Glenn Petersen — Pohnpei and the Saudeleur dynasty; Nan Madol

## Primary Sources
- Carolinian navigation apprenticeship oral traditions (held by pelu families; documented by Lewis and Gladwin)
- Spanish colonial records (Mariana Islands colonization) — Archivo General de Indias, Seville
- German colonial records — Bundesarchiv Koblenz
- Japanese mandate records — National Archives of Japan

## Archaeological Sources
- Nan Madol, Pohnpei — ongoing excavation; dating c. 1100–1628 CE
- Latte stone sites (Marianas) — monumental stone pillar structures; Chamorro culture
- Micronesian atoll settlement sequences
