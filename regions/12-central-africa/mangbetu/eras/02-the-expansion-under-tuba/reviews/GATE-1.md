## Notes

The opening achieves something structurally unusual: the hook scene foregrounds an aesthetic practice (the coiffure-building, the elongated head) that a Western-trained reader might reflexively treat as the "exotic element" — but the text never treats it as such. Nasakara's form is a crown. The coiffure is built the way a crown is polished: deliberately, expertly, by someone who learned from watching her mother. The head-binding is introduced through its outcome (the long oval skull) and then explained backwards through a process (the infant, the cloth, the months) that naturalizes it entirely within Mangbetu logic.

The musician does structural work beyond atmosphere: the note that music is "as structural as the long avenue of palm trees... as structural as the placement of the Ne's own seat" articulates the court's total-artwork principle without naming it or explaining it. The reader is allowed to see it.

One potential tension: Schweinfurth is mentioned in passing as "who would arrive two decades later" — this is a forward-reference that names an outside perspective. The treatment is careful (the mention is bracketed and does not shift the text's center of gravity) but the Panel will likely note it. It earns its place because it quietly marks the court's temporal horizon without lingering on the European gaze.
