# B-Kalinago-3 — Douglas Taylor

Douglas Taylor (1902–1975) was the principal linguist of Island Carib/Kalinago
and Garifuna. His *Languages of the West Indies* (1977) remains the authoritative
analysis of the Kalinago language as recorded by Breton and as preserved in
Garifuna. Critically for this chapter, Taylor analyzed the gender-differentiated
nature of Kalinago speech — men and women used different vocabulary for some
concepts — which the Breton dictionary preserves imperfectly (Breton's primary
interlocutors were men). Taylor's work identifies which dictionary entries reflect
the full community's speech and which reflect only one gender's usage.
