## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — Review of Roman Era 1 Opening

**Overall:** The opening tells the Roman story from inside, which is what the project requires. I am watching carefully because Rome is the civilization most likely to be told from a position of assumed superiority, and the opening must not allow that. So far, it does not. But the danger is not gone.

**What works:**
- The Livy handling is correct: "whether Brutus himself existed as Livy drew him, these are questions the sources cannot answer and the tradition does not ask." This respects the tradition without surrendering critical distance. It is exactly the right posture.
- The toga passage, connecting it to the Inca cumbi cloth, suggests a world where clothing is social grammar. This is inside-voice. A Roman citizen would have understood the toga this way.
- "Romani" as default. Good. Keep it.

**What doesn't:**
- "The most influential political engineering of the ancient Mediterranean." This is a ranking. Even if true, it centers Rome above its contemporaries. The Athenian demokratia, the Carthaginian suffetes, the Phoenician city-state constitutions all existed. The sentence should be cut or reframed.
- The Etruscan craftsmen from Veii "whose skill the Romani hired because they did not yet possess it themselves" risks implying that the Romani eventually became self-sufficient and surpassed. The Etruscans deserve their own acknowledgment, not a subordinate clause in Roma's origin story.

**The question I'd push on:**
Who are the Latini? Who are the other peoples on these hills before Roma names them Roman? The origin story Romulus tells is a foundation myth. The people who were already there had their own stories. The chapter need not center those stories, but it must acknowledge that Roma was built in a landscape that was already inhabited, already organized, already storied. The Sabine women are in the tradition. Are they in the chapter?
