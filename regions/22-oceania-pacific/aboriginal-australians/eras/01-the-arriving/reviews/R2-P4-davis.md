# P-4 — Wade Davis — R2 Review of Notes (Production Bible)

**Overall:** The production bible promises disciplinary depth. The question is whether the chapter will deliver it. The fire section with Gammage and the stars section with Hamacher are the test cases.

**What works:**
- The triple-redundancy mechanism (song/country/kinship) as the structural answer to "why 65,000 years?" is a genuine insight. Knowledge encoded in three different media, each reinforcing the others, so that the system is resilient to the failure of any one. This is not an assertion of persistence. It is an explanation of persistence.
- The fire section plan correctly identifies that method-level detail is required. "WHICH plants, WHICH season, WHICH sequence." If the chapter delivers this, it will pass the Davis test on ecology.
- Hamacher's *The First Astronomers* as a source for the star section is the right choice. Aboriginal astronomical knowledge is finally being documented at disciplinary depth by Aboriginal and non-Aboriginal scholars working together. The chapter should reflect this.

**What doesn't:**
- The notes do not yet commit to specific examples. "The Pleiades appear at dawn when certain food sources are ready." WHICH food sources? The Davis test is specificity. If the chapter says "the Pleiades signal the arrival of emu eggs," that is specific. If it says "the Pleiades signal seasonal changes," that is evasion.
- The water knowledge system is underrepresented. In arid Australia, water knowledge is at least as critical as fire knowledge. The chapter mentions water in the opening (Maku heading for water) but the notes do not dedicate a section to water knowledge. Consider whether the Songline walk itself can serve as the vehicle for water knowledge: the song encodes water sources, and Maku's walk is a demonstration of that encoding.
- The kinship section (Section 4) must not become an anthropological diagram. Skin groups and moiety systems are endlessly complex. The chapter needs ONE scene that makes the system visible through practice, not through explanation. A stranger arriving in another nation's country and declaring their skin name. What happens next? Show it.

**The question I'd push on:**
The ecology section must reach the level where a reader could ALMOST do it. Not "they burned the land in patterns" but: cool-season burn on this type of vegetation, spaced at this interval, because fire promotes this regrowth which attracts this game. The reader should finish the fire section understanding the LOGIC of the system, not just that it exists.
