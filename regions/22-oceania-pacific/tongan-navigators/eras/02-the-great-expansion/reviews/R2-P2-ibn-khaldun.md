## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (Round 2)

**Overall:** The structural engine is correctly identified. The prestige-exchange system as self-reinforcing loop is the right mechanism. But the notes must go further: what are the conditions that make this system work, and what are the conditions that would break it?

**What works:**
- "Connection to Tongatapu makes you somebody, and being somebody requires you to maintain the connection." This is the engine stated clearly. The self-reinforcing logic is sound.
- The terminal question (overextension, the need for secular lines) correctly identifies the seed of Era 3. The chapter should plant this seed without resolving it.

**What needs strengthening:**
- The notes should identify the ecological and geographic conditions that make the network possible. The western Pacific's island density, the trade wind patterns, the seasonal variation in sailing conditions. The network is not just a political choice. It is enabled by geography and constrained by it.
- The distinction between the kalia as a diplomatic instrument and the network as a political system needs to be sharper. The kalia is the technology. The Tu'i Tonga's mana is the currency. The exchange obligation is the mechanism. The chapter should make this hierarchy of causation explicit in the prose, not just in the notes.
