## Baseline Voice

Established in the opening: Castle-Spare. Short declarative sentences. Periods where another register would use commas. The voice places things: a person, a castle, a document. It does not parenthesize or qualify; it states and moves on. Analytical content is carried in the same register as narrative. Sentence rhythm: medium length for scene-setting, short for emphasis, period-stopped.

Exemplary baseline passages:
- "The castle came first. Always the castle."
- "He was right to tremble."
- "What is knowable: the north did not rise again in William's reign."
- "A tool that worked was kept. A tool that could be repurposed was repurposed."

## Full Scan Findings

**Finding 1 — P3 (Minor)**
Location: Lanfranc section, paragraph 5
Text: "The Church was not a spiritual comfort to the Norman conquest. It was its central administrative mechanism."

The second sentence's phrase "administrative mechanism" is slightly modern-institutional in register, sitting one degree outside the chapter's voice. The content is correct; the word "mechanism" is the only slight intrusion. Acceptable within P3 tolerance. Author may substitute "instrument" or "engine" if desired, but not required.

**Finding 2 — P3 (Minor)**
Location: Body at Caen section, final paragraphs
Text: "outlasted the Norman name, outlasted the distinction between Norman and English that was the conquest's premise."

The triple anaphora ("outlasted... outlasted... outlasted") is slightly more lyrical than the chapter's standard Castle-Spare register. It is effective and earned by the landing's weight, so the judgment call is to retain it. But the author should be aware that the landing section's rhythm is slightly warmer than the chapter's center. This may be intentional and appropriate for a close.
