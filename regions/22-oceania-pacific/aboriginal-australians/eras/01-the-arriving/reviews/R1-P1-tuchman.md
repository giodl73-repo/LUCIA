# P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of "When the Ancestors Walked" Opening

**Overall:** This opening earns the reader's attention through a deceptively quiet device: a man walking and singing, with the stakes rising in every paragraph until the reader understands they are watching someone walk through the constitution of a continent.

**What works:**
- The structural escalation is superb. Paragraph 1: a man sings while walking. Paragraph 2: the song IS the country. Paragraph 4: the Songline crosses the continent. Paragraph 6: the song carries the law. Each turn deepens what the reader thought they understood. This is how you build momentum without explosions.
- "A person walking a Songline was not taking a hike. A person walking a Songline was walking through the constitution of a continent." This is the pivot sentence. It reframes everything that came before. The narrative arc of the opening is essentially complete at this line.
- The perentie track is an excellent small detail — it grounds the scene in physical tracking while anticipating the ecological knowledge theme.

**What doesn't:**
- The deep-time paragraph (the penultimate one, beginning "Sixty-five thousand years") shifts register. For six paragraphs we are walking with Maku; then suddenly we are in a reflective essayistic mode discussing what "we" can and cannot know. The shift is handled well — the honesty is genuine — but it breaks the walking rhythm. Consider whether this passage can be woven into the chapter body instead, preserving the opening's unbroken walk.
- The final line is clean but may be slightly too neat. "The younger men walked behind him, learning where the water was" wraps the bow. The San opening's final line ("The girl rested her chin on her knees and waited") has more open space. Consider whether the landing can leave something more unresolved.

**The question I'd push on:**
Where does the drama come from in the full chapter? The Songline walk is contemplative and beautiful, but a 5,000-word chapter needs turns. What complications will drive the middle sections? What will the reader not see coming?
