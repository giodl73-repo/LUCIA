## BOARD — B3 (Fraser) — Romani Era 1: O Lungo Drom

**Overall:** The historical and documentary evidence is accurately presented. The Byzantine references are correctly identified and appropriately interpreted. The Shahnameh story is handled with proper scholarly caution.

**Accuracy findings:**

1. **Mount Athos text (c. 1068):** The chapter describes this as a hagiographic text mentioning Athinganoi summoned to deal with wild animals. This is a reasonable summary of the relevant passage, though the dating and the association with the Roma specifically are matters of scholarly debate. The chapter correctly does not assert that the Athinganoi of this text are certainly the ancestors of the modern Roma. **Appropriate handling.**

2. **Monemvasia document (1283):** The chapter references a document mentioning Athinganoi as leather tanners and metalworkers. This reference is consistent with the documentary record. The Monemvasia evidence is among the more secure early references to Romani presence in the Balkans. **Accurate.**

3. **The Shahnameh story:** The chapter correctly identifies this as a Persian literary source, correctly dates the Shahnameh's completion to c. 1010, and correctly notes that the story is told for Persian purposes. The caveat that it "says more about Persian assumptions... than about the people it describes" is exactly right. **Well handled.**

4. **Mahmud of Ghazni's raids:** Correctly stated as seventeen raids between 998 and 1030. This is consistent with the historical record. The hypothesis linking the raids to Romani departure is correctly presented as one theory among several.

5. **Manzikert (1071):** Correctly dated and accurately described as the Seljuk defeat of the Byzantine army. The chapter correctly uses this as context for the transit through Armenia.

6. **The Athinganoi/Roma connection:** The chapter presents this connection without insisting on certainty, which is appropriate. The identification of Byzantine Athinganoi with the ancestors of the modern Roma is probable but not certain, and some scholars (including Soulis and others) have argued for a distinct Athinganoi group. The chapter's treatment is appropriately cautious.

7. **The European name derivations (Tsigane, Zigeuner, Zingaro, etc. from Athinganoi):** Correctly traced. The English "Gypsy" from "Egyptian" is correctly noted as based on a medieval misidentification. **Accurate.**

8. **One factual note:** The chapter states that Roma in the Balkans lived "on the margins of Byzantine and then post-Byzantine society." The transition from Byzantine to post-Byzantine (Ottoman) rule in the Balkans was gradual, occurring between the 14th and 15th centuries. Since the chapter covers up to c. 1300, the Roma would have been primarily within Byzantine territory at this point. The "post-Byzantine" qualifier is slightly premature for the era covered. **Recommendation:** Consider adjusting to "Byzantine society" without the "post-Byzantine" addition, since this era ends c. 1300.

**Verdict:** PASS. One minor recommendation (post-Byzantine timing). No factual errors requiring correction.
