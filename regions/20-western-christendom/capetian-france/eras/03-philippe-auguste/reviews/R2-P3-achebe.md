# R2-P3 — Chinua Achebe
## Panel-2 Review: Notes, Philippe Auguste et la Grande Expansion

**Round:** 2
**Reviewer:** Chinua Achebe (P-3)
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The notes address my key concern directly: the Albigensian Crusade is planned with both the Capetian frame and the southern reality held simultaneously. The technique is correct — "hold the Capetian frame while letting the weight of what it consumed press against the edges." The Guillaume de Tudèle *Canso* as counter-voice from the south is precisely the right choice: an Occitan source, hostile to the crusade, rendered briefly within the chapter, not as a corrective but as the sound of the other world pressing against the dominant frame.

The Ingeborg section raises a challenge I want to foreground: Ingeborg is not incidental. She is the era's refutation, held in permanent suspension. The notes plan to give her a full Figures treatment. I would ask that her presence in the chapter's narrative be proportional — she appears not just in figures.md but in the arc itself, as a recurring fact the reader returns to. Philip can heal hundreds of strangers. He cannot — will not — heal his marriage. The reader should feel this dissonance, not be told it.

The Cathar communities need at least one sentence that names them on their own terms: the *bonshommes*, the *parfaits*, the communities of southern France. Not "heretics" (the crusade's term) but the term they used for themselves, appearing once, so the reader knows the world that is being consumed. This is a small thing. It matters.

The note on Philip's Jewish policy (1182 expulsion, 1198 readmission) is correctly included. The chapter should render this in the Capetian register — the logic of the decision from inside the crown's perspective — without editorializing, while letting the reader understand what expulsion means in practice: people, property, displacement.

The voice plan (Sacred-Legal) is apt. This is a culture that made legal language into an instrument of sacred authority. The two were not separate. The chapter's voice should carry both simultaneously.
