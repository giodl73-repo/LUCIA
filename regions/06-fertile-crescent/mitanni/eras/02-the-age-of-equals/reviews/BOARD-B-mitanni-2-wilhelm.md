## B-mitanni-2 — Gernot Wilhelm — Board Review of The Age of Equals

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Hurrian language, religion, and culture; Mitanni political structure; the Indo-Aryan/Hurrian relationship.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The Hurrian cultural section is substantially accurate. The description of Hurrian as a language with agglutinative verb morphology (chains of suffixes) is correct.
- The Shaushka/Ishtar distinction is correctly handled: Shaushka as a Hurrian form of Ishtar with her own cult identity.
- The Kumarbi cycle is accurately described, including the succession myth and its transmission to the Hittite world and eventually Greek tradition.
- The Hurrian hymn from Ugarit is correctly described. The notation system, the diatonic scale reference, and the honest uncertainty about whether it records melody or harmonic framework are all sound.
- The Nuzi archives material is well used. Tulpunnaya as a woman with legal standing and Tehip-Tilla's adoption-as-land-transfer practice are both attested in the published record.
- The Nuzi ware pottery description is accurate: white/cream slip with geometric decoration in dark paint.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The chapter gives the Hurrian world appropriate weight. It is not merely background for the *maryannu* elite, which is the most common failure in popular treatments of Mitanni.
- The multilingual, multiethnic character of the kingdom is correctly represented.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The characterization of Mitanni as "in its daily life, a Hurrian kingdom with a chariot aristocracy at its apex" is precisely the formulation the scholarship supports.
- The inclusion of Hurrian material culture, religion, mythology, music, and legal practice gives the Hurrian world its due dimensionality.

**What needs correction:**
- The Indo-Aryan technical terms in the Kikkuli manual (*aika, tera, panza, satta*) are conventionally rendered as *aika, tera, panza, satta* in the scholarly literature, which matches the chapter. However, these are specifically the ordinal or training-interval terms, and their status as numerals versus technical terms is debated. The chapter presents them as numbers used for counting intervals, which is the mainstream interpretation but not universal. A minor point, and not incorrect as stated.

**Verdict:** APPROVED
