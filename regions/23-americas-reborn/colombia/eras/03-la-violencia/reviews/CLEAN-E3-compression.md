## Findings

**Finding 1 — Anticipatory explanation before Rosa's scene**
**Location:** "La Tierra" section, paragraph before the Rosa account
**Quoted:** "When partisan violence arrived, the land question and the party question collapsed into each other. A Liberal smallholder in a Conservative-controlled municipality found that his property rights were as secure as his party's ability to defend them — which is to say, not secure at all."

**Issue:** This analytical statement immediately precedes Rosa's scene and explains in advance what the scene will show. The scene demonstrates exactly this collapse. The preceding explanation reduces the scene's impact by telling before showing.

**Recommendation:** Move or cut the analytical statement. Let Rosa's scene do its own work. The reader will understand what happened. The next section ("El Bandolero") can carry any structural observation that is strictly necessary.

**Finding 2 — Redundant framing around "La Gaitanada"**
**Location:** Opening two sentences of "La Gaitanada" section
**Quoted:** "In February 1948, two months before his death, Jorge Eliécer Gaitán led his people to the Plaza de Bolívar in complete silence. This requires some description to understand as an act."

**Issue:** "This requires some description to understand as an act" is a writer's note to the reader — a meta-commentary that breaks the chapter's voice. The description should follow without announcing itself. Trust the reader.

**Recommendation:** Delete the second sentence. Open directly: "One hundred thousand people came to the Plaza de Bolívar in complete silence."

**Finding 3 — Double articulation of terminal condition**
**Location:** "El Bandolero" final paragraph and "El General y el Acuerdo" final paragraph
**First instance:** "The violence had found a way to sustain itself after the conditions that produced it had been nominally resolved."
**Second instance:** "The National Front agreement closes the era; it does not close the conditions that produced it."

**Issue:** The same observation — that the National Front resolved the surface without the structure — is made twice in two different sections. The second instance (in the landing section, via the chapter text's implicit argument) is stronger and earns its place. The first is anticipatory redundancy.

**Recommendation:** Remove the first instance. Let "El Bandolero" end with González still in the mountains in 1965, without editorial summary of what this means. The landing will say it.

**Finding 4 — Trailing explanation after García Márquez Nobel quotation**
**Location:** figures.md, García Márquez entry, final paragraph
**Quoted:** "He received the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1982, in Stockholm, and told the Swedish Academy: 'The country that would have been ours is one we are still building.'"

**Issue:** This is a strong close for the García Márquez entry. It does not need anything after it. The current text stops here. Confirmed: figures.md ends on this line. **No action required — this passes.**
