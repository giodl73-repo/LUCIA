**GATE-1 Verdict: PASS (28/30, target 24+)**

**Notes:** The saga voice is working. The spare, declarative register is distinctive and sustained. The structural engine (ship-as-expansion-logic) is established from the first line. The opening successfully tells Lindisfarne from the Norse side without romanticizing or condemning. The voice is a genuine discovery for the project: it sits between the maritime and oral-tradition registers in the Voice Spectrum, taking the forward momentum of steppe prose and the material groundedness of maritime prose, but with the saga's unique quality of saying less than it means.

**Innovation flagged:** The saga voice's use of understatement as a scoring mechanism for Honesty. "The wind had been fair" after the Lindisfarne description does more ethical work than any editorial restraint could. The culture's own narrative practice (saga understatement) IS the no-verdict discipline. Worth tracking for Voice dimension.
