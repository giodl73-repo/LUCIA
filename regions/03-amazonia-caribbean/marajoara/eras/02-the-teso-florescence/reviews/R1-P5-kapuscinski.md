# R1-P5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski — Panel Review of Opening

**Chapter:** Marajoara — Era 2: The Teso Florescence
**Round:** Panel-1
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The smell of exposed mud and stranded fish is the best sentence in the opening. Not because smell is exotic or dramatic — but because it is the smell of the dry season arriving, the smell of the flood withdrawing, and it grounds the entire temporal logic of the Marajoara world in a single sensory note before the first action begins. I have sat in many places at the moment when a flood pulls back: the smell of that moment is always the same, everywhere on earth — organic, slightly fermented, alive with the promise of fertility — and the opening earns it because the woman begins her work in that smell. She works in the exact moment of the season's turning. This is correct.

The fish-rib tool is excellent. The red slip, the black slip — the chapter knows what these are made of, I trust, and the chapter should tell us. Not in a technical note, but in the woman's hands: what does red slip feel like to apply? How thick is it? Does it dry fast in the dry-season air? These are the textures that separate a chapter from a description.

What is missing from this opening: sound. The Camutins teso complex at its peak holds a large community. Where are the other women? Do they work in groups? In silence? Is there speech during the urn-painting? The woman is alone on her mat, which may be accurate — perhaps the most elaborate work is done alone, in a particular state — but the chapter should know this and the opening should gesture toward it. The sound environment of the teso in the dry season — the calls from the fish weirs, the children on the mound, the sound of other clay being worked nearby or not nearby — is as much a part of the world as the smell.

The vessel's weight is excellent: "a grown woman could fit inside it." This lands. It makes the scale of the urn visceral in a way that no measurement could. Keep this.

**The question I'd push on:** What does the woman see from where she sits? She is on the teso, five meters above the plain. In the early dry season, the flood is retreating. What is the visual horizon? This image — the mound above the withdrawing water, the woman's hands on the vessel, the horizon of seasonal change — is the chapter's central image. Give it a sentence.
