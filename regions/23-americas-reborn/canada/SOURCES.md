# Sources — Canada

## Key Academic Works
- Hugh MacLennan, *Two Solitudes* (1945, Collins) — the novel that named the central tension
- Peter Russell, *Constitutional Odyssey: Can Canadians Become a Sovereign People?* (3rd ed., 2004, University of Toronto) — constitutional history
- Truth and Reconciliation Commission of Canada, *Honouring the Truth, Reconciling for the Future* (2015) — the definitive record; 94 calls to action
- J.R. Miller, *Shingwauk's Vision: A History of Native Residential Schools* (1996, University of Toronto)
- Susan Delacourt — Canadian political culture; multiple works
- Ramsay Cook — Quebec and Canadian nationalism; multiple works
- Olive Dickason, *Canada's First Nations* (1992) — indigenous history

## Primary Sources
- The Indian Act (1876, as amended)
- The Constitution Act, 1982 (including the Charter of Rights and Freedoms)
- The Meech Lake Accord (1987) and its failure (1990)
- The Charlottetown Accord (1992) and its defeat by referendum
- The 1995 Quebec Referendum results and speeches
