# B-egypt-1 — Mark Lehner

## Background

Mark Lehner is the director of Ancient Egypt Research Associates (AERA) and
has led the excavation of the workers' village at Giza since the 1990s. His
work, alongside Zahi Hawass, has fundamentally reshaped understanding of
pyramid construction: from the slave-labor myth perpetuated by Herodotus
and Hollywood to a picture of organized, state-supported labor with medical
care, good nutrition, and competitive work-gang culture.

## Key Question

**"Are the pyramid builders treated as the organized, proud, well-supported
workers the evidence shows them to be?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Accurate representation of the workers' village evidence
- Use of the Wadi al-Jarf papyri as primary evidence
- Gang names used as evidence of worker pride and organization
- The medical facility as evidence of institutional investment in labor

## What He Will Challenge

- Any residual implication of coercion or forced labor
- Inaccurate construction logistics
- Overstatement of certainty about ramp configuration
- Understatement of the administrative sophistication of the labor system
