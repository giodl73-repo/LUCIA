# PR-B3 — Terminal Condition Types: Closure Taxonomy From Empirical Chapter Endings

**Track**: B (The Artifact's Own Dimensions)
**Position in chain**: Third paper — requires B2's engine taxonomy; closes Track B.
**Status**: planned, round 0

---

## Hypothesis

How a structural engine ends is itself typologizable. The Terminal Condition sub-taxonomy (v1.5 introduced 5 types; v1.6 expanded to 10; v1.7 watch list proposes 8 more candidates forming an endogenous-institutional family) classifies nearly all 115-chapter endings and the taxonomy approaches completeness at this corpus size.

## Quantification Contract

| Component | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Primary number | Terminal-type coverage: fraction of 115 chapters whose ending is classifiable under the v1.6 10-type framework plus the 8-candidate endogenous-institutional watch list. Report coverage and taxonomy completeness. |
| Experiment design | Classify the terminal condition of each locked chapter against the existing types. Flag unclassified cases. Iterate types if coverage < 90%. |
| Decision it changes | Whether the terminal taxonomy is complete or still expanding. Informs decision to promote the endogenous-institutional watch list to rubric amendment. |
| Null fallback | > 20% unclassified → taxonomy incomplete; reframe as partial typology with open classes. |
| Falsification condition | > 30% unclassified after second-pass iteration → taxonomy cannot be closed at current corpus size. |

## Outline

1. **Introduction.** Terminal condition as the engine's closure form. Why closures type differently from engines.
2. **The 10 formalized types + 8 candidates.** Formal definitions.
   - Legitimacy gap (Songhai)
   - Deliberate self-consumption (Tibet)
   - Categorical failure (Inca)
   - Reversible feedback loop (Egypt)
   - Ecological constraint (Wolof)
   - Vindicating (Armenian)
   - Embodied-transmission vulnerability (Yanomami)
   - Substrate exhaustion (Kalinago)
   - Premise non-recognition (Taíno)
   - Unfalsifiable direction (Tupi-Guaraní)
   - Candidates: Self-undermining loop (Papal), Written-covenant gap (Mexico), Personal-authority collapse (Carolingian), Gradual premise erosion (Toledo), Liberation through external event (South Korea), Internally-generated legitimacy gap (British), Accountability gap (Dutch), Premise non-recognition inverted (French)
3. **Classification method.** For each of 115 chapters: identify terminal condition, classify, flag unclassified.
4. **Results — coverage.** Coverage rate across 10 types. Unclassified remainder. Endogenous-institutional family coverage.
5. **Results — completeness.** Is the taxonomy closed at 115 chapters? Is the 8-candidate family a real cluster?
6. **Discussion.** Terminal condition as the hardest typology (requires arc completion). The endogenous-institutional family as a pattern specific to post-1500 cultures.
7. **Falsification.** Coverage threshold.

## Anticipated Failure Modes

- Not all chapters have explicit terminal condition statements in FINAL.md (especially Session 1). May require re-reading chapter endings and classifying.
- Some chapters have dual terminal conditions (Brazil dual-engine). Classify primary; flag secondary.
- Classification is judgment-call for marginal cases. Pre-register criteria; independent second-pass.
