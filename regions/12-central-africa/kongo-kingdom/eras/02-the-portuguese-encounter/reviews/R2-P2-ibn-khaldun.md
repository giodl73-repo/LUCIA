## Assessment of the Notes

The notes have named the engine correctly: "covenant argument confronting commercial logic." The terminal condition is also correctly identified: the Kongo theory of binding obligation does not map onto the Portuguese commercial-colonial system's actual decision-making architecture.

What the notes have not yet fully specified is the mechanism of the commercial system's non-responsiveness. In my understanding of political systems, a state fails to enforce a norm not because it lacks will, but because the enforcement threatens the interests of the actors who have the power to enforce it. The Portuguese crown's non-enforcement of the slave trade's restrictions was not willful malice — it was structural: the revenue from the trade flowed to actors powerful enough to resist regulation.

**Recommendation:** The "Governor's Calculation" section should name specific goods and their value — not impressionistically but as a structural fact. If a provincial governor could trade ten captives for enough copper and textiles to outfit an army of a hundred, the math of the calculation is clear. The chapter needs that math, even approximately.

**The 'asabiyya question:** The notes name the erosion of group solidarity but don't specify the mechanism. Here is the precise mechanism: the Kongo political system held together through a system of tributary relationships — provincial governors sent tribute to Mbanza Kongo and received recognition and protection in return. The slave trade offered an alternative: a provincial governor could acquire goods from the Atlantic system that made him more powerful locally, reducing his dependence on Mbanza Kongo. The tributary relationship was not replaced by the slave trade; it was made optional by it. Optionality is what breaks solidarity.

**The structural adequacy of the reply:** The notes say João III's replies were "structurally inadequate." This needs precision. João III did, in fact, issue regulations. He did, in fact, respond to Afonso's letters with at least formal gestures of concern. The inadequacy was not that the Portuguese king ignored the letters — it was that the regulations he issued had no enforcement mechanism in Kongo territory. The chapter must show why: the Portuguese Crown's jurisdiction extended to its own traders at the coast; it did not extend to the interior network through which captives moved. The legal architecture of Portuguese overseas administration left the interior beyond reach, not because the Crown didn't care but because it had never built the apparatus to reach it.

## The Question I'm Satisfied On

The engine is named. The terminal condition follows logically from the engine's own mechanism. This is correct. The chapter is ready to write.
