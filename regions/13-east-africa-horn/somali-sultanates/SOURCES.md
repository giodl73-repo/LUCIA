# Sources — Somali Sultanates

## Primary

- Ibn Battuta, *Rihla* — accounts of Mogadishu and the Horn in the fourteenth century
- Ahmad ibn Fartuwa (partial applicability) — Kanem source mentioning Horn connections
- Portuguese chronicles — sixteenth century accounts of the Ethiopian-Adal conflict
- Burton, Richard F. *First Footsteps in East Africa*. 1856. — the Harar visit

## Academic

- Lewis, I.M. *A Modern History of the Somali: Nation and State in the Horn of Africa*. 4th ed. 2002. James Currey. — the foundational political history
- Trimingham, J. Spencer. *Islam in Ethiopia*. 1952. Oxford University Press. — the Islamic dimensions
- Cassanelli, Lee V. *The Shaping of Somali Society: Reconstructing the History of a Pastoral People, 1600–1900*. 1982. University of Pennsylvania Press.
- Hersi, Ali Abdirahman. "The Arab Factor in Somali History." PhD dissertation, UCLA, 1977.
- Pouwels, Randall L. *Horn and Crescent: Cultural Change and Traditional Islam on the East African Coast, 800–1900*. 1987. Cambridge University Press.
- Turton, E.R. "The Ajuran Empire." *International Journal of African Historical Studies* 8:4 (1975).

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on the Ajuran hydraulic state and its water engineering specifically
- Recent scholarship on the xeer customary law system
- Analysis of Harar's urban culture and its distinct Harari identity
