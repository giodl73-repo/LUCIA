# Sources — Burundi

## Primary / Oral

- Burundian royal traditions — court historians; ganwa genealogies
- Emile Mworoha (ed.), *Histoire du Burundi: Des origines à la fin du XIXe siècle* (1987) — the foundational scholarly synthesis by Burundian historians
- German colonial administrative records — the early twentieth century documentation

## Academic

- Vansina, Jan. *La légende du passé: traditions orales du Burundi*. 1972. Musée Royal de l'Afrique Centrale.
- Lemarchand, René. *Rwanda and Burundi*. 1970. Pall Mall Press. — the comparative political history
- Chrétien, Jean-Pierre. *The Great Lakes of Africa: Two Thousand Years of History*. 2003. Zone Books.
- Prunier, Gérard. *The Rwanda Crisis*. 1995. Columbia University Press. — regional context
- Weinstein, Warren. *Political Conflict and Ethnic Strategies: A Case Study of Burundi*. 1976. Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs, Syracuse University.
- Malkki, Liisa. *Purity and Exile: Violence, Memory, and National Cosmology Among Hutu Refugees in Tanzania*. 1995. University of Chicago Press. — post-1972 context

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on the karyenda and its ritual significance in the Burundian tradition
- Detailed scholarship on the Bezi-Batare rivalry and its historiography
- Analysis of Burundian court poetry and cattle aesthetics
- Recent historical work on the pre-colonial Hutu-Tutsi categories in Burundi specifically
