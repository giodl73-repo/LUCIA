# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Angkor Wat bas-reliefs (12th century) — historical and mythological scenes
- Sdok Kak Thom inscription (1052 CE) — account of the devaraja ritual and early Angkor history
- Zhou Daguan, *A Record of Cambodia* (1297 CE) — Chinese envoy's eyewitness account of Angkor
- Angkor Thom Bayon inscriptions — Jayavarman VII's hospital network

## Key Academic Works

- David Chandler, *A History of Cambodia* (4th ed., 2008) — standard scholarly history
- Michael Coe, *Angkor and the Khmer Civilization* (2003)
- Claude Jacques and Michael Freeman, *Angkor: Cities and Temples* (1997)
- Roland Fletcher et al. — Greater Angkor Project archaeological surveys
- Damian Evans — LiDAR surveys revealing the extent of Angkor's urban landscape

## Needed

- Recent scholarship on the collapse debate (climate, hydraulic failure, Thai pressure)
- Studies on the post-Angkor period and the move to Phnom Penh
- Analysis of Jayavarman VII's Buddhist turn and its social consequences
