## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski — PANEL-2 Review of Hammurabi's Babylon Notes

**Focus:** Sensory plan.

**The notes identify the sensory gaps correctly.** Section 1 revision (add Esagila precinct), Section 3 (canal at Sippar intake), Section 5 (breweries, grain measures, the gagum). This is good.

**What I want in the chapter:**
- The heat. Every outdoor scene must acknowledge that the Babylonian plain in summer is punishing. The stele is warm under the hand because the sun is merciless. The canal workers dig in the pre-dawn cool because by midday the air does not move.
- The mud brick. Babylon is not stone. It is river mud shaped into bricks and dried in the sun, and the entire city has the color of the plain it sits on. When it rains (rarely) the streets become slippery and the walls soften. When the wind blows the dust rises from the unpaved streets and coats the face and the hands.
- The water. The canal that runs through the city is the lifeline. The sound of water in the channels. The green of the irrigated date gardens. The contrast between the irrigated zone (green, productive, alive) and the dry plain beyond the canal system (brown, cracked, empty).
- The bitumen. The Babylonians waterproofed everything with bitumen from the natural seeps near Hit, upriver. The smell of hot bitumen on a canal wall, on a brick foundation, on the hull of a river boat. This is a smell specific to Mesopotamia.
- The dates. Date palms lined the canals and the temple precincts. The dates were a staple food, a trade commodity, and a marker of the irrigated zone. A city with date palms is a city with water. A city without them is dying.

**The karum scene needs expansion.** The merchant walking from the karum to the Esagila should be a sensory journey: from the noise and dust and donkey smell of the trading district, through the gate, into the quieter precincts near the temple, where the air smells of burnt offerings and the pavement gives way to the courtyard where the stele stands. This transition carries the structural argument (from commerce to cosmic order) in physical sensation.

**Recommendation:** Write it. But give me the city.
