# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Babur, *Baburnama* — trans. Wheeler Thackston (Modern Library, 2002)
- Abul Fazl, *Akbarnama* and *Ain-i-Akbari* — trans. H. Beveridge (3 vols.)
- Jahangir, *Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri* (Jahangirnama) — trans. Wheeler Thackston (1999)
- Francois Bernier, *Travels in the Mogul Empire* (1670) — French physician's account
- Aurangzeb's letters (Ruqaat-i-Alamgiri)

## Key Academic Works

- John F. Richards, *The Mughal Empire* (1993, New Cambridge History of India)
- Muzaffar Alam and Sanjay Subrahmanyam — Mughal political culture
- Ebba Koch, *Mughal Architecture* (1991); *The Complete Taj Mahal* (2006)
- Bamber Gascoigne, *The Great Moghuls* (1971)
- Ruby Lal, *Domesticity and Power in the Early Mughal World* (2005)
- Audrey Truschke, *Aurangzeb* (2017) — revisionist study

## Needed

- Recent studies on Mughal provincial governance and revenue records
- Art historical synthesis of Mughal miniature painting traditions
- Comparative study of Mughal administrative vocabulary in Persian and vernacular
