# Sources — Buganda

## Primary / Oral

- Buganda royal traditions — maintained by the kabaka's court historians (abateekateka)
- Apolo Kagwa, *Ekitabo kye Mpisa za Baganda* (Customs of the Baganda), 1905 — the foundational inside account by a senior Buganda chief
- Apolo Kagwa, *Bassekabaka be Buganda* (Kings of Buganda) — the royal chronicles
- Stanley, Henry Morton. *Through the Dark Continent*. 1878. — the Mutesa I encounter

## Academic

- Kiwanuka, M.S.M. *A History of Buganda: From the Foundation of the Kingdom to 1900*. 1971. Longman.
- Rowe, John A. *Lugard at Kampala*. 1969. Makerere University.
- Low, D.A. *Buganda in Modern History*. 1971. University of California Press.
- Southwold, Martin. *Bureaucracy and Chiefship in Buganda*. 1961. East African Institute of Social Research.
- Hansen, Holger Bernt. *Mission, Church and State in a Colonial Setting: Uganda 1890–1925*. 1984. Heinemann.
- Hanson, Holly E. *Landed Obligation: The Practice of Power in Buganda*. 2003. Heinemann.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on Mutesa I's diplomatic strategy and its documentation
- Studies on the Buganda bark cloth industry and its cultural significance
- Analysis of the Buganda Agreement of 1900 and its long-term political consequences
- Scholarship on Buganda's internal clan politics and the kabaka's management of them
