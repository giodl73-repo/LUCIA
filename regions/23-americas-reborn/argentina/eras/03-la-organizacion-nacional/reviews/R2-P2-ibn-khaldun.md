# R2-P2 — Ibn Khaldun
## Panel Round 2 — Notes Review

**Chapter:** Argentina Era 3 — La Organización Nacional y la Generación del 80
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The structural engine is clearly named and the terminal condition is correctly identified. The Ley Sáenz Peña as the legitimacy-gap resolution is precise: the oligarchic loop produced the mass society that then outgrew the loop's capacity to govern it. Yrigoyen's 1916 election is the right terminal moment.

My concern with the section plan: Section 2 (The Pampa and the Plan) risks becoming a survey section — the railway network, the immigration statistics, the Campaña del Desierto. This is the section most likely to drift into textbook register. The production bible should name a specific human anchor for this section. Julio Roca is the right figure, but the section needs to enter through his experience, not his policy record. What did Roca see when he crossed Patagonia during the Campaña? What did he think Argentina was becoming? He is not a monster in the Argentine telling — he is the builder. The chapter should render him as the builder, with the cost of the building visible but not moralized.

Carlos Pellegrini as the son-of-Italian-immigrant who became the president of the oligarchy is exactly the right complication. He embodies the engine's own contradiction: the immigrant who completes his assimilation by defining the terms of belonging so narrowly that the next wave of immigrants cannot meet them. Use him.

The 1890 Baring crisis belongs in the chapter — briefly. It is the moment the European-validation loop nearly collapsed: Argentina defaulted on its foreign debt, Pellegrini had to stabilize the economy without British credit. The crisis revealed the structural dependency of the Generación del 80's model. It recovered, which made the model seem validated again. This is important causal context for why the model continued despite its demonstrated fragility.

**Cleared for drafting — the engine and terminal condition are sound. Anchor Section 2 in Roca's perspective. Include the 1890 Baring crisis as structural evidence, not merely background.**
