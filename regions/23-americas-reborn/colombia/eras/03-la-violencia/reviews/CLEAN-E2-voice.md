## Register Assessment

**Assigned register:** Discover (Magical-Realism-as-Realism)
**Register family:** Rupture & Diaspora / Revolutionary (structural-violence variant)

The discover register for this chapter is the voice García Márquez identified when he described his grandmother narrating miracles with the same tone as weather reports. Applied to La Violencia, it means: matter-of-fact narration of the extraordinary and the murderous, accumulative sentences, no explanatory interruption, the fantastic and the terrible sitting side by side at the same narrative height. It is a voice that does not gasp.

## Voice-Random-Paragraph Test

Three paragraphs selected at random:

**Paragraph 1 (from "La Máquina"):**
"After Gaitán's assassination, the Conservative government of Mariano Ospina Pérez responded to the Liberal riots not with conciliation but with consolidation. Police units in the departments were placed under Conservative political control. Armed groups — called chulavitas after the town in Boyacá where the model was developed — were organized under police cover to systematically kill Liberal leaders in specific municipalities."

Register check: Specific. Named. Factual. The sentence structure is declarative and accumulative. The chulavita detail — "named after the town in Boyacá where the model was developed" — is exactly the discover register: the extraordinary (state-organized assassination) stated as ordinary bureaucratic fact. **Passes.**

**Paragraph 2 (from "La Tierra"):**
"Most of Latin America resolved its colonial land question through the hacienda: large estates worked by dependent peons, sharecroppers, or indigenous communities. This created poverty, but poverty has its own stability."

Register check: This is the E-1 borderline finding. The voice here is analytical rather than observational. "Most of Latin America resolved" is a comparative framework that places the paragraph outside the Colombian interior and above it. The register has slipped from discover to academic comparative. **Flag.**

**Paragraph 3 (from landing):**
"He did not finish with the wars. The wars finished with him. That was the only way they finished in Colombia — not through victory, not through resolution, but through the exhaustion of the men who had fought them."

Register check: The chiastic structure ("he did not finish / the wars finished him") is the discover register's characteristic move: the reversal of agency that shows how La Violencia operated on people rather than being operated by them. "Not through victory, not through resolution" — the triple negative accumulation is consistent with the chapter's pause mechanism. **Passes strongly.**

## Specific Flags

**Flag 1 — Register Slip**
**Location:** "La Tierra," opening two sentences
**Issue:** "Most of Latin America resolved its colonial land question through the hacienda" — this is textbook comparative register. The reader is briefly placed above Colombia, in a comparative view from outside. The discover register should keep us in Tolima.
**Suggested revision approach:** Enter Section 3 from inside the campesino's relationship to land before explaining the regional distinction. Example approach: begin with the specific fact of smallholder ownership in Tolima — "A coffee farm in the mountains above Salento might be fifteen cuadras, cleared by one family's hands over a generation" — then draw the contrast from inside rather than from above.

**Flag 2 — Analytical Voice**
**Location:** "El Bandolero," final paragraph
**Issue:** "The 'social bandit' framework — drawn from Eric Hobsbawm's typology and applied by Sánchez and Meertens to the Colombian case" — this is a momentary emergence of academic register (citing a typology by name). It is handled with appropriate acknowledgment in figures.md but in the chapter itself it briefly places the reader in a sociology seminar rather than in the Colombian mountains.
**Suggested revision approach:** Remove the Hobsbawm attribution from the chapter (it is properly credited in figures.md). State the phenomenon directly: "By the mid-1950s, men like González had moved beyond the partisan logic that had produced them. They had communities who needed them and communities who feared them, and the distinction between protection and predation was not always clear, even to them."
