# Sources — African Diaspora

## Key Academic Works
- W.E.B. Du Bois, *The Souls of Black Folk* (1903) — the foundational text of African-American intellectual life
- C.L.R. James, *The Black Jacobins* (1938) — the Haitian Revolution
- Orlando Patterson, *Slavery and Social Death* (1982, Harvard) — the comparative sociology of slavery
- Sidney Mintz & Richard Price, *The Birth of African-American Culture* (1976) — cultural retention and transformation
- Henry Louis Gates Jr., *The Signifying Monkey* (1988) — the African-American literary tradition
- Robin D.G. Kelley, *Freedom Dreams: The Black Radical Imagination* (2002)
- Saidiya Hartman, *Lose Your Mother: A Journey Along the Atlantic Slave Route* (2007)

## Primary Sources
- Frederick Douglass, *Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, An American Slave* (1845)
- Harriet Jacobs, *Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl* (1861)
- W.E.B. Du Bois — collected works (Library of America edition)
- Marcus Garvey — collected speeches and writings
- The Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade Database (slavevoyages.org) — the most comprehensive quantitative record
