## BOARD — B-meroe-2 — Laszlo Torok — Review of Meroe Era 3

**Overall:** The political and institutional analysis is sound. The kandake institution is correctly treated as structural, the treaty terms are accurately represented, and the Meroitic-Roman diplomatic encounter is framed from the Meroitic perspective without distortion.

**Accuracy confirmed:**
- The kandake institution is correctly described as an office with military and executive authority, not as an honorary title. The temple reliefs showing kandakes at the same scale as kings, in smiting poses, are accurately cited.
- The Treaty of Samos terms (Maharraqa border, tribute cancellation, Dodekaschoinos as shared zone, Philae as shared religious site) are correctly represented.
- The characterization of the campaign as a coordinated military operation with multiple objectives (Aswan, Philae, Elephantine) is consistent with the combined evidence.
- Akinidad's role is appropriately hedged: the debate over his relationship to Amanirenas is acknowledged.
- The Strabo/"andreia" discussion is well handled: the Roman category is identified as Roman, and the Meroitic self-representation is offered as counterpoint.

**Corrections needed:**
1. The chapter states that Meroitic envoys negotiated "in Greek, most likely, the diplomatic language of the eastern Mediterranean." This is a reasonable inference given Meroitic contact with Ptolemaic Egypt, but it is not documented. The language of the negotiation is not recorded. Consider: "They negotiated, most likely through interpreters, in a diplomatic exchange that bridged Meroitic, Greek, and Latin." **Severity: Minor.** The inference is reasonable but should be marked as inference.

**Cultural authenticity assessment:** The chapter's most important achievement is maintaining Meroe's center of gravity throughout the narrative. The kandake is the protagonist. Rome is the counterpart. The treaty is Meroe's achievement, not Rome's concession. This framing is consistent with the institutional evidence and with the Meroitic self-representation in temple art. The Strabo source is correctly positioned as Roman testimony, not as neutral history. Approved.

**Verdict:** 1 minor correction. Chapter approved.
