# R2-P4 — Wade Davis
## Wayúu, Era 3: The Great Cattle Era — PANEL-2 Review (Notes)

The production bible handles my main concern from the first round — the systems must be shown in transformation, not at rest — through the section plan's progressive structure. Section 2 shows the cattle adoption as active ecological learning, not passive acceptance. Section 3 shows the pütchipü'üi operating at method level in a multi-clan dispute. Section 5 shows the mochila entering trade circuits as an expanding system, not a static artifact. These are the right moves.

The specific concern I want to carry forward into the chapter:

**The ecological learning scene in Section 2 must be at method level.** The note says: "A Wayúu herder who has spent his life reading goat behavior brings that knowledge to cattle." This is exactly right in principle. In practice, the chapter must show WHAT that transfer of knowledge looks like — specifically. What does a Wayúu herder observe about cattle that is the same as goats (follow water, seek shade at midday) and what is different (cattle can range further, require more water per head, are less agile in rocky terrain but better in open scrubland)? The ecological comparison is the intellectual act. Show the herder making it. Do not tell me he brings prior knowledge — show the prior knowledge operating in a new context.

**The mochila pattern as information density.** Section 5 mentions that "the pattern is the woman's signature on the transaction." This is good. But what specifically does a trained reader see in an Epinayuu mochila versus a Pushaina mochila versus a Wouriyu mochila? Not in exhaustive catalog, but enough to demonstrate that the visual vocabulary is real and readable. One moment where someone reads a mochila — recognizes the clan, reads something of the maker's biography — would transform the claim from assertion to evidence.

**Absence of romanticization.** The terminal condition (overgrazing, ecological degradation) is the chapter's best protection against romanticization. A Wayúu world that worked perfectly and then was destroyed by outside forces would be a parable. A Wayúu world that worked brilliantly, was maintained through sophisticated adaptive systems, and then ran into limits that were partly generated by its own success — that is a complex human story. Make sure Section 6 carries this complexity. The meanwhile is most of life, and the meanwhile eventually changes. The chapter should not end elegiacally.

**Summary:** The notes are strong. The chapter must execute the ecological learning scene at method level, give the mochila pattern real specificity, and land without romanticism — with the complexity intact.
