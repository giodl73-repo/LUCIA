# Sources — Peru

## Key Academic Works
- José Carlos Mariátegui, *Seven Interpretive Essays on Peruvian Reality* (1928; trans. 1971, University of Texas) — the foundational Indigenismo Marxist analysis
- Julio Cotler, *Clases, estado y nación en el Perú* (1978) — political sociology
- Peru Truth and Reconciliation Commission (CVR), *Final Report* (2003) — the definitive documentation of the internal conflict
- Degregori, Carlos Iván — Sendero Luminoso; *How Difficult It Is to Be God* (2012)
- Michael Moseley, *The Incas and Their Ancestors* (1992, Thames & Hudson) — the standard archaeological survey
- Gary Urton — quipu studies; *Signs of the Inka Khipu* (2003)
- Ruth Shady — Caral excavations; the oldest civilization in the Americas

## Primary Sources
- Pedro de Cieza de León, *Chronicle of Peru* (1553) — the most reliable of the early Spanish accounts
- Felipe Guaman Poma de Ayala, *Nueva Corónica y Buen Gobierno* (c. 1615) — an indigenous Andean account of the conquest and colonial system, written to the King of Spain; extraordinary
- The 1986 Amnesty International report on Peru — early documentation of military abuses
