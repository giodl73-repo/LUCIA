# Panel-2 Review — P-2 Ibn Khaldun (Targeted: Notes / Production Bible)
**Era:** Jamaica Era 5 — The Sound of Freedom
**Date:** 2026-04-17

The notes have answered my primary structural question: the terminal condition is named ("unfalsifiable direction") and it is correct. The prophetic system maintains itself through its own deferral. This is analytically precise. I am satisfied with the engine identification.

**What the notes have correctly identified:**

The three-generation asabiyya chain — Garvey to Selassie to reggae — is present in the notes and should be made structurally explicit in Section 3 (What Rastafari Knew). The chain is: Garvey provides the prophetic framework (look to Africa); Selassie provides the fulfillment (the Black king is crowned); Rastafari converts the fulfillment into a living theology; reggae encodes the theology in formal musical structure and carries it globally. Each step is necessary; none could have skipped the previous.

**What I want strengthened:**

The Michael Manley section needs more structural analysis. Manley attempted democratic socialism in a country where the United States regarded the Caribbean as its strategic backyard, where the IMF controlled credit access, and where the garrison system meant that political competition resolved in violence. The relationship between the music's liberatory claims and the political economy of 1970s Jamaica is not just context — it is the engine's most important test. The music said: Babylon will fall. Manley's government was Babylon falling, in miniature, and Babylon — in the form of capital flight, IMF conditionality, and political violence — did not allow it. What did the Rastafari community make of this? The chapter should address it.

**The garrison-music interface:**

The notes mention but do not fully analyze the structural relationship between the garrison system and the music. Key analytical point: the garrison dons and the reggae artists shared the same social substrate (Kingston yards) and were in complex relationship — not simply opposed. Sound systems were often controlled or protected by garrison leaders. The music was played at political rallies that were building the garrison structure the music criticized. This is not hypocrisy; it is the lived contradiction of a culture producing its critique from inside the system it critiques. The chapter should hold this contradiction open, not resolve it.

**The terminal condition — one refinement:**

The "unfalsifiable direction" terminal condition is correct but should be placed precisely: the terminal condition is not the prophecy's unfalsifiability alone, but the specific historical moment (Marley's death, 1981) at which the prophetic voice is at its most globally powerful and its social substrate (Trench Town as generative community) is being superseded by the garrison-dancehall dispensation. The prophecy is not spent. The social structure that generated it is being replaced. This is the engine's ending.

**Overall:** The structural engine is solid. Section 3 and Section 5 carry the analytical weight; make sure both sections deliver at method level, not summary level.
