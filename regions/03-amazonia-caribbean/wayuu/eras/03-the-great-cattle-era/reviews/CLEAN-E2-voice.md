## Wayúu, Era 3: The Great Cattle Era

**Date:** 2026-04-17

**Task:** Hunt register drift. Textbook voice, modern commentary, any paragraph that sounds like it was written for a Western audience rather than from inside the culture. Voice-random-paragraph test: three randomly selected paragraphs read in isolation — does the voice identify the culture?

## Random Paragraph Test

**Paragraph 1 (The Word Between Clans, midpoint):** "The solution Jayariyuu proposed was not admission of guilt but acknowledgment of relationship. The Epinayuu would provide the Wouriyu with six head of cattle from the next year's calf crop. Not as payment for the dead calves, which would imply the Epinayuu accepted legal responsibility for the deaths. As a gift of solidarity between eirruku who shared the Serranía water and therefore shared obligations to each other's survival."

READING: Desert-pastoral, legal, precise. The distinction between payment and gift is rendered in the culture's own categories, not in modern legal terminology. The voice identifies the culture without any surrounding context. PASS.

**Paragraph 2 (The Animals That Became Wayúu, ecological knowledge):** "He knew that goats moved upwind in the morning because the dew-laden air carried moisture before the sun burned it off. He knew that cattle, heavier and less agile, would not push through *dividivi* thicket the way goats did, but would find the open channels between thickets and follow them toward low ground where water pooled after rain."

READING: Empirical, observational, knowledge-from-observation. Reads as the interior of an expert practitioner. No textbook register, no academic distance. The Guajira's landscape is active in the sentence structure. PASS.

**Paragraph 3 (What the Colonial Record Cannot Measure, final):** "What Narváez y la Torre recorded as a failure of colonial administration was, from inside the Alta Guajira, simply how the Wayúu world worked. The women held the authority. The matrilineal network held the wealth. The words, carried by the palavrero between parties who could not speak to each other directly, held everything together."

READING: Short declaratives building to the holding image. The repetition of "held" is rhythmic and structural, not accidental. The perspective is explicitly from inside the Alta Guajira, stated. Voice identifies the culture. PASS.

## Full Chapter Scan

**Section-by-section drift assessment:**

OPENING (integrated): Clean. The voice is at its most specific here. Zero drift.

THE ANIMALS THAT BECAME WAYÚU: One drift risk at "The pattern of observation and adoption was not new." This leans toward authorial summary. Minor. Does not break the register; it is framed as Seyuuya's (or a Wayúu herder's) implicit awareness. Acceptable.

THE WORD BETWEEN CLANS: Cleanest section. The palavrero's calculation is sustained throughout in the culture's own categories (acknowledgment vs. payment, precedent vs. justice, the settlement that holds vs. the settlement that doesn't). Zero drift.

WHAT THE COLONIAL RECORD CANNOT MEASURE: One sustained risk: the section is structurally about a Spanish governor. The voice must come from inside the Wayúu perspective even while rendering the governor's perspective. It does this correctly through the framing device ("from inside the Alta Guajira, simply how the Wayúu world worked") and through the section's ultimate landing, which attributes the gap to the Wayúu's own system rather than to Spanish failure. The colonial document is used as evidence about the encounter, not as the frame. PASS.

THE PATTERN TRAVELS: No drift. The mochila is rendered as a Wayúu object throughout: practical first, then carrying information, then biography/cosmology. The analytical observation ("A Spanish merchant who bought a mochila at Riohacha got a bag. A Wayúu reader who saw the same bag got a person") is in the voice and works structurally.

THE PENINSULA HOLDS: One passage to watch: "By 1850, every other large territory in northern South America had been absorbed into Spanish and then republican administrative structures." This is necessary geographical context but it is the chapter's most external sentence. It does not drift, because it is immediately returned to the peninsula ("The Guajira Peninsula... remained, functionally, Wayúu territory"), but it does briefly place the reader in an external vantage point. Acceptable given that the landing recovers fully.

## Verdict: CLEAN

No revisions required from voice perspective. Apply E-1's revision (one sentence); it improves voice as well as judgment.
