| Dimension | Score | Evidence |

| Scene     | 5/5   | Opens with a specific, vivid moment: horses stumbling off a ship onto a beach, Wolof men watching from the shallows. A person (Lancarote de Freitas) is named, a place (mouth of the Senegal River) is anchored, and stakes are present from the first line. The reader is dropped into the scene before any context arrives. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Wolof world emerges naturally through action: the buur's stable as authority, the gewel as institutional memory, the jon as a defined social category, the Burba Jolof as paramount chief managing a confederacy's geometry. None of this is explained to the reader; it is simply the operating reality. The confederacy's political logic (north-south trade axis, coastal vs. inland power balance) shapes the prose without being taught. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | Zero outside judgment. The Portuguese are visitors in the Wolof world, not the other way around. The Wolof assess the Portuguese, not vice versa. The horse-for-captive trade is presented in Wolof terms (jon as a legal category, the exchange as an extension of existing practice) without modern apologetics or condemnation. Zurara's "puzzled respect" is the outsider's limitation rendered as inside characterization (v1.2 technique). |
| Texture   | 4/5   | Strong sensory ground: horses stumbling on sand, legs stiff, nostrils wide, flanks dark with sweat. The beach, the shallows, the longboat. The Saharan trade's material goods (salt, dried fish, cotton cloth, gold dust, kola nuts). The ceddo warriors. However, the middle and closing passages become more analytical and lose some physical grounding. The Burba Jolof's deliberation could use a sensory anchor (where is he sitting? what season?). |
| Voice     | 4/5   | The prose has a distinctive diplomatic register: measured, observational, calculating. It matches the "discover" voice well. The rhythm shifts between close observation and political geometry. The closing paragraph's repetition ("would be made again and again") echoes the gewel's accumulative cadence. Minor wobble: "the geometry of his own confederacy shifting beneath him" leans slightly toward modern analytical language. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | The opening creates genuine narrative tension: a trade axis is shifting, a confederacy's balance is being quietly destabilized, and the final sentence ("each time with the balance a little harder to hold") opens a question the reader wants answered. The pull is structural, not a cliffhanger trick. |
