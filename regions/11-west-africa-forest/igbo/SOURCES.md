# Sources — Igbo

## Primary / Archaeological

- Igbo-Ukwu excavations — Shaw, Thurstan; 1959–1964; the bronzes and their context
- Igbo oral traditions — maintained by community historians and lineage elders
- Colonial administrative records — Nigerian National Archives, Enugu and Ibadan
- Chinua Achebe, *Things Fall Apart* (1958) — literary primary source; the Igbo world from inside

## Academic

- Shaw, Thurstan. *Igbo-Ukwu: An Account of Archaeological Discoveries in Eastern Nigeria*. 2 vols. 1970. Faber & Faber.
- Achebe, Chinua. *Morning Yet on Creation Day*. 1975. Heinemann. — essays on Igbo culture and literature
- Henderson, Richard N. *The King in Every Man: Evolutionary Trends in Onitsha Ibo Society and Culture*. 1972. Yale University Press.
- Isichei, Elizabeth. *A History of the Igbo People*. 1976. Macmillan.
- Northrup, David. *Trade Without Rulers: Pre-Colonial Economic Development in South-Eastern Nigeria*. 1978. Oxford University Press.
- Uchendu, Victor C. *The Igbo of Southeast Nigeria*. 1965. Holt, Rinehart and Winston.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent archaeological surveys of Igboland (post-Shaw; settlement and agricultural archaeology)
- Studies on the Arochukwu oracle's commercial network specifically (Aro traders, routes, scale)
- Scholarship on the masquerade institution as governance (contemporary anthropological work)
- Analysis of chi in Igbo philosophy (Achebe's own essay "Chi in Igbo Cosmology" is essential)
