# R1-P1 — Barbara Tuchman — Panel Review of The Rise of Sargon (Opening)

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Artifact:** opening.md

## What Works

The opening line is perfect. "Sargon poured the wine for the king of Kish." Nine words, and I know where I am, who I am watching, and what the power dynamic is. The reader does not need to be told that a cupbearer is subordinate to a king. The act of pouring tells it.

The arc is clean: a man who pours wine becomes the man who holds the cup. The final line mirrors the first without repeating it, and the space between them is the story of the world's first empire. This is earned closure, not a trick.

The handling of the birth legend is skilled. "Whether the story is true or useful does not matter here. What matters is what the story told the people who repeated it." This lets the reader hold the legend without believing it and without dismissing it.

The thirty-four battles, the neck stock at Enlil's gate, the weapons washed in the Lower Sea. These are specific. They land. The specificity makes the conquest real without requiring a battle-by-battle account.

## What Needs Work

The middle section on Ishtar's favor (paragraph 5) is necessary but risks stalling. The theological explanation of divine election is important, but it reads closer to exposition than scene. I want to feel Ishtar's presence the way I feel the wine being poured. Can the divine election be dramatized rather than explained? The tradition about Ishtar appearing to Sargon could be a scene rather than a summary.

The march south is told in results rather than experience. "Sargon marched against him." "Sargon fought thirty-four battles." These are declarative and strong, but they are all outcomes. One moment from the march itself, a single detail from a single battle, would ground the conquest in a body rather than a list.

## The Question I'd Push On

Where is Sargon's body? He pours wine, he stands in court, he washes his weapons in the sea. But between the court and the sea there is a march of hundreds of miles through the hottest plain in the world, and the man who walks it is never sweating, never tired, never standing in dust. The opening needs one physical moment from the conquest itself to keep Sargon embodied through the middle.
