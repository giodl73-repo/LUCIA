# Sources — Republic of China

## Key Academic Works
- Jonathan Spence, *The Search for Modern China* (3rd ed., 2012, Norton) — the standard comprehensive history
- Rana Mitter, *A Bitter Revolution: China's Struggle with the Modern World* (2004, Oxford)
- Jay Taylor, *The Generalissimo: Chiang Kai-shek and the Struggle for Modern China* (2009, Harvard)
- Shelley Rigger, *Why Taiwan Matters: Small Island, Global Powerhouse* (2011, Rowman & Littlefield)
- Evan Osnos — China and Taiwan; *Age of Ambition* (2014) and related work
- Kerr George, *Formosa Betrayed* (1965) — the 228 Incident from an American witness

## Primary Sources
- Sun Yat-sen, *The Three Principles of the People* (Sanmin Zhuyi, 1924)
- Lu Xun — selected stories and essays; the literary conscience of the May Fourth generation
- The 228 Incident Research Report (1992) — the first official Taiwanese government acknowledgment
- Lee Teng-hui's apology for the 228 Incident (1995) and related speeches

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Academia Sinica Historical Archives (Taipei) — the most complete Republican-era archive
- Hoover Institution Archives — Chiang Kai-shek diaries (donated; partially digitized)
- 228 Memorial Foundation oral history collection
- Contemporary Taiwan identity survey data — Election Study Center, NCCU
