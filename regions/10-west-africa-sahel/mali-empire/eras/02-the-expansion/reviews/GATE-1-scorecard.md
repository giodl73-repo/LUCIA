# GATE-1 Scorecard — "When the Empire Reached the Sea" Opening

**Scorer:** LUCIA Rubric v1.1
**Date:** 2026-04-14

## Scores

| Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Scene     | 5/5   | The opening drops the reader into a specific, unrepeatable moment: a captain kneeling in the courtyard at Niani, telling Abu Bakr what happened to the fleet. The canoes, the color of the water, the disappearances — this is a scene with physical immediacy and dramatic stakes from the first line. "The canoes came back wrong" is a five-word hook that creates instant disorientation and pull. |
| World     | 5/5   | The Mande world's logic emerges without explanation. The jelilu stand at the courtyard's edges, listening. The mansa receives news on a raised platform. The Kurukan Fuga is invoked not as anthropological context but as the force that will generate the chapter's central question. The Dyula merchants, the goldfields, the salt trade — all appear as the furniture of a lived world, not as items in a briefing. The jelilu's distinction between "died" and "did not come back" reveals an epistemological precision that belongs to the oral tradition, presented from inside. |
| Honesty   | 5/5   | No outside judgment. No forbidden words. No modern framing. Abu Bakr's Atlantic voyage is presented as the jelilu present it — with honest uncertainty about the outcome and no attempt to impose modern conclusions. "The jelilu do not say he died. They say he did not come back. There is a difference." This is Principle 13 (Honest Uncertainty) working at full strength. The opening never steps outside the Mande worldview. |
| Texture   | 4/5   | "The red dust of the courtyard," the water changing "from brown to green to a blue so deep it was almost black," the current that "pulled away from the land," canoes that "simply did not return" — these are vivid and specific. The courtyard at Niani has physical presence. However, the opening is lighter on sensory anchoring than the Era 3 opening (which had dust, shea butter, woodsmoke, harmattan haze). The captain's account is visual but the courtyard scene itself could use one more grounding detail — heat, smell, the sound of the courtyard. Holds at 4 rather than 5. |
| Voice     | 5/5   | The prose carries the oral tradition's cadence: accumulative ("He said the water changed... He said the current... He said canoes began..."), declarative ("Abu Bakr could have stopped there"), and weighted with the jeli's precision about what is known and what is not. The rhythm belongs to this story. The final paragraph's question — "what happens when the covenant holds and the king does not?" — lands with the weight of a proverb. No register breaks. |
| Pull      | 5/5   | The opening creates a double pull. First: the mystery of the fleet (what took the canoes? where did the current lead?). Second: the structural question announced in the final paragraph (the covenant tested by bad kings). The reader wants to follow both threads. Abu Bakr's decision to go himself is a narrative bomb — the king vanishes, and the reader must continue to learn what his absence means. |
| **Total** | **29/30** | |

## Gate Check
- **Threshold: 24+/30** — **PASSED** (29/30)

## Notes
The opening is exceptionally strong in Scene, World, Honesty, Voice, and Pull. The one dimension held below maximum is Texture — the courtyard scene is vivid but could be grounded with one additional sensory detail (the heat of the day, the smell of the Niger, the sound beyond the captain's voice). This is a minor note. The opening's greatest achievement is the voice: the jeli's epistemological precision about what is known and what is not known, rendered not as commentary but as the narrative's own method. The distinction between "died" and "did not come back" is the opening's signature moment — it establishes the chapter's relationship to uncertainty in a single sentence.

The structural engine is clearly signaled: the Kurukan Fuga tested by succession crisis. The final paragraph's question is the chapter's thesis, and it arrives earned.

Em-dash count: 3 (limit: 5). Clean.
