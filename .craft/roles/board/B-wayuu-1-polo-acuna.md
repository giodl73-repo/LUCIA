# B-Wayúu-1 — José Polo Acuña

## Background

José Polo Acuña is a Colombian historian specializing in the political and social history of the Guajira Peninsula during the colonial and early republican periods. His *Indígenas, poderes y mediaciones en La Guajira en la transición de la Colonia a la República (1750–1850)* (2012) is the most detailed scholarly study of the Great Cattle Era, drawing on the Riohacha archive, the Archivo General de Indias, and regional ecclesiastical records. He has also published on indigenous resistance, the pütchipü'üi tradition in colonial context, and the economic history of the Caribbean coast.
