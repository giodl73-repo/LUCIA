# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Kazakh oral tradition — zhyrau and akyn poetry (transcribed from 19th century onward)
- Russian imperial records — the tsarist documentation of zhuz submissions and steppe administration
- Zheti Zhargy (Seven Statutes) — transmitted through oral legal tradition; first recorded texts 19th century
- Abylai Khan correspondence — diplomatic exchanges with Russia and Qing China

## Key Academic Works

- Martha Brill Olcott, *The Kazakhs* (2nd ed., 1995) — standard political history
- Nicola Di Cosmo, Allen Frank, and Peter Golden, eds., *The Cambridge History of Inner Asia: The Chinggisid Age* (2009)
- Virginia Martin, *Law and Custom in the Steppe: The Kazakhs of the Middle Horde and Russian Colonialism in the Nineteenth Century* (2001)
- Daniel Prior — Kazakh oral epic tradition
- Peter Golden — Kazakh formation and Türkic connections
