# Panel-2 Review — Chinua Achebe
## Hausa Era 3: The Manufacturing Peak (v2 notes)

**Focus:** Inside voice, cultural dignity, the Bori section

The composite figures are handled correctly. Ibrahim is named and given a specific professional context, with the composite disclosure in figures.md. The senior Bori woman is unnamed, which is right — the Bori tradition's practitioners are not typically named in the sources for this period, and naming her would be invention without basis. The anonymity is honest.

**The Bori section:** I am watching the word "practices." The notes say "the Bori as trust technology" and "Bori practice." These are outside-category framings that can leak into the prose. The chapter should not describe the Bori as a "practice" from the outside. It should inhabit the Bori as a reality from the inside. The spirits are real in Kano. The senior Bori woman does not "practice" the Bori; she works with the spirits. This is the difference.

**On the trade:** The notes frame the slave trade correctly as "human extraction from the south, inside the Hausa system's own categories." This is right. The chapter must be specific about what those categories were — the Maliki legal tradition's distinctions between who could be enslaved and who could not, the qadi's administrative machinery, the sarki's taxation of the slave markets. These are not apologies. They are the historical reality rendered with precision.

**On the outside validation risk:** The notes flag this. I am adding one specific warning: the phrase "the cloth that North African merchants called Kano cloth" risks centering the North African observer's naming as the product's identity. Kano had its own name for its cloth. If the chapter uses the North African term, it should be framed as the North Africans' name for a thing the Kano guild already knew.

**Verdict:** Ready to execute. The Bori section is the critical test. If it holds inside-voice register throughout, the chapter will pass the Achebe Test.
