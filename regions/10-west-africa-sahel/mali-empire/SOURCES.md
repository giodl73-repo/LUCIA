# Sources — Mali Empire

## Primary / Oral

- Sundiata Epic — oral tradition maintained by Mande jelilu; Djibril Tamsir Niane's transcription (1960) is the standard accessible version
- Ibn Battuta, *Rihla* (Travels), c. 1355 CE — first-hand account of the Mali court
- Al-Umari, *Masalik al-Absar fi Mamalik al-Amsar*, c. 1342 CE — account of Mansa Musa's hajj based on Cairo witnesses
- Ibn Khaldun, *Kitab al-Ibar* — history of the western Sudan
- Catalan Atlas, 1375 — European map depicting Mansa Musa

## Academic

- Levtzion, Nehemia. *Ancient Ghana and Mali*. 1973. Methuen.
- Niane, Djibril Tamsir. *Sundiata: An Epic of Old Mali*. 1965. Longman.
- Conrad, David C. *Empires of Medieval West Africa*. 2005. Chelsea House.
- Hunwick, John O. *Timbuktu and the Songhay Empire*. 1999. Brill.
- Gomez, Michael A. *African Dominion: A New History of Empire in Early and Medieval West Africa*. 2018. Princeton University Press.
- Levtzion, Nehemia & Spaulding, Jay (eds.). *Medieval West Africa*. 2003. Markus Wiener.
