## The Chronicle (60 points)

| Immersion   | 9/10  | The reader lives inside the principate: the Forum Augustum, the insulae of Roma, the road through Gaul, the Danube frontier. The city section (baths, streets, fullers' shops, thermopolia) is the most immersive passage in the Roman book. |
| Evidence    | 9/10  | Dense: the Forum Augustum's decorative program, the aqueduct count, Juvenal's complaint about noise, the Teutoburg Forest (legions XVII-XIX never reused), the Constitutio Antoniniana of 212 CE, Gaius's three categories, Marcus Aurelius's Meditations (quoted in translation). All verifiable. |
| Progression | 8/10  | The structural engine (the fiction as load-bearing architecture) drives the chapter from the settlement through the city, the infrastructure, the law, and the emperors. The fiction is maintained, tested (Nero, Domitian, the Year of the Four Emperors), and recovered (the adoptive emperors). Scores 8 rather than 9 because the chapter covers the widest timespan (27 BCE-192 CE) and the breadth occasionally costs depth. |
| Figures     | 8/10  | Augustus is presented through his buildings, not psychologized. Marcus Aurelius is given the Meditations and the Danube and the Commodus decision. Gaius is characterized through his work. The emperor catalog (Section V) is efficient but some figures (Trajan, Hadrian) receive less space than their significance warrants. |
| Voice       | 9/10  | The voice expands: longer sentences, the weight of marble and inscription, the managed acoustics. The legal section sustains the register through institutional description. The city section is the voice at its most sensory. The landing returns to the Forum and the geese, tying the book together. |
| Landing     | 9/10  | The closing seeds Christianity through the metaphor of water spreading through brick. The marble/brick opposition from the opening returns with a new meaning: the marble must account for something that entered through the brick. The geese/augures/Senate refrain continues the book-level structural device. |
