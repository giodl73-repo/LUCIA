# R2-P4 — Wade Davis
## Panel Review (NOTES): The Classic Taíno

The notes identify the right target for Section 3: the areíto at method level, using Pané's recordings of the mythology as evidence of what the living library contains. This is the correct approach. What I want to flag is the specific level of technical detail required.

**The cohoba ceremony:**

The notes flag this as an open question. I want to specify what is needed: the cohoba ritual (inhalation of hallucinogenic powder, likely from Anadenanthera peregrina seeds, through forked tubes called tabaco, with the cacique purging before the ceremony to cleanse himself for the zemí consultation) is one of the most precisely documented Taíno practices in Pané and in the early Spanish accounts. The process: the preparation of the powder, the carved platform (the duho) where the cacique sits, the inhalation, the period of altered perception during which the zemí communicates, the cacique's subsequent report to the community of what the zemí has said. This is not a detail to mention in passing. This is the areíto's analytic counterpart: where the areíto is the community's collective transmission medium, the cohoba ritual is the individual practitioner's interface with the zemí. Show both. Show the epistemological complementarity.

**The cave mythology:**

The Cacibajagua emergence narrative (the first people come out of a cave when the sun catches the guardian who was supposed to stop them from leaving, and those who linger at the entrance are turned into birds or trees) is one of the most beautiful and specific cosmological accounts in the Caribbean record. Pané recorded it. It belongs in the areíto library section. The narrative is not "about" creation in an abstract sense — it is about the transformation between states of being that is the fundamental Taíno cosmological concern. The dead become zemís. The living become ancestors. The transformation is continuous. The areíto is part of what maintains it.

**What the notes are missing:**

The women's manioc knowledge deserves method-level treatment. The processing of bitter manioc — grating, pressing out the prussic acid in the tipití press, heating and drying into casabi — is as technically demanding as any navigational or pharmaceutical knowledge in the project. The conuco mound system (which maintains soil moisture and drainage better than flat planting, and which is the material foundation of the areíto's community feeding requirement) should be shown at the level of how it works, not what it produces.

**Overall:** The method-level ambition is right. Deliver it in execution.
