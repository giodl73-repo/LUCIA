# Sources — Celts (Irish, Welsh, Breton, Cornish, Scots Gaelic)

## Primary Sources

- *Lebor Gabála Érenn* (Book of the Invasions) — 11th-century compilation of earlier oral tradition; mythological history of Ireland's origins
- *Táin Bó Cúailnge* (The Cattle Raid of Cooley) — earliest long narrative poem in any vernacular European language; preserved in *Lebor na hUidre* (c. 1100)
- *Senchus Már* — the great Irish brehon law compilation; 7th–8th century texts
- *Cyfraith Hywel* (Laws of Hywel Dda) — Welsh law code; 10th century, surviving in 13th-century manuscripts
- *Annals of Ulster*, *Annals of the Four Masters* — chronological records of Irish events
- *Vita Sancti Columbae* (Life of Columba) by Adomnán — c. 697 CE; primary source for Iona and the peregrini
- *Historia Brittonum* attributed to Nennius — 9th century; Welsh historical tradition including early Arthur material
- Book of Kells — c. 800 CE; Iona/Irish manuscript; primary visual evidence of Celtic spiritual aesthetics
