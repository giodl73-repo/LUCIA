**Notes:** A strong chapter that earns its score across all dimensions. The Du Fu through-line is the chapter's greatest structural achievement, turning a well-known period into a personal story without losing the systemic analysis. The poetry passages, particularly "Spring Prospect" and the Chengdu cottage poem, function simultaneously as evidence, characterization, and emotional weight. The structural analysis (Section II) satisfies Ibn Khaldun's demand for causal depth without breaking voice. The farmer passage addresses the "view from below" concern effectively within a short space. The landing is effective though not exceptional.

**Innovations flagged:** 1

**Innovation: Poetry as Structural Evidence**
The chapter uses Du Fu's and Li Bai's actual poems not as decoration or illustration but as primary evidence, characterization, and structural turning points simultaneously. "Spring Prospect" functions as the chapter's emotional hinge, the rebellion section's evidence base, and Du Fu's character revelation in a single eight-line poem. This technique treats poetry as a knowledge system (per Davis's demand) while satisfying narrative craft requirements (per Tuchman) within the same textual move.
