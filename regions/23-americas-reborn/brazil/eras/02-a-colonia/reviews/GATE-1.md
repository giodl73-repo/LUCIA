## Notes

**What works exceptionally well:**
- The call-and-response structure embedded directly in the prose body (not as epigraph or block break) is a new application of the register. The four-line Q&A section ("Call: Who brought the orixás...") operates both as narrative and as demonstration of the knowledge system — the knowledge system performing itself.
- "It could not be confiscated. It could not be inventoried." — this is the No Verdicts technique at its best: the honesty belongs to the culture's own epistemology, not to editorial restraint.
- The Serra da Barriga naming ("the hill with the name of a belly, as though the land itself had swallowed something") is textural and thematic simultaneously — the land's body echoing the people's survival.
- Em-dash count: 0. Register maintained without mechanical correction.

**What to watch in the chapter:**
- The call-and-response structure must be sustained at 5000-word length without becoming mechanical. The Q&A insertion is effective once; at full chapter length it will need to modulate — appearing at structural pivots, not at regular intervals.
- The engenho section risks becoming a survey if not anchored to a specific person by the chapter's middle. The opening earns this from Zumbi's perspective; the chapter must find an equivalent inside voice for the sugar economy.
- Voice score held at 4 rather than 5 due to one explanatory sentence. Chapter draft should eliminate this class of lapse entirely.

**Gate decision:** PASSED. 29/30. Proceed to PANEL-1.
