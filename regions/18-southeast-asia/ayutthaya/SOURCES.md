# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Ayutthayan royal chronicles (*Phongsawadan*) — multiple recensions
- Dutch and Portuguese merchant accounts (16th–17th century)
- Chinese tribute records — Ayutthaya's extensive tribute relationship with Ming
- Japanese records — Japanese merchant and ronin communities in Ayutthaya

## Key Academic Works

- David Wyatt, *Thailand: A Short History* (2003) — standard political history
- Lorraine Gesick, ed., *Centers, Symbols, and Hierarchies: Essays on the Classical States of Southeast Asia* (1983)
- Chris Baker and Pasuk Phongpaichit, *A History of Thailand* (3rd ed., 2014)
- Dhiravat na Pombejra — Ayutthayan foreign relations and trade
- Barend Jan Terwiel — Thai social history

## Needed

- Studies on the reconstruction of Ayutthayan court culture from fragmentary sources
- Analysis of the Japanese and Persian communities in 17th-century Ayutthaya
- Recent archaeological work on the Ayutthaya urban site
