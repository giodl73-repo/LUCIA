# Sources — Balts (Lithuanians, Latvians, Old Prussians)

## Primary Sources

- Gediminas's letters (1323) — the invitations to western settlers; surviving in multiple copies
- *Livonian Rhymed Chronicle* — 13th-century German account of the conquest of Latvia and Estonia
- Henry of Latvia, *Chronicon Livoniae* — the primary Latin chronicle of Baltic Christianization; 13th century
- *Grunwald* sources — Polish-Lithuanian chronicles; Długosz's account
- The Teutonic Order's chronicles and records — *Dusburg*, *Wigand von Marburg*
- *Hypatian Chronicle* — Russian source with extensive Baltic material

## Secondary Sources

- Christiansen, Eric. *The Northern Crusades*. Penguin, 1997.
- Mažiulis, Vytautas. *Prūsų kalbos etimologijos žodynas*. 4 vols. 1988–1997. (Old Prussian linguistic heritage)
- Rowell, S.C. *Lithuania Ascending: A Pagan Empire Within East-Central Europe 1295–1345*. Cambridge University Press, 1994.
- Bojtár, Endre. *Foreword to the Past: A Cultural History of the Baltic People*. CEU Press, 1999.
- Urban, William. *The Baltic Crusade*. 2nd ed. Lithuanian Research and Studies Center, 1994.
- Kiaupa, Zigmantas, Jūratė Kiaupienė, and Albinas Kunevičius. *The History of Lithuania Before 1795*. Lithuanian Institute of History, 2000.
