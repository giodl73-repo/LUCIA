# Sources — Middle Kingdom Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Coffin Texts — Middle Kingdom wooden coffins; democratized afterlife spells
- "The Tale of Sinuhe" — literary papyri, multiple copies; the defining Middle Kingdom narrative
- "The Eloquent Peasant" — Papyrus Berlin; justice and eloquence as political themes
- "The Instruction of Amenemhat" — royal instruction literature; kingship and betrayal
- Lahun Papyri — administrative, medical, and mathematical documents from the workers' town at the pyramid of Senusret II
- Semna dispatches — fortress administrative records from Nubia; the logistics of frontier control

## Academic

- Callender, Gae. "The Middle Kingdom Renaissance." In *The Oxford History of Ancient Egypt*, ed. Ian Shaw. 2000.
- Parkinson, R.B. *Poetry and Culture in Middle Kingdom Egypt*. 2002. Continuum. — the literary tradition in context
- Quirke, Stephen. *Egyptian Literature 1800 BC*. 2004. Golden House. — translations with commentary
- Grajetzki, Wolfram. *The Middle Kingdom of Ancient Egypt*. 2006. Duckworth. — political and administrative history
- Wegner, Josef. *The Mortuary Temple of Senwosret III at Abydos*. 2007. — royal cult in the Twelfth Dynasty
- Robins, Gay. *The Art of Ancient Egypt*. 1997. Harvard University Press. — Middle Kingdom artistic tradition
