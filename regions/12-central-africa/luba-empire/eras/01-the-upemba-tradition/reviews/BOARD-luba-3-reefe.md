## Historical Accuracy Assessment

**Periodization:** The chapter's dating of the Upemba tradition as c. 500–1500 CE is consistent with the archaeological chronology and with my own treatment of the pre-kingdom period in *The Rainbow and the Kings*. The chapter correctly presents this as the material foundation for the kingdom rather than as the kingdom itself.

**Kalala Ilunga:** The chapter names him once, correctly, as the horizon of Era 2 — not as a figure in the Upemba tradition itself. The statement "When Kalala Ilunga came from the west" is consistent with the oral tradition's account of the founding. My own treatment of the founding narrative — that it encodes a real political process of integration rather than necessarily the biography of a single individual — is not required to appear in this chapter; the chapter is about the era before the founding, and naming the founding figure as a future event is appropriate.

**The bulopwe concept:** Bulopwe is correctly described as a quality of sacred kingship that can be transmitted, not merely a hereditary succession. The chapter does not attempt to define bulopwe for the Upemba tradition period; it holds it as a concept that will arrive with the kingdom. This is correct.

**The oral tradition's span:** The chapter is appropriately cautious about the oral tradition's reach into this period. The note in figures.md that the oral tradition's recoverable layers begin with the Kalala Ilunga foundation is accurate.

**The Katanga copper belt:** Described correctly as the source of the handa ingots. The chapter notes the ingots' appearance at Lake Kisale in approximately the 8th century CE; this is consistent with the archaeological record as I understand it.

## Corrections Required

**Correction 1 (required):**
The chapter states that the exchange network included "salt from the north." The primary salt source in the Luba world was not to the north of Lake Kisale but involved routes to the west (toward the upper Kasai) and south. The Katanga region had some salt deposits, and salt also came from the upper Lualaba. "Salt from the north" is geographically imprecise. Recommend changing to "salt from the upper Lualaba routes" or simply "salt from the interior trade routes" to avoid the directional error.

**Correction 2 (clarification):**
The figures.md cites Reefe 1981 as the source for "Kalala Ilunga's historical status." I want to note that my treatment in *The Rainbow and the Kings* Chapter 1 is cautious about the historicity of Kalala Ilunga as an individual; I treat the founding narrative as encoding political process. The chapter's phrasing — "carrying the sacred principle of kingship that would become bulopwe" — is a narrative representation that I accept as appropriate for this project's genre while noting that the oral tradition's accounts should be understood as political memory rather than biography.

---

## Overall Assessment

The chapter is historically responsible. It stays within what the evidence supports, is honest about the limits of the record, and does not project the kingdom's structure backward onto the Upemba tradition. The narrative reconstruction is plausible and the figures are handled with appropriate disclosure in figures.md.

**Recommendation: PASS with Correction 1 required before FINAL.**
