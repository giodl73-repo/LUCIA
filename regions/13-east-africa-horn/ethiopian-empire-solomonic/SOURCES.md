# Sources — Ethiopian Empire (Solomonic)

## Primary

- Kebra Nagast — the foundational Solomonic text; trans. E.A. Wallis Budge (1922); more recent translations available
- Ethiopian royal chronicles — Amharic and Ge'ez; the Ba'eda Maryam chronicles; Menelik II's chronicles
- Haile Selassie I, *My Life and Ethiopia's Progress* (autobiography) — primary source on the twentieth century
- League of Nations speech (1936) — Haile Selassie's address; widely available

## Academic

- Tadesse Tamrat. *Church and State in Ethiopia 1270–1527*. 1972. Oxford University Press. — the foundational political history
- Pankhurst, Richard. *The Ethiopians: A History*. 1998. Blackwell. — comprehensive general history
- Marcus, Harold G. *A History of Ethiopia*. Updated ed. 2002. University of California Press.
- Donham, Donald & James, Wendy (eds.). *The Southern Marches of Imperial Ethiopia*. 1986. Cambridge University Press.
- Prouty, Chris. *Empress Taytu and Menilek II: Ethiopia 1883–1910*. 1986. Ravens Educational. — Adwa and the court behind it
- Levine, Donald N. *Greater Ethiopia: The Evolution of a Multiethnic Society*. 1974. University of Chicago Press.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on the Oromo expansion and Oromo-Solomonic interactions
