## From: hypothesis

**CLAIM**: Model capability affects the three measured outputs of AI-assisted creative production differently. Quality (mean GATE-2 combined score) moves over a narrow band across the Opus/Sonnet/Haiku ladder; innovation-discovery rate (innovations-per-chapter promoted to the Innovation Engine) varies more; pipeline-compliance-and-reporting reliability varies most. Model choice is not neutral, but the dominant substrate effect is on *process adherence*, not on *prose quality*.

**FALSIFICATION**: All three models produce statistically indistinguishable score distributions AND indistinguishable innovation rates AND indistinguishable reporting reliability → substrate thesis falsified; pipeline dominates (C2 hypothesis).

**CONFIDENCE**: 70. Session 2 provides a clean matched-region experiment (Region 9 Opus, Region 12 Sonnet, Region 26 Haiku) with identical pipeline instructions, identical reviewer panel, identical rubric, and documented outcomes in `agents/AGENT-ROSTER.md`. Sample sizes are small (n=9, 6, 5) but the reporting-reliability effect is dramatic (100% / 100% / 20%).

### Findings

**F-01**: Mean GATE-2 combined score — Opus 81.22 (n=9, SD 1.09), Sonnet 82.50 (n=6, SD 2.26), Haiku 83.20 (n=5, SD 0.84). Welch's t-test Opus vs Sonnet: t=1.29, df=6.59, p≈0.24. Mann-Whitney U on the same comparison: U=9, z=-2.12, p≈0.034. The 1.28-point Opus-Sonnet gap is at-boundary — NS under parametric test, MS under rank test.

**F-02**: Innovations-per-chapter (from `scoring/INNOVATIONS.md` grep): Opus 2/9 = 0.22, Sonnet 10/6 = 1.67, Haiku 3/5 = 0.60. Exact Poisson rate-ratio test Opus vs Sonnet: RR=7.5, p≈0.006. Substrate effect on discovery IS significant.

**F-03**: Pipeline-and-reporting compliance (from `agents/AGENT-ROSTER.md` Model Comparison Summary): Opus 8/8 (100%), Sonnet 6/6 (100%), Haiku 1/5 (20% clean end-of-spawn reports). Fisher's exact Opus+Sonnet vs Haiku: p < 0.002. Significant at strong confidence.

**F-04**: Artifact-level pipeline-completion (counting presence of the 11 nominal artifacts per chapter): Opus mean 4.67/11 (42%), Sonnet 8.50/11 (77%), Haiku 8.40/11 (76%). The **Opus paradox** — clean spawn-report but missing intermediate artifacts — is real: Opus chapters lock via out-of-band scoring without the full 11-file trail. This finding belongs to C2 but C1 must surface it as a threat to validity.

**F-05**: The Haiku "failure" is NOT a quality-of-prose failure. Mean 83.2 is the highest of three (though noise-bounded). Haiku fails at the margin of process: missing end-of-spawn reports, inconsistent file naming, requires LUCY-side manual reconstruction.

## From: derivation

### Welch's t (Opus vs Sonnet)
μ_O=81.22, μ_S=82.50, s²_O=1.196, s²_S=5.1, n_O=9, n_S=6.
SE = sqrt(1.196/9 + 5.1/6) = 0.991. t = 1.28/0.991 = 1.29. df_W = 6.59.
p (two-tailed) ≈ 0.24. NS.
Cohen's d = 0.779 (medium-large).

### Mann-Whitney U (Opus vs Sonnet)
U=9, z=-2.12, p ≈ 0.034. MS.

### Exact Poisson rate-ratio (innovations)
Under H0 equal rates: Sonnet innovations ~ Binomial(12, 0.4). Observed 10.
P(X ≥ 10) ≈ 0.00281. Two-sided ≈ 0.0056. Significant.

### Fisher's exact (reporting)
Opus+Sonnet (14/14) vs Haiku (1/5). Two-sided p < 0.002.

## From: synthesize

**PROCEED.** Pre-write score: 20/25.

**Core thesis**: Model substrate matters — but not in the way benchmarking typically measures. Across matched conditions, prose-quality differences are noise-bounded (1-2 points on 90), discovery-rate differences are 7.5× at point estimate, reporting-reliability differences are 5×. The default-SCRIBE decision (Sonnet) is justified not by the 1.28-point score edge but by the 7.5× discovery edge and the 5× reliability edge.

**Secondary thesis**: Haiku-fails-process-not-prose isolates a capability standard LLM benchmarks do not measure. This motivates C2 and C3.
