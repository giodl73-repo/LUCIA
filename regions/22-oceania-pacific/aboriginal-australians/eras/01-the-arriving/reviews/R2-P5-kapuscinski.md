# P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — R2 Review of Notes (Production Bible)

**Overall:** The sensory plan is better in the notes than in the opening. But the notes are promises. The chapter must pay them.

**What works:**
- The fire section will be a sensory feast if done right. Fire has sound, smell, heat, visual dynamics. A cool-season burn in spinifex country produces specific effects: the crackle and rush of the fire front, the smell of burning resin, the heat on the face, the black ground afterward, the green shoots emerging days later. If the chapter puts the reader inside the burn with Ngali, it will be the most physically present passage in the entire manuscript.
- The rock art section plan promises to put the reader inside the rock shelter. This must happen. The reader must feel the stone overhead, see the light falling across the painted surface, smell the dust. The Kimberley shelters have a specific quality: the sandstone is warm, the shadows are deep, the paintings emerge gradually as the eyes adjust.

**What doesn't:**
- The stars section risks being the least sensory. Stars are visual, but the act of reading the sky is also a body experience: lying on your back on warm sand, the coolness of night, the sheer scale of the sky in country with no light pollution. The section must ground the astronomical knowledge in the physical experience of looking up.
- The kinship section has no sensory plan at all. Kinship is abstract. But the declaration of skin names happens between bodies: two people facing each other, the words spoken, the immediate shift in behavior that follows. This needs to be a physical encounter.

**The question I'd push on:**
The smell of the country. I asked for it in PANEL-1 and it is noted in the revision plan but not yet specified. Western Desert after rain smells of resin and mineral dust and something green rising from the red earth. Dry season smells of heat and stone. These are specific. The chapter needs them.
