# Sources — Republic of Korea (South Korea)

## Key Academic Works
- Bruce Cumings, *Korea's Place in the Sun: A Modern History* (1997, Norton) — the standard English-language history
- Bruce Cumings, *The Origins of the Korean War* (2 vols., 1981, 1990, Princeton)
- Gi-Wook Shin, *Ethnic Nationalism in Korea* (2006, Stanford)
- Katharine H.S. Moon — Korean civil society and women's movements
- Ha-Joon Chang, *Bad Samaritans: The Myth of Free Trade and the Secret History of Capitalism* (2008) — the developmental state argument
- Euny Hong, *The Birth of Korean Cool* (2014) — hallyu and its origins
- Hwang Sok-yong — Korean novelist; the literary conscience of the democracy movement

## Primary Sources
- The May 18th Gwangju Uprising records — Gwangju Metropolitan City archives
- The Jeju 4.3 Commission Report (2003)
- The Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) Korea reports (2010–present)
- BTS speeches, interviews, and music — as primary cultural sources
