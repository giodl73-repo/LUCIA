**Innovations flagged for INNOVATIONS.md consideration:**

1. **Canonical-juridical evidence as both epistemic medium and structural engine**: The chapter uses the formal citation of actual canon law texts (the Unam Sanctam's exact words, the confession canon's penalty clause) as structural proof that the papacy's system operated at this level of specificity. This may qualify as a new application of the v1.3 "Primary Art as Structural Evidence" technique to legal-juridical cultures where the text is simultaneously the culture's primary art form and its governing instrument.

2. **Legitimacy-debt as a named terminal condition**: The chapter identifies a terminal condition not previously in the v1.6 taxonomy. The legitimacy gap (noted in v1.5) is close but not identical: the v1.5 instance (Songhai) involves military authority failing to generate commercial-legal legitimacy. The papal instance involves a spiritual-juridical authority whose fiscal machine erodes the spiritual credibility the juridical claims require. This is a distinct mechanism: not a gap between two kinds of authority, but a self-undermining loop within one authority's own apparatus. Worth logging for potential taxonomy expansion.
