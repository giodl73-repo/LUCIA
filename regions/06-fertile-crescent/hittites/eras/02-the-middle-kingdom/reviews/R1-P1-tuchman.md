## P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of Hittites Era 2 Opening

**Overall:** This opening does something I rarely see: it makes a constitutional document into a page-turner. The litany of murders in paragraph two has the cumulative force of a body count in a crime novel, and the pivot to "Then he laid down the law" is perfectly timed.

**What works:**
- The opening three sentences are flawless. "Not a boast. Not a list of conquered cities. A confession." That is scene construction at its sharpest: the reader knows instantly that something unusual is happening.
- The parade of killings in paragraph two builds with terrifying momentum. Each sentence is one murder shorter than the story deserves, which is exactly right: the compression itself IS the horror. You do not need to dwell. The speed tells the reader what kind of kingdom this was.
- "Then he laid down the law." A four-word paragraph that turns the entire piece. This is pacing.
- The closing image — tablet on the wall vs. knife in the dark — gives the reader something to carry forward. It is not a cliffhanger; it is an honest question the chapter will answer.

**What doesn't:**
- The middle section (paragraphs 5-6), where the argument shifts from narrative to analysis of the theological mechanism, loses some of the physical momentum. I understand why it is there: the reader needs to grasp why the edict might work. But it reads slightly more like explanation than narration for about six sentences. Could the theological argument be shown rather than stated? A scene of the court assembly invoking the tablet, perhaps?
- "The harvests failed. The borders shrank. Enemies pressed in." These are excellent sentences but they arrive as claims without specific anchoring. Which harvests? Which borders? The chapter will presumably answer, but the opening could gesture toward one specific loss.

**The question I'd push on:**
The edict's text is quoted once, powerfully. But the opening does not yet show us what happens AFTER the edict. The pull depends on the reader wanting to know if it worked. Can the chapter deliver on that promise with historical evidence, or will it have to speculate? The opening has earned a high bar; the chapter must clear it.
