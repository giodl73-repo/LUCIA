# R2-P5 — Ryszard Kapuściński
## Panel Review: PANEL-2 — Notes

**Chapter:** Parthia / Era 3: The Roman Standoff
**Reviewer:** Ryszard Kapuściński (P-5)
**Stage:** PANEL-2

---

The production bible has addressed my concerns from PANEL-1. The Euripides scene will be physically developed. Isidore of Charax will walk the Silk Road. Abgar gets one sensory flash before the record's silence. I am satisfied with the intent.

The notes' most important physical promise is the caravanserai: "camel dung, cedar smoke, dried fruit in the saddlebags." I want the chapter to deliver exactly this — not generic desert smell but the specific mix that distinguishes a functioning Silk Road waystation from any other dusty camp. A caravanserai at its best is a miniature city: the smell of fires from a dozen directions, the sound of camels complaining in three languages, the particular silence after the gates close at dusk when two hundred merchants decide simultaneously that they are safe for the night. The customs moment — what goes in the ledger, who counts it, how the silver changes hands — should carry this physical texture.

**The physical gap I want addressed:**

The Armenian buffer. The notes mention "Armenian proxy wars" in the Equilibrium section but don't develop this. Armenia is where the Roman-Parthian standoff had its most continuous physical existence — armies actually campaigned there, met there, negotiated there, repeatedly. What did the Armenian highlands look like to a Parthian army moving north? What did the climate do to the cavalry? The mountain passes were not the Syrian plain. I want at least one physical passage that locates the standoff in a different landscape than Carrhae — the cold, the altitude, the green of the Armenian valleys as a surprise after the plateau. This creates sensory variety for the reader and also demonstrates that the Parthian military system operated across different terrains, not only the flat plain where it is most famous.

**What I will be checking in the chapter draft:**

- Is Ctesiphon given a physical address before the theater scene begins? One street, one smell, one specific building material.
- Does the customs post have a body in it? Who sits there? What does he write?
- Is the Abgar landing sensory before it is epistemic? The physical image first, then the record's silence.
