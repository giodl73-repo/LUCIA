## B-lunda-1 — Jan Vansina — Board Review of The Lueji-Chibinda Foundation

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Foundational reconstruction of Lunda history from oral tradition (Kingdoms of the Savanna, 1966); methodology for using oral tradition as historical evidence (Oral Tradition as History, 1985).

---

**Historical accuracy:**

- The chapter's characterization of the founding tradition as carrying "several reasons" for the brothers' departure is accurate and represents good historical practice. The oral tradition does give varying accounts. The chapter correctly treats this multiplicity as evidence about the tradition's navigational strategy rather than selecting one account as definitive.

- The lukanu's characterization is accurate: it is the object in which Lunda legitimacy resided, not merely a symbol of it. The chapter's rendering of the bracelet as an object with accumulating ancestral authority — each generation's wrist in the worn copper — is consistent with the tradition's own account of the object.

- The bulopwe's characterization is broadly accurate. The bulopwe was a recognized quality of authority associated with the Kalala Ilunga lineage, understood as both hereditary and visible in its bearers. The chapter's rendering of it as recognizable — "the bulopwe man reads the country the way the country reads the bulopwe man" — is consistent with how the tradition discusses it, though this specific formulation is the chapter's own construction rather than a quoted source.

- The dating convention (c. 1600-1650 CE) is the scholarly reconstruction from genealogical counting; the chapter uses it appropriately without claiming more certainty than the evidence supports.

- The Imbangala connection: the chapter notes that the departing headmen "seed the Imbangala and others in the west." This relationship is contested in the scholarship — the origins of the Imbangala are complex and not simply reducible to disaffected Lunda headmen. The chapter does not make a strong causal claim (it says "these become," not "caused"), but a future revision should perhaps note that the relationship between early Lunda departures and the Imbangala communities is one that scholars continue to debate, rather than treating it as established.

**Cultural authenticity:**

- The founding act — Lueji holding the lukanu and transmitting it to Chibinda Ilunga — is rendered in a way consistent with how the tradition presents it: as a founding act, not merely a marital transaction. The chapter does not reduce the transmission to romance or to political calculation; it shows it as what the tradition says it is: constitution.

- The Chibinda Ilunga figure is correctly characterized as a hunter rather than a warrior or a conqueror. The tradition consistently emphasizes his hunting identity, and the chapter is faithful to this.

- The composite figure technique for Katema is acceptable — the tradition does not name specific departing headmen in most versions, and the chapter discloses this in figures.md.

**What the chapter gets right:**

- The lukanu as structural engine operating across the full chapter, not merely as an introductory symbol.
- The source situation is handled with genuine honesty — the chapter acknowledges what the tradition preserves and does not and does not project false certainty onto the founding.
- The female authority is treated as the tradition treats it: as a specific, recognized form of legitimate power, not as an anomaly requiring explanation.

**What needs correction:**

- The Imbangala reference should be hedged slightly. Recommend: "some of whom would eventually be associated with the communities of the west that the tradition calls related to the Lunda departures — the Imbangala and others whose origins the scholarship continues to discuss."
- The phrase "grandson of the founding hero's own name" (in reference to Chibinda Ilunga) is slightly awkward. In the tradition, the genealogy is: Kalala Ilunga is the founding Luba hero; Chibinda Ilunga is identified as his son in most versions. "Grandson" is not how most versions identify him. Recommend revising to "son of Kalala Ilunga."

**Verdict: APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS**

Conditions: (1) Hedge the Imbangala reference. (2) Correct "grandson" to "son" in the Chibinda Ilunga genealogy.
