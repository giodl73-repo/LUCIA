# Sources — Ptolemaic Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Rosetta Stone — British Museum; trilingual priestly decree, 196 BCE; key to hieroglyphic decipherment
- Edfu temple inscriptions — the most complete set of Ptolemaic royal theological texts
- Papyri Zenon — third century BCE administrative archive; the economy of Ptolemaic Egypt
- Manetho, *Aegyptiaca* — Egyptian priest writing in Greek; the king-list tradition

## Academic

- Bowman, Alan K. *Egypt After the Pharaohs: 332 BC–AD 642*. 1986. University of California Press.
- Hölbl, Günther. *A History of the Ptolemaic Empire*. 2001. Routledge.
- Fraser, P.M. *Ptolemaic Alexandria*. 3 vols. 1972. Oxford University Press. — the definitive study of Alexandria
- El-Abbadi, Mostafa. *Life and Fate of the Ancient Library of Alexandria*. 1990. UNESCO.
- Tyldesley, Joyce. *Cleopatra: Last Queen of Egypt*. 2008. Profile Books.
- Thompson, Dorothy J. *Memphis Under the Ptolemies*. 1988. Princeton University Press.
