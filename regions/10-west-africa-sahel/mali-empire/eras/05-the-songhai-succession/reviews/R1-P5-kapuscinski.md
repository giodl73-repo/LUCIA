## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski — Review of "The Songhai Succession" Opening

**Overall:** The voice is right. The structure is right. But the body is not yet fully present. The opening is doing more thinking than feeling, and a chapter about contraction needs the physical reality of contraction: the quieter compounds, the empty roads, the river that carries different sounds.

**What works:**
- "The dust of the dry season lay over everything, and beyond the walls the road to Gao stretched east along the river, and the river carried sounds the jelilu in Niani could not hear yet." This is the opening's best sensory moment. The dust, the road, the river, the sounds. The physical world is carrying the political argument.
- "The compounds beyond the palace wall were quieter than they had been." This is the right instinct: the contraction has a sound, and the sound is quieter. The word "quieter" does real work here.
- "At Gao the Niger widened into a reach of water that could carry war canoes as easily as trade vessels." The river as physical fact and strategic reality simultaneously. This is the kind of sentence that makes geography feel like destiny without claiming it was.

**What doesn't:**
- The first paragraph has no physical ground. Era 3 opened with dust and shea butter and woodsmoke and harmattan haze within the first two sentences. This opening begins with the genealogy, which is auditory, but the reader has no body in the space. What does the court look like in 1449? Is the mud-brick cracked? Is the thatch patched? Is the mansa's platform smaller than it was? The physical signs of contraction should be visible before the structural analysis explains them.
- The Gao section needs the river. Not the idea of the river — the river itself. The color of the water, the smell of the mud, the sound of the current against the hulls of canoes. Gao should hit the reader's senses as forcefully as Niani hit them in Era 3.
- The kora gets one mention ("The kora held its low notes") but no physical description. What does the instrument look like in the jeli's hands? What does the sound do to the air in a smaller room?

**The question I'd push on:**
Can the full chapter make the reader feel the difference between the Niani of Musa's departure and the Niani of this era? The contraction should be visible in the physical world: fewer caravans, quieter markets, the road to the north less traveled, the compounds where courtiers once lived now empty or repurposed. Show the change in the architecture, the sounds, the foot traffic. The analysis is already strong. The body needs to catch up.
