# BOARD Review — B-peru-1 — Charles Walker
## La Conquista y El Virreinato — Peru Era 3

**Role:** Historical accuracy, uprising military geography and chronology, figure characterization
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

## Overall Assessment

The chapter is historically accurate in its essentials. The military chronology is correct. The characterization of Micaela Bastidas as the operational commander — not a supporting figure — reflects the archival record. The execution sequence in Section VI is accurate to the primary documentation. No factual errors that would require correction.

---

## Specific Observations

**Military chronology:** The chapter places the initial arrest of Arriaga in November 1780 and the siege of Cusco in "February and March 1781." This is accurate. The capture in April 1781 is correct. The execution date of May 18, 1781 is correct.

**Micaela's role:** The chapter's treatment of Micaela is the strongest characterization in the chapter. Her letters are quoted in spirit (the "we are losing the moment" and "if you will not march, I will march" passages reflect her documented correspondence accurately). Her tongue removal before the garroting is historically correct. The sequencing — her execution before Condorcanqui's, forcing him to watch — is correct.

**Pumacahua:** The chapter correctly identifies Mateo García Pumacahua as "of Chinchero" and as commander of indigenous auxiliary troops. The characterization — "a man whose ayni ran through a different network" — is historically defensible and does not flatten him into a simple colonial collaborator. He will later, in 1814, lead his own uprising against Spanish rule. The chapter does not need to narrate this; the characterization leaves the complexity intact.

**The failed quartering:** The chapter is accurate that the horses could not complete the quartering and that Condorcanqui was beheaded instead. Historical accounts vary on whether the failure was due to the number of horses or the binding. The chapter correctly leaves this as "the accounts differ."

**Túpac Katari and the Aymara theater:** The chapter correctly identifies Julián Apaza / Túpac Katari as the leader of the Aymara uprising in the south and gives the 184-day siege of La Paz as the duration. This is accurate. The chapter correctly keeps the two theaters distinct, noting "the rebellion was not one thing."

**One minor flag:** The chapter states the Spanish "abolished the corregidor system within a decade." More precisely: the reforms of the Bourbon reorganization (culminating in the intendancy system, which replaced the corregidor system) were already in process before the rebellion but were accelerated and completed by 1784 — within three years, not a decade. The chapter's claim is defensible but slightly imprecise. Suggest: "within a few years." Not a material error; the causal relationship (rebellion precipitated reform acceleration) is correct.

---

## Verdict

Historically accurate. One minor chronological imprecision noted (corregidor abolition timeline). Cleared for FINAL.

**Correction required:** Change "within a decade" to "within a few years" in Section VII.
