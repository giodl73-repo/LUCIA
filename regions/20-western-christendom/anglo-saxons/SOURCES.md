# Sources — Anglo-Saxons

## Primary Sources

- *Beowulf* — Old English heroic poem; 8th–11th century compilation; Nowell Codex
- Bede, *Historia Ecclesiastica Gentis Anglorum* — completed 731 CE; foundational history of the English Church and people
- Alfred the Great, Preface to the *Pastoral Care* — c. 890; the king's own statement of his reform program
- *Anglo-Saxon Chronicle* — begun under Alfred, c. 890; continued to 1154; multiple manuscript versions
- *Laws of Æthelberht* — c. 600 CE; earliest surviving vernacular law code in Germanic Europe
- *Laws of Ine*, *Laws of Alfred*, *Laws of Cnut* — the legislative tradition from Wessex to united England
- *The Wanderer*, *The Seafarer*, *The Dream of the Rood* — Old English elegies; Exeter Book and Vercelli Book
- *Lindisfarne Gospels* — c. 715–720 CE; the definitive visual artifact of Northumbrian Christianity
- Asser, *Life of King Alfred* — c. 893; Welsh bishop's biography of Alfred; contested but indispensable
