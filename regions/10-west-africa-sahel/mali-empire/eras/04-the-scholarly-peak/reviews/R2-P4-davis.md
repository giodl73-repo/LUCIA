# P-4 -- Wade Davis -- Review of "The Scholarly Peak" Notes (Round 2)

**Overall:** The notes demonstrate genuine commitment to intellectual depth. The section plan for "What They Wrote" is the right approach. But I need to see specifics, not plans for specifics.

**What works:**
- The distinction between custodianship and original production is the right frame. The chapter must show Timbuktu as a center of intellectual production, not a branch library.
- The composite-figure approach for al-Kabari, honestly disclosed, is the right solution for the evidentiary challenge.
- The economics of the book trade -- "a book could be worth more than salt" -- integrates knowledge into the material economy. This is how you avoid the floating-intellect problem.

**What needs strengthening:**
- The notes mention astronomy, mathematics, and medicine as disciplines but do not yet name specific texts, methods, or contributions. The chapter on al-Azhar or Oxford would name specific scholars, specific works, specific intellectual debates. Timbuktu deserves the same. Name them. What astronomical observations were made? What mathematical methods were used for inheritance calculations? What medical texts circulated?
- The Mukhtasar of Khalil ibn Ishaq is named -- good. But the chapter needs to show WHY this text dominated Maliki jurisprudence and what Timbuktu's scholars added to it. The commentary tradition is not passive reception; it is active intellectual engagement. Show the engagement.

**The question I'd push on:**
Can you name three specific intellectual contributions that Timbuktu's scholars made to the broader Islamic world during this period? Not "they studied astronomy" but "they calculated X" or "they disputed Y's interpretation of Z." If the record supports it, use it. If the record is thin for this specific period, be honest about it and use the broader tradition's documented achievements while flagging the temporal gap. But do not settle for generalities. Generalities are what every other account of Timbuktu offers. This chapter must be different.
