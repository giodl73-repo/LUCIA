## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — Review of Notes (Targeted)

**Focus:** Sensory architecture.

**What works:**
- The section plan acknowledges sensory grounding as a requirement for every section. Good.
- Section 5 (The Princess Crosses) is designed around a physical journey, which gives natural sensory opportunities: the route, the landscape, the logistics of travel.

**What needs strengthening:**
- The Khabur triangle is well-watered steppe, not desert and not river plain. The sensory palette should be specific: green in the rainy season, golden-brown in the dry months, the Khabur River cutting through cultivated fields, the smell of irrigation water and ripening grain. This is not Babylon's baked mud plain or Egypt's river corridor. It is its own landscape.
- The chariot section must be physical. A war chariot is leather, wood, bronze, two horses, two men, dust, speed, and the sound of wheels on packed earth. The reader should feel the vibration of the platform under the driver's feet. The Kikkuli manual describes specific exercises: trotting, cantering, the horses worked to a lather and then walked cool. Build the sensory palette from the source.
- Washukanni is unlocated, but Alalakh and Nuzi are excavated. Use their material culture (painted pottery, cylinder seals, architectural plans) to build a sensory composite. Disclose the method in notes but render the result as lived experience.
- The diplomatic letters are clay tablets. Clay has weight, texture, smell (especially when fresh). The scribes worked with wet clay and cut reeds. The letters were physical objects before they were diplomatic instruments. Let the reader hold the tablet.
