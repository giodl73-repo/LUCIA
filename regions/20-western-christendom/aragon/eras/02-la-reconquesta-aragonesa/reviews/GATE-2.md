## Innovations Flagged

**Potential innovation — Source Asymmetry as Dual-Column:** The chapter renders the Aragonese notarial system's encounter with the acequia amin through the image of the dual-column register: Arabic original and Catalan translation in separate columns, two systems of categorization that do not map cleanly onto each other, the discrepancies generating the disputes that generate the precedents that generate the Tribunal de las Aguas. This is a specific application of the "source asymmetry as narrative technique" framework (v1.5 amendment) but at a new level: the asymmetry IS the institution-building mechanism, not just the evidentiary condition. The gap between the columns is the compact's working material. Consider flagging for INNOVATIONS.md.

**Potential innovation — Institutional Persistence as Landing (v1.1 extension):** The Tribunal de las Aguas landing — a living institution in Valencia today, tracing its roots to the acequia amin's office of 1238 — extends the v1.1 "culture's own persistence mechanism as close" to the specific case of a legal institution with continuous operation from the medieval period to the present. The persistence mechanism is not oral tradition or grammatical structure but a functioning court still hearing disputes under rules set by the mudéjar community of this era. Consider whether this represents a new category of persistence mechanism worth logging.
