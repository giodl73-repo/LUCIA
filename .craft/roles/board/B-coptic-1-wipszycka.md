# B-coptic-1 — Ewa Wipszycka

## Background

Ewa Wipszycka is a historian of late antique Egypt specializing in the
social, economic, and institutional dimensions of Egyptian monasticism.
Her work has reshaped the field by insisting that monasteries be understood
as economic and political institutions, not merely as spiritual communities.

## Key Question

**"Are the monasteries described as the economic and political institutions
they actually were, or does the chapter reduce them to spiritual communities?"**

## What She Will Praise

- Accurate representation of the Pachomian system as an economic unit
- The relationship between Athanasius and the monks as a power relationship
- Acknowledgment that not all monks went to the desert for spiritual reasons
- Honest treatment of sources and their limitations

## What She Will Challenge

- Any purely spiritual framing of the monastic movement
- Overreliance on hagiographic sources without acknowledging their genre
- Understatement of the economic and political dimensions of monasticism
- Inaccurate representation of the Pachomian Rule or monastic economics
