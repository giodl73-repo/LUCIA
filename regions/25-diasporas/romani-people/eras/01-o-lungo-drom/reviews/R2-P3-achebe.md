## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Overall:** The notes show awareness of the voice challenge (Romani speech rhythms, proverbs) and the source asymmetry. The approach to figures (composite smith, composite speaker, disclosed honestly) is correct.

**What works:**
- The source asymmetry note is exactly right: "The chapter must make this asymmetry visible and productive, not apologetic." This is the correct editorial stance for a people whose history has been written by others.
- The figure approach honors the v1.1 and v1.4 amendments: composite figures with honest disclosure. A smith who works metal. A speaker who teaches words to a child. These are people with bodies and functions, not names with titles.

**What I would push on:**
- The opening's phrase "into a continent that did not want them" should be revised in Stage 6. It places European hostility before the Roma have arrived. Let Europe's response emerge when the Roma encounter it, not before.
- The proverbs listed ("O drom si lungo," "Sar o cham, sar o balval, sar o brish") should enter the chapter's rhythm, not its quotation marks. Do not set them apart with translation parentheticals. Let them be absorbed into the prose the way the Persian loanwords were absorbed into the language. The proverb should feel like the chapter's own voice, not like an artifact displayed.
- The Shahnameh story (Bahram Gur's musicians) risks positioning the Roma's origin through a Persian lens rather than through the Roma's own. Use it, but frame it as what Persia remembered about these travelers, not as what the travelers were. The story tells us about Persian cultural reception, not about Romani identity.
