## Notes

Passes GATE-2 decisively (53/60 vs. 48 threshold). Zero em-dashes. Voice is consistent throughout. The structural engine (alphabet as civilization-technology) is the chapter's backbone and every section serves it. Terminal condition is a variant worth noting: the engine's terminal condition (loss of sovereignty) is also its vindication (the script survives what the kingdom does not). This is a new pattern: **vindicating terminal condition** where the engine's test is also its proof-of-concept.

**Potential innovation:** The "vindicating terminal condition" pattern is distinct from the five terminal-condition types in v1.5 (legitimacy gap, deliberate self-consumption, categorical failure, reversible feedback loop, ecological constraint). Here, the terminal condition does not end the engine; it confirms it. The engine was built specifically to survive the terminal condition, and it does. Worth logging.

**Source asymmetry technique:** The Tigran passage uses source asymmetry as structural evidence for the engine (without the script, the king's story belongs to Rome). This is a specific application of the v1.5 "source gap as cultural honesty" technique, but turned toward the engine's argument rather than merely toward historiographic transparency.
