# Sources — Haiti

## Key Academic Works
- C.L.R. James, *The Black Jacobins: Toussaint L'Ouverture and the San Domingo Revolution* (1938; rev. 1963, Vintage) — the foundational account; essential
- Laurent Dubois, *Avengers of the New World: The Story of the Haitian Revolution* (2004, Harvard) — the best modern scholarly treatment
- Laurent Dubois, *Haiti: The Aftershocks of History* (2012, Metropolitan) — the long aftermath; the indemnity
- Sidney Mintz, *Sweetness and Power: The Place of Sugar in Modern History* (1985) — the plantation economy and its global context
- Karen McCarthy Brown, *Mama Lola: A Vodou Priestess in Brooklyn* (1991, California) — Vodou as lived practice
- Leslie Manigat — Haitian political history; multiple works
- Edwidge Danticat — Haitian literature; *Breath, Eyes, Memory* (1994) and others

## Primary Sources
- Toussaint Louverture's letters and memoirs (written in captivity at Fort de Joux)
- Jean-Jacques Dessalines's proclamations (1803–1806)
- The 1801 Haitian Constitution (written by Toussaint)
- The Act of Independence (January 1, 1804)

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- French colonial archive records — Archives Nationales d'Outre-Mer, Aix-en-Provence
- The New York Times / Washington Post coverage of the 1825 indemnity (recently rediscovered and republished 2022)
- UN peacekeeping mission records (MINUSTAH) and cholera investigation
- Vodou oral traditions and ceremonial records (require community access protocols)
