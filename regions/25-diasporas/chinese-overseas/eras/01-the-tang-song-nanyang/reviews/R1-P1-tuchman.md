## P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of The Tang-Song Nanyang (Opening)

**Overall:** This opening works because it has a person with a specific skill doing a specific thing. The water-reading is narrative, not exposition. I would keep reading.

**What works:**
- The first three paragraphs are excellent narrative construction. The water-color reading opens at method level, then the family history gives temporal depth (three generations, each crossing a different political landscape), then the single-line paragraph "The junk carried what Quanzhou produced and what the Nanyang wanted" pivots cleanly to the cargo. This is good pacing.
- The cargo paragraphs work as story because each item carries its own logic (porcelain breaks, camphor sublimates, sandalwood rots). The goods are not listed; they are narrated. The reader learns what can go wrong, and that is tension.
- "The list was his livelihood. It was also his law." This is the moment the opening finds its argument. Two short sentences after a long accumulation. Good instinct.

**What doesn't:**
- The closing paragraph loses momentum. After the sharp specificity of the cargo and the guanxi explanation, the final image (Lin standing at the stern watching the water change) is too calm. The opening began with a man reading water to navigate; it should close with something at stake, not with a man watching scenery. What is ahead of him? What could go wrong? What has changed since his grandfather's time that makes this crossing different?
- The guanxi paragraph, while necessary, is the one place the opening slips toward explanation. "It was not friendship... It was not kinship... It was the practical knowledge that..." This is the author defining a concept rather than showing it in action. Can you show guanxi working through a scene rather than defining it through negation?

**The question I'd push on:**
Where is the story's tension? This opening establishes a world beautifully, but a world is not yet a story. Lin has been doing this for twenty years. What is different about this crossing? What is at risk? The opening needs a thread of complication, however subtle, to earn the pull that the closing paragraph claims.
