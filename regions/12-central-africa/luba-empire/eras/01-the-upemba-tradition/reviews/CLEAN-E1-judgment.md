## Manual Pass — Implicit Judgment, Hidden Hierarchy, Anachronistic Morality

**Finding 1 — P3 (borderline):**
"long before anyone thought to call it that" (Section 1, "Lake Kisale sits in a shallow depression...")

The phrase "long before anyone thought to call it that" when referring to "a surplus economy" is a borderline case. It could be read as: "before modern economists invented the category." This is technically an anachronism — applying the shadow of a modern category (surplus economy) even while nominally rejecting it. The alternative: simply describe what was happening without the parenthetical gesture toward the modern term at all. Recommended fix: "created the conditions for wealth and for the specialists that wealth sustains — long before the Upemba shore had a name for what was happening there." This preserves the temporal gesture without invoking a Western economic category.

*Author decides: P3 severity. The current phrasing is not a violation but may invite a reader to supply the modern frame.*
