### Anachronistic Morality Check

The chapter faces an unusual challenge: the subject matter (residential school system, forced cultural suppression) has a clear modern moral verdict. The question is whether the chapter renders this verdict in the voice of a modern moral observer or whether it lets the evidence carry the weight without authorial instruction.

**Clear passes:**
- "The practice was different from the documents." No adjective. The reader supplies the judgment.
- "The system had done what it was designed to do." No verdict on the design. The design is visible; the verdict is the reader's.
- "Survival was not passive. It was a decision made daily under conditions designed to make it impossible." This states the structural fact without emotional stage direction.
- The Crowfoot section makes no claim about justice or injustice. It states the two understandings and names the gap.

**One flag — the Oblate theology paragraph:**
"to save the souls of Indigenous children, you had first to empty them of what they had been given. Salvation required a vessel that was not already occupied." This is rendered from inside the Oblate worldview and is accurate as a description of their theology. However, "The child who arrived speaking Secwepemctsin and carrying the grandmother's songs was, in the Oblate understanding, not a vessel waiting to be filled but a vessel that needed to be emptied" — the word "emptied" is the Oblate's own framing. The sentence is working. No change required; noting for the record that the framing is deliberately the Oblate's own.

**One flag — the RCAP paragraph:**
"The report was real; its reception demonstrated how much of the story had not changed." This is a mild verdict. "How much of the story had not changed" is an outside-observer observation. However, it is a factual observation (the government declined to implement the recommendations; this is documented) rather than an emotional verdict. Borderline; acceptable as it stands.

**One flag — the McClung editorial passage (noted by GATE-2):**
"This is not a verdict on McClung's contributions, which were real and lasting." This is authorial metacommentary. The sentence explicitly names what the text is and is not doing. This is the most direct authorial intrusion in the chapter. Flagging for E-2 to address — this is a voice problem as much as a judgment problem.

**Result:** Chapter passes judgment audit. One flag (McClung metacommentary) passed to E-2.
