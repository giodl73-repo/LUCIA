# Sources — Roman Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Oxyrhynchus Papyri — the largest papyrus archive from antiquity; ongoing publication by Egypt Exploration Society
- Periplus of the Erythraean Sea — first century CE merchant guide; India trade from Egyptian ports
- Papyri from the Fayyum — tax records, census documents, private letters
- Temple inscriptions — Esna, Dendera, Philae; Roman emperors as pharaohs

## Academic

- Bowman, Alan K. *Egypt After the Pharaohs: 332 BC–AD 642*. 1986. University of California Press.
- Bagnall, Roger S. *Egypt in Late Antiquity*. 1993. Princeton University Press.
- Young, Gary K. *Rome's Eastern Trade: International Commerce and Imperial Policy, 31 BC–AD 305*. 2001. Routledge.
- Casson, Lionel. *The Periplus Maris Erythraei: Text, Translation, and Commentary*. 1989. Princeton University Press.
- Wipszycka, Ewa. *The Second Gift of the Nile: Monks and Monasteries in Late Antique Egypt*. 2018. Journal of Juristic Papyrology Supplements.
- Frankfurter, David. *Religion in Roman Egypt: Assimilation and Resistance*. 1998. Princeton University Press.
