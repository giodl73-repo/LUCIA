# Sources — Yoruba / Ile-Ife / Oyo

## Primary / Oral

- Ifa divination corpus — the odù Ifá; oral sacred texts; multiple transcription projects ongoing
- Yoruba oral historical traditions — maintained by court historians; king lists of Ile-Ife and Oyo
- Samuel Johnson, *The History of the Yorubas* (1897, published 1921) — the foundational Yoruba historical synthesis by a Yoruba scholar

## Academic

- Law, Robin. *The Oyo Empire c.1600–c.1836*. 1977. Oxford University Press. — the definitive political history
- Willett, Frank. *Ife in the History of West African Sculpture*. 1967. Thames & Hudson.
- Drewal, Henry John & Pemberton, John III. *Yoruba: Nine Centuries of African Art and Thought*. 1989. Center for African Art, New York.
- Abimbola, Wande. *Ifa: An Exposition of Ifa Literary Corpus*. 1976. Oxford University Press (Ibadan). — the Ifa system explained from inside
- Bascom, William. *Ifa Divination: Communication Between Gods and Men in West Africa*. 1969. Indiana University Press.
- Peel, J.D.Y. *Religious Encounter and the Making of the Yoruba*. 2000. Indiana University Press.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent archaeological work at Ile-Ife (Ogundiran et al.; Atlantic-era excavations)
- Studies on the Oyo Mesi constitutional system and its historical record
- Scholarship on the Yoruba diaspora religions (Candomblé, Santería) and their African connections
- Updated analysis of the Ife bronze casting technique and its technological tradition
