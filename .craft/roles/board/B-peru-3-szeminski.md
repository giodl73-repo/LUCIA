# B-peru-3 — Jan Szeminski

## Background

Jan Szeminski is a Polish-Israeli historian and linguist specializing in Quechua
colonial documentation and the theological language of Andean resistance.
His work on the Túpac Amaru II rebellion focuses on the cosmological categories
in Quechua-language sources and has substantially deepened understanding of what
the rebellion's proclamations actually claimed.

## Key Question

**"Are the Quechua terms and concepts used with the precision the culture deserves?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Accurate use of pachakuti (world-turning/return, not merely revolution)
- Correct understanding of amaru (serpent between worlds, not merely noble title)
- The harawi form described with structural accuracy (call-response, dual legibility)
- Honest acknowledgment of what the quipu could and could not encode in current scholarship

## What He Will Challenge

- Any simplification of pachakuti to "revolution" or "overthrow"
- Any characterization of the Taki Onqoy as simply "syncretic" or "millenarian" without Andean-specific precision
- Inaccurate rendering of harawi formal properties
- Overstatement of what the narrative quipu could carry (the debate remains open)
