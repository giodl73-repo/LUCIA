## E-2 — Voice Keeper

**Task:** Hunt register drift. Textbook voice. Modern commentary. Any paragraph that sounds like it was written for a Western audience rather than from inside the culture. Voice-random-paragraph test: three randomly selected paragraphs, read in isolation.

**Random paragraph 1 (Heeren XVII section, third paragraph):**
> "In 1609, the Amsterdam Exchange Bank was established, partly to facilitate the VOC's capital needs, partly to stabilize the florin against the silver reales flooding in from Spanish America. In the same decade, the Amsterdam Stock Exchange developed the instruments that made VOC shares not merely an investment but a market: forward contracts, options, short-selling. A VOC share could be sold to a stranger without the stranger ever meeting the company's directors. The chain of accountability grew another link longer. The distance grew."

Voice identification from prose rhythm alone: Counting-House register. Precise, transactional, the financial instruments named without apology, the analytical conclusion ("the distance grew") delivered as a ledger entry. Correct.

**Random paragraph 2 (Batavia section, canal-grid passage):**
> "The canal grid was a catastrophe. Amsterdam's canals worked in Amsterdam because Amsterdam's climate was temperate and its canals flushed regularly with North Sea tides. Batavia's canals sat in tropical heat six degrees south of the equator, in a region of monsoon flooding and year-round humidity. The canals became sewers. The sewers became vectors for malaria, dysentery, cholera. VOC employees in Batavia died at rates the company tracked with the same precision it tracked nutmeg prices: in some years, a third of the European staff died within six months of arrival. The company's staffing files list the replacements in the same columns as the cargo manifests. The dead were a cost of operations, budgeted in advance."

Voice identification: Counting-House register. The canal failure is a budget line item; the deaths are budgeted in advance; the precision of "in some years, a third" is ledger-weight. Correct. No drift.

**Random paragraph 3 (Rampjaar section, final paragraph of the section):**
> "The solidarity that held was not commercial solidarity — it was not shared financial interest in a dividend stream. It was something older and more difficult to manufacture: the solidarity of a people who believed they were defending a specific way of living in a specific place against an army that intended to end it."

Voice check: This paragraph operates at higher rhetorical register than the surrounding sections — "something older and more difficult to manufacture" and "a specific way of living in a specific place" are close to literary prose rather than ledger prose. However, the paragraph is the chapter's analytical climax, and the Counting-House register permits this modulation at landing points (as the Italian City-States model established). The sentence is the chapter's balance-sheet final line; it is in register. Retained.

**Em-dash count (full chapter audit):** 0 em-dashes in chapter.md. 0 in figures.md. Total: 0. Voice discipline confirmed.

**Overall E-2 verdict:** The voice is the same instrument at the opening, the middle, and the close. The register never wavers into textbook ("the economy was based on..."), generic literary ("as the sun set over Amsterdam..."), or academic commentary. Three-paragraph test passed. Zero em-dashes confirmed.
