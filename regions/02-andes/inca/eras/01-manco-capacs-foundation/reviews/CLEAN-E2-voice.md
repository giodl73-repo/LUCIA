# CLEAN-E2 — Voice Keeper — Manco Cápac's Foundation (Era 1)

**Lens:** Consistency of register, rhythm match to culture, voice breaks.

## Voice Assessment

The dominant register is vertical-layered: deliberate, weight-bearing prose that moves through altitude zones. This is the right register for the Andean world. The prose does not rush. Sentences carry the weight of thin air and long distances.

### Strongest Voice Moments
1. **The lake opening** — "the light is different. Thinner." — two words that establish the altitude register for the entire chapter.
2. **The quipucamayoc scene** — "at each knot his fingers paused, and in the pause was a reading" — the rhythm of reading knots.
3. **The closing** — "Everything the empire would become was already here, in the valley, in the thread, in the knots, in the cold. Waiting." — the list-rhythm gathers threads and the single-word sentence closes with the weight of a stone falling.

### Voice Breaks
1. **"This pattern, labor exchanged for labor with chicha as the ceremonial medium, was called ayni at the household level and minka at the communal level"** (Section II) — This is an informational sentence in a narrative chapter. The terminology delivery feels like a textbook insert. Consider integrating the terms into a scene: show ayni happening, name it in context.

2. **"The anthropologist John Murra, studying the economic records of the early colonial period, called this the 'vertical archipelago'"** (Section III) — Academic attribution breaks the inside voice. The concept should arrive through the Inca's practice, not through its modern interpreter.

3. **"Irene Silverblatt's work on gender in the Andean world shows that..."** (Section V) — Same issue. Academic attribution. The claim about women's textile production being structural should be shown through the ayllu's own logic, with Silverblatt cited only in figures.md or notes.

### Em-Dash Count
- Chapter total: 7 em-dashes. Within the limit of 8.

### Register Consistency
The chapter maintains its vertical register in 5 of 6 sections. Section VI (The Valley Holds) is slightly more summary-like than the others, but the closing paragraphs recover the register fully.

## Fixes Required
1. Integrate ayni/minka terminology into a scene rather than delivering it as definition
2. Remove or reframe the Murra scholarly attribution
3. Remove or reframe the Silverblatt scholarly attribution
4. Minor: tighten Section VI opening paragraphs to match register of other sections

## Severity: MODERATE — the voice breaks are localized and fixable, but the academic attributions need reframing.
