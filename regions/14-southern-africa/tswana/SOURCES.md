# Sources — Tswana / Batswana

## Primary / Oral

- Tswana oral traditions — maintained by court specialists; merafe histories
- Burchell, William J. *Travels in the Interior of Southern Africa* (1822–24) — the earliest detailed account of Tswana towns
- Moffat, Robert. *Missionary Labours and Scenes in Southern Africa* (1842) — London Missionary Society account

## Academic

- Schapera, Isaac. *A Handbook of Tswana Law and Custom*. 1938. Oxford University Press. — foundational ethnographic study
- Schapera, Isaac. *The Tswana*. 1953. International African Institute.
- Tlou, Thomas & Campbell, Alec. *History of Botswana*. 1984. Macmillan Botswana.
- Parsons, Neil. *King Khama, Emperor Joe, and the Great White Queen: Victorian Britain Through African Eyes*. 1998. University of Chicago Press. — the 1895 London mission
- Comaroff, Jean & John L. *Of Revelation and Revolution*. 2 vols. 1991, 1997. University of Chicago Press. — the missionary encounter and Tswana transformation
- Morton, Fred. *When Rustling Became an Art: Pilane's Kgatla and the Transvaal Frontier, 1820–1902*. 1996. Philip.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Archaeological surveys of Tswana great town sites (Dithakong, Molepolole)
- Studies on the kgotla institution and its governance functions specifically
- Recent scholarship on Khama III's political philosophy and governance
- Analysis of the Bechuanaland Protectorate's relationship to the apartheid state
