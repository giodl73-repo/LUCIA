## Innovations to Log

**Innovation 1: Corrido-Narrative Register**
- Dimension: Voice
- Source: Mexico Era 4, this chapter
- Description: A new voice register derived from the corrido — Mexico's oral-journalistic ballad tradition. Characteristics: declarative forward motion, named figures as refrain anchors, third-person facticity building to refrain-weight at paragraph closes, period as pause mechanism, zero em-dashes as emergent property. Distinct from Griot-Cadence (first-person authority), Oral-Tradition (proverb-weight), and Prophetic-Migratory (eschatological horizon). The corrido reports rather than performs; accumulates toward the refrain rather than carving toward the aphorism. Sustained at ~4,500 words without break. Recommend promotion to Voice Spectrum as new entry under Revolutionary/Reformist cluster or as standalone entry.

**Innovation 2: Written-Covenant-Gap Terminal Condition**
- Dimension: Progression
- Source: Mexico Era 4, this chapter
- Description: A terminal-condition type not captured by the current v1.6 framework. The state that issued the revolutionary covenant (Constitution of 1917, Article 27) is the same state that systematically fails to implement it for class-interest reasons. Distinct from Premise non-recognition (v1.6 Type 4) — the state recognizes the premises fully (it wrote them into the constitutional text) and defers their fulfillment deliberately. The terminal condition is not endogenous weakness or exogenous refusal but internal political calculation: the coalition that made the revolution cannot implement it without fracturing itself. Proposed name: "Recognized-but-deferred" or "Written-covenant gap." Candidate for v1.6 extension to 11 terminal-condition types.

## Notes for Era 5

Era 5 (El México del PRI, 1920–2000) should open with the promise of Cárdenas's land reform (1934–1940) as partial covenant delivery, and trace the PRI's institutionalization of revolutionary rhetoric as a substitute for revolutionary delivery. The 1968 Tlatelolco massacre and the 1994 Zapatista uprising are the era's terminal conditions — both moments when the covenant's continuing non-delivery becomes visible and violent. The corrido-narrative voice should continue into Era 5, tracking how the corrido tradition responds to the PRI's appropriation of revolutionary symbolism.
