# Sources — Mexico

## Key Academic Works
- Octavio Paz, *The Labyrinth of Solitude* (1950; trans. 1961, Grove Press) — the essential statement of Mexican national character and its contradictions
- José Vasconcelos, *La Raza Cósmica* (1925) — the mestizaje ideology; read critically alongside indigenous critiques
- Eric Wolf, *Sons of the Shaking Earth* (1959) — the historical anthropology of Mesoamerica into Mexico
- Alan Knight, *The Mexican Revolution* (2 vols., 1986, Cambridge) — the standard scholarly account
- John Womack, *Zapata and the Mexican Revolution* (1969, Knopf) — definitive Zapata study
- Carlos Monsiváis — Mexican culture and society; multiple works
- Elena Poniatowska, *La Noche de Tlatelolco* (1971) — the massacre; oral testimony; essential

## Primary Sources
- Bernardino de Sahagún, *Florentine Codex* (c. 1540–1580) — the encyclopedic record of Nahua civilization
- The Plan de Ayala (Zapata, 1911)
- The Mexican Constitution of 1917
- Zapatista communiqués (1994–present) — available at EZLN archives online

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- INALI (Instituto Nacional de Lenguas Indígenas) — documentation of 68 indigenous languages
- Mexico City archaeological archive — Templo Mayor excavation records
- Drug war testimony and human rights documentation (FUNDEM, CMDPDH)
- Contemporary indigenous governance documentation — municipalities by usos y costumbres
