## Notes

**Passes at 29/30. Target was 24+.**

**Structural observation:** The cold water-pipe functions as a material-object engine in the v1.5 sense — not symbol but mechanism. Its properties (cold, present, untouched, politically unchosen) determine the narrative's logic. The absence of smoke IS the Tobacco Protest's proof.

**Voice identification:** Court-pressure register — a variant of the court voice (see STYLE-GUIDE) inflected by siege. Not the Heian court's layered aesthetic observation, not the Mughal court's ceremonial grandeur. The Qajar version: a court that measures everything against what it can and cannot concede. The rhythm is deliberate rather than flowing — sentences that end with weight, not with embellishment.

**Em-dash count:** 1. Within ≤5 limit. The voice naturally avoids them — the register pauses with periods, not dashes.

**One possible revision note (minor):** Paragraph 3 ("He had survived the Russian ambassador's demands...") is slightly inventory-heavy — a list of the shah's durabilities. It does important work (establishes stakes) but the third repetition of "He had survived..." begins to feel mechanical. Consider whether one of the three instances could be collapsed. This is a P3-level note, not a gate issue.
