## Baseline (established in opening)

**Register:** River-pastoral. Accumulative sentences building like a current gathering tributaries, punctuated by short declarative stops. Grounded, material, weight-bearing. ### Section III (Cattle/City): ON REGISTER
The herdsman paragraph is the chapter's longest sustained passage and maintains register throughout. The pottery paragraph is technically precise without shifting into textbook voice. "The Kerma potter's hands, working without the wheel, produced what the wheel could not" — on register, carries analytical weight in the culture's own terms.

### Section IV (Tombs Speak): ON REGISTER
The cemetery-as-book metaphor is the only moment that edges toward literary commentary ("The whole cemetery reads like a book whose chapters grow longer..."). **P3 flag.** The metaphor is brief and does not recur. The retainer burial paragraph is the chapter's most disciplined voice passage.

### Section V (Egypt Watches): ON REGISTER
This is the section most at risk of voice shift (Egyptian perspective entering). The voice holds: the Egyptian material is presented as evidence about Egypt, not as the chapter's primary frame. "You do not waste ritual energy on enemies who do not matter" and "You do not repeatedly curse a people who pose no threat" are short declarative stops that maintain the chapter's own voice while discussing Egyptian actions.

### Section VI (Landing): ON REGISTER
The voice returns to the opening's weight. "He wrote in earth and bone and gold, and the writing has lasted four thousand years" is the chapter's strongest single analytical sentence, fully on register. The landing images (horns facing east, hands near faces, shell ornaments) echo Section I without repeating it. "The river ran north. The gold went with it. The cattle stayed." — perfect close.
