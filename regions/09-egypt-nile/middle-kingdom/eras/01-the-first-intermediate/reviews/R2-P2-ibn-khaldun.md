## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (The Fragmentation)

**Focus:** Structural engine definition and sustainability across arc.

**Assessment:** The structural engine is correctly identified: the reversal of the ma'at feedback loop. This is precisely the kind of cyclical mechanism I recognize in every dynastic cycle. The forward loop (king-ma'at-flood-surplus-center-construction) running backward (low flood-local surplus-autonomous periphery-fragmented ma'at) is clean, specific, and culture-derived.

**What works in the plan:**
- The terminal condition is well-defined: the loop runs backward until Theban reunification restarts it. Era 1 closes before the resolution, which is correct.
- The section plan makes the engine visible: Section II traces the loosening, Section III grounds it ecologically, Sections IV-V show the productive consequences.
- The distinction between the Herakleopolitan model (cautious continuity) and the Theban model (aggressive innovation) is the right structural contrast for Section VI.

**What needs sharpening:**
- The engine must be VISIBLE in the prose, not just named in the notes. Each section should advance the loop one turn. The opening establishes the reversal (Khety counting grain = surplus staying local). Section II should show the loosening of the center. Section III should show the ecological trigger. Section IV should show the productive consequence (Ankhtifi as self-made authority). Section V should show the cultural consequence (new voices). Section VI should show the political geography the reversal produced. Section VII should name what the reversal left behind.
- The plan risks treating the climate data (Section III) as a standalone digression. It must be integrated into the loop: low flood is not a separate cause; it is the mechanism by which the loop's direction reverses.
- The Instruction of Merikare deserves more weight. It is the only text in which a king of this period reflects on what went wrong. It is the engine's own self-diagnosis.

**Recommendation:** Proceed to WRITE. The engine is sound. Keep it visible in every section.
