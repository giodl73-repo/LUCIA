# Sources — Soninke / Wagadu

## Primary / Oral

- Dausi epic — oral tradition maintained by Soninke jeli (griots); multiple versions; foundational narrative
- Al-Bakri, *Kitab al-Masalik wa'l-Mamalik* (The Book of Routes and Realms), c. 1068 CE — the most detailed Arabic description of the Ghana Empire
- Al-Idrisi, *Nuzhat al-Mushtaq*, c. 1154 CE — geographic account including the western Sudan

## Academic

- Levtzion, Nehemia. *Ancient Ghana and Mali*. 1973. Methuen. — foundational study
- Monteil, Charles. *Les Empires du Mali: Étude d'histoire et de sociologie soudanaises*. 1929. — early scholarly treatment
- Dieterlen, Germaine & Sylla, Youssou. *L'Empire de Ghana*. 1992. Karthala. — the Dausi and oral tradition
- Launay, Robert. *Beyond the Stream: Islam and Society in a West African Town*. 1992. University of California Press. — Dyula trading communities
- Levtzion, Nehemia & Spaulding, Jay (eds.). *Medieval West Africa: Views from Arab Scholars and Merchants*. 2003. Markus Wiener.
- Connah, Graham. *African Civilizations: An Archaeological Perspective*. 3rd ed. 2016. Cambridge University Press.
