## E-1: Judgment Auditor Report

**Scope:** chapter.md and figures.md reviewed for outside-lens violations, forbidden words, anachronistic morality, romanticization or demonization.

**Forbidden words scan:** Clear. No instances of "primitive," "advanced," "tribe," "Dark Ages," "discovered by Europeans," "New World," or prohibited framings detected.

**Forbidden framing scan:** Clear. No "While Europe was doing X, these people were doing Y" constructions. No "remarkably sophisticated for" constructions. No "before contact" framing.

**Anachronistic morality check:**

One sentence requires adjustment. In the section "What Was Built":

> "The founding paradox was not merely a moral failure; it was an institution, with its own mechanisms and its own legal protections, and those mechanisms would require a catastrophic war to dismantle."

The phrase "moral failure" edges toward authorial verdict. Replace with: "The founding paradox was not merely a stated contradiction; it was an institution, with its own mechanisms and its own legal protections, and those mechanisms would require a catastrophic war to dismantle."

This removes the evaluative frame while preserving the structural observation.

**Romanticization check:** The founding figures are handled without romanticization. Madison's anguish and complicity are presented simultaneously. Jefferson's contradictions are named without excuse or condemnation. Hamilton's death is stated plainly without elevation to martyrdom. Allen's accomplishment is stated without sentimentalization. Passed.

**Demonization check:** No figure is presented as purely villainous. The delegates who were "comfortable" with slavery at the Convention are present but not named as monsters. The Federalists who passed the Alien and Sedition Acts are presented within the logic of their own political fears. Passed.

**Achebe Test:** The founding paradox is stated from inside the republic's own logic. The enslaved people in the chapter are individuated (Allen, Sally Hemings, Banneker). No moral instruction from above. The chapter does not tell the reader how to feel about the founders. Passed.

**Verdict:** One revision required. All other judgment concerns: clear.

## E-3: Compression Editor Report

**Scope:** Re-explanation, filler, redundancy between sections.

**Re-explanation check:** The chapter does not re-explain its scenes. The Three-Fifths Compromise is quoted and named as evasive; the chapter does not then explain why it was evasive. The St. George's incident is narrated; the chapter does not then explain what it showed. The Banneker-Jefferson exchange is rendered; "The exchange between Banneker and Jefferson is not primarily a story about Jefferson's hypocrisy" in figures.md is near-borderline — it names what the exchange is NOT, which edges toward pre-empting the reader's interpretation. However, it redirects immediately to a more precise framing ("It is a story about the republic's founding language being turned on itself"), which earns its place.

**Redundancy check:** The founding paradox is stated in the opening, developed through each section, and closed in the final paragraphs. The restatements are progressively enriched rather than circular — each section adds a new facet (Convention → Free African Society → Paris → election of 1800 → Banneker's letter) rather than repeating the same point. No redundancy.

**Filler check:** One passage in figures.md (Madison entry) risks slight inflation:

> "He was also five feet four, slight, easily overlooked in a crowd, and spoke in a voice that required people near him to lean forward."

This is specific and grounded, but the physical description immediately following it ("He sat near the front of the Convention chamber specifically so that he could hear everything") makes the point more economically. Consider cutting the physical description or tightening to: "He was slight and quiet-voiced, and he sat near the front of the Convention chamber specifically so that he could hear everything." Saves six words; retains the specificity.

**Word count check:** chapter.md is approximately 4,850 words. Within 4,500–5,500 range. figures.md is approximately 1,400 words. Within 500–1,500 range.

**Verdict:** One optional tightening in figures.md (Madison physical description). No required changes. Chapter is not padded and is not thin.
