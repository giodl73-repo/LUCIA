# CLEAN-E3 — Compression Editor — "The Manufacturing Peak"

## Re-explanation Check

### Section I
- No re-explanation detected. The dye pit scene shows the process; no paragraph follows to explain what it meant.

### Section II
- "The system worked because each city needed what the others provided." — This sentence introduces a paragraph that then lists exactly what was just described in the three preceding paragraphs (Kano needed Katsina, Katsina needed Kano, etc.). **The preceding paragraphs already showed the interdependence.** The summary paragraph re-explains.
- **Recommendation:** Cut "The system worked because each city needed what the others provided." Begin the paragraph with "Kano needed Katsina's northern connections to sell its cloth." The reader already knows the system works; showing the specific dependencies is the work this paragraph should do.

### Section III
- "The system did not see itself as a contradiction. The sarki taxed the dye pits and the slave markets with the same administrative machinery." — The first sentence summarizes; the second shows. **Recommendation:** Cut "The system did not see itself as a contradiction." The showing is stronger.

### Section IV
- "The Bori ceremony was not entertainment. It was a healing practice, a social institution, a knowledge system with its own specialists, its own training, its own hierarchy." — The first sentence ("not entertainment") defends against a misreading the text has not invited. The preceding description already established the Bori as institutional. **Recommendation:** Cut "The Bori ceremony was not entertainment."
- "This was not improvisation." — Flagged by E-1 and E-2 as well. Triple flag. **Cut.**

### Section V
- "It would be another century before the critique found its voice in Usman dan Fodio, another century before the jihad remade the Hausa world." — This is a chronological summary that tells the reader what the chapter chose not to show. The chapter's strategy is to end at the peak, with the tensions visible but unresolved. Naming the jihad explicitly steps outside this strategy and tells the reader the ending of a different story. **Recommendation:** Cut or reduce. The phrase "in its own time, make them something else" in the final paragraph already carries this weight without naming names.
