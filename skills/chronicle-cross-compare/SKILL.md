You are running /chronicle-cross-compare for: {{chapters}}

Compare two or more locked chapters from different cultures to find patterns
that improve the system — style guide, rubric, pipeline, and templates.

---

## Input

- **Chapters**: Paths to 2+ locked (FINAL) `chapter.md` files from different cultures

The chapters should be from different culture types (e.g., steppe and court,
maritime and urban, oral tradition and literate empire) to reveal where the
system flexes well and where it strains.

---

## Step 1 — Read All Chapters

Read each chapter completely. For each, note:
- Culture type (steppe, court, maritime, oral tradition, urban/market, etc.)
- Voice register used
- How the 13 principles manifested in practice
- What the chapter did exceptionally well
- Where the chapter struggled or the format strained

---

## Step 2 — Identify Cross-Cultural Patterns

### Patterns That Work Across Cultures

Techniques or approaches that succeeded in multiple chapters regardless
of culture type. These are candidates for promotion in the style guide
or rubric:
- Structural choices that maintained immersion across different worlds
- Narrative devices that handled transitions effectively
- Ways of grounding abstract concepts in physical detail

### Patterns Specific to One Culture Type

Techniques that work for steppe but not court, or for maritime but not urban.
These inform the Voice Spectrum section of the Style Guide:
- Rhythmic patterns tied to culture type
- Section structures that suit one type but not another
- Sensory grounding strategies specific to an environment

### Where the Format Strains

Places where the ~5000-word chapter structure, the 6-section plan, or the
13 principles created tension with what the culture's story needed:
- Cultures that need more or fewer sections
- Eras where the arc does not fit the development-deepening-landing shape
- Principles that conflict with each other for specific culture types

---

## Step 3 — System Improvement Recommendations

For each finding, recommend whether to change:

**Style Guide** — New principles, revised voice spectrum entries, updated
forbidden words/framings based on what the chapters revealed.

**Rubric** — Scoring anchors that need refinement based on what "good"
actually looks like across cultures. Dimensions that may need splitting
or recombining.

**Pipeline** — Stages that were too heavy or too light for certain culture
types. Review focuses that missed issues or caught false positives.

**Templates** — Section structures that need variants for different culture
types. Word targets that should flex.
