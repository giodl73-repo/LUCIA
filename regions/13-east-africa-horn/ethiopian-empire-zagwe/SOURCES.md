# Sources — Ethiopian Empire (Zagwe)

## Primary / Archaeological

- Lalibela church complex — the buildings themselves; ongoing conservation and archaeological work
- Zagwe royal chronicles — fragmentary; preserved in later Ethiopian royal chronicles
- Church of Imrahana Kristos records — earlier Zagwe church; paintings and inscriptions

## Academic

- Gerster, Georg. *Churches in Rock: Early Christian Art in Ethiopia*. 1970. Phaidon. — the classic photographic and architectural study of Lalibela
- Plant, Ruth. *Architecture of the Tigre, Ethiopia*. 1985. Ravens Educational.
- Phillipson, David W. *Ancient Ethiopia*. 1998. British Museum Press. — Axumite-Zagwe transition
- Tadesse Tamrat. *Church and State in Ethiopia 1270–1527*. 1972. Oxford University Press. — the transition to the Solomonic period
- Pankhurst, Richard. *The Ethiopians: A History*. 1998. Blackwell.
- Mercier, Jacques. *Art That Heals: The Image as Medicine in Ethiopia*. 1997. Prestel. — Ethiopian Christian visual culture

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent archaeological survey of the Lalibela complex (post-1990)
- Studies specifically on the Zagwe dynasty as distinct from the broader Ethiopian narrative
- Scholarship on the Agaw people's pre-Christian religious traditions and their influence on Zagwe Christianity
- Analysis of the Jerusalem symbolism in the Lalibela church layout
