# Sources — Hawaiian People

## Primary Sources
- The Kumulipo (Hawaiian cosmogonic chant) — translated by Martha Beckwith (1951, University of Chicago Press); the original is the fullest statement of Hawaiian cosmological thought
- David Malo, *Hawaiian Antiquities (Mo'olelo Hawai'i)* (1898; translation by N.B. Emerson) — the earliest systematic Hawaiian account of traditional society, written by a Hawaiian scholar
- Samuel Kamakau, *Ruling Chiefs of Hawaii* (1961, Kamehameha Schools Press) — Hawaiian historian writing in Hawaiian; translated accounts of the ali'i period
- Queen Lili'uokalani, *Hawaii's Story by Hawaii's Queen* (1898) — the overthrow from inside; essential primary source

## Key Academic Works
- Marshall Sahlins, *Historical Metaphors and Mythical Realities* (1981) and *How "Natives" Think* (1995) — the Cook controversy; Hawaiian cosmology and history
- Gananath Obeyesekere, *The Apotheosis of Captain Cook* (1992) — the other side of the Cook controversy
- Patrick Kirch, *How Chiefs Became Kings: Divine Kingship and the Rise of Archaic States in Ancient Hawai'i* (2010, University of California Press)
- Patrick Kirch, *A Shark Going Inland Is My Chief: The Island Civilization of Ancient Hawai'i* (2012)
- Lilikala Kame'eleihiwa, *Native Land and Foreign Desires* (1992, Bishop Museum Press) — the Great Mahele and its consequences
- Noenoe Silva, *Aloha Betrayed: Native Hawaiian Resistance to American Colonialism* (2004, Duke)
