## Historical Accuracy Assessment

**The acequia system and the Tribunal de las Aguas:** The chapter's treatment of the acequia amin and the Tribunal de las Aguas is historically defensible. The chapter correctly notes that the Tribunal is one of the oldest continuously functioning courts in Europe and that its procedural roots connect to the Islamic acequia amin institution. The figures.md composite-figure disclosure is appropriate and honest; the institution is documented, the individual at this specific moment is not. The connection between the 1238 mudéjar retention and the subsequent acequia documentation in the Aragonese archives is correctly drawn.

**Minor correction — acequia nomenclature:** The chapter refers to "the Rascanya acequia" — this is one of Valencia's historical acequia systems and is correctly named. The use is plausible for a 1238 scene. Confirmed.

**The capitulació terms:** The chapter's description of the surrender terms — free departure with safe conduct, mudéjar status for those who remain, protection of mosques and law courts, continuation of customary agricultural practices — is accurate to what the historical record indicates. The *Llibre dels Fets* and subsequent Aragonese administrative records confirm these broad terms. The specific formulation "mosques converted only where the population had emptied" is a reasonable characterization of what occurred in the immediate aftermath.

**Abu Zayd:** The chapter's treatment of Abu Zayd is accurate: his conversion is documented (papal records confirm), his land grants were restored as Christian vassalage, and the characterization of his position as a political adaptation rather than simple betrayal is defensible.

**The Rodrigo de Lizana case:** I cannot confirm the specific name "Rodrigo de Lizana" from the sources available for this review — the *Justicia*'s confrontation with James I over a frontier lord is documented in the Aragonese archives, but the specific case and name should be verified against the primary record before FINAL. This is a minor factual flag.

**Zayyan ibn Mardanish's family history:** Correctly rendered. The Wolf King (Muhammad ibn Mardanish) was Zayyan's grandfather; this is established in the Arabic sources. The characterization of Zayyan's legitimacy as rooted in Valencian family history rather than pure Almohad appointment is accurate and important. Confirmed.

**The Majorcan comparison:** Historically accurate and important. The chapter correctly notes that the Majorcan Muslim population's fate was harder than Valencia's. This contrast is documented and represents an important corrective to any narrative that presents James I's Valencian compact as a general policy rather than a situation-specific calculation.

## Cultural Representation Assessment

The chapter represents the mudéjar community with appropriate dignity. The acequia amin figure — composite, disclosed — gives the community agency and expertise rather than rendering them purely as the object of James's calculation. The figures.md treatment of Zayyan is the strongest element: a governor who governed in conditions of defeat, who negotiated for fifty thousand people in circumstances he could not reverse. This is the mudéjar and Almohad experience rendered with dignity.

One recommendation: the chapter could note, even briefly, that the protection the mudéjars received was not uniform across the Valencian kingdom — that conditions varied significantly between urban centers, where the formal compact terms were better enforced, and rural areas, where the incoming Aragonese noble class exerted more direct pressure on the tenant communities. Burns's own scholarship documents this variation. The chapter's current framing ("the compact held, for a century and more, at the cost of constant pressure") is accurate but could be made more specific.

## Source Assessment

The *Llibre dels Fets* is used correctly. Burns (1984) is appropriately credited as the scholarly foundation for the acequia material. Bisson (1986) provides the constitutional framework. The source base is sound.

**Flag for FINAL:** Verify "Rodrigo de Lizana" name against the Aragonese royal archive records for the *Justicia* confrontation with James I. If the name cannot be confirmed, replace with "a Valencian frontier lord" — the institutional point holds without the specific name.

---

## Overall Assessment

This chapter represents the Valencia conquest of 1238 accurately, with appropriate attention to the complexity of the mudéjar arrangement and the constitutional context of pactisme. The acequia amin figure is the chapter's intellectual achievement: a knowledge system rendered at method level, through a body, in a specific moment. The Tribunal de las Aguas as landing mechanism is historically defensible and culturally significant.
