## From: hypothesis

**CLAIM**: LUCIA's forward-only rubric-amendment policy produces stable evolution across v1.0 → v1.7: zero reversed amendments, additive structural diff, small counterfactual re-score delta on early chapters.

**FALSIFICATION**: ≥ 3 reversals (pre-registered rule) OR mean |delta| > 8 points on pilot sample.

**CONFIDENCE**: 80. Measurements are structurally robust — a reversal is a textual-semantic pattern visible in git diff; a score delta is bounded by the 60-point Chronicle range. Main uncertainty: pilot sample size (3–5 chapters, not the 20 plan.md originally specified).

**Prior evidence**:
1. Supports: RUBRIC.md changelog labels every amendment as "Amended" or "Types Expanded" or "[new technique]"; no revert/withdraw language. Git log shows monotonic growth (244→302 lines; 1,961→4,289 words).
2. Supports: All 15 rows in INNOVATIONS.md Amendment History table are textually additive.
3. Weakens: v1.5 cluster promotion rule is a candidate refinement of v1.0's 2-instance trigger. Under pre-registered classifier, this is Refinement — the 2-instance trigger is *preserved* ("Innovations can still promote via the instance trigger at any time. The cluster trigger supplements it — it does not replace it").
4. Supports: Preliminary git-diff measurement — 243 of 244 v1.0 lines preserved in v1.7 (99.6%).

**Findings**:
- F-01: Data is deterministically measurable from RUBRIC.md git history and INNOVATIONS.md Amendment History table.
- F-02: Falsification thresholds pre-registered in plan.md, unchanged here.

## From: competitors

**Status quo 1**: Retroactive rescoring (re-apply new rubric to all prior artifacts).
**Status quo 2**: Silent amendment (edit without version bump).

**Named competitors**:
- **Ontology evolution** (Stojanovic; OntoClean) — stronger policy with bidirectional migration. We acknowledge forward-only is weaker; argue it suits archival scores. [NEED-A]
- **Rubric calibration** (Jonsson & Svingby; Wolfe) — tracks rater drift, assumes fixed dimension set; does not address emergent dimensions. [NEED-B]
- **Test-taker fairness** (AERA/APA/NCME Standards 2014) — legitimizing ancestor. A test-taker should be judged by the standard in force when tested. [NEED-C]
- **ML benchmark versioning** (HELM; BIG-bench) — convergent practice in a different domain. [NEED-D]
- **SemVer** — metaphor only; orthogonal.

**Whitespace**: Explicit, structurally measurable account of forward-only rubric amendment as stability-producing policy for discovery-driven quality standards in AI-assisted creative production, with pre-registered falsification thresholds and a novel forward-overlay metric.

**Findings**:
- F-03: Test-taker fairness (AERA/APA/NCME Standards) is the key legitimizing citation.
- F-04: Ontology evolution is the strictly-stronger alternative; forward-only's weaker-but-sufficient positioning is defensible for archival-score settings.
- F-05: Forward-overlay is a new metric not found in prior literature.
