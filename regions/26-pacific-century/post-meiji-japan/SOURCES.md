# Sources — Post-Meiji Japan

## Key Academic Works
- Marius Jansen, *The Making of Modern Japan* (2000, Harvard) — the standard comprehensive history
- John Dower, *Embracing Defeat: Japan in the Wake of World War II* (1999, Norton) — Pulitzer Prize; the definitive account of the occupation
- John Dower, *War Without Mercy: Race and Power in the Pacific War* (1986) — the racial dimensions of the Pacific War
- Iris Chang, *The Rape of Nanking* (1997) — the Nanjing Massacre; contested but essential
- Gavan McCormack — Japan in East Asia; multiple works on memory politics
- Kenneth Pyle, *Japan Rising: The Resurgence of Japanese Power and Purpose* (2007)
- Andrew Gordon, *A Modern History of Japan* (3rd ed., 2013, Oxford) — the standard textbook

## Primary Sources
- The Meiji Constitution (1889)
- The postwar Japanese Constitution (1947), especially Article 9
- John Hersey, *Hiroshima* (1946) — the first comprehensive account; still essential
- Hibakusha testimony collections — Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum
- The Tokyo War Crimes Tribunal transcripts (1946–1948)

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Japanese National Archives — Meiji era state-building documents
- Unit 731 documentation — partially available through Chinese and US archives
- Comfort women testimony collections — Korean and Chinese academic archives
- Contemporary Yasukuni Shrine political documentation
