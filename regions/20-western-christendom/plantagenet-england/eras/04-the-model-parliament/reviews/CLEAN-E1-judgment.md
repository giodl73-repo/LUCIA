## Manual Pass — Findings

**Finding 1 — Borderline P3**
*Location:* Section III, paragraph on the Declaration of Arbroath: "The men who would write the Declaration of Arbroath in 1320 — a letter to the Pope signed by the Scottish nobility — would assert, in terms that echo the *quod omnes tangit* principle with a sharpness that suggests they had been thinking about it..."

*Quoted text:* "in terms that echo the *quod omnes tangit* principle with a sharpness that suggests they had been thinking about it"

*Violation type:* Mild teleological frame — the sentence implies the Scots were responding to the English parliamentary formula, positioning the English institution as the reference point from which Scottish thinking is measured.

*Assessment:* P3 — borderline. The author's intent is to register the parallel, not to claim priority. But "suggests they had been thinking about it" subtly centers the English formula. The sentence can be strengthened by attributing the parallel to the shared common inheritance of Roman legal thought (*quod omnes tangit* was Roman civil law, available to both traditions) rather than to English-Scottish transmission.

*Recommendation:* Revise to: "The men who wrote the Declaration of Arbroath in 1320 drew on the same Roman legal principle — *quod omnes tangit ab omnibus approbetur* had been in the canonical texts for centuries — and gave it a sharpness the English Parliament had not yet found."

**Finding 2 — Clean**
*Location:* Section III, discussion of the Laws of Hywel Dda.

*Assessment:* Handled with complete integrity. The Welsh legal tradition is named on its own terms, its difference from English law described as difference rather than deficiency. "Where Welsh law had treated certain offenses as matters for compensation between kinship groups, English law made them capital crimes" — this is description from inside the distinction, not judgment from above it. Clean.

**Finding 3 — Clean**
*Location:* Section V on Edward II: "He was not stupid. He was not cowardly."

*Assessment:* Defensive framing sometimes reveals a hidden judgment it is trying to disclaim. But in this case the opening negatives serve the argument being made: the chapter is identifying what kind of unfitness Edward II exhibited. The form is honest. Clean.
