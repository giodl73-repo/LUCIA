# R2-P5 -- Kapuscinski (Targeted Review of Notes)

**Chapter:** Hayastan -- The Ancient Kingdom
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Assessment of the Production Bible

The notes show awareness of my concern. Section II is designated for the Highland Plateau as environment, and the notes list volcanic stone, altitude, rivers flowing outward, and Ararat. These are the right elements. But planning to include sensory detail is not the same as including it. The chapter must deliver what the notes promise.

## Specific Sensory Requirements

1. **The Highland Plateau.** The Armenian Plateau sits at 1,500-2,000 meters. The air is thin. The stone is dark volcanic tufa and basalt. The light has a particular quality at altitude: sharp, clear, the sky a deep blue that lowland people do not see. Ararat is visible from the plain around it like a second sky. In winter, the snow covers everything and the cold is killing. In summer, the grass is green and short and the sheep move across slopes that look soft from a distance but are basalt underneath. The chapter needs at least two passages where the reader is standing on this land.

2. **The sound of Armenian.** The notes flag this in Section V. Deliver it. Armenian has a distinctive phonology: aspirated stops (p' vs. p, t' vs. t, k' vs. k), a uvular fricative, retroflex consonants. Let the reader hear a phrase of Armenian. Transliterate it. Let the sounds sit on the page. This is the v1.3 body-as-instrument standard applied to a linguistic knowledge system.

3. **Avarayr.** The notes designate 700 words. The battle needs one physical detail before the analysis: the dust, the weight of armor, the horses. Even one sentence of the Avarayr plain -- a wide valley between mountains -- would ground the symbolism in the body.

4. **Khor Virap.** Gregory's pit. This is one of the most physically dramatic sites in Armenian memory: a deep well-shaft carved into rock at the foot of Ararat. The mountain looming above, the darkness below. If the chapter mentions the pit, it must put the reader in it.

## Verdict

The sensory plan is present in the notes. The chapter must execute it. I will be reading with my body.
