## Notes

**Above target.** The 29/30 is a genuine Gate passage. The opening does something difficult: it renders the 1493 Columbus encounter entirely in Kalinago terms without ever explaining those terms to the reader. The result is that the famous "discovery" moment is not present — what is present is a familiar Kalinago situation, handled in familiar Kalinago ways, being misread by people the Kalinago have not yet categorized as a new kind of problem.

**The v1.2 Amendment (Outsider's Bias as Inside Characterization):** The treatment of the Spanish recorder is a strong instance of this technique. "The category he reached for when the Kalinago warriors came off the water in their war canoes was the one his world had prepared for people who did not immediately offer deference: dangerous, wild, eaters of men." This is not the author correcting the Spanish; it is the Kalinago story registering that someone is recording it wrong. The bias characterizes the recorder, not the culture.

**The one point deduction** is for a micro-register drift in the "man directing the wing-cloth canoes" section. The ironic distance on Columbus ("had already decided, before he saw it") is controlled but slightly external to the full inside-voice standard. Could be tightened in FIXES to stay at Kalinago-eye-view throughout.

**Em-dash count:** 0. The maritime-island register finds its own pause rhythm through periods and short declarative sentences. The benchmark is already achieved at the opening stage.

**Flag for PANEL-1:** The structural engine (inter-island raiding as political structure / sea-as-defense-geography) is already present in the opening. Panel should assess whether the chapter needs to deepen this or whether the engine is sufficiently planted here.
