# Sources — Aragon

## Primary Sources

- James I of Aragon, *Llibre dels Fets* (Book of Deeds) — the king's own autobiography; primary source for 13th-century Aragonese expansion
- *Llibre del Consolat de Mar* — the Catalan maritime law code; foundational commercial law text
- *Furs de València* — the law code established for the conquered kingdom of Valencia, 1261
- Ramon Muntaner, *Crònica* — 14th-century chronicle of the Catalan Company and Aragonese Mediterranean empire
- Bernat Desclot, *Crònica del Rei en Pere* — chronicle of Peter III and the Sicilian Vespers
- The *Generalitat* records — documents of the Catalan parliament and fiscal administration

## Secondary Sources

- Burns, Robert I. *Muslims, Christians and Jews in the Crusader Kingdom of Valencia*. Cambridge University Press, 1984.
- Hillgarth, J.N. *The Spanish Kingdoms 1250–1516*. 2 vols. Oxford University Press, 1976–1978.
- Bisson, Thomas N. *The Medieval Crown of Aragon*. Oxford University Press, 1986.
- Runciman, Steven. *The Sicilian Vespers*. Cambridge University Press, 1958.
- Setton, Kenneth M. *Catalan Domination of Athens 1311–1388*. Medieval Academy of America, 1948.
