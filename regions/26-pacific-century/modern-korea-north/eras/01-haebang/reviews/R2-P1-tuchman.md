# PANEL-2 Review — Barbara Tuchman (Narrative Craft Gap Response)

**Reviewer:** Barbara Tuchman (P-1)  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Focus:** Middle section summary risk; scene specificity

---

The production bible is strong. I'm pushing back on one structural choice: the treatment of Section 3 (The Soviet Installation) as machinery exposition.

"The Communist Party cells: who are these people? Where did they come from?" — this is a great question, and the answer could be either abstraction or scene. The notes say "Show raising it. Don't say 'A military would be raised.'" I agree absolutely. So here's what I want to see in Section 3:

**Instead of summarizing the land reform, show one land reform.**
- Name a family. Name a landlord. Name a piece of earth. Show the redistribution happening on a specific morning to specific people. The reader will understand land reform from the texture of one redistribution, not from being told it happened widely.

**Instead of saying "Party organization began," show one organizing meeting.**
- A room. A dozen Korean communists. One of them (maybe Park Hon-yong) standing up and laying out the structure. One moment where someone questions, and one moment where the authority (Soviet or Korean) shuts down the question. You've just shown both the organizing AND the intolerance for doubt in a single scene.

**Instead of saying "The military began," show the first soldiers.**
- Raw recruits. An officer who has studied in the Soviet Union. The first drill. The moment when they understand they are no longer individuals but parts of a machine. One scene.

This is not asking for encyclopedic coverage. It's asking for specificity. The reader understands systems better from one concrete instance than from summary. And you'll find that one good scene automatically suggests the next scene, the next section, the architecture of the whole thing.

**Verdict:** The production bible structure is correct. Push hard on Section 3 to make it scene-specific, not machinery-summary. Do that, and the sensory depth Kapuscinski wanted will come with it.
