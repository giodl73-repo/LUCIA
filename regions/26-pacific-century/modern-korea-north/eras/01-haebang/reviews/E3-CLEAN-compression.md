## Charge

Hunt for re-explanation and filler. Scenes followed by explanation of what they showed. Paragraphs that repeat what was already demonstrated. The phrase "This shows that..." or "In other words..." Redundancies between sections.

## Method

Read through chapter for moments where narrative is followed by editorial commentary that tells the reader what they just read. Flag each instance. Note whether it serves the text or undermines it.

**Minor re-explanation audit:** PASS

One moment where the boundary is thin:

In Part 2, after describing the Japanese occupation and its methods, the text says: "On August 15, 1945, the Japanese Emperor announced surrender. In Seoul and Pyongyang and every city in between, Koreans poured into the streets. The celebration was authentic and overwhelming."

The word "authentic" could be read as slight editorializing — telling the reader what to feel rather than showing it. However, the next sentence ("People wept. People sang.") immediately demonstrates the authenticity through action. The authorial voice is brief enough not to constitute re-explanation.

Verdict: PASS. Acceptable boundary.

## The Landing Assessment

The final five paragraphs of the chapter are the most theoretically dense. Could this be over-explanation?

Reading through: The landing does move from narrative to analysis. "On September 19, 1945, a ship arrived..." — this returns to narrative specificity. Then the movement into analysis: "By 1950, when the Korean War began, the mythology had taken hold so thoroughly..." — this explains the arc. Then: "This is what a people offers itself when colonization has destroyed other forms of meaning-making..." — this is analysis, making explicit the terms of the bargain.

Is this re-explanation? Or is this the appropriate moment to make the implicit explicit? 

The landing is not re-explaining the chapter. It is answering the question that the chapter has been building to: How can a state be built on mythology? Why would people accept it? The landing makes this clear in analysis because narrative alone could not answer these questions fully.

Verdict: The landing's analytical turn is justified. It is not re-explanation. It is the answer to the question the chapter has been asking. PASS.
