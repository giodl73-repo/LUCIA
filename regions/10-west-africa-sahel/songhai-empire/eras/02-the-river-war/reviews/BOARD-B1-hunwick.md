## B-1 — John Hunwick — Board Review of The River War

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Translator and annotator of al-Sadi's Tarikh al-Sudan; specialist in the Arabic chronicle tradition of the western Sudan.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The date of 1468 for the capture of Timbuktu is consistent with the scholarly consensus based on al-Sadi.
- The seven-year, seven-month, seven-day siege of Djenne: correctly handled as formulaic oral-tradition duration. Al-Sadi gives this figure, and the chapter's acknowledgment that the number is traditional rather than precise is appropriate.
- Sunni Ali's death in 1492: al-Sadi and the Tarikh al-Fattash both record his death while returning from a campaign, likely by drowning. The chapter's honest acknowledgment of disagreement between sources on circumstances is correct.
- The characterization of al-Sadi as hostile to Sunni Ali is accurate. Al-Sadi wrote from Timbuktu's scholarly perspective and consistently portrays Sunni Ali as impious and tyrannical.
- The Tarikh al-Fattash's more sympathetic treatment is also accurately noted, though I would add that the Fattash's dating and authorship are contested in current scholarship (Nobili and others have raised questions about later interpolations).
- Muhammad Ture's coup in 1493 and the fatwa: consistent with the chronicle accounts.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The treatment of the Songhai state's relationship with Islam reflects the scholarly consensus: Sunni Ali operated within a framework that included both older Songhai religious traditions and Islam, without fully committing to either as the basis of state legitimacy.
- The scholars' role as commercial infrastructure (Maliki legal framework enabling trans-Saharan trade trust) is a well-supported interpretation.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The dual-source approach (using both chronicles while acknowledging their biases) is sound methodology.
- The structural interpretation of the scholar-ruler tension (competing forms of legitimacy rather than personal hostility) is strong.
- The honest-uncertainty handling of formulaic numbers and contested details.

**What needs correction:**
- Minor: the chapter refers to "the Tarikh al-Fattash" as "written closer to the events." This needs qualification. The Fattash's composition history is complex; portions may have been written or revised considerably later than Mahmud Kati's lifetime. The chapter should say "attributed to a perspective closer to the events" or similar.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (one minor factual qualification needed)
