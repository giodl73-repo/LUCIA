# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Laguna Copperplate Inscription (900 CE) — the oldest known written document from the Philippines
- Chinese tribute records (Ming) — Tondo's 1373 and 1405 tribute missions
- Spanish colonial chronicles (Legazpi expedition accounts, 1570–71)
- *Relación de las Islas Filipinas* (Miguel de Loarca, 1582)

## Key Academic Works

- Antoon Postma — decipherment and analysis of the Laguna Copperplate Inscription
- William Henry Scott, *Barangay: Sixteenth-Century Philippine Culture and Society* (1994) — essential
- Laura Lee Junker, *Raiding, Trading, and Feasting: The Political Economy of Philippine Chiefdoms* (1999)
- Vicente Rafael — Spanish colonialism and Philippine culture
- Zeus Salazar — Pantayong Pananaw (Filipino-centered historiography)

## Needed

- Archaeological work on pre-colonial Manila Bay settlement sites
- Studies on the Tondo polity's relationships with other Philippine and regional polities
- Analysis of the transition from Tondo to Spanish Manila
