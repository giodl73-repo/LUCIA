# CLEAN — E-2 Voice Keeper
## Safavids Era 1: The Founding

**Baseline voice:** Ceremonial-proclamatory. Weighted declarative sentences. The rhythm of a khutba: formal, building, settling into silence. No em-dashes. Analytical weight carried in the same register as narrative weight.

---

## Findings

**P2 — Finding 1**

Baseline voice (Section I):
> "He was twelve when he emerged from Gilan with a small following. He was thirteen when he won his first battles in the Pontic region, gathering Qizilbash fighters as he moved. He was fourteen when he took Tabriz."

Shifted voice (Section III):
> "The problem was immediate."

*Nature of shift:* This is not a register shift but a rhythm observation. "The problem was immediate" is a functional transitional sentence that serves the section well. But the three short declarative sentences in Section I have more weight than this opener. A slightly more weighted entry into Section III would align with the baseline. This is minor.

*Suggested revision:* Not strictly necessary, but "The problem arrived before the solution" or "What the declaration required was not yet present" would carry more ceremonial weight.

*Severity:* P3 (borderline).

---

**P3 — Finding 2**

> "Outside, if the window gave onto the royal garden, the claim was doubled."

*Nature:* The conditional "if the window gave onto the royal garden" softens the chapter's usual declarative confidence. The baseline voice does not hedge. The paragraph would be stronger if the window was simply giving onto the garden: "Outside the window, the royal garden made the same claim in water and cypress."

*Severity:* P3 (author decides).

---

**P3 — Finding 3**

Section VI, landing sequence:
> "The dynasty would continue for two more centuries, through eras that dwarfed the founding in architectural achievement and diplomatic sophistication. Shah Abbas I would rebuild Isfahan into one of the great cities of the Islamic world."

*Nature:* These two sentences are future-oriented and use the forward-looking "would" construction. This is the standard LUCIA landing technique (naming the future without narrating it) and is used correctly. But the phrasing "eras that dwarfed the founding" implies a comparison hierarchy — the founding is smaller than what follows. The founding does not need to be measured against what it produced. The founding is the declaration; what follows is the consequence. The phrasing could be revised to: "The dynasty would continue for two more centuries. Shah Abbas I would rebuild Isfahan." Simple. The comparative "dwarfed" introduces a ranking the chapter doesn't need.

*Severity:* P3 (author decides).

## Voice-Random-Paragraph Test

Three paragraphs selected:

**Paragraph from Section II:** "Tabriz in the autumn of 907 AH smelled of dye and horses..." — Can the reader identify the culture from the rhythm alone? Yes. The opening detail (smell, season, date in AH format) and the physical specificity identify this as a Persianate court culture in a moment of transformation. Voice holds.

**Paragraph from Section IV:** "The Safavid aesthetic was not decoration. It was argument. The naqqash..." — Can the reader identify the culture from the rhythm alone? Yes. The declarative opening ("was not / was") followed by the specific Arabic term and the theological argument embedded in description is the chapter's established register. Voice holds.

**Paragraph from Section V:** "Chaldiran, in August of 920 AH: a broad plain in northwestern Iran..." — Can the reader identify the culture from the rhythm alone? Yes. The colon-introduced location, the specificity of the dating (AH calendar leads), and the factual-weight delivery are all consistent with the baseline. Voice holds.

**Em-dash count:** 0. Well within limit.

- Finding 1 (P3): Author decides. Minor rhythm note for Section III opening.
- Finding 2 (P3): Author decides. The conditional "if" softens; a revision strengthens.
- Finding 3 (P3): Author decides. "Dwarfed" introduces an implicit comparison; removal strengthens the landing.

No P1 or P2 violations found. Voice is consistent throughout.
