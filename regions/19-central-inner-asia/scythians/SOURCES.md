# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Herodotus, *Histories*, Book IV — the primary ancient account of Scythian culture and geography
- Hippocrates, *On Airs, Waters, and Places* — Greek medical-ethnographic observations on steppe peoples
- Strabo, *Geography* — later synthesis of Greek knowledge of the Pontic steppe
- Archaeological record: the Scythian kurgans of the Pontic steppe (Chertomlyk, Solokha, Tovsta Mohyla)

## Key Academic Works

- Renate Rolle, *The World of the Scythians* (1989)
- Esther Jacobson, *The Art of the Scythians* (1995)
- David Braund — Scythians and the Greek Black Sea colonies
- Askold Ivantchik — Scythian history and archaeology
- Michael Vickers and David Gill — Scythian gold and Greek craftsmanship

## Needed

- Recent archaeological surveys of the Pontic steppe kurgan landscape
- Studies on the relationship between Scythian art and Chinese and Siberian animal styles
- Analysis of the Scythian-Greek hybrid communities of the Black Sea colonies
