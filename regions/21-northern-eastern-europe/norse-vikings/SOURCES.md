# Sources — Norse / Vikings

## Primary Sources

- *Prose Edda* by Snorri Sturluson — c. 1220; the systematic account of Norse mythology and skaldic poetics
- *Poetic Edda* — 13th-century collection of earlier mythological and heroic poetry; *Völuspá*, *Hávamál*, etc.
- *Íslendingabók* by Ari Þorgilsson — c. 1122; the earliest history of Iceland; foundational source
- *Egils Saga*, *Njáls Saga*, *Laxdæla Saga* — the major Icelandic sagas; 13th-century prose narratives of the settlement age
- *Grœnlendinga Saga* and *Eiríks Saga Rauða* — the Vinland sagas; Columbus preceded by five centuries
- *Heimskringla* by Snorri Sturluson — c. 1230; the kings' sagas; narrative history of Norse kingship
- Runestones — especially the Jelling stones (Denmark, c. 965) — the primary visual-textual record
- Ibn Fadlan's *Risala* — 922 CE; Arab diplomat's account of the Volga Norse; the most vivid outside observation
