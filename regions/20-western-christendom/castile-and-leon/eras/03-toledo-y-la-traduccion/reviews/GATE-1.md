## Dimension Notes

**Scene (5/5):** The drop into Gerard's arrival is complete — person (Gerard of Cremona), place (Toledo, specifically its streets, the cathedral workshops), moment (1145, the arrival, the first encounter with the smell), and stakes (a scholar who has come from Lombardy for a single text and will stay forty years). The opening paragraph earns its score on the first read.

**World (5/5):** Toledo's world emerges through its own grammar. The Zocodover's Arabic etymology is not explained as a sign of convivencia — it is just what the market is called. The three-language translation chain (Arabic > vernacular > Latin > scholarly Latin of a mathematician) shows the world's actual workings without naming them as "multicultural exchange." Alfonso VII's imperial ambition explains the funding of translation through its own internal logic, not through modern approval.

**Honesty (5/5):** The opening meets the v1.2 Inside-Voice Honesty standard. When the opening says "Not because the conqueror was wise, though Alfonso was not foolish. Not because tolerance was a policy, though it was, unevenly, sometimes" — this refuses both romanticization and condemnation by embedding the complexity in the culture's own pragmatic terms. The market logic ("a manuscript is worth money") is not cynical editorializing but the opening's own characterization of how the city worked. No verdict on convivencia; the mechanism is shown and the reader forms the verdict.

**Texture (5/5):** The sensory range is exceptional — the smell of two cities, the cedar and tannery and manuscript dust, the cold river, the candles shortening, Abubacer's "unhurried cadence." This is not decorative; the smells establish the two-city nature of Toledo before any explanation arrives.

**Voice (4/5):** The register is scholarly-precise without being academic. The long sentence that explains Alfonso VII's title and Toledo's role in sustaining it is the one passage that risks pulling toward essay-mode, but recovers immediately. One point deducted not for failure but for the slight institutional explanation in the penultimate paragraphs — well-controlled but visible.

**Pull (5/5):** The specific pull of "he had come for Ptolemy's Almagest" is strengthened by the forty-year answer and the students' note that "the Almagest was the least of what Toledo contained." The closing image — Gerard bending over manuscripts, Abubacer's voice moving through Arabic in the candlelight — earns its weight as a terminal image.

**Em-dash count:** 1 (within the ≤5 limit for openings)
