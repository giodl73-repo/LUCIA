# Sources — Capetian France

## Primary Sources

- Suger of Saint-Denis, *Vita Ludovici Grossi Regis* (Life of Louis VI) and *De Administratione* — 12th century; the Capetian chronicle and the theory of Gothic architecture
- Joinville, *Vie de Saint Louis* — c. 1309; personal memoir of Louis IX's crusade; the great biographical source for the saint-king
- Guillaume de Nangis, *Chronique latine* — 13th-century royal chronicle
- *Grandes Chroniques de France* — the official Capetian history, compiled at Saint-Denis
- Records of the *Parlement de Paris* — from 1254; the most extensive medieval French administrative record
- Philip IV's anti-Templar dossiers — 1307; the trial records
- *Ordonnances des rois de France* — the royal legislation series

## Secondary Sources

- Duby, Georges. *France in the Middle Ages 987–1460*. Blackwell, 1991.
- Hallam, Elizabeth M. *Capetian France 987–1328*. Longman, 1980.
- Baldwin, John W. *The Government of Philip Augustus*. University of California Press, 1986.
- Le Goff, Jacques. *Saint Louis*. Notre Dame Press, 2009.
- Jordan, William Chester. *The French Monarchy and the Jews*. University of Pennsylvania Press, 1989.
- Strayer, Joseph R. *The Reign of Philip the Fair*. Princeton University Press, 1980.
- Bony, Jean. *French Gothic Architecture of the 12th and 13th Centuries*. University of California Press, 1983.
- Lot, Ferdinand and Robert Fawtier. *Histoire des institutions françaises au moyen âge*. 3 vols. PUF, 1957–1962.
