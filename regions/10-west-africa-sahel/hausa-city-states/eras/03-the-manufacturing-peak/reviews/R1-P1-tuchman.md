# Panel-1 Review — Barbara Tuchman
## Hausa Era 3: The Manufacturing Peak (Rewrite v2)

**Voice:** Narrative craft — pacing, scene construction, arc

Ibrahim al-Katsinawi is the right anchor. A merchant assessing damaged cloth is a better entry than a dyer in his pit because the merchant is in motion, making judgments, negotiating — the scene has dramatic stakes built in. The cloth that arrives at the wrong weight tells you more about the guild system in three sentences than the original opening's extended process description did in three paragraphs.

What I am watching: Ibrahim's interiority is established but he is not yet tested. The opening works as atmosphere and argument. The full chapter must give him (or a figure like him) at least one scene where a decision is difficult — not just assessment, but choice under pressure. The competition needs a moment where someone loses.

The brass weight ending is correct. It is the kind of image that should return. But the full chapter must earn it; the weight cannot simply reappear as decoration at the close. It has to have meant something throughout.

**On Amina:** The reference is present in the opening ("the walls were already going up") and framed through Ibrahim's merchant logic. For the full chapter's Amina section, I need a scene — not a summary of campaigns, a scene. The moment of mud and mosquitoes the cross-compare called for. The opening has primed the reader to receive Amina as supply-chain logic. The chapter must then make her human.

**On the Bori:** The drums in the opening are well-placed. The reader files them under "present but not explained." The chapter must return to them and give them weight. They cannot remain ambient background. If the mosque-Bori coexistence is the structural engine, the engine must run in the narrative, not just be gestured at.

**The question I would push on:** What does Ibrahim want, beyond a good cloth price? A merchant with only professional motivations is a function, not a person. Give him something that the system cannot give him — or something the system threatens to take from him — and the arc will have stakes beyond the intellectual argument.
