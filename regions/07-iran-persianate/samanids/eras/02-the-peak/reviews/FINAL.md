## Innovations Flagged

### Innovation 1 — Court-Translational Voice Register
**Dimension:** Voice
**Source:** Samanids Era 2, chapter.md
**Description:** A new voice register sustained at full chapter length (~5,100 words). Characteristics: precise and observational; bilingual in intellectual reference world (Arabic scholarly vocabulary and Persian poetic tradition); analytical weight carried in the same register as narrative; pausing mechanism is the period and colon. Distinct from Urban/scholarly (Abbasid) which tends toward density in a single intellectual tradition; distinct from Philosophical-Devotional (Tibetan) which builds meaning clause by clause; distinct from Argumentative/Text-Centered (Talmudic) which questions and accumulates. The defining feature: the prose inhabits two intellectual traditions simultaneously, and its authority derives from that double inhabitation.
**Rubric candidate:** Addition to Voice Spectrum in STYLE-GUIDE.md, with the Samanid chapter as exemplar.

### Innovation 2 — Conceptual Pivot Word
**Dimension:** Evidence / Progression
**Source:** Samanids Era 2, opening.md and chapter.md
**Description:** A single untranslated word (*farr*) serves as the structural anchor for the full chapter — introduced in the opening, given contextual definition without scholarly gloss, returned to in the landing, and doing the analytical work of the era's central question throughout. Related to "Primary Art as Structural Evidence" (v1.3) but distinct: the pivot word is a linguistic artifact concentrating the culture's worldview into a recoverable utterance rather than a physical object or cultural practice. The word arrives and defines itself through context; the reader understands it as an insider without being taught it as an outsider.
**Rubric candidate:** Log in INNOVATIONS.md; assess at 2-instance threshold.
