# Sources — Brazil

## Key Academic Works
- Gilberto Freyre, *Casa-Grande & Senzala* (1933; trans. *The Masters and the Slaves*, 1946) — foundational and contested; the racial democracy thesis
- Sérgio Buarque de Holanda, *Raízes do Brasil* (1936) — the cordial man; essential for understanding Brazilian social character
- Boris Fausto, *A Concise History of Brazil* (1999, Cambridge) — the standard survey
- Thomas Skidmore, *Black into White: Race and Nationality in Brazilian Thought* (1974) — the racial ideology
- Leslie Bethell, ed., *Brazil: Empire and Republic 1822–1930* (1989, Cambridge)
- Ana Paula Glinfskoi Thé — Candomblé and Afro-Brazilian religion
- Eduardo Viveiros de Castro — Amazonian indigenous cosmology; *The Inconstancy of the Indian Soul* (2011)

## Primary Sources
- Gilberto Freyre — multiple works on Northeastern Brazil and the plantation society
- Joaquim Nabuco, *Abolitionism* (1883) — the abolitionist case in Brazil's own terms
- Carolina Maria de Jesus, *Quarto de Despejo (Child of the Dark)* (1960) — the favela from inside; essential
