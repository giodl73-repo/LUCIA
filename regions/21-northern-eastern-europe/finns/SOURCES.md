# Sources — Finns

## Primary Sources

- Mikael Agricola, *Se Wsi Testamenti* (New Testament in Finnish, 1548) — the foundational text of written Finnish
- Mikael Agricola, *Rucouskiria* (Prayer Book, 1544) and *ABC-kirja* — the complete Agricola corpus
- *Kalevalaic runo poetry* — oral tradition; collected in the 19th century by Elias Lönnrot as the *Kalevala* (1835, 1849)
- Swedish administrative records of Finland — castle and taxation records
- Peace of Nöteborg (1323) — the border treaty between Sweden and Novgorod dividing Finland

## Secondary Sources

- Jutikkala, Eino and Kauko Pirinen. *A History of Finland*. 6th ed. WSOY, 2003.
- Sihvo, Hannes. *Karjalan kuva: Karelianismin taustaa ja vaiheita autonomian aikana*. 1973.
- Pentikäinen, Juha Y. *Kalevala Mythology*. Indiana University Press, 1989.
- Salo, Unto. *Suomi ja Häme, Häme ja Satakunta*. 2000.
- Tarkiainen, Kari. *Ruotsin itämaa: Suomen historia 1150–1809*. SLS, 2010.
- Mäkinen, Virpi. *Property Rights in the Late Medieval Discussion on Franciscan Poverty*. 2001.
