## BOARD — Gunther Holbl — Review of Ptolemaic Egypt, Era 1: The Founding

**Overall assessment:** The chapter is historically sound and institutionally sophisticated. The dual-legitimacy argument is correctly framed and accurately executed. The political chronology is reliable. I have a few corrections and one suggestion.

**Factual accuracy:**

1. **Ptolemy's age at the body-snatching.** The chapter describes him as "a man in his sixties" at the Memphis scene. The body was intercepted c. 321 BCE, and Ptolemy was born c. 367 BCE, making him approximately 46, not in his sixties. The crowning in 305 BCE would put him at approximately 62. The opening scene conflates the body's arrival (321 BCE) with the crowning (305 BCE) in a single visual. **Correction needed:** either adjust the age to mid-forties for the body's arrival, or clarify that the scene as written is the crowning, not the interception.

2. **Perdiccas's invasion.** The chapter states Perdiccas tried to cross "at the eastern branch of the Nile" and that "two thousand of his men drowned or were taken by crocodiles." The crossing attempt was near the fortress of Camelou Teichos (the Camel's Fort) in the vicinity of Memphis, not at Pelusium as the chapter implies. The two thousand figure is from Diodorus and is approximately correct. **Minor correction:** adjust the location reference.

3. **Demetrius of Phalerum.** The chapter correctly identifies him as the Library's architect but should note that his role is debated. Some scholars attribute the mature Library more fully to Ptolemy II's reign. The chapter's notes acknowledge this, but the chapter text presents Demetrius's role with more certainty than the evidence supports. **Suggestion:** add a qualifier ("tradition credits Demetrius" or similar).

4. **Ptolemy II's glass sarcophagus.** The chapter states that Ptolemy II replaced Alexander's gold coffin with glass. This comes from Strabo, but the detail is uncertain and may refer to a later Ptolemy. **Suggestion:** add "according to Strabo" or "the later sources say."

**Institutional accuracy:**

5. The priestly evaluation process is presented at the right level of generality. The specific criteria (temple maintenance, offerings, sacred animal cults) are correct. The Satrap Stela is correctly dated and its significance accurately described.

6. The co-regency between Ptolemy I and Ptolemy II (285-283 BCE) is correctly represented.

7. The characterization of Arsinoe II's political role is accurate and appropriately cautious.

**Overall verdict:** Sound. Four corrections needed, all minor. The chapter's institutional argument (symbol-accumulation as legitimacy) is an effective organizing principle that accurately represents the dynasty's founding strategy.
