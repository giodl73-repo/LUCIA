## The Gate — 30 Points (Openings)

The Gate scores the opening of each chapter. It asks whether the reader is
caught from the first lines — placed in a specific moment, given a living world
without a lecture, and pulled forward by honest, textured prose.

| Dimension | Points | Question |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Scene     | 0–5    | Does it open in a specific moment — a person, a place, a heartbeat of time? |
| World     | 0–5    | Does the culture's own reality emerge naturally — cosmology, values, logic — without being explained? |
| Honesty   | 0–5    | Free from outside judgment? No "primitive," no "advanced," no anachronistic morality? |
| Texture   | 0–5    | Sensory detail, material culture, landscape — can you feel where you are? |
| Voice     | 0–5    | Does the prose rhythm belong to this story? Not a textbook, not a lecture? |
| Pull      | 0–5    | Does it earn your desire to keep reading? Genuine momentum, not tricks? |

## Scoring Guidance — The Gate

Scores run 1–5. A 0 is reserved for a dimension that is absent by design (rare
and must be noted). Each anchor below is one sentence describing what that score
looks like in practice.

### Scene (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | The opening is an abstract statement with no anchoring moment, person, or place. |
| 2 | A time or place is mentioned, but it floats — no one is present, nothing is happening. |
| 3 | A specific moment exists, and a person or place is present, but the scene lacks sensory or dramatic immediacy. |
| 4 | The reader is placed in a vivid, particular moment with a clear sense of who or what anchors it. |
| 5 | The opening lands like a drop into cold water — wholly specific, alive, unavoidable. |

### World (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | No sense of a distinct culture; could be anywhere or any time. |
| 2 | Cultural markers are present (names, objects) but the cosmology or values remain opaque. |
| 3 | The world's logic is visible and consistent, though it is shown more than felt. |
| 4 | The culture's way of seeing things — its categories, its stakes — shapes the prose naturally. |
| 5 | The world has a gravity of its own; you understand its rules without being taught them. |

### Honesty (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | The opening contains explicit outside judgment — words like "primitive," "barbaric," or implied pity. |
| 2 | No overt judgment, but the framing subtly ranks the culture against a modern baseline. |
| 3 | The culture is treated on its own terms, though occasional neutral-but-distancing language appears. |
| 4 | The prose inhabits the culture's perspective without editorializing or sentimentalizing. |
| 5 | The culture is so fully itself that no outside vantage point is even available to the reader. |

**v1.2 Amendment — Inside-Voice Honesty:** The highest-scoring chapters ground the No Verdicts discipline in the culture's own historiographic practice rather than applying it as an external editorial constraint. Three techniques discovered: (1) **Open Silence** — when the oral tradition deliberately leaves a story unresolved, the text honors that silence rather than filling it with modern certainty (Abu Bakr II's Atlantic voyage: "They say he did not come back. There is a difference."). (2) **Outsider's Bias as Inside Characterization** — when an outside observer's account is used, their biases become evidence about THEM, not about the culture ("He measured it against his own system and found it wanting. The Malians were not measuring."). (3) **No-Verdict Decline via Cultural Attribution** — when telling decline, the refusal to judge is attributed to the culture's own voice, not to editorial restraint ("This was not a fall. The jelilu would not use the word."). To score 5, the honesty should feel like it belongs to the CULTURE, not to the project's rules.

### Texture (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | No sensory detail; the opening could have been written without any research. |
| 2 | Generic sensory language ("the air was warm," "the ground was dry") that fits any setting. |
| 3 | Specific details are present — food, tools, materials — but they remain a list rather than a felt world. |
| 4 | Sensory and material detail is woven into action and thought; it grounds without overwhelming. |
| 5 | The texture is so specific and so embedded that the culture's material life is inseparable from the prose. |

### Voice (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | The prose reads like a textbook or encyclopaedia entry — informational, affectless. |
| 2 | There is a narrative voice, but it is generic literary prose with no particular character. |
| 3 | A distinctive register is present, but it occasionally lapses into lecture or explanation. |
| 4 | The prose rhythm is consistent and belongs to this story — it does not wander into other modes. |
| 5 | The voice is unmistakable and inseparable from the culture being depicted; it could not tell any other story. |

### Pull (0–5)

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | The opening provides no reason to continue; it ends as inertly as it began. |
| 2 | There is mild interest, but it relies on the subject's inherent novelty rather than earned momentum. |
| 3 | The reader wants to continue, though the pull is gentle and could be broken by any distraction. |
| 4 | A genuine question or tension is open; turning the page feels like a want, not a duty. |
| 5 | The opening creates a pull so specific and internal to the story that abandoning it feels like a loss. |

## The Chronicle — 60 Points (Chapters)

The Chronicle scores the full chapter. It asks whether the culture lives on the
page for its entire length — grounded in real evidence, shaped by a clear arc,
animated by dimensional people, and closed at the right weight.

| Dimension   | Points | Question |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Immersion   | 0–10   | Does the reader live inside this culture's world the whole chapter? |
| Evidence    | 0–10   | Specific stories, named people, real events — historically grounded? |
| Progression | 0–10   | Does the era's arc emerge? Does it build, not repeat? |
| Figures     | 0–10   | Are leaders and people vivid, dimensional, real? |
| Voice       | 0–10   | Consistent tone throughout? No register shifts? |
| Landing     | 0–10   | Does it close at the right weight? |

---

## Scoring Guidance — The Chronicle

Scores run 1–10. Each band below describes what that range looks like.

### Immersion (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | The chapter is encyclopaedic summary; the culture is described, never inhabited. |
| 3–4   | Moments of immersion appear but dissolve into exposition; the reader is frequently pulled out. |
| 5–6   | The reader spends most of the chapter inside the world, with occasional breaks in the frame. |
| 7–8   | Immersion is the default state; lapses are brief and recovery is quick. |
| 9–10  | The reader does not leave the world; the chapter sustains a complete inside perspective from first line to last. |

### Evidence (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | Claims are generic and ungrounded; no named people, places, or events. |
| 3–4   | Some specificity, but examples are loose or feel illustrative rather than historical. |
| 5–6   | Named figures and real events are present; the chapter has a factual skeleton. |
| 7–8   | Evidence is woven into narrative; stories feel drawn from the historical record, not invented for effect. |
| 9–10  | The chapter is dense with specific, verifiable texture — names, objects, events — and none of it feels forced. |

**v1.3 Amendment — Knowledge-System-as-Evidence:** The highest-scoring chapters don't just cite evidence — they let the culture's own knowledge system BE the evidence. Three techniques discovered: (1) **Primary Art as Structural Evidence** — a culture's own artistic production (Tang poetry, Benin bronzes) used not as decoration but as primary evidence, characterization, and structural turning points simultaneously. The poem or plaque does the analytical work. (2) **Disciplinary Depth** — a single specific exercise (Maliki inheritance calculation, navigational star fix) shown at the level of method, transforming generic "they had scholarship" into verifiable intellectual practice. (3) **Body-as-Instrument** — for kinesthetic knowledge systems (Polynesian navigation, San tracking), rendering the knowledge through sensation in the reader's body, not through procedural description. To score 9+, evidence should emerge from the culture's own epistemic medium, not from the author's citations about it.

**v1.5 Amendment — Source Asymmetry as Narrative Technique:** When the source base is partial, external, or asymmetric, the chapter should turn the limitation into a technique rather than apologizing for it. Six approaches confirmed: **twin-source narration** (Soninke — outside source for material detail, inside source for interpretive framework, tension between them productive); **chronicler-within-chronicle** (Kanem-Bornu — when the primary source is a single named author, make the chronicler the anchor figure); **outsider-as-structural-evidence** (Wolof, Benin — the outsider's account reveals the structural relationship, not the culture's character); **anonymous builder** (Swahili — for archaeological cultures, embodied unnamed figure turns anonymity into collective voice); **primary art as evidence** (Tang — the culture's own creative production does the analytical work); **source gap as cultural honesty** (Soninke/Dausi — the tradition's own silences are honored, not filled). To score 9+, the chapter should make the reader feel the source situation as part of the story, not as a constraint external to it.

**v1.5 Amendment — Knowledge System at Method Level (extends v1.3):** Five additional instances confirm that the v1.3 Disciplinary Depth technique applies broadly: the dyer's nose-to-vat assessment as intellectual act (Hausa), the navigator's hull-reading as embodied knowledge (Tongan), the inheritance-to-algebra escalation (Abbasid), the Sanskrit-to-Tibetan translation at syllable level (Tibet), the law-code case as public theology (Babylon). The principle: show the knowledge system at the level of HOW IT WORKS — the specific exercise, the physical practice, the method — not at the level of what it produced. Any chapter that claims "they had a sophisticated tradition" without showing the tradition operating at method level has not met the v1.3 standard.

### Progression (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | The chapter has no arc; it circles the same material or lists facts without movement. |
| 3–4   | A loose chronological structure exists, but the chapter does not build toward anything. |
| 5–6   | The era's arc is present; the reader can feel that time has passed and things have changed. |
| 7–8   | The chapter builds with purpose; each section adds something that makes the next section necessary. |
| 9–10  | The progression feels inevitable — the chapter arrives where it must, having earned every step. |

**v1.1 Amendment — Structural Engine:** The highest-scoring chapters find the culture's own structural mechanism and make it the narrative engine. Not context, not background — the PLOT. The Kurukan Fuga's self-consuming redistribution logic, the Mourning War's grief-violence cycle, the eland as single-species integration pivot. To score 9+, the chapter should identify the culture's own logic and let it drive the arc the way a character's flaw drives a novel. Chronology alone is not progression.

**v1.4 Amendment — Book-Level Arc:** When a culture has multiple eras written as a book (3+ eras), the structural engine should sustain across the full arc. Three books confirm the technique: **Mali** (Kurukan Fuga's redistribution logic planted in Era 1, self-consuming in Era 3, recursive in Era 5), **Inca** (mit'a/vertical archipelago/quipu threads planted in Era 1, stressed in Era 4, broken/preserved in Era 5), **Rome** (res publica/libertas/mos maiorum/law planted in Era 1, tested in Era 2, weaponized in Era 3, transformed in Eras 4-5). The pattern: find the culture's own persistence mechanism, plant it in the first era, trace its transformations through subsequent eras, and let the final era's close return to the opening era's images. The book-level arc is not a requirement for individual chapters but becomes the standard when a full book is written. The culture's persistence mechanism delivers the book's final note: the jelilu still singing, the quipu still holding, the law still holding.

**v1.5 Amendment — Material Object as Structural Principle:** A physical object or material from the culture's own practice can serve as the chapter's organizing principle — not as metaphor but as mechanism. The object's properties determine the narrative's logic. Confirmed across 6 cultures: the curtain shielding the mai IS Kanem-Bornu's political theory made physical; the ship's clinker-built flexibility IS Norse social flexibility; the stone edict's durability IS Maurya's paradox of vulnerability outlasting power; the bronze plaque IS Benin's governance medium; the clay tablet IS Sumer's compulsion to record; the fortress-canal IS Urartu's state-building cycle. To score 9+, a material-object engine must derive from the culture's own relationship to that object, not from the author's symbolic imposition.

**v1.5 Amendment — Terminal Condition as Culture-Specific:** The structural engine's ending must be as specific to the culture as the engine itself. Five terminal-condition types confirmed: **legitimacy gap** (Songhai — military authority can't generate commercial-legal legitimacy), **deliberate self-consumption** (Tibet — the patron system performs correctly when the king dies), **categorical failure** (Inca — the information system encounters an event outside its categories), **reversible feedback loop** (Egypt — the cosmic loop runs backward when capacity is exceeded), **ecological constraint** (Wolof — the tsetse fly makes cavalry a flow, not a stock, and the supply route becomes the decisive variable). The terminal condition should follow from the engine's own logic, not from external shock.

**v1.5 Amendment — Non-Chronological Organizing Principle:** For cultures without a linear chronological arc, the chapter needs a non-temporal organizing principle — a species (San/eland), a landscape (Aboriginal/Country), a commercial space (Swahili/storehouse), a sensory map (Hausa/city-by-smell), a page (Jewish Talmud). Confirmed across 5 cultures. These chapters score highest when the organizing principle comes from the culture's own center of gravity: the eland for a hunting culture, the storehouse for a trade culture, the page for a text-centered culture. Chronology is not the only way to build progression; integration around a culture-specific center can be equally powerful.

### Figures (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | People are named but function as labels; no interior life, no particularity. |
| 3–4   | Figures have distinguishing traits, but they remain instruments of the story rather than presences. |
| 5–6   | Key figures are vivid enough to be remembered; their decisions make sense on their own terms. |
| 7–8   | Leaders and ordinary people alike are dimensional; the reader understands them without being told what to think. |
| 9–10  | The figures are genuinely alive — contradictory, surprising, fully human — and the chapter is unimaginable without them. |

**v1.1 Amendment — Honest Limits as Technique:** When the record cannot support a figure's interiority, the gap itself becomes characterization. Explicitly acknowledging what we cannot know (Inari Kunate's thoughts, /Twi!kxao's specific identity) deepens trust rather than breaking it. For oral/archaeological cultures, composite figures drawn from multiple ethnographic accounts are valid when honestly disclosed in figures.md. A score of 7+ is achievable even with thin records if the chapter handles the thinness as craft rather than evasion.

**v1.4 Amendment — Archaeological Figures:** For cultures where the archaeological record preserves structures but not names, the benchmark is embodied presence, not naming. A figure does not need a name, but a figure needs a body. A figure described only through an outside observer's account is a figure at one remove. A figure described through their own sensory experience is a figure in the room. (See Swahili anonymous builder vs. Ghana merchant-at-one-remove for the distinction.)

### Voice (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | The chapter shifts registers freely — encyclopaedic, then lyrical, then academic — with no governing tone. |
| 3–4   | A dominant register exists, but it breaks under the pressure of complex information. |
| 5–6   | The voice is consistent through most of the chapter; slippage is occasional and recovers. |
| 7–8   | The chapter sustains its register even through dense evidence and transitions. |
| 9–10  | The voice never wavers; it is the same instrument at the opening, the middle, and the close. |

**v1.4 Amendment — Voice Spectrum:** 16 voice types have been confirmed at chapter length across 27 cultures. Each culture requires its own voice register — inseparable from that culture's worldview, sustained without breaks, carrying both narrative and analytical weight. The voice is not style applied to content; it IS the culture's way of speaking on the page. Confirmed registers include: **Griot cadence** (Mali), **Steppe** (Mongol), **Maritime** (Tongan), **Oral-tradition** (San, Aboriginal), **Vertical** (Inca), **Hydraulic** (Khmer), **Sedimentary** (Sumer), **Monument** (Egypt), **Urban/scholarly** (Abbasid), **Argumentative** (Jewish Talmud), **Legal-public** (Roman), **Inscriptional** (Maya), **River** (Songhai), **Saga-Spare** (Norse), **Maritime Accumulative** (Swahili), **Philosophical-Devotional** (Tibet), **Edict-Inscriptional** (Maurya), and additional registers for Trade (Ghana), Chronicler (Kanem-Bornu), Diplomatic (Wolof), Scholarly-revolutionary (Fulani), and Urban-manufacturing (Hausa). To score 9+, the voice must: (1) be discovered from the culture's own expressive traditions, not borrowed from another culture's register; (2) sustain across ~5000 words without breaking into textbook or generic literary prose; (3) carry analytical argument in the same register as narrative — the rhythm does intellectual work, not just atmospheric work; (4) produce zero or near-zero em-dashes as an emergent property of the register's own rhythm.

### Landing (0–10)

| Band  | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| 1–2   | The chapter ends abruptly or trails off; there is no sense of weight or closure. |
| 3–4   | A closing is present, but it summarizes rather than lands; it tells the reader what was just read. |
| 5–6   | The close has presence; it feels like an ending rather than a stop. |
| 7–8   | The landing carries the chapter's full weight; it opens outward without overstating. |
| 9–10  | The final lines are inevitable — the only way this chapter could have ended — and they resonate past the page. |

**v1.1 Amendment — Culture's Own Persistence as Close:** The highest-scoring landings let the culture's own persistence mechanism deliver the final line. The jelilu still singing (oral tradition as continuity), the present-tense grammar of Haudenosaunee's own name ("people who ARE building"), the dual stream of oral and written memory. To score 9+, the landing should find the culture's own way of enduring and let that — not authorial summary — carry the final weight.

## How Rubric Versions Work

The rubric evolves through the innovation engine:

1. **Innovation logged** — A scorer notices an exceptional technique the rubric
   did not anticipate and logs it in `INNOVATIONS.md` with source, dimension,
   and description.
2. **Threshold reached** — When 2 or more innovations appear in the same
   dimension or category, a rubric amendment is proposed.
3. **Amendment reviewed** — The amendment is discussed against the existing
   anchors. Does it raise or clarify the bar? Does it fit the rubric's voice?
4. **Version incremented** — An approved amendment produces a new rubric
   version (v1.0 → v1.1 → … → v2.0 for major restructuring). The version
   header at the top of this file is updated, and the change is recorded in
   `INNOVATIONS.md`.
5. **Forward-only application** — New bars apply to chapters scored after the
   amendment. Scores already recorded are never retroactively adjusted.

The goal is a rubric that grows sharper as LUCIA is written — not one that
imposes a fixed standard before the project has discovered its own best work.
