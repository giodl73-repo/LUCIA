## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (Targeted)

**Focus:** Structural engine and terminal condition.

**What works:**
- The engine is correctly identified: the fiction of equality maintained through calibrated reciprocity. This is a genuine structural mechanism, not a theme imposed from outside.
- The terminal condition is specific: the system fails when one participant refuses to pretend. This is stronger than "the system collapsed" because it identifies the *mechanism* of failure, not just the event.
- The section plan builds toward the gold-complaint section as the structural turn, which is the right instinct. The system's fragility should become visible through its own operating logic (shortfalls in gifts reveal cracks in brotherhood).

**What needs strengthening:**
- The notes should specify the economic base more precisely. The chariot system required bronze (copper + tin), horses (breeding stock + pasture), and specialized labor (wheelwrights, trainers, grooms). Where did the tin come from? Who controlled the supply routes? The brotherhood system was not just diplomatic; it was an economic network. If the chapter does not show the supply chains, the structural engine will feel political rather than material.
- Section 6 (the gold complaints) should be framed not just as diplomatic friction but as structural evidence: when the gifts thin, the underlying economy is straining. Tushratta's complaints are not personal grievances; they are system diagnostics.
