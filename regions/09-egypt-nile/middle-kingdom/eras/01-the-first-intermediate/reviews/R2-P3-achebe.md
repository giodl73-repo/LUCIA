## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — Review of Notes (The Fragmentation)

**Focus:** Non-elite voice representation and cultural ownership.

**Assessment:** The plan directly addresses my primary concern from Panel-1. Section V dedicates ~700 words to the Eloquent Peasant, and the notes correctly frame this as a literary revolution, not a scholarly curiosity. The Coffin Texts' democratization of the afterlife is linked to the same broadening of voice. This is good.

**What works in the plan:**
- Ankhtifi (Section IV) and the Eloquent Peasant (Section V) together give the chapter two non-royal registers: the self-made nomarch and the commoner who uses language as power. This is the range the era requires.
- The notes correctly flag the dating uncertainty of the Eloquent Peasant and propose handling it with honest disclosure. This is the right approach. The text's concerns belong to this era regardless of its precise composition date.
- The voice register description ("administrative-literary") correctly names the challenge: the chapter must hold both the administrator's cadence and the literary tradition's questioning without one overriding the other.

**What needs watching:**
- Section V must not treat the Eloquent Peasant as an exhibit. The text must be entered from inside, the way the opening enters Khety's world from inside. The peasant must speak, not be described as speaking.
- The Coffin Texts material risks being treated as background information. The democratization of the afterlife is a theological revolution with material consequences: anyone who could afford the spells could claim eternal life. Show what this meant practically. What did a coffin cost? Who could afford one?
- Watch for the temptation to make the Fragmentation a story about progress: "the disorder produced creativity." This is true, but it must not become a verdict. The creativity existed alongside genuine suffering, and the texts themselves record both.

**Recommendation:** Proceed. But give the Eloquent Peasant a body and a voice, not just a literary-critical frame.
