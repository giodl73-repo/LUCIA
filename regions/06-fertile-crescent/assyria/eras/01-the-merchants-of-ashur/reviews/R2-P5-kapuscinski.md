## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — Review of Notes (Targeted)

**Focus:** Sensory plan.

**Section 3 (The Road) is the chapter's sensory test.** If the reader feels the journey from Ashur to Kanesh, the chapter will have a body. If not, it will remain what the opening already is: a document with a voice but without a temperature.

**What the road needs:**
- The heat of the steppe in summer. The cold of the mountain passes. The change in air as altitude increases.
- Donkeys. The sound of them. The smell of them. The way they move under load, deliberate and unhurried. The merchant who travels with donkeys moves at their pace, not his own.
- Water. Where it is and where it is not. The steppe is dry. The springs are known. Missing one means death.
- Night camps. The sky over the steppe is enormous and the stars are precise. These merchants traveled by known routes, but the route was not a road. It was a knowledge of where to stop.

**Section 2 (The City) needs:**
- The Tigris. The sound of water in a hot country. The cliff on which the temple stood, the view from the temple over the river plain.
- The workshops. Weaving sounds different from metalwork sounds different from clay-pressing sounds. A city has a specific acoustic texture.

**Section 4 (The Karum) needs:**
- Kanesh was Anatolian, not Mesopotamian. The air, the vegetation, the building materials were different from Ashur. The merchant arriving at Kanesh arrived in a different landscape. Show the difference.

**Verdict:** The plan has the right sections for sensory grounding. The execution will determine whether the chapter has a body or only a brain.
