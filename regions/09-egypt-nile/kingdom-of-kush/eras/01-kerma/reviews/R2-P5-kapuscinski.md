## Sensory Map for the Chapter

The notes identify sensory targets for each section. Good. Let me be more specific about what each section needs.

**Section 1 (Burial):** Already strong from the opening. Add: the color of the deffufa in afternoon light (terracotta, sun-baked, the color deepening as shadow crosses it). The mass of the structure from below. The silence after the corridor is sealed.

**Section 2 (River/Chokepoint):** This is the weakest sensory section in the plan. The Third Cataract: the sound of water over granite, the narrowing of the river, the speed of the current, the spray. The desert edge: the abrupt transition from green Nile margin to red-brown sand. The heat. This landscape is extreme, and the chapter must make the reader feel the extremity that shaped the kingdom.

**Section 3 (Cattle/City):** The most important sensory section for establishing the pastoral knowledge system. The smell of a cattle herd at dawn. The dust kicked up by hooves. The sound of lowing carried across flat ground. The feel of a long-horned cow's flank under a herdsman's hand. The Kerma pottery: the black-topped redware, burnished to a shine that catches light. Show a potter's hands.

**Section 4 (Tombs):** The grave goods are sensory objects. Gold, faience, ivory. The weight of gold in the hand. The blue-green of Egyptian faience against the dark leather of a Kerma garment. The bone needles, the mica ornaments. These objects are the culture's material signature, and they should be felt as objects, not catalogued as evidence.

**Section 5 (Egypt Watches):** The Egyptian fortresses at the Second Cataract are massive mud-brick structures on the riverbank. What does a military frontier look and feel like? The walls, the river below, the view south into territory the garrison is watching. The Execration Texts: clay pots inscribed with names and deliberately smashed. The sound of breaking pottery. The dust.

**Section 6 (Landing):** The mounds seen from the river. The long view. The silence. Let the final image be visual and spatial: the mounds still standing, the river still flowing north.
