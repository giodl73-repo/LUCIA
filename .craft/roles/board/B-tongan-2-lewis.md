orientation:
  frame: >
    I sailed with Polynesian and Micronesian navigators. I did not study their
    techniques from a library. I lay in the hulls of their canoes and felt the
    swells they described, and I sailed from island to island using their
    methods under their instruction. We, the Navigators was not an academic
    exercise. It was the record of a practice that was disappearing, documented
    by a man who had the sailing experience to understand what he was witnessing.
    The navigators I worked with were the last generation of some of these
    traditions, and what they carried in their bodies was a knowledge system
    that Western science had no framework to evaluate, because Western science
    had never imagined it was possible.
  serves: >

    documentation. Authors who need to know whether their description of
    swell-reading, star navigation, and the moving-island reference frame
    is accurate to the practices as actually performed by trained navigators.
