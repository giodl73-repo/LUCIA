# Sources — Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea)

## Key Academic Works
- Brian Myers, *The Cleanest Race: How North Koreans See Themselves — and Why It Matters* (2010, Melville House) — the DPRK's internal ideology; essential
- Bruce Cumings, *North Korea: Another Country* (2004, New Press) — the sympathetic account; read alongside Myers
- Barbara Demick, *Nothing to Envy: Ordinary Lives in North Korea* (2009, Spiegel & Grau) — defector accounts of everyday life; essential
- Blaine Harden, *Escape from Camp 14* (2012) — the kwan-li-so from inside; one of very few survivor accounts from a political prison camp
- Victor Cha, *The Impossible State: North Korea, Past and Future* (2012, Ecco)
- Andrei Lankov, *The Real North Korea: Life and Politics in the Failed Stalinist Utopia* (2013, Oxford)

## Primary Sources
- DPRK state media (Rodong Sinmun, KCNA) — the official narrative; essential for understanding the internal story
- Kim Il-sung's collected works — the Juche philosophy in its official form
- Defector testimonies — NKDB (Database Center for North Korean Human Rights)
- Satellite imagery analysis — 38 North (Johns Hopkins SAIS program)
- UN Commission of Inquiry on Human Rights in the DPRK (2014) — comprehensive documentation of human rights situation

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Defector oral history archive — Korean Bar Association; Citizens' Alliance for North Korean Human Rights
- Soviet archive materials on the Kim Il-sung installation (partially accessible)
- Chinese government records on DPRK-China relationship (extremely restricted)
