## Baseline Register

Established in the opening: court/ceremonial/observational. Characteristics:
- Sentences lengthen when court deliberateness requires; shorten at moments of stillness
- Every detail carries weight; no decorative language
- Interior perspective: reader is inside the chamber, inside the court's logic
- Zero em-dashes
- Present-tense interiority, past-tense narration — the present is the court's self-understanding; the past is what can be observed from outside it
- No register for reader orientation ("it is important to understand," "to see why," "in other words")

## Voice Scan

**Finding 1 — P2 (Single sentence departure)**
Chapter text, Section 3: "Keim's work on women and labor in the Mangbetu state documents the hierarchies within the subject populations..."

This sentence names the scholar directly and uses the present-tense citation form ("documents"). This is academic register intruding into court-ceremonial voice. The information is important (and the honest-limits requirement makes the source citation appropriate in figures.md), but in the main chapter body it breaks the register.

Recommended fix: recast without naming the scholar. The content — the hierarchical distinctions within subject populations, the different positions of captured women versus Mangbetu noblewomen — is all that is needed. The source attribution belongs in figures.md, not in the chapter's body.

**Revised version:** "The distinctions within the subject populations were specific: women from captured or tributary groups provided labor and were bound within the court's household structures in ways that differed significantly from the position of Mangbetu noblewomen."

This carries the same content in the chapter's own register.
