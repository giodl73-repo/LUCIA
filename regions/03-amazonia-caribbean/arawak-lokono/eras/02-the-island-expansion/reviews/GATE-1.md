## Notes

**Strongest dimension:** Scene and World tied. The reader is so fully inside Kurima's perspective that the network logic (obligation as what makes you "real in other people's worlds") lands as felt experience, not anthropological observation.

**Most distinctive technique:** The structural engine is already visible in the opening — the network grows through accumulated individual decisions, not command or conquest. This framing has narrative consequences: the opening doesn't just hook the reader, it announces what the whole chapter will argue about how a civilization expands.

**Minor flag — Texture:** The goods are named (dried fish, clay vessels, cord) but the physical process of loading — the weight, the balance, the feel of the canoe settling — could be sharpened one more degree. The water color-change passage is excellent; the loading scene could match it.

**Voice note:** Zero em-dashes confirmed. The maritime-accumulative register finds its pauses in the period and the comma-chain. Consistent with v1.4 voice discipline.

**Innovation flag — none this stage:** The opening is excellent but does not introduce a technique the rubric doesn't anticipate. Flag for GATE-2 after chapter complete.
