## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuściński — Review of The Fragmentation (Opening)

**Overall:** I can see the granary but I cannot smell it. The opening is politically and structurally accomplished, but the bodies are missing. Khety is a mind making calculations. He needs to be a body in a room.

**What works:**
- "Khety son of Tefibi counted the grain." The opening sentence is perfect in its plainness. It is the sentence of a man who has more important things to do than be dramatic, and that plainness IS the drama. The sentence sounds like the administrative records it describes.
- The accumulation of inventories ("tax records and the land surveys and the lists of cattle and the inventories of copper tools and linen bolts and beer jars") has a physical rhythm, the rhythm of a scribe unrolling a papyrus and running his finger down a column. This is texture through structure.
- The final paragraph works because it is short and because the river is suddenly a character. After eight hundred words of human calculation, the river reasserts its indifference. This is the right ending.

**What doesn't:**
- No heat. Asyut is in Upper Egypt, and Upper Egypt is hot. The granary would be a specific temperature, would smell of stored emmer and dust and whatever material the walls were made of. Khety's scribes would be sweating over their papyrus. The ink would dry faster in the heat. The physical world of this scene is underdeveloped.
- No sound. What is outside the walls? Is it quiet? Is there the sound of construction, of militia drilling, of the river? The political tension is rendered through information but not through atmosphere. A reader should be able to hear the scene.
- Khety himself has no body. He counts, he knows, he calculates, but I do not know what he looks like, how he moves, whether he is old or young, tired or alert. The nomarch inscriptions sometimes describe physical vigor. Give him a body.

**The question I'd push on:**
Put me in the room. I want the granary: its size, its smell, the quality of the light, the feel of the grain between fingers. The political story is strong enough to carry the chapter. The sensory story needs to carry the reader's body into the scene. One paragraph of physical immersion would transform this opening from strong to exceptional.
