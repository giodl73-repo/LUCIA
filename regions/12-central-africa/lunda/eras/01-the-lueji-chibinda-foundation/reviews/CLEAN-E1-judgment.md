## Inside Lens / Achebe Test

Does the chapter tell the culture's story from inside? Checked paragraph by paragraph.

The passage in Section III: "These departures were not, from the Lunda perspective, a loss. They were the first evidence that the political culture was transmissible — that it could travel in a person..." The phrase "from the Lunda perspective" is the key signal. This is the author speaking from outside the culture's perspective, annotating it. This is a mild violation of the inside-lens principle. Revision recommended: embed this observation in the world's own logic rather than naming the perspective from outside.

**Revision required — Section III, final paragraph before the section break:**

Change: "These departures were not, from the Lunda perspective, a loss. They were the first evidence that the political culture was transmissible — that it could travel in a person, in the ceremonies and titles a person carried, and take root somewhere new."

To something like: "The departures were not a loss. Katema had left with what he had carried from the center: the titles, the ceremonies, the knowledge of how authority was supposed to be organized. Somewhere to the west, that knowledge was becoming a community. It had traveled in him. This was what the lukanu's authority could do, though no one had yet named it."

All other passages pass the inside-lens test.
