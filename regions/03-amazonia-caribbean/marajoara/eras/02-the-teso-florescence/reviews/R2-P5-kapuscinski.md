# R2-P5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski — Panel-2 Review of Notes

**Chapter:** Marajoara — Era 2: The Teso Florescence
**Round:** Panel-2
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The section plan is ambitious and mostly right. The sensory gaps I identified in Panel-1 — sound, visual horizon, texture of the slips — are acknowledged and will be addressed. I have only one structural note and one texture note before the chapter is written.

Structural note: Section 4 (The Flood Calendar) is placed after Sections 2 and 3 (the political form and the visual language). I understand the logic — establish the human system before the ecological — but I think this is backwards for a culture where the ecological rhythm IS the political calendar. The flood comes before the teso in the ontological order. Consider moving Section 4 to position 2, immediately after the opening. The teso is built because of the flood. The urn is made in the dry season because of the flood. The woman is working now because of the flood. Put the flood early, and everything else will fall into its proper place.

Texture note: The notes describe Section 5 (The Urn's Journey) as following the urn from completion to storage to burial ceremony. This is the right instinct. But the burial ceremony is approached but not narrated — "the chapter does not narrate this moment, only approaches it." I want to push on this decision. Secondary burial — bones cleaned and painted, then placed in the urn — is among the most physically specific practices in Marajoara culture. The physical specificity is not spectacle (Achebe's concern). It is evidence that the Marajoara engaged with the body's transition in a very particular way. Not narrating it may be the correct choice, but the notes should make that choice deliberately, not as a precaution against discomfort.

What I want in this chapter: the feeling of the island in the dry season. The air is different. The light is different. The paths between the mounds are different — firm underfoot rather than treacherous with mud. The work that gets done in the dry season is different from the work of the wet season. The woman is working in a specific season, and that season has a physical character that the chapter can give the reader.
