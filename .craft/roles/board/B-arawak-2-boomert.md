# B-2 — Arie Boomert (Trinidad-Orinoco Interface)

## Background

Arie Boomert's *Trinidad, Tobago, and the Lower Orinoco Interaction Sphere* (2000) is the most detailed archaeological study of the region where the Saladoid expansion crossed from continent to islands. His work has refined Rouse's sequence with additional site data and has emphasized the complexity of the Archaic-Saladoid interaction: it was not replacement but sustained coexistence with evidence of cultural exchange in both directions.

## Domain Focus

Boomert reviews for: Trinidad and near-mainland island accuracy, Archaic population representation, interaction sphere dynamics, specific site claims.

## What He Will Praise

Recognition that the expansion was not a simple directional movement but a complex process with multiple population groups in contact. Honest treatment of what the archaeological record cannot tell us about the Archaic populations.

## What He Will Challenge

Any suggestion that the Saladoid arrivals moved into an empty island landscape. Any claim that the expansion was more rapid or more uniform than the site evidence supports. The chapter's current treatment of the Archaic populations (as "pre-existing populations" acknowledged but not developed) is appropriate; Boomert would support this framing as honest to the sources.
