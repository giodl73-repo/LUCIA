# Sources — Xhosa

## Primary / Oral

- Xhosa izibongo (praise poetry) — maintained oral tradition
- Mqhayi, Samuel Edward Krune. *Ityala Lamawele* (1914) — the first Xhosa novel
- Peires, Jeff B. — interviews with Nongqawuse survivors (documented in *The Dead Will Arise*)
- Rubusana, Walter Benson. *Zemk'iinkomo Magwalandini* (1906) — collected Xhosa literary tradition

## Academic

- Peires, J.B. *The Dead Will Arise: Nongqawuse and the Great Xhosa Cattle-Killing Movement of 1856–7*. 1989. Ravan Press. — the definitive study
- Peires, J.B. *The House of Phalo: A History of the Xhosa People in the Days of Their Independence*. 1981. Ravan Press. — the pre-colonial and colonial history
- Mostert, Noel. *Frontiers: The Epic of South Africa's Creation and the Tragedy of the Xhosa People*. 1992. Alfred A. Knopf.
- Beinart, William & Bundy, Colin. *Hidden Struggles in Rural South Africa: Politics and Popular Movements in the Transkei and Eastern Cape, 1890–1930*. 1987. James Currey.
- Jordan, A.C. *The Wrath of the Ancestors* (novel, 1940; trans. 1980). — fictional treatment of Xhosa political dilemmas from inside

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on the Nongqawuse prophecy and its political context (post-Peires)
- Studies on the school/traditional tension in Xhosa society as a political division
- Analysis of Samuel Mqhayi's work in the context of African nationalism
- Archaeological surveys of Xhosa frontier war sites
