# R2-P1 — Barbara Tuchman — Panel Review, Round 2 (Notes)

**Text reviewed:** notes.md — The Lueji-Chibinda Foundation
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

The production bible is solid. The six-section structure is the right shape for this material. I am particularly pleased that the notes address my main concern from Round 1 directly: Section I will now include "a specific case — one dispute she adjudicates, one person standing before her." That is the right solution. The meeting scene in Section II is planned as a specific setting — "dry-season riverbank? the edge of her compound?" — which is correct thinking, though the author needs to choose one, not leave three options open.

My remaining concern is Section III, the period between arrival and transmission. The notes describe "the political figures around Lueji who must be convinced" but then say "or who do not need to be convinced, because the logic is clear to anyone who understands both forms of authority." This is an either/or that the chapter cannot leave open. Either there is resistance and the chapter shows it being overcome, which gives narrative tension, or there is natural consensus and the chapter shows how that consensus forms, which is a different kind of scene. Both can work. What cannot work is skipping the moment entirely.

The headmen who left — the ones who departed and founded their own communities — are the chapter's best source of tension for Section III. They left because they did not accept the marriage or the logic of the Chibinda Ilunga authority. That departure, shown in one or two specific figures if possible, or even as a single unnamed headman deciding to take his people west, gives the chapter the complication it needs before the resolution.

The landing as planned — the bracelet moving, the titleholder traveling — is correct. Do not add more to it.

**Recommendation:** Resolve the either/or in Section III. Commit to showing resistance through the departing headmen, and give one of them enough presence to be a character, even briefly.
