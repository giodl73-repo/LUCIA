# Sources — Melanesian Peoples (Papua New Guinea)

## Key Academic Works
- Bronisław Malinowski, *Argonauts of the Western Pacific* (1922) — the founding study of the Kula Ring exchange system; foundational for economic anthropology
- Marshall Sahlins, *Stone Age Economics* (1972) — the gift economy and the big-man system
- Andrew Strathern, *The Rope of Moka: Big-Men and Ceremonial Exchange in Mount Hagen, New Guinea* (1971, Cambridge) — definitive study of the moka system
- Marilyn Strathern, *The Gender of the Gift* (1988, California) — gender and exchange in Melanesian societies
- Don Kulick & Margaret Willson — Sepik River societies and ceremonial systems
- Tim Flannery, *The Future Eaters* (1994) — ecological history of New Guinea and Australia
- Jack Golson — Kuk Swamp excavations; highland agriculture dating

## Archaeological Sources
- Kuk Swamp (Western Highlands Province) — evidence of agriculture c. 10,000 BCE; UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Bismarck Archipelago sites — early human occupation; Lapita transition
- Sepik River archaeological surveys
