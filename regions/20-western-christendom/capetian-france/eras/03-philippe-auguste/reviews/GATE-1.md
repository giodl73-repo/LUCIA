## Notes

This opening achieves the full score through one structural decision: anchoring the entire era's meaning — the transformation of France from a nominal kingdom to a dominant European power — in a single afternoon (Bouvines) and a single rider arriving at dusk. Everything radiates from that moment: backward to Philip's thirty-four years of patient work, forward to the eighteen months he has left to consolidate it.

The Château Gaillard latrine-shaft detail earns full marks for Honesty (v1.2 amendment): the story is told from inside the French court's own pleasure in it, not as an authorial quip. God did not appear to have been given the assignment. The culture owns the irony.

The voice is not borrowed from any existing register. It is its own — call it **Sacred-Legal**: the prose moves with the gravity of feudal obligation and the cadence of royal chancellery, layered over a genuine narrative momentum. This should be logged in the voice register when the chapter is scored.
