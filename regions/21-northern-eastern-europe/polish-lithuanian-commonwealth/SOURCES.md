# Sources — Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth

## Primary Sources

- *Rocznik Kapituły Krakowskiej* and other Polish annals — the chronicle record
- *Henrician Articles* (1573) and *Pacta Conventa* — the constitutional documents of the elected monarchy
- *Edict of Warsaw Tolerance* (1573) — the religious freedom guarantee
- Copernicus, *De Revolutionibus Orbium Coelestium* (1543) — the Commonwealth's greatest scientific text
- Jan Długosz, *Annales seu Cronicae incliti Regni Poloniae* — 15th century; the major Polish chronicle
- Statutes of Lithuania — the *Statute of 1529*, *1566*, *1588*; the Lithuanian legal tradition
- *Volumina Legum* — the collected legislation of the Polish sejm

## Secondary Sources

- Davies, Norman. *God's Playground: A History of Poland*. 2 vols. Columbia University Press, 1982.
- Lukowski, Jerzy. *Liberty's Folly: The Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth in the Eighteenth Century*. Routledge, 1991.
- Stone, Daniel. *The Polish-Lithuanian State 1386–1795*. University of Washington Press, 2001.
- Frost, Robert I. *The Oxford History of Poland-Lithuania, Vol. 1: The Making of the Polish-Lithuanian Union, 1385–1569*. Oxford University Press, 2015.
- Polonsky, Antony. *The Jews in Poland and Russia*. 3 vols. Littman Library, 2010–2012.
- Sysyn, Frank E. *Between Poland and the Ukraine: The Dilemma of Adam Kysil, 1600–1653*. Harvard Ukrainian Research Institute, 1985.
