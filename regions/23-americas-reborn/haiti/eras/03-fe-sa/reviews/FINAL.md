## Innovations Flagged

**Vodou-Revolutionary register (Voice dimension):** A new prose voice register discovered in this chapter. Not previously in the Voice Spectrum. Distinct from:
- **Call-and-Response (African Diaspora, Middle Passage):** which is collective survival — what the trade could not confiscate.
- **Scholarly-Revolutionary (Fulani, Dan Fodio):** which is a distributed correspondence network becoming a governing order.

**Vodou-Revolutionary:** The ceremony's structure (invocation, possession, covenant) becomes the prose's organizing rhythm. The building sentence (the call) is answered by a short declarative (the lwa's response, the fact of the burning, the fact of independence). The gap between the call and the answer is the voice's pause mechanism — held, not interrupted. The register operates at paragraph level, not sentence level. Analysis (the network, the organizational structure) arrives in the same voice as the ceremony, without register shift. Zero em-dashes at full chapter length.

**Cross-applicability candidates to watch:** Vodou traditions in Cuba (Regla de Ocha/Santería), Brazilian Candomblé and Umbanda contexts, Haitian diaspora literary traditions (Danticat). Any culture where African diasporic religious tradition was simultaneously the medium of spiritual practice and the structure of political resistance.
