## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — Review of Notes (Targeted)

**Focus:** Structural engine and terminal condition.

The engine is now clearly named: withdrawal-as-authority, self-consuming, terminal at Chalcedon. This is sound. The mechanism (renunciation -> sanctity -> followers -> organization -> power -> collision with imperial power) follows a recognizable cycle of group solidarity.

The material-object candidate (the cell) is promising but needs to do structural work, not just decorative work. The cell should change shape as the chapter progresses: sealed fort, cave, room-within-compound, room-within-fortress. If the cell is the material object, its increasing enclosure by institutional walls should track the engine's progression from solitary to collective to political.

The section plan is solid. My concern is Section 8 (Road to Chalcedon). The theological argument between Cyril and the Chalcedonians is complex. The chapter must not turn into a theology seminar. The theological dispute should be rendered as a power struggle between Alexandria and Constantinople in which theology is the medium, not the content. The reader should understand that Chalcedon is about who controls the definition of orthodoxy, not about whether Christ has one nature or two.

**Remaining gap:** The chapter plan does not address the relationship between monastic authority and imperial taxation. Monks were exempt from corvee labor and taxation. This created an economic incentive for withdrawal that coexisted with the spiritual motive. Not all who went to the desert went for God. The chapter should acknowledge this without cynicism.
