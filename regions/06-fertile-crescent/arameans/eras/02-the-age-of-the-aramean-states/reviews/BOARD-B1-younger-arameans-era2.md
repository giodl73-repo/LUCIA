## B-1 — K. Lawson Younger Jr. — Board Review of Arameans Era 2

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Political history of the Aramean states, Neo-Assyrian source analysis, Iron Age Levantine geopolitics.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The Qarqar coalition narrative is accurately represented. The force counts from the Kurkh Monolith are correctly cited. The identification of Hadad-ezer in the Assyrian text with Ben-Hadad in the Israelite text is noted as uncertain, which is the correct position; I distinguish them in my own work, and the chapter's handling is appropriately cautious.
- Hazael's characterization as *mar la mammana* ("son of nobody") is accurate to Shalmaneser III's annals. The Tel Dan Stele attribution to Hazael is correctly presented as near-consensus but not universal.
- The Sfire treaty quotations are accurately rendered. The curse formulas are drawn from the published text.
- The date of Bit Adini's absorption (856 BCE) is consistent with the standard chronology.
- The chapter correctly notes Gindibu the Arab's camel contingent at Qarqar, which is a small but significant detail for the caravan-network argument.
- Ahab's death at Ramoth-Gilead is noted. This is the traditional identification; the Israelite text says Ahab died in battle against Aram-Damascus, though the precise connection to Qarqar-era politics is debated.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The chapter successfully presents the Aramean states as independent political actors rather than mere foils for Assyria or Israel. The structural-engine concept (language-as-infrastructure) is a legitimate analytical framework that captures the Aramean system's distinctive quality.
- The source-asymmetry acknowledgment is well-handled. The chapter notes that the Aramean voice survives primarily in stone inscriptions while the Israelite and Assyrian sources provide the bulk of the narrative framework.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The *bit* naming convention and its connection to clan-based political structure is accurately presented and analytically productive.
- The coalition-as-commercial-calculation argument is a sound structural reading of the Qarqar alliance.
- The treatment of Shalmaneser's victory claim at Qarqar as potentially overstated is the correct scholarly position.

**What needs correction:**
- Minor: The chapter states that Shalmaneser III's campaign against Dammasq in 841 BCE included ravaging the Ghouta orchards. The orchard destruction is attested in the Assyrian annals, but the chapter should note that this was during a campaign against Hazael specifically (Shalmaneser campaigned against Dammasq multiple times, and the orchard destruction is from the 841 campaign).
- Minor: The Barada River's role in powering bellows for the chariot workshops is a reasonable inference from the city's geography but is not directly attested in the archaeological or textual record. The chapter should note this as inference rather than established fact, or simply describe the forges without specifying the water-powered mechanism.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (two minor corrections as noted above)
