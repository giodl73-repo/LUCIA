## P-2 — Ibn Khaldun — PANEL-2 Review of Notes

**Focus:** Structural engine definition and dynamics

The notes now name the engine (guanxi as pre-legal infrastructure) and the terminal condition (political power catching up with commercial power). This is structurally sound. The mechanism (reputation-based enforcement scaling through network growth) is correctly identified.

**What the notes get right:**
- The distinction between the engine (guanxi) and the driver (Song commercial revolution) is important. The network is old; the volume is new. The Song boom does not create the system; it supercharges it. This is the correct causal sequence.
- The terminal condition is planted but not triggered in this era. Good. The chapter should feel like a system at its most confident, with the vulnerability visible only to the structurally attentive reader.

**What still needs work:**
- The section plan for Section 3 (Song Boom) must guard against becoming an economics lecture. The shibosi officer is the correct anchor, but the officer must be a person, not a vehicle for explaining Song monetary policy. What does the shibosi see, worry about, calculate? What is his relationship to the merchants he regulates? Is he tempted by the trade he taxes?
- The dialect-group question remains unaddressed in the section plan. If the Nanyang has Hokkien, Cantonese, and Teochew networks operating in the same ports, how do they relate? Do they compete? Cooperate? Maintain separate huiguan? This is the asabiyyah question: the solidarity of the group is what makes the network function, but solidarity by its nature excludes. The chapter should show, in at least one scene, what happens when a trader from a different dialect group enters a Hokkien-dominated port.
- The Yuan period (Open Question 4) must be included. The era boundary is 700-1400. The Mongol-Yuan period is when the network peaks and when the first significant anti-Chinese violence occurs (the Mongol invasions of Java create blowback against Chinese communities). This is structurally essential: the engine's first encounter with the terminal condition.

**Recommendation:** Include a brief Yuan passage in Section 4 or add a Section 4b. The Yuan-era expansion and the first friction it generates is the structural preparation for the Ming ban that opens Era 2.
