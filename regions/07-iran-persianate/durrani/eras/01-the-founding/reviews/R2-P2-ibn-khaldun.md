## Assessment of the Production Bible

The structural engine is correctly identified. I am satisfied that the author has named it precisely: the Loya Jirga's consensus-distribution mechanism, conditional on continued military success and fair distribution by Pashtunwali's own accounting. This is the correct analytical spine. It is the asabiyyah engine in Pashtun form.

The terminal condition identification — **legitimacy gap** — is accurate. The gap between military reach and administrative capacity is the Durrani empire's fundamental structural problem, and the author has understood it from the correct direction: not as a failure of ambition but as a rational limit of the franchise model. Ahmad Shah's withdrawal from Delhi was the engine's own logic, correctly applied. The empire could not administer Delhi without transforming itself into something its Pashtun base would not support.

## What the Notes Must Still Clarify

The section plan places the distribution mechanism in Section 3, after the campaigns. This is chronologically correct but structurally risky: the chapter should establish the mechanism's PRINCIPLE in Section 1 (the Loya Jirga itself is a distribution event — the khans are being offered authority in exchange for their consensus) so that Section 3 can show the principle in military-plunder operation rather than introducing it for the first time.

The Mohammadzai-Sadozai tension is correctly planted. I want the chapter to be clear about what Hajji Jamal Khan's acquiescence costs him — not in sentiment but in structural terms. If the Sadozai hold the kingship, the Mohammadzai's route to power is through military command. This is the deal. Plant it. It ripens into Era 2.

## On the Panipat Withdrawal

The author has framed the withdrawal correctly (rational within the engine's logic) but must be careful not to present it as a free choice with no costs. The withdrawal had consequences: the Maratha Confederacy was wounded but not destroyed; it would reconstitute. The Mughal rump state at Delhi was preserved by Ahmad Shah's departure rather than being absorbed. The chapter should acknowledge these structural consequences without judging the decision.

## Verdict

The production bible is ready. The engine is named, the terminal condition seeded, the sections planned. Proceed to WRITE with confidence in the structural spine. The voice note is correct: the chapter needs two rhythms (jirga deliberate, campaign kinetic) within one governing register.

**No structural gaps that would prevent WRITE.**
