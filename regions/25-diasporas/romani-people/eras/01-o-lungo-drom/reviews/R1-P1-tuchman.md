## P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of Romani Era 1 Opening

**Overall:** This opening does something I have rarely seen: it makes language the protagonist. Not a person, not a place, but a living vocabulary carrying the evidence of a thousand-year journey in its syllables. It works. The reader is pulled forward by the accumulation of words-as-footprints, and the mystery of the departure (no one wrote down why they left) generates genuine pull.

**What works:**
- The opening gambit is bold and correct: starting with the word "drom" and tracing it to Sanskrit. This is the equivalent of Tuchman's "pick up a letter from the archive and build the scene around it" — except the archive is the language itself. The word IS the primary source.
- "Every borrowing is a footprint. The language is the map." This is the kind of sentence that earns its aphoristic weight through the evidence that precedes it. It does not feel clever; it feels earned.
- The paragraph on why no one wrote down the departure is the strongest structural move. It reframes the absence of evidence as the first fact about this people's social position. The source gap becomes characterization.
- The linguistic stratigraphy paragraph (Persian loanwords older than Armenian, older than Greek) gives the reader a felt sense of time passing through the journey's layers. This is pacing through linguistics, and it builds.

**What doesn't:**
- There is no human body in this opening until the very end, and even then only implied. No one stands, walks, lifts, carries, or sweats. The opening is brilliant but cerebral. I would not say "put a person in the first paragraph" — the word-as-protagonist strategy is working — but somewhere before the halfway point, a human being should be doing something physical. Even a brief image: a smith at a forge, a musician tuning, a mother calling to a child using words her grandmother used in Rajasthan.
- The final paragraph, while strong in its conceptual landing ("the first word in a sentence that has not ended"), is slightly abstract. The best landings in LUCIA give you one final image. This gives you a metaphor. Consider closing with an image from the road itself.

**The question I'd push on:**
Can this voice sustain 5,000 words? The linguistic-accumulative rhythm is extraordinary in an 800-word opening, but it relies on the revelation of words and their origins for its energy. The chapter will need to move beyond linguistic evidence into social structure, craft, music, and movement. Will the voice hold when the words are no longer the subject?
