## B-mitanni-3 — Trevor Bryce — Board Review of The Age of Equals

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Hittite political and military history; Mitanni-Hittite relations; Late Bronze Age chariot warfare.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The chariot description is substantially accurate. The composite bow, the spoked wheel, the two-man crew (driver and warrior), the rear axle placement are all correctly described for the Late Bronze Age type.
- The *maryannu* as a social class tied to chariot warfare is correctly represented. The land-grant/military-obligation system documented at Nuzi is accurately described.
- The Kikkuli manual's attribution is correct: a Hittite-language text with Indo-Aryan technical terms. The training regimen described (interval work, graduated conditioning) matches the published text.
- Suppiluliuma's foreshadowing at the chapter's end is appropriately restrained. He is named and his strategic posture is described without narrating events that belong to Era 3. This is correct for the chronological scope.
- The description of chariot construction (wheelwright, leatherworker, bronze smith) and supply chain dependencies (copper from Cyprus/Taurus, tin from distant sources) is accurate.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The military dimension of the *maryannu* system is correctly presented as foundational to the diplomatic system, not as a separate topic. The connection between chariot capability and diplomatic standing is the right analytical framework.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The sensory description of chariot training (dust, hooves, grooms checking tendons) grounds the military analysis in physical reality without overstating what can be known.
- The structural argument that the brotherhood system rested on a chariot-force equilibrium is sound and well supported.

**What needs correction:**
- The composite bow's effective range ("a hundred paces") is conventional but imprecise. Effective range varied with bow weight, arrow type, and whether the target was armored. The phrase is not wrong but is less precise than the chapter's general standard. Suggest: "at distance" or simply describe the effect rather than specifying range.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (one minor correction)
