# B-amarna-3 — William Murnane

## Background

William Murnane was a professor of Egyptology at the University of
Memphis. His *Texts from the Amarna Period in Egypt* is the standard
English-language collection of translated Amarna-period texts. He was
meticulous about the distinction between what the evidence states and
what scholars have inferred, and his work provides the textual foundation
for any serious study of the period.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter stay close to what the primary sources actually say,
and does it handle uncertainty honestly?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Direct quotation from the Great Hymn and Restoration Stela
- Honest handling of the Smenkhkare/Neferneferuaten succession confusion
- Nefertiti's disappearance treated as an open question
- The boundary stelae used as primary evidence of Akhenaten's own declarations

## What He Will Challenge

- Any resolution of the succession debate beyond what evidence supports
- Misattribution or inaccurate paraphrase of primary texts
- Speculation about Nefertiti's fate presented as established fact
- Any anachronistic comparative claims (e.g., Hymn-to-Psalm comparisons)
