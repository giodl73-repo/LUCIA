# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *Đại Việt sử ký toàn thư* (Complete Annals of Đại Việt) — the standard Vietnamese dynastic chronicle
- *Bình Ngô đại cáo* (Proclamation of Victory over the Wu, 1428) — Lê Lợi's declaration of independence
- Vietnamese stele inscriptions — particularly from the Temple of Literature
- Chinese records (Ming Veritable Records) — on the occupation and resistance

## Key Academic Works

- Keith Weller Taylor, *The Birth of Vietnam* (1983) — foundational for early Vietnamese history
- Nola Cooke and Li Tana — Vietnamese expansion south (Nam tiến)
- Alexander Woodside, *Vietnam and the Chinese Model* (1971)
- Liam Kelley — Vietnamese identity and Chinese influence debates
- John Whitmore — Lê dynasty politics and culture

## Needed

- Studies on the relationship between Vietnamese and Cham cultures during the southern expansion
- Analysis of the Trưng Sisters revolt as historical memory and modern symbol
- Recent scholarship on the Tây Sơn dynasty (1778–1802)
