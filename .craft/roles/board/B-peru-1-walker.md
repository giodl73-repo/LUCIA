# B-peru-1 — Charles Walker

## Background

Charles Walker is Professor of History at UC Davis and the author of the most
comprehensive recent scholarly account of the Túpac Amaru II rebellion. His work
draws on extensive archival research in Spain and Peru and has substantially
revised earlier accounts of the uprising's scope and social composition.

## Key Question

**"Is the historical record honored in its specificity, not flattened into a morality tale?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Accurate military chronology and geography
- Micaela Bastidas rendered as the operational commander she was
- The internal division of the Andean coalition treated as structural, not moral
- The execution scene rendered accurately in its sequence

## What He Will Challenge

- Any conflation of the Quechua and Aymara theaters
- Pumacahua presented as simply colonial rather than as a figure of divided obligations
- Overstatement of the uprising's unity or understatement of its fractures
- Any factual errors in the execution sequence
