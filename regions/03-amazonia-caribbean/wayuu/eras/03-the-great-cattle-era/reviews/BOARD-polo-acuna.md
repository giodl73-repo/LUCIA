# Board Review — José Polo Acuña
## Wayúu, Era 3: The Great Cattle Era

**Reviewer:** José Polo Acuña (colonial history, Guajira Peninsula, indigenous-colonial relations)
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The chapter's use of the Narváez y la Torre archive is historically grounded and the paraphrase captures the documented administrative frustrations accurately. The pütchipü'üi dispute scenario is consistent with the colonial record's documented case parameters. The matrilineal inheritance/Spanish patrilineal law asymmetry is correctly identified and correctly framed: the Wayúu system was not designed as resistance, but its properties in contact with Spanish law produced exactly the gap the chapter describes.

**Required correction:** The phrase "By 1750, herds of several hundred cattle were moving across the Alta Guajira" should be qualified. The Riohacha trade records suggest substantial herds by mid-century, but specific numbers for individual eirruku herds at this date are not directly attested. Recommend: "By mid-century, herds of considerable size were moving across the Alta Guajira under eirruku management."

**Minor notation:** Clan name spellings vary across the documentary record ("Epinayuu" vs. "Epieyu" in older sources). The chapter's consistent use of "Epinayuu" is appropriate for this period; a note in the sources file acknowledging the variant spellings would be useful for readers.

**Verdict:** APPROVED with one required correction and one minor notation.
