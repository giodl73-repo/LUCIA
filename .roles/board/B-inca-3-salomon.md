# B-inca-3 — Frank Salomon

## Background

Frank Salomon is Professor Emeritus of Anthropology at the University of
Wisconsin-Madison. His work spans Andean ethnohistory, colonial-era indigenous
texts, and living Andean practices. His study of the Tupicocha quipu keepers
(The Cord Keepers, 2004) demonstrated that the quipucamayoc tradition survived
in modified form centuries after the conquest. His edition of the Huarochiri
Manuscript provided a window into Andean mythology as recorded by an Andean
author within the colonial system.

## Key Question

**"Can you hear the Andean voice through the colonial noise?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Successful navigation of the colonial filter without being paralyzed by it
- Correct use of Quechua terminology
- Treatment of Andean concepts on their own terms
- Honest acknowledgment of epistemological limits

## What He Will Challenge

- Over-reliance on any single colonial chronicler
- Translation of Andean concepts into European categories (ayllu as "clan," mit'a as "corvee")
- Failure to acknowledge the filter when using Spanish sources
- Sentimentalizing the loss of the quipucamayoc tradition instead of describing it precisely
