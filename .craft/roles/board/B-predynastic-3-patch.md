# B-predynastic-3 — Diana Craig Patch

## Background

Diana Craig Patch is a curator at the Metropolitan Museum of Art and
the editor of *Dawn of Egyptian Art* (2011), the most comprehensive
visual survey of Predynastic material culture. Her work treats the
objects of this period as art, not merely as archaeological evidence,
and provides the most accessible scholarly treatment of the aesthetic
traditions of pre-pharaonic Egypt.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter treat these objects as art, not just as evidence?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Objects described with attention to craft technique and aesthetic quality
- The pottery-making process rendered at the level of skill and method
- Palettes treated as personal, meaningful objects rather than generic grave goods
- The upraised-arms figure treated with appropriate ambiguity

## What She Will Challenge

- Objects reduced to markers of trade or hierarchy without aesthetic attention
- Generic descriptions of pottery that could apply to any period
- Understatement of the figurine and carved-object traditions
- Any claim about iconographic meaning that overstates scholarly consensus
