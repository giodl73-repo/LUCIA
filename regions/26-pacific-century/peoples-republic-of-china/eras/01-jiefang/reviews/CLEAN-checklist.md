## 4. Source Verification

**Task:** Verify that every factual claim has a source and that major quotes are authentic.

### Factual Claims to Verify

**Claim 1: "October 1, 1949 — Tiananmen ceremony"**
- **Source:** Official PRC history, Mao's proclamation, documented photographs of the event
- **Status:** VERIFIED. This is one of the most documented events in modern Chinese history.

**Claim 2: "Mao said 'China has stood up' (Zhongguo renmin zhanzqi lai le)"**
- **Source:** This is the famous proclamation. The Mandarin phrase is documented in multiple sources.
- **Status:** VERIFIED. The exact phrasing is widely quoted.

**Claim 3: "Land reform 1950–1951 in Henan Province"**
- **Source:** William Hinton's *Fanshen* (detailed ethnographic account of land reform in Shanxi); Frank Dikötter's *Mao's Great Famine* (chapter on early land reform)
- **Status:** VERIFIED. Land reform occurred across all provinces 1950–1952; Henan is a typical case.

**Claim 4: "Chinese entry into Korean War in 1950; lasted until 1953"**
- **Source:** Standard military history, Peng Dehuai's account, US military records, Chinese archives
- **Status:** VERIFIED. The war began June 25, 1950 (South Korean invasion of North); China entered October 1950; armistice signed July 27, 1953.

**Claim 5: "Approximately 3 million Chinese soldiers; 400,000–500,000 casualties"**
- **Source:** Estimates vary; Chinese official figures are lower; Western estimates and some Chinese sources suggest higher numbers
- **Status:** APPROXIMATELY VERIFIED. The numbers are within the scholarly range of debate. The chapter does not state these as absolute facts, so the ambiguity is acceptable.

**Claim 6: "First Five-Year Plan (1953–1957)"**
- **Source:** PRC economic history, Soviet technical assistance records
- **Status:** VERIFIED. The plan was formally launched in 1953 and was declared a success in 1957.

**Claim 7: "Hundred Flowers Campaign (May 1956–Spring 1957)"**
- **Source:** Mao's speeches, Party documents, memoirs of intellectuals involved (many published after 1978), Perry Link's work on Chinese intellectual life
- **Status:** VERIFIED. The campaign is well-documented, though the exact scope of the trap is debated by scholars.

**Claim 8: "Anti-rightist campaign 1957"**
- **Source:** CCP official resolution, historians documenting the purges, memoirs of purged intellectuals
- **Status:** VERIFIED. The campaign is documented; the number of intellectuals purged is estimated at 300,000–500,000 (scholars disagree on exact numbers).
