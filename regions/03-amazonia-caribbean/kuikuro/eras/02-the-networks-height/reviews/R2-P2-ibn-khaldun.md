# Panel Review — R2-P2 — Ibn Khaldun
## Production Bible Review | The Network's Height | Kuikuro Era 2

---

The production bible answers my primary question from Round 1: the economic foundation is addressed through the terra preta section, and the gift exchange section maps the material flows of the Quarup. This is correct.

What I now need to see resolved before the chapter is written:

**On the terminal condition:** The notes describe the terminal condition well — population imbalance destroys the giving/receiving balance the Quarup requires. But the chapter must plant this vulnerability from within the system's own logic, not as external commentary. The best approach: in the gift exchange section, show what happens when one community cannot give adequately. Not as a future scenario — as a present pressure that the anetü at the Quarup must manage. A community that suffered flooding, disease (not the epidemic yet — an ordinary bad season), or a drought year cannot give at the same level. What mechanisms does the network have for handling this imbalance? If it has mechanisms, show them — that shows the system's resilience. If it does not, that shows the structural vulnerability the epidemic will eventually find.

**On the structural engine's visibility:** The chapter organizes around the Quarup ceremony — correct. But the structural engine (Quarup as network-maintenance technology) must be visible in how the ceremony's phases work, not just stated. The gift exchange IS the economic rebalancing. The wrestling matches ARE the status hierarchy maintenance that prevents the imbalance of gifts from becoming a status insult. The mourning songs ARE the acknowledgment that the network's human fabric has changed and must be re-woven. Each phase does specific structural work. The chapter should show this work operating, not just the ceremony's surface.

**On Open Question 5 (hosting rotation):** If the anetü of Kuhikugu is the one being mourned, there is a structural puzzle: how does a community host the ceremony that mourns its own leader? The living anetü of the mourned chief's lineage? A successor? This detail matters because it shows the ceremony's design for continuity — how the system handles the loss of one of its most important nodes.

The notes are ready. Write the chapter.

— Ibn Khaldun
