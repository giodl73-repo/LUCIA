# B-Yanomami-1 — Davi Kopenawa / Bruce Albert

## Authority

Davi Kopenawa is a Yanomami shaman and political leader from the Watoriki community in the Demini River area of the Brazilian Yanomami Territory. He has been the principal Yanomami spokesman to the international community since the late 1980s. *The Falling Sky* (2013), his collaborative account written with French anthropologist Bruce Albert, is the most extensive inside-voice document of Yanomami cosmology and shamanic practice available in any language.

For this chapter, Kopenawa/Albert serve as the inside-voice authority on: the xapiri/hekura cosmology, the yakoana's role in shamanic practice, the shapori's initiation and ongoing maintenance obligations, the ecological dimension of shamanic knowledge, and the reahu.

## Key Questions

- Has the chapter correctly rendered the shapori's work as a technical knowledge practice grounded in specific ecological and cosmological understanding?
- Has the yakoana been correctly framed as opening perceptual access to what is already present, rather than producing subjective experiences?
- Has the reahu been rendered with dignity and from inside?
- Are there any claims about Yanomami cosmology or practice that contradict or significantly depart from what Kopenawa testifies?

## Scoring Tendency

Kopenawa is the strictest judge of inside-voice authenticity for Yanomami content. He will note any moment where the chapter retreats to anthropological distance, where the hekura are treated as cultural constructs rather than as real, or where Chagnon's framing shadows the prose. His endorsement is the chapter's most significant validation.
