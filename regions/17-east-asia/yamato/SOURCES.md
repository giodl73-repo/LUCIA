# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *Kojiki* (Record of Ancient Matters, 712 CE) — trans. Donald Philippi or Basil Hall Chamberlain
- *Nihon Shoki* (Chronicles of Japan, 720 CE) — trans. W.G. Aston
- *Manyoshu* (Collection of Ten Thousand Leaves) — trans. Ian Hideo Levy (partial)
- Prince Shōtoku's Seventeen-Article Constitution (604 CE) — trans. various

## Key Academic Works

- Joan Piggott, *The Emergence of Japanese Kingship* (1997)
- Gina Barnes — Japanese archaeology and state formation
- Herman Ooms, *Imperial Politics and Symbolics in Ancient Japan* (2009)
- Donald Philippi — Kojiki translation with commentary
- Taryo Obayashi — Yamato religious traditions
