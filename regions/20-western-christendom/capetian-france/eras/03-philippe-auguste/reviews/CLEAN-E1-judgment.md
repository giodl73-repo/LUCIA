## Anachronistic Morality Check

Scanning for modern moral sensibility visible in the text.

**Finding 1 — Béziers paragraph:**
"The south of France who had developed a theology that the Roman Church called Cathar and that called itself simply Christian."

CLEAN. The framing is from inside both frames simultaneously: what the Church called them, and what they called themselves. Neither is endorsed.

**Finding 2 — Ingeborg section:**
"The man who healed hundreds of strangers by touch could not, or would not, perform this one act that the Church and his own theology demanded of him."

BORDERLINE. This sentence has the structure of a verdict: it implies that Philip's refusal was a moral failure by the standards of his own theology. But within the Capetian worldview, this is a factual observation, not modern moralizing. Philip's own clergy and the papacy made exactly this argument to Philip's face. The judgment is his contemporaries', not ours. RETAIN.

**Finding 3 — Béziers close:**
