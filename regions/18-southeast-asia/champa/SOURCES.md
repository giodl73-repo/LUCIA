# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Cham Sanskrit and Old Cham inscriptions (largest pre-modern epigraphic corpus in Southeast Asia)
- My Son sanctuary inscriptions (4th–13th century CE)
- Vietnamese annals (*Đại Việt sử ký toàn thư*) — Cham-Vietnamese relations
- Chinese dynastic records — tribute missions and maritime contacts

## Key Academic Works

- Georges Maspero, *The Kingdom of Champa* (1928, tr. 1949) — foundational study
- Po Dharma — Cham language and history
- Andrew Hardy, Mauro Cucarzi, and Patrizia Zolese, *Champa and the Archaeology of My Son* (2009)
- Kenneth Hall — Cham maritime trade
- Rie Nakamura — Cham religious traditions and diaspora communities

## Needed

- Recent archaeological work on the Cham port cities
- Studies on the Cham Balamons and Bani religious traditions
- Analysis of the 1371 sack of Hanoi and its regional significance
