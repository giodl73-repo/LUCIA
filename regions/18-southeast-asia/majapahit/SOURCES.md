# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Mpu Prapanca, *Nagarakretagama* (1365 CE) — court poem describing the empire's extent and court culture
- Mpu Tantular, *Sutasoma* (14th century) — source of the Bhinneka Tunggal Ika motto
- *Pararaton* (Book of Kings) — Javanese chronicle covering Ken Angrok through Majapahit
- Chinese records (Zheng He expeditions) — Majapahit as tribute-sender and trading partner

## Key Academic Works

- Theodore Pigeaud, *Java in the 14th Century* (1960–63) — translation and commentary on the Nagarakretagama
- Slamet Muljana, *A Story of Majapahit* (1976)
- Jan Wisseman Christie — Javanese political economy and epigraphy
- Kenneth Hall — Majapahit maritime networks
- Stuart Robson — Old Javanese literary tradition

## Needed

- Archaeological work on the Trowulan capital site
- Studies on the actual extent of Majapahit's mandala control vs. direct sovereignty
- Analysis of the transition from Majapahit to Islamic sultanates in Java
