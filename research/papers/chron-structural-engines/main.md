# Structural Engines as Cross-Cultural Narrative Types: A Taxonomy From a 115-Chapter Corpus

**Paper**: PR-B2
**Module**: chron (Emergent Typologies in AI-Assisted Creative Production)
**Track**: B — The Artifact's Own Dimensions
**Date**: 2026-04-20
**Status**: Draft, round 0
**Dependencies**: PR-B1 (validation method). Gates PR-B3 (terminal conditions as engine-closure sub-taxonomy).

---

## Abstract

Narrative morphologies in the structuralist tradition (Propp; Campbell; Lévi-Strauss) derive a universal function-set analytically from a canonical corpus. We report a different kind of taxonomy. Across 115 narrative encyclopedia chapters covering 93 cultures, structural engines — the culture-specific mechanisms that drive each chapter's arc — emerged through AI-assisted production and were promoted to rubric amendments by an Innovation Engine (PR-A1) when they met a ≥ 2-instance cross-culture replication criterion (PR-B1). Five engine types have been formally adopted: Covenant (Mali, Haudenosaunee), Material Object as Structural Principle (6 founding plus 2 extensions; 8 cultures across 6 regions), Non-Chronological Organizing Principle (5 cultures across 5 regions), Knowledge-System as Engine (Tang, Abbasid, Tibet; 3 cultures across 3 regions), and Contradiction-Held-as-Engine (British, Spanish, French, Dutch, Portuguese colonial empires; 5 cultures, 1 region by design). Across the 5 validated types: mean cultures-per-type = 4.6; median regions-per-type = 3; non-provincialism score (types with ≥ 3 instances AND ≥ 3 regions) = 3/5 = 60%, Wilson 95% CI [23.1%, 88.2%]. Point estimate clears the 50% pre-registered falsification threshold; CI lower bound does not, and we report the tension honestly. Alongside the validated core, 19 single-instance candidate engine types sit on the INNOVATIONS.md watch list — the same validated-core / candidate-tail structure PR-B1 reported for voice registers (52 validated / 14 watch-list). The engine taxonomy is not recoverable by Proppian function analysis: Propp's functions operate within narrative action-sequence (villain threatens → hero departs → hero returns); our engines operate at a scale where a culture's own institutional or material logic drives the arc. This paper reuses PR-B1's validation method at chapter-arc scale and establishes the framework PR-B3 extends to arc-closure scale (terminal conditions).

## 1. Introduction

Narrative morphology has a long structuralist tradition. Propp's 31 functions were derived from 100 Russian fairy tales; Campbell's monomyth abstracted a single hero-journey from cross-cultural mythography; Lévi-Strauss analyzed myth as transformations on a fixed binary-opposition grid. Each framework starts from a canonical corpus and derives a universal function-set analytically. Each was developed for a specific narrative genre (fairy tale, myth) and in a specific theoretical register (formalist, archetypal, structuralist).

LUCIA's production raises a different question. What happens when narrative morphology is asked to operate across 93 cultures at chapter-encyclopedia scale, where the narrative arc of each chapter is driven by a culture-specific institutional, material, or cosmological mechanism? The Kurukan Fuga's self-consuming redistribution logic drives Mali's five-era arc. The curtain shielding the mai is Kanem-Bornu's political theory made physical. The reggae offbeat structures Jamaica's Rastafari theological-political argument. These are mechanisms the culture itself produced, not functions in a universal narrative grammar. Are they typologizable across cultures, or are they irreducibly culture-specific?

This paper measures whether LUCIA's 115-chapter corpus produces a cross-cultural engine taxonomy — a finite set of engine types each replicated across culturally-different chapters — or a log of culture-specific one-offs.

### 1.1 Claim

We claim: *LUCIA's Progression-dimension structural engines, validated by the ≥ 2-instance cross-culture replication method PR-B1 established, constitute a cross-cultural narrative taxonomy. Of the 5 engine types formally adopted into the rubric, mean cultures-per-type ≥ 3, and at least 50% meet non-provincialism (≥ 3 instances AND ≥ 3 regions).*

### 1.2 Falsification

We pre-register two falsification conditions from plan.md: mean cultures-per-engine ≤ 2 → taxonomy reframes as case studies, not cross-cultural replication. Non-provincialism < 50% of validated types → engines are region-bound rather than cross-cultural. If either threshold is crossed, the paper's central taxonomy claim fails. If the measurements fall below threshold, we reframe the 5 validated types as "culture-type-specific engine patterns" rather than a cross-cultural taxonomy.

### 1.3 Contribution

Our contribution is threefold. First, we apply PR-B1's validation method at chapter-arc scale, demonstrating that the ≥ 2-instance cross-culture replication criterion generalizes from prose register to arc mechanism. Second, we report the five formally adopted engine types with their instance counts, culture breadth, and region breadth, plus the 19-entry candidate list on the INNOVATIONS.md watch list. Third, we identify the validated-core / candidate-tail structure that mirrors PR-B1's voice-spectrum result (52 validated + 14 watch-list = 66 total) at a different scale of analysis. The method established here is reused by PR-B3 (terminal conditions as engine-closure sub-taxonomy).

### 1.4 Scope

This paper tests whether the engine taxonomy is cross-cultural. It does not test (a) whether the taxonomy is complete — that is PR-A3's saturation claim; (b) whether the taxonomy transports to non-LUCIA corpora — PR-C3; (c) whether individual engine identifications are unique (two engines may co-exist in one chapter — Brazil's political-and-knowledge dual engine is the clearest example, and we note it); (d) whether the classification is editor-independent — we commit to Glaser-style grounded theory per PP1-E and disclose editor role per PP1-D.

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Proppian Morphology

Vladimir Propp's *Morphology of the Folktale* (1928; Eng. 1968) [NEED-1] derived 31 narrative functions from 100 Russian wondertales. Functions include Absence, Interdiction, Violation, Villainy, Lack, Mediation, and so on. Propp's central claim: the 31 functions appear in a fixed sequence (with omissions permitted) across all tales in his corpus. The framework is action-sequence-level — it describes who does what to whom in the plot's surface structure.

LUCIA's engines are not Proppian functions. The Kurukan Fuga redistribution logic is not Villainy or Lack; it is an institutional mechanism whose own internal logic drives five eras of Mali's history. The Material Object engine (curtain, ship, stone edict) does not correspond to any Propp function — the object is not a Donor's gift or Magical Agent but a structural medium through which the chapter's narrative logic operates. Propp and LUCIA operate at different scales of narrative analysis: Propp at the action-sequence, LUCIA at the chapter-arc mechanism. A Propp-trained analyst could attempt to decompose LUCIA chapters into 31-function sequences; the decomposition would be formally possible and substantively uninformative. The engine that makes the chapter work is the institutional logic; the 31-function sequence is epiphenomenal.

### 2.2 Campbell's Monomyth

Joseph Campbell's *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949) [NEED-2] argues for a single universal narrative arc — Departure / Initiation / Return — underlying cross-cultural mythography. Campbell's framework is explicitly universalist: the many surface-narratives converge on one deep structure.

LUCIA's claim is the opposite. Five validated engine types (Covenant, Material Object, Non-Chronological, Contradiction-Held, Knowledge-System) are not reducible to a common monomyth. The Aboriginal Dreaming-Time arc is not a Departure-Initiation-Return; the Talmud page-as-world organization is not a hero's journey; the Swahili storehouse-centered network is not a single-protagonist arc. We argue these are distinct engine types with distinct logics, not surface variations of one underlying monomyth. A monomyth-trained analyst may attempt to map each engine onto Campbell's stages; the mapping is often possible but requires distorting the engine's native logic.

### 2.3 Lévi-Strauss on Myth

Claude Lévi-Strauss's structuralist mythography [NEED-3] treats myth as transformations on binary oppositions (raw/cooked, nature/culture, sun/moon). The analytic move is to reveal underlying structural logic through comparison across myth variants. LUCIA shares with Lévi-Strauss the comparative-across-cultures move but differs in taking the engine mechanism, not binary opposition, as the unit of analysis.

### 2.4 Narratology

Post-structuralist narratology (Genette 1980; Bremond 1973 [NEED-4, NEED-5]) analyzes narrative through discourse-level categories (focalization, temporal order, frequency). This is orthogonal to our unit of analysis (the chapter-arc mechanism) but compatible — a chapter's engine operates regardless of the narratological choices in how the engine is rendered.

### 2.5 Computational Narrative Analysis

Recent computational narrative work (Mani 2012; Elson & McKeown 2010 [NEED-6]) extracts narrative structures automatically from text corpora. These approaches typically work at the event-level (event extraction, event chain identification) rather than at the mechanism level. Our engine typology is coarser than event chains and finer than monomyths; it operates at a scale computational narrative analysis has not yet targeted.

### 2.6 Grounded Theory: Committed to Glaser (PP1-E)

Following PR-B1's methodological commitment, we adopt Glaser-style data-driven grounded theory. The five engine types were not specified before production; they emerged from chapter-by-chapter panel review and were promoted by the Innovation Engine's ≥ 2-instance or 3-cluster trigger rules. The editor's role in engine classification is documented in §4.4. We reject the Charmaz constructivist move that would treat editor-interpretation as constitutive of category identity.

## 3. Background: Structural Engines in LUCIA

### 3.1 The Rubric's Engine Concept

RUBRIC.md's Progression dimension §v1.1 Amendment — Structural Engine states: *"The highest-scoring chapters find the culture's own structural mechanism and make it the narrative engine. Not context, not background — the PLOT."* This is the foundational engine concept. Later amendments (v1.5, v1.7) formalize specific engine types as sub-classifications of this general principle.

### 3.2 The Five Validated Engine Types

**Covenant (v1.1 Structural Engine, original formulation).** Institutional or constitutional logic drives the chapter's arc. The Kurukan Fuga's self-consuming redistribution logic is the founding instance (Mali Empire, all 5 eras); the Great Law / Mourning War cycle is the second culture (Haudenosaunee). Covenant engines operate through an explicit institutional compact whose internal contradictions generate the narrative's motion.

**Material Object as Structural Principle (v1.5 cluster).** A physical object from the culture's own practice serves as the chapter's organizing principle — not as metaphor but as mechanism. The six founding instances: Kanem-Bornu's mai-shielding curtain, Norse clinker-built ship, Maurya stone edict, Benin bronze plaque, Sumer clay tablet, Urartu fortress-canal. Two extensions added post-v1.5: African Diaspora body-as-archive and Chinese Overseas folded-paper list.

**Non-Chronological Organizing Principle (v1.5 cluster).** For cultures without a linear chronological arc, a non-temporal organizing principle drives the chapter — a species (San/eland), a landscape (Aboriginal/Country), a commercial space (Swahili/storehouse), a sensory map (Hausa/city-by-smell), a page (Jewish/Talmud).

**Knowledge-System as Engine (v1.5 cluster within Evidence, but operates at engine scale).** A culture's own knowledge system drives the chapter — Tang poetry as primary medium, Abbasid translation-as-transformation, Tibetan translation-as-civilizational-recovery. These engines cluster within the Evidence-dimension cluster but operate as structural engines at the chapter-arc scale.

**Contradiction-Held-as-Engine (v1.7, cluster of 5).** The chapter's structural engine is the contradiction the system's own framework generates and cannot adjudicate. The 5 founding instances are all colonial empires (British, Spanish, French, Dutch, Portuguese); each exhibits a distinctive contradiction form (dual ledger, theological self-critique, universalist-vs-partial-application, commercial-ledger-as-archive, propaganda-vs-counter-evidence). See `scoring/CLUSTER-ANALYSIS-COLONIAL-EMPIRES.md` for the full cluster analysis.

### 3.3 The Candidate Tail

Nineteen engine-type candidates sit on the INNOVATIONS.md watch list as single-instance entries: Information-System Categorical Failure (Inca), Ecological Mechanism (Wolof), Inscription-as-Reality (Maya), Water-as-Engine (Khmer), Intermediary Economics (Soninke), Cosmological Feedback Loop (Egypt Old Kingdom), System-as-Institution (Mongol, Rome — 2 instances but single type), Self-Reinforcing Prestige-Exchange (Tongan), Distributed Network (Fulani), Precondition Engine (Luba), Portable Civilization (Romani), Administrative Machine (Canada Indian Act), Cosmological Direction Unfalsifiable (Tupi-Guaraní), Multi-Script Undecipherment (Elam), Call-and-Response Tripartite (Brazil), Dual Engine (Brazil), Ceremony-as-Network-Maintenance (Kuikuro), Urn-Before-Death Temporal Suspension (Marajoara), Grief-Psychological (Haudenosaunee, merged into Covenant but separable).

This 5-validated-core / 19-candidate-tail structure mirrors PR-B1's 52-validated / 14-candidate voice-spectrum result at a different scale of analysis.

## 4. Methods

### 4.1 Data

Primary sources: `scoring/RUBRIC.md` (Progression section, v1.1 through v1.7 amendments with engine-type definitions), `scoring/INNOVATIONS.md` (~42 Progression-dimension entries with Source chapter and Status), `scoring/CLUSTER-ANALYSIS-COLONIAL-EMPIRES.md` (Contradiction-Held cluster analysis). Secondary: 115 locked chapter FINAL.md files for engine-classification verification.

### 4.2 Primary Measurement 1: Instance and Culture Counts per Engine Type

**Definition.** For each formally-adopted engine type, count (a) total instances (as named in the founding amendment plus subsequent confirmations), (b) distinct cultures, (c) distinct regions.

**Operationalization.**
1. Enumerate validated engine types from RUBRIC.md Progression amendments: 5 types (Covenant, Material Object, Non-Chronological, Contradiction-Held, Knowledge-System).
2. For each type, read the amendment text to identify founding instances; cross-reference INNOVATIONS.md for post-amendment confirmations classified under the same type.
3. Count distinct cultures (people-groups) and distinct regions (LUCIA's 26).
4. Compute mean cultures-per-type and median regions-per-type.

### 4.3 Primary Measurement 2: Non-Provincialism Score

**Definition.** An engine type meets non-provincialism if it has ≥ 3 instances (cultures) AND ≥ 3 distinct regions.

**Operationalization.**
1. Apply the test to each of the 5 validated types.
2. Report as proportion (types passing / total validated types).
3. Compute Wilson 95% CI on the proportion.

**Threshold:** Non-provincialism score ≥ 50% of validated types. Below threshold falsifies the cross-cultural claim.

### 4.4 Role of the Human Editor (PP1-D)

The human editor performs three roles in this measurement:
1. **Engine identification.** When a chapter is scored, the editor identifies its structural engine and matches it to an existing named type or flags it as a candidate.
2. **Rubric amendment drafting.** When the Innovation Engine fires on an engine type (per PR-A1), the editor drafts the amendment text defining the type.
3. **Classification consistency.** The editor is the single source of classification for all 115 chapters. A second-editor reproducibility study would strengthen the editor-independence claim; we flag it as companion measurement.

The 5 validated engine types, their founding instances, and the 19-entry candidate list reflect this editor's judgment. We commit to Glaser-style grounded theory (§2.6) which accepts editor-positioning without making it constitutive.

### 4.5 Pre-Registered Thresholds

From plan.md:
- Mean cultures-per-engine ≤ 2 → taxonomy reframes as case studies.
- Non-provincialism < 50% → engines are region-bound.

### 5.2 Non-Provincialism Score

Engine types meeting ≥ 3 instances AND ≥ 3 regions:

| Type | Cultures | Regions | ≥3/≥3? |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| Covenant | 2 | 2 | NO (both fail) |

| Non-Chronological | 5 | 5 | YES |
| Contradiction-Held | 5 | 1 | NO (regions fail) |
| Knowledge-System | 3 | 3 | YES |

**Non-provincialism score: 3 of 5 = 60%**, Wilson 95% CI [23.1%, 88.2%].

Point estimate 60% is above the pre-registered 50% threshold. The Wilson CI lower bound of 23.1% dips below 50%, reflecting the small sample (n=5 validated types). We report this tension honestly: at n=5, a single re-classification would swing the point estimate by 20 percentage points. A larger validated-type sample (awaiting future adoption of candidates) would narrow the CI substantially.

### 5.3 Interpretation of the Two Falsifiers

**Covenant fails both tests** (2 cultures, 2 regions). Covenant has been formally adopted as an engine type since v1.1 (the original Structural Engine amendment), but has only 2 founding-instance cultures. If the criterion is strictly that all validated types must meet ≥3/≥3, Covenant is a candidate for re-classification as a 2-instance validated but not-yet-cross-regional type. We do not re-classify here because the v1.1 amendment is canonical within the rubric.

**Contradiction-Held fails the region test** (5 cultures, 1 region). All 5 founding instances are Region 24 (Colonial Empires). This is not a taxonomy failure — the engine type is specifically about extractive colonial systems, and Region 24 is the region where such systems are concentrated in LUCIA's geographic organization. A region-bound engine type can still be cross-cultural (five distinct colonial empires) even when all instances cluster geographically. We flag this as a culture-type-specific pattern and argue it is a feature, not a bug.

Re-counting non-provincialism with Contradiction-Held included as "cross-cultural-within-region" passes: 4 of 5 = 80%. Excluding it strictly by the ≥3-regions rule: 3 of 5 = 60%. We report the strict number as primary and the interpretive number as a robustness check.

## 6. Discussion

### 6.1 Cross-Cultural Engine Taxonomy

The 5 validated engine types span 23 cultures across 9 distinct regions (counting union of all types). Mean cultures-per-type 4.6 is substantially above the 2.0 threshold. The taxonomy is empirically cross-cultural in the sense that each validated type has instances drawn from genuinely different cultures, and at least three types span multiple regions. The validated core is small (5 types) because formal adoption into the rubric requires the Innovation Engine's trigger to fire — a conservative bar that produces high-confidence types at the cost of a large candidate tail.

### 6.2 The Contradiction-Held Region-Boundedness

Contradiction-Held-as-Engine fails the strict ≥3-regions test because all 5 founding instances are colonial empires in Region 24. This is not a methodological failure; it reflects the engine type's culture-type specificity. Some engine types are inherently domain-bound — contradiction-held-as-engine specifically characterizes extractive systems, and extractive systems cluster in Region 24 of LUCIA's geographic organization. A non-colonial instance (e.g., late-Roman *libertas* invoked against *libertas*, or late-Ming Neo-Confucian self-critique) would satisfy the region-breadth test; we flag this as companion measurement and note that the v1.7 amendment text anticipates such future instances.

### 6.3 Scale-Dependent Validation Ratios

PR-B1 reported 52 validated / 14 candidate voice registers (79% validated); PR-B2 reports 5 validated / 19 candidate engines (21% validated). The ratios invert. Our explanation: prose-level registers have chapter-length material to validate against (every paragraph contains voice), while arc-level engines have one opportunity per chapter (each chapter has exactly one dominant engine). At 115 chapters, prose-level replication is well-sampled; arc-level replication is sparser. This matches PR-A3's scale-dependent saturation finding (Voice saturates faster than Structural Engine at the adoption level, but Structural Engine "saturation" is deceptive because the candidate tail is long).

The implication: Track B's three papers measure the same underlying phenomenon (typology emergence in AI-assisted production) at three different observational substrates, with predictable scale-dependent sample-size effects.

### 6.4 Why Propp and Campbell Don't Apply

The Proppian 31 functions operate at action-sequence level (who does what to whom in the plot). Our engines operate at mechanism level (what institutional or material logic drives the chapter's arc). A Proppian decomposition of Mali Era 1 would identify Villainy (Sumanguru's tyranny), Hero's Mediation (Sundiata's rise), Struggle, Return — the surface plot. The Kurukan Fuga engine is not visible at this decomposition level because it is the institutional logic that makes the plot unfold, not a plot move. Campbell's monomyth similarly operates at a single-arc level that cannot accommodate the 5 distinct engine types we validate.

We are not claiming Propp and Campbell are wrong for their target domains; we are claiming they are not the right tools for engine-level analysis. The engine taxonomy is orthogonal to function sequences and monomyth stages.

### 6.5 Cross-Paper Dependency (PP1-C)

This paper explicitly reuses PR-B1's validation method (≥ 2-instance cross-culture replication, Wilson CIs, editor-role disclosure). The next paper, PR-B3, applies the same method at arc-closure scale: classify the terminal condition of each of 115 chapters under the v1.6 10-type framework plus the v1.7 8-candidate endogenous-institutional watch list. PR-B3 thereby completes Track B's scale-ascending chain (prose → arc → closure).

### 6.6 What This Paper Does Not Establish

1. Engine-type completeness — PR-A3 measures saturation (Structural Engine saturates fast at aggregate level, but with long candidate tail).
2. Transportability — PR-C3.
3. Model-independence — PR-C1.
4. Editor-independence — flagged as companion reproducibility study.

## 7. Threats to Validity

### 7.1 Small Sample at Validated-Types Level

n=5 validated types produces a wide Wilson CI on the 3/5 = 60% non-provincialism score: [23.1%, 88.2%]. The point estimate clears the threshold; the lower bound does not. Future adoption of candidate engine types (projected at ~2-3 per year at current rate) would narrow the CI materially. A paper at n=15 validated types would produce a much tighter CI on an analogous point estimate.

### 7.2 Editor Classification as Variable

The 5 validated types and their 23 instance-cultures reflect the editor's classification judgment. A different editor might classify Knowledge-System's Tang instance as Knowledge-System (the dominant interpretation) or as Material Object (the Tang poem as material-text object — an interpretation that would move the count). We commit to Glaser-style grounded theory that accepts editor-positioning without making it constitutive, and flag reproducibility study as companion measurement.

### 7.3 Dual-Engine Chapters

Some chapters have two simultaneous engines (Brazil's Palmares counter-polity + axé knowledge system — the Dual Engine candidate). We classify by the editor's primary identification; secondary engines are counted under the Dual Engine candidate type but may also be claimed by their primary engine type. This double-counting is minimal (n=1 confirmed: Brazil).

### 7.4 Region-Binding vs. Region-Breadth

The Contradiction-Held type's Region 24 concentration is design-bound rather than a measurement artifact. Our non-provincialism test treats this as a failure case at point estimate; a region-stratified test (does the engine have ≥ 3 instances *given the region constraint*?) would move Contradiction-Held to passing. We report both framings.

### 7.5 Propp / Campbell Comparisons Are Logical, Not Empirical

§2.1 and §2.2 argue Proppian and Campbell frameworks are not the right tools; we do not run Propp or Campbell decomposition on LUCIA chapters to verify empirically. A companion study could test whether Propp's 31 functions fit LUCIA chapters as well as or worse than our 5 engine types. We predict the 5 engines will fit better at arc-level; Propp at action-level.

### 7.6 n=1 Project

The engine taxonomy is LUCIA's. Transportability is PR-C3.

## 8. Conclusion

Five engine types have been formally adopted into LUCIA's rubric through the Innovation Engine's ≥ 2-instance trigger (PR-A1): Covenant (2 cultures across 2 regions), Material Object (8 across 6), Non-Chronological (5 across 5), Knowledge-System (3 across 3), Contradiction-Held (5 across 1). Mean cultures-per-type: 4.6, well above the 2.0 falsification threshold. Non-provincialism (types with ≥ 3 instances AND ≥ 3 regions): 3 of 5 = 60% at point estimate, Wilson 95% CI [23.1%, 88.2%] — point estimate above the 50% threshold, lower bound below. We report the CI honestly and attribute the width to small sample size.

Alongside the 5 validated core types, 19 single-instance engine-type candidates sit on the INNOVATIONS.md watch list — the same validated-core / candidate-tail structure PR-B1 reported for voice registers (52 validated + 14 watch-list). The two ratios invert (79% validated in Voice; 21% validated in Engine) because engines have one per-chapter observation while registers have sentence-level observations. The pattern is consistent with scale-dependent validation from PR-A3's saturation framework.

The engine typology is not recoverable by Propp's 31-function morphology or Campbell's monomyth: those frameworks operate at action-sequence and single-arc scales respectively, while our engines operate at mechanism scale. This paper reuses PR-B1's validation method at chapter-arc scale and establishes the framework PR-B3 extends to arc-closure scale.

Narrow claim, honestly-bounded uncertainty, validated against pre-registered thresholds: LUCIA's 5 formally-adopted structural engine types satisfy the cross-cultural taxonomy criteria at their current sample size, with long-candidate-tail structure characteristic of emergent typologies in AI-assisted production at this corpus scale.
