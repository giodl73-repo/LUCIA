# Panel Review R1 — P-1 Barbara Tuchman
## The High Wagadu — Opening

**Voice:** Barbara Tuchman — Narrative craft, pacing, scene construction, arc

---

The opening earns its reader. It does not trick the reader — it builds forward by making the walk itself the question. The cold sand, the tight cotton, the muffled drum — these are the right details, chosen because they ground the reader in a body before the body has to mean anything. By the time the political theology arrives ("not superstition — it is political theology"), the reader has already accepted it, because they have been walking in the girl's shoes for five paragraphs.

What works especially well is the decision to honor the Dausi's silence about her name. "Her name is not in any document" — this is the right opening gambit for a chapter about a culture that preserved its history in oral performance rather than written record. The limitation becomes the story's first fact. That is craft.

The segue to the economic system (the crossing, the two towns) is handled cleanly. The chapter pivots from the girl to the geography without breaking the covenant frame: "Wagadu was wealthy because it sat at the crossing" arrives with the force of explanation rather than intrusion because the reader already understands that the crossing and the covenant are the same thing. The twin-city explanation is one of the stronger structural-as-character passages in the Sahel corpus.

The one question I would push on: the final sentence — "The drum speaks." — is powerful as an ending, but the opening has not yet shown us what the drum's voice means to the living world of the chapter. We need the chapter to earn that beat by showing us the twin cities in the drum's full resonance. The opening promises a drum; the chapter must deliver the world that drum holds together.

**The question I'd push on:** The chapter's task is to show the intermediary engine at work over three centuries. The opening grounds us in the covenant's ritual logic. How will the chapter render the system's internal momentum — the tax rates, the nugget-dust distinction, the military capacity — in the same voice register as this opening? The risk is that the economic sections drift into exposition. The voice must carry the covenant frame through the mechanics.
