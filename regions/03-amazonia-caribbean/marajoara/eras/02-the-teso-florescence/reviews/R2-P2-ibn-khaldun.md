# R2-P2 — Ibn Khaldun — Panel-2 Review of Notes

**Chapter:** Marajoara — Era 2: The Teso Florescence
**Round:** Panel-2
**Date:** 2026-04-17

---

The production bible has found the engine. The ceramic knowledge system as the medium of political legitimacy — this is precisely the structural principle I was asking for in Panel-1. The mechanism is clear: the woman who controls the visual language controls the community's claim about its dead, and that claim is where social authority resides.

What the notes must still clarify before the chapter is written: the relationship between ceramic authority and teso construction authority. The notes say "the women who hold the ceramic knowledge are also the women whose lineage authority organizes the community's return to the teso each season." Is this established in the archaeological record, or is this an inference? If it is an inference, it needs to be flagged as such — both in the chapter and in the figures.md. A structural claim of this importance cannot rest on an unexamined assumption.

The terminal condition is correct: the teso-construction project dissolves when its costs exceed its returns. But the notes should push one step further: what does "return" mean in this society's terms? The return is not simply agricultural productivity — it is the community's ability to bury its dead in the proper manner, which requires the specialist's presence, which requires the concentrated settlement pattern of the teso. The dissolution of the teso system is simultaneously a demographic, economic, and cosmological crisis. These three registers should not be separated in the chapter; they should be shown as one.

The chiefdom vs. heterarchy question is handled correctly: present through material evidence rather than resolving a theoretical debate. Good. The chapter's task is to show what the power relations felt like from inside the teso, not to adjudicate between modern scholarly positions.

**Remaining pressure point:** The section plan puts the florescence-and-horizon section last. I would urge the author to consider whether the terminal condition should be seeded earlier — not narrated, but present as an undertone. If the flood pattern is already changing in the period the chapter describes, the woman making the urn is already working in a world that is beginning to shift, even if she cannot feel it. The urn that endures longer than the florescence is more powerful if we understand the seeds of the contraction were present at the peak.
