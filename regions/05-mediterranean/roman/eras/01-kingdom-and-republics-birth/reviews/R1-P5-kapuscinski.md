## P-5 — Ryszard Kapuscinski — Review of Roman Era 1 Opening

**Overall:** I believe this opening. It has the weight of a place that is becoming something. The marsh becoming a Forum. The hills becoming a state. But I want to be there more completely. I want the heat and the insects and the quality of light on the Tiber in the late afternoon.

**What works:**
- The Forum as drained marsh. This is essential. Every great capital was once a small, unpromising place, and showing Roma as that place prevents the reader from projecting the marble city backward.
- The Brutus funeral as the opening event. I have covered state funerals in many countries, and they always tell you more about the living than the dead. This one tells you what the Romani were promising themselves.
- The toga paragraph. Clothing is always political. Always.

**What doesn't:**
- I want more light. What does the Italian light do to tufa stone? To terracotta? To the surface of the Tiber? The visual palette is muted, and early Roma was not muted. The Etruscan terracotta on the Jupiter temple was brightly painted.
- The opening moves from scene (funeral) to geography to myth to constitution. The transitions are clean but the pacing after the funeral is expository. Can the constitutional argument be delivered through a scene rather than through analysis?

**The question I'd push on:**
What does the Tiber sound like? A river that floods is not a quiet river. The sound of the water, the smell of the mud when it recedes, the insects that breed in the standing water on the low ground. Roma lived with this river the way a family lives with a difficult relative. The chapter should let the reader hear it.
