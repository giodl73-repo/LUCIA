# Sources

## Primary Sources

- *Shiji* (Records of the Grand Historian) by Sima Qian — the fullest early account of the Xiongnu
- *Han shu* (Book of Han) — continued coverage of Xiongnu-Han relations
- *Hou Han shu* (Book of the Later Han) — the split into Northern and Southern Xiongnu
- Diplomatic correspondence preserved in the Chinese annals — the heqin negotiations

## Key Academic Works

- Nicola Di Cosmo, *Ancient China and Its Enemies: The Rise of Nomadic Power in East Asian History* (2002) — essential
- Thomas Barfield, *The Perilous Frontier: Nomadic Empires and China* (1989)
- Gideon Shelach — Xiongnu archaeology
- Ursula Brosseder and Bryan Miller, eds., *Xiongnu Archaeology* (2011)
- Christopher Beckwith, *Empires of the Silk Road* (2009)
