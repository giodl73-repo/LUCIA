# Sources — Kingdom of Kush

## Primary / Archaeological

- Piye's victory stele — Cairo Museum; the conquest of Egypt as narrated by the Kushite king himself
- Kerma burial excavations — Bonnet, Swiss Archaeological Mission; tumuli, deffufa, retainer burials
- El-Kurru and Nuri pyramid cemeteries — Reisner excavations; Twenty-Fifth Dynasty royal burials
- Jebel Barkal temple complex — archaeological records; Kushite and Egyptian phases
- Kawa inscription texts — Taharqa's building records and theological statements

## Academic

- Welsby, Derek A. *The Kingdom of Kush: The Napatan and Meroitic Empires*. 1996. British Museum Press.
- Kendall, Timothy. *Kush: Lost Kingdom of the Nile*. 1982. Brockton Public Library.
- Bonnet, Charles. *The Ancient Kingdom of Kerma*. Ongoing excavation reports, University of Geneva.
- Török, László. *The Kingdom of Kush: Handbook of the Napatan-Meroitic Civilization*. 1997. Brill.
- Morkot, Robert. *The Black Pharaohs: Egypt's Nubian Rulers*. 2000. Rubicon Press.
- Edwards, David N. *The Nubian Past: An Archaeology of the Sudan*. 2004. Routledge.
