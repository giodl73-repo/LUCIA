# Sources — Ndebele

## Primary / Oral

- Ndebele izibongo (praise poetry) — royal praise traditions for Mzilikazi and Lobengula
- Moffat, Robert. Missionary journals and correspondence from Matabeleland, 1850s–1860s — direct observation of Mzilikazi's court
- Selous, Frederick Courteney. *A Hunter's Wanderings in Africa*. 1881. — contemporary observations of Matabeleland

## Academic

- Cobbing, Julian. "The Ndebele Under the Khumalos, 1820–1896." PhD dissertation, Lancaster University, 1976. — the foundational study
- Ranger, Terence. *Revolt in Southern Rhodesia 1896–7: A Study in African Resistance*. 1967. Heinemann. — the First Chimurenga; covers the Ndebele rebellion
- Beach, D.N. *The Shona and Zimbabwe 900–1850*. 1980. Heinemann. — context for the Ndebele arrival on the plateau
- Hughes, Heather. *The First President: A Life of John L. Dube, Founding President of the ANC*. 2011. — South African Ndebele context
- Bhebe, Ngwabi. *Christianity and Traditional Religion in Western Zimbabwe 1859–1923*. 1979. Longman. — the missionary encounter in Matabeleland
- Kenyon, Ian. "The Ndebele State." In *A History of Central Africa*. — overview of the kingdom's organization

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Comprehensive study of the amahole system and Shona incorporation into the Ndebele state
- Scholarship on Lobengula's concession negotiations from Ndebele documentary sources
- Analysis of the 1896 First Chimurenga from Ndebele perspectives
- Studies on Gukurahundi (1983–84) and its historical relationship to Ndebele political identity
- Scholarship on the South African Ndebele (KwaNdebele) as a distinct community
