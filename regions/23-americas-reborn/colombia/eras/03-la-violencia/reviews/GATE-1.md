## Dimension Notes

**Scene (5/5):** The timestamp (1:05 PM), the specific street (Carrera Séptima), the named actors (Gaitán, Roa Sierra, Castro), and the compressed timeline (ten minutes / ten years) create one of the strongest scene openings in the Colombia arc. The crowd's behavior — gathering warmly with no particular urgency, then turning — is exactly right for the setting.

**World (5/5):** The world's logic emerges from inside: the chicha ban as class politics, the party allegiance as local inheritance, the pays politico / país nacional not as sociological concept but as Gaitán's own diagnosis delivered in his own words in his own plazas. The country's five-region geography is implied without being listed. The reader understands the stakes without being explained them.

**Honesty (5/5):** The v1.2 amendment standard is met. The refusal to judge the violence is attributed to the culture's own naming practice — "they called it La Violencia, the noun" — not to editorial restraint. Castro's praise ("spontaneous revolution") is presented as his frame, not the chapter's. The 200,000 dead is stated without adverb.

**Texture (5/5):** The sensory anchors are structural: the smell of the Carrera Séptima establishes normalcy before the shot; the orange glow of the fires establishes rupture; the cloud cover of the Andes becomes the metaphor for la Violencia as condition. Kapuściński's standard — "can you feel where you are?" — is answered on the first paragraph.

**Voice (4/5):** The discover register — magical-realism-as-realism, accumulative, matter-of-fact — holds through most of the opening. "Spontaneous revolution. He meant it as praise." is the register at full strength. One passage ("something more diffuse and more intimate") briefly slides toward analysis-voice before recovering. Not enough to damage the chapter; the García Márquez close is the register's strongest moment. Em-dash count: 0. ✓

**Pull (5/5):** Three pull mechanisms operating simultaneously: the "ten minutes / ten years" compression creates narrative urgency; the Castro cameo (named, embodied, placed in the wrong history) creates the sense of the world tilting; the García Márquez close names the man who will try to find the language for all of this without telling us he succeeded. The reader wants to follow all three threads.
