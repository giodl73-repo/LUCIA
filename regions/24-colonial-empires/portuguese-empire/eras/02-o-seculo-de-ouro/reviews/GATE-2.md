## Notes

Combined score: 30 (GATE-1) + 55 (GATE-2) = 85/90. Above the manuscript mean target of 75+.

**Innovations to flag:**

1. **The mythologizing-text as empire-from-within evidence.** Using Camões's *Os Lusíadas* not as context but as primary structural evidence — letting the epic's grammar (its exclusions and its inclusions, the gods it invokes, the people it places as obstacles rather than protagonists) do the work of characterizing the empire's self-understanding. This is a variant of the v1.3 "Primary Art as Structural Evidence" technique, adapted for a situation where the art is consciously propagandistic: the propaganda's limits are as revealing as its content. May warrant logging if it appears again in other colonial empire chapters.

2. **Counter-figure from within the empire's own production.** Pinto as the empire's internal ambivalent witness — not a colonial subject speaking back, but a participant who cannot sustain the empire's official narrative about itself. His *Peregrinação* is used as the empire's own dissent, produced by the empire, preserved by the empire, and published after the empire's Golden Century had already ended. This is distinct from Source Asymmetry as Technique (v1.5) because the asymmetry is internal: two voices from within the same culture arriving at incompatible accounts of the same events.
