# B-amarna-2 — Barry Kemp (Amarna)

## Background

Barry Kemp is Professor Emeritus of Egyptology at Cambridge and has
directed the excavation of Tell el-Amarna since the 1970s. His work
has revealed the material reality of Akhenaten's city: its hasty
construction, its workers' village where traditional deities persisted
in domestic contexts, and the gap between the king's theological vision
and the lived experience of the people who built his capital.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter engage with what the archaeology actually shows
about life at Akhetaten, including the evidence that the workers
did not share the king's theology?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Workers' village evidence of domestic deities (Bes, Taweret)
- Accurate description of city construction (mud brick, plaster, talatat)
- The open-air temple as theological architecture
- Water delivery by donkey (no spring at the site)

## What He Will Challenge

- Any suggestion that the Aten cult transformed ordinary people's beliefs
- Inaccurate description of the city's layout or construction
- Overstatement of the city's grandeur (it was built fast and it showed)
- Any failure to distinguish between official theology and lived practice
