## BOARD — B-coptic-1 — Ewa Wipszycka — Review of The Desert Fathers

**Overall:** The chapter handles the institutional dimension better than most treatments of the desert fathers. The Pachomian monastery as economic unit, the patriarch-monk power relationship, the tax-exemption motive for withdrawal: these are present and correctly represented. This is not a purely spiritual account. It acknowledges the material and political reality.

**What is accurate:**
- The Pachomian system described as an economic unit (reed mats, farming, surplus distribution) is correct and supported by the Pachomian Lives and the archaeological evidence.
- The relationship between Athanasius and the monks as a reciprocal power relationship is accurately described. Athanasius did cultivate the monks as a political base. The monks did serve as his constituency.
- The tax-exemption motive (Section IV) is correctly identified and honestly presented: "Not everyone who went to the desert went for God."
- The Valens conscription attempt and the Thebaid riots are accurately placed.

**What needs correction:**
1. **Pachomian monasteries and women:** The chapter mentions "two for women" but does not name them or describe their organization. The women's communities under Pachomius's sister (traditionally named Maria or Mary) were organized under the same rule. A sentence acknowledging the women's communities as organizationally parallel, not subsidiary, would strengthen the account.
2. **Jerome's translation of the Rule:** The chapter correctly notes that Jerome translated from Greek, not Coptic. It could add that Jerome visited Egypt and that his Latin Preface to the Rule contains his own observations about the Pachomian system, which are a valuable (if biased) source.
3. **Monastic landholding:** The chapter describes the Pachomian economy through craft production (reed mats). By the late fourth century, the larger monasteries also held agricultural land. Shenouda's White Monastery was a major landholder. The economic picture should include land, not just craft.

**Factual errors:** None identified.

**Verdict:** Accurate with minor additions recommended. The institutional dimension is present and correctly weighted. The chapter does not reduce monasticism to a spiritual movement. Approved with suggested additions.
