## E-1 — The Judgment Auditor — CLEAN pass on The Great Expansion

**Violations found:** 3

1. Section II, para 1: "it was the fact that outsiders, centuries later, would have the most difficulty accepting" — P3 (Borderline): This names an outside perspective. The sentence works because it denies the outsider's framing, but the word "outsiders" introduces a vantage point the chapter otherwise avoids. Consider cutting "and it was the fact that outsiders, centuries later, would have the most difficulty accepting, because their maps showed blue void between the island dots and their ships crossed the Pacific as if crossing a desert, dreading the distances" and letting "The Tongans knew the distances" follow directly from "The ocean was not empty."

2. Section III, final analytical paragraph: "It accomplished what European navigation would accomplish only with the chronometer, the sextant, and the nautical chart" — P2 (Implicit comparison): This measures the Polynesian system against European navigation. The system does not need to be validated by comparison to European instruments. The sentence should describe what the system accomplished on its own terms without referencing what Europeans needed to do the same thing.

3. Section IV, para 4: "The system was not patriarchal in the way that word implies to a modern reader" — P2 (Modern commentary): The phrase "in the way that word implies to a modern reader" is an authorial aside to a modern audience. The chapter should simply describe the system's structure and let the reader draw their own conclusions. Cut "in the way that word implies to a modern reader."

**Verdict:** 3 fixes needed (0 P1, 2 P2, 1 P3)
