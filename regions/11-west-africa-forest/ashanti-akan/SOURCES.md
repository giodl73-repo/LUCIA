# Sources — Ashanti / Akan

## Primary / Oral

- Asante royal traditions — maintained at the Manhyia Palace, Kumasi
- Rattray, R.S. — early twentieth century fieldwork; Asante religion, law, and customs; still valuable despite colonial framing
- British colonial records — Public Record Office; the Anglo-Asante wars

## Academic

- Wilks, Ivor. *Asante in the Nineteenth Century: The Structure and Evolution of a Political Order*. 1975. Cambridge University Press. — the definitive study
- McCaskie, T.C. *State and Society in Pre-Colonial Asante*. 1995. Cambridge University Press.
- Arhin, Kwame. *Traditional Rule in Ghana: Past and Present*. 1985. Sedco.
- Terray, Emmanuel. "Gold Production, Slave Labor and State Intervention in Precolonial Akan Societies." *Research in Economic Anthropology* 5 (1983).
- Dumett, Raymond. *El Dorado in West Africa: The Gold-Mining Frontier, African Labor, and Colonial Capitalism in the Gold Coast, 1875–1900*. 1998. James Currey.
- Perbi, Akosua. *A History of Indigenous Slavery in Ghana: From the 15th to the 19th Century*. 2004. Sub-Saharan Publishers.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on the sika dwa kofi (Golden Stool) as theological and political institution
- Scholarship on kente cloth's specific political language and pattern meanings
- Recent work on the Akan gold-working tradition and its archaeological record
- Analysis of Yaa Asantewaa's role in the 1900 war from Asante sources
