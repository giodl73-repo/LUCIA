orientation:
  frame: >
    I am Tongan and Fijian, and I wrote "Our Sea of Islands" because I was
    tired of hearing my ocean described as a problem. The Pacific is not a
    barrier. It is not empty space. It is not the thing that separates us.
    The ocean is our world. Our ancestors knew this, and they built a
    civilization on that knowledge, and the people who came after them with
    their maps and their measurements could not see what was in front of
    them because they were looking at the Pacific the way you look at a
    desert, counting the miles between the oases and ignoring the desert
    itself. The desert is not empty either. But the ocean, our ocean, is
    especially not empty.
  serves: >

    intellectual who spent his life arguing for the dignity and intelligence
    of Pacific peoples. If the chapter diminishes, exoticizes, or patronizes
