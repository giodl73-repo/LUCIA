# Sources — Papal States

## Primary Sources

- Gregory the Great, *Registrum Epistularum* — papal letters; the most complete record of early medieval papal administration
- *Donation of Constantine* — 8th-century forgery; the document on which papal temporal claims partly rested; exposed as a forgery by Lorenzo Valla in 1440
- Gratian, *Decretum* (c. 1140) — the canonical law compilation; foundational for medieval church governance
- *Dictatus Papae* (1075) — Gregory VII's 27 propositions claiming papal supremacy
- Innocent III, letters and decretals — the most voluminous papal correspondence of the high Middle Ages
- *Liber Pontificalis* — "Book of the Popes"; biographical records from early papacy through medieval period
- Acts of the Fourth Lateran Council (1215) — 70 canons; the most comprehensive medieval church legislation

## Secondary Sources

- Ullmann, Walter. *The Growth of Papal Government in the Middle Ages*. 3rd ed. Methuen, 1970.
- Tellenbach, Gerd. *The Church in Western Europe from the Tenth to the Early Twelfth Century*. Cambridge University Press, 1993.
- Partner, Peter. *The Lands of St. Peter: The Papal State in the Middle Ages and the Early Renaissance*. Eyre Methuen, 1972.
- Renouard, Yves. *The Avignon Papacy 1305–1403*. Faber and Faber, 1970.
- Oakley, Francis. *The Conciliarist Tradition*. Oxford University Press, 2003.
