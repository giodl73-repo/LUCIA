## BOARD — Alan Bowman — Review of Julio-Claudian Egypt

**Domain:** Roman Egypt administration, papyrology, social stratification

**Overall:** The chapter handles the documentary evidence well. The use of Petesouchos as an embodied figure drawn from the papyri is exactly right, and the papyri-as-theme approach (the province that documents itself) is a genuine insight into what makes Roman Egypt distinctive as a historical subject.

**Factual accuracy:**
- The claim that Egypt fed Rome for "four months of the year" is within the scholarly range (estimates vary from one-third to one-quarter of Rome's grain supply). Acceptable.
- The annona feeding "approximately 200,000 Roman citizens for free" is consistent with Augustan-era estimates. Acceptable.
- The laographia as a marker of ethnic hierarchy is correctly described. The exemption of Greeks and Jews is accurate for the Julio-Claudian period (Jewish status became contested later).
- Cornelius Gallus's recall and suicide are accurately described. The inscriptions at Philae are the primary evidence.
- The Gnomon of the Idios Logos is correctly dated as reflecting Augustan-era regulations in a later copy.

**Corrections needed:**
1. "Petesouchos" as a figure from Tebtunis tax receipts: the name is plausible (Petesouchos is a common name in the Fayyum, meaning "gift of Sobek"), but the chapter should make clear whether this is a specific documented individual or a representative composite. If composite, figures.md should acknowledge this. The papyri contain many Petesouchoi; attributing a specific set of tax receipts to one requires precise citation.
2. The census "recorded the number of people in his household, their ages, and their distinguishing physical marks" is accurate: the Roman census in Egypt (the 14-year cycle) did record physical descriptions as identifying marks, a practice unique to Egypt among Roman provinces. This is a strong detail.
3. "We know more about the daily life of a farmer in the Fayyum under Tiberius than we know about most senators in Rome." This is defensible as a rhetorical claim but should be understood as referring to the volume of documentary evidence, not necessarily the depth of individual biography. Acceptable with this understanding.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The treatment of the ethnic hierarchy (Greek, Jewish, Egyptian) is accurate for the Julio-Claudian period. The chapter correctly notes that the laographia was the clearest marker.
- The 38 CE pogrom description is consistent with Philo's account. The detail about Flaccus being recalled for political offenses, not for the pogrom, is important and correct.

**Verdict:** One minor correction needed (Petesouchos attribution). Otherwise accurate and well-grounded in the documentary evidence.
