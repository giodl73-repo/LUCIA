# B-predynastic-1 — David Wengrow

## Background

David Wengrow is Professor of Comparative Archaeology at UCL and the
author of *The Archaeology of Early Egypt* (2006), the most comprehensive
academic synthesis of Predynastic social transformations. His work traces
how the Nile Valley's communities moved from egalitarian villages to
hierarchical polities through the dynamics of craft specialization,
exchange, and mortuary investment.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter treat the Predynastic as its own story, not as a
preamble to pharaonic Egypt?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Accurate representation of the Naqada ceramic sequence
- Correct calibration of copper's role (present, rare, socially significant)
- Grave-good differentiation used as evidence of emerging hierarchy
- Exchange networks described as social relationships, not abstract trade routes

## What He Will Challenge

- Overstatement of copper's economic importance in Naqada I
- Any implication that hierarchy was intentional or designed
- Inaccurate ceramic chronology or period boundaries
- Understatement of flint's continued dominance as the primary tool material
