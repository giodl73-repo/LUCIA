## Sensory Plan Assessment

I am reading the production bible for its sensory architecture. Let me be direct about what I find.

The section plan correctly identifies the two rhythms I asked for: the jirga (deliberate, circling) and the campaigns (forward, kinetic). This is the right distinction. The Kandahar assembly and the march to the Punjab are different physical experiences and must feel different to read.

## Where the Plan Is Strong

Section 2 commits to putting the reader on the road — terrain-specific, the army's physical movement visible. Good. Section 4 commits to putting the reader in the court — lamp, ink, the sensory reality of the chancery. Good. These are the two places where historical writing most often fails to ground itself, and the notes have flagged both as requiring physical rendering.

## Where the Plan Needs to Be More Specific

The Panipat section (Section 5) currently commits to grounding the battle through Viswasrao's death. This is right — a specific death in a specific moment is the battle's physical anchor. But the section plan needs to also commit to the road TO Panipat: the army marching from Kandahar or Peshawar, the Khyber Pass or the Punjab plains, the season (January — cold, early morning frost on the battlefield). The battle's geography should be felt before the battle happens.

Also: the landing section (Section 6) returns to the green wheat image, which is strong as a structural close. But the notes should specify what the wheat becomes — ripened grain is the image of fulfillment. Ahmad Shah dying in Kandahar, his body returned to the city where the assembly gave him the wheat crown, is the sensory close I want: the man in the city that made him, the empire he leaves behind him not yet broken but already showing the seams.

## One Addition

The notes mention Elphinstone (1815) as a source. Elphinstone was in Peshawar and Kandahar within living memory of Ahmad Shah's reign. He recorded physical descriptions of Kandahar's bazaars, the fort, the road patterns. This material should ground Section 1's revision of the opening — the assembly's physical setting. Use Elphinstone's eye as a source for the physical, while being alert to his interpretive frame (which should be treated as outsider-as-structural-evidence, per the v1.5 rubric).

## Verdict

The sensory plan is adequate but should be enriched specifically in Section 5 (the Panipat road, not just the battle) and Section 6 (Ahmad Shah's death in Kandahar as physical close). No gate-level concern. Proceed to WRITE with these additions noted.
