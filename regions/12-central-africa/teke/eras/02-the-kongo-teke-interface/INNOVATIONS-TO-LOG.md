### Innovation: Strategic Non-Documentation as Cultural Voice
- **Source:** regions/12-central-africa/teke/eras/02-the-kongo-teke-interface/
- **Date:** 2026-04-16
- **Dimension:** Evidence / Honesty
- **Description:** The chapter uses the Teke broker's professional requirement not to write things down as a double technique: it explains the source gap (why the Teke pre-colonial record is thin) AND characterizes the culture (information is the resource that cannot be given away). The absence of documentation is not an archival accident but a deliberate strategic choice embedded in the commercial logic. The broker who keeps no ledger is being faithful to the same principle that makes him effective: revealing nothing to anyone who is not supposed to know it.
- **Why it works:** Extends the v1.5 "source gap as cultural honesty" technique into a new category — cultures where non-documentation is an active strategic practice rather than a technical limitation. Previous instances treated the gap as absence to be acknowledged. This technique finds the cultural LOGIC for the absence and makes that logic part of the story. Applicable to any commercial, intelligence, or secretive culture where the record's thinness follows from the culture's own epistemological practice.
- **Status:** logged (1 instance — watching for 2nd instance from other commercial or secretive cultures with thin written records)

### Innovation: Information Asymmetry as Structural Engine
- **Source:** regions/12-central-africa/teke/eras/02-the-kongo-teke-interface/
- **Date:** 2026-04-16
- **Dimension:** Progression
- **Description:** The broker's engine is pure information asymmetry: knowing the price on both ends of the route while controlling access to the middle. Unlike previous structural engines (covenant, psychological mechanism, knowledge system, material object), this engine is entirely relational — it has no physical embodiment, no institutional form, no named covenant. It lives in the gap between what one trading partner knows and what the other knows. Terminal condition: the engine applies unchanged to the slave trade, but the commodity's properties differ from copper's in ways the calculation alone cannot measure.
- **Why it works:** New structural engine category for intermediary cultures. Previous engines were positive (the covenant creates; the river controls; the object embodies). This engine is negative — it works through withholding. Second instance of intermediary economics as structural engine (first: Soninke, v1.5), with a distinct mechanism: where the Soninke used a sacred covenant to legitimate the broker position, the Teke use pure information asymmetry enforced through logistical infrastructure. Potential cluster with Soninke intermediary economics — watching for 3rd instance to propose amendment.
- **Status:** logged (2 instances with Soninke — watching for 3rd instance to propose amendment)
