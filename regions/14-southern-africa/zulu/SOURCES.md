# Sources — Zulu

## Primary / Oral

- Zulu izibongo (praise poetry) — royal praise poems for each king; collected and published
- Stuart Archive — James Stuart's early twentieth century interviews with Zulu oral informants; the most important primary source collection
- Fynn, Henry Francis. *The Diary of Henry Francis Fynn*. Ed. Stuart & Malcolm. 1950. — early European observations of Shaka's court

## Academic

- Guy, Jeff. *The Destruction of the Zulu Kingdom: The Civil War in Zululand, 1879–1884*. 1979. Longman. — the post-conquest period
- Hamilton, Carolyn. *Terrific Majesty: The Powers of Shaka Zulu and the Limits of Historical Invention*. 1998. Harvard University Press. — Shaka's historicity and mythology
- Laband, John. *Rope of Sand: The Rise and Fall of the Zulu Kingdom in the Nineteenth Century*. 1995. Jonathan Ball.
- Morris, Donald R. *The Washing of the Spears*. 1965. Simon & Schuster. — the Anglo-Zulu War; narrative history
- Cobbing, Julian. "The Mfecane as Alibi." *Journal of African History* 29:3 (1988). — the revisionist argument
- Wright, John B. — multiple articles on Zulu history from Shona oral sources

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Studies on Isandlwana from Zulu sources specifically
- Analysis of the Inkatha Freedom Party's use of Zulu cultural politics
