## P-3 — Chinua Achebe — Targeted Review of Notes (PANEL-2)

**Focus:** Egyptian priestly agency; the inside-voice problem.

The notes now identify Manetho as the Egyptian voice, and the Satrap Stela as the key document for the priestly alliance. This is the right direction. But the section plan for Section 2 ("The Priestly Alliance") must go further.

**What the chapter must show:**
- The priests of Ptah at Memphis did not simply "crown" Ptolemy. They conducted an evaluation. They had criteria. The chapter must show what those criteria were and how Ptolemy met them. This is the Egyptian knowledge system operating at method level.
- The Satrap Stela is written in Egyptian, by Ptolemy's scribes, for an Egyptian audience. Its rhetoric is pharaonic rhetoric. The chapter should show the stela's language as evidence of the Egyptian priestly establishment directing how Ptolemy presents himself. He did not choose to write in Egyptian. They required it.
- Manetho's *Aegyptiaca* is not merely a king-list translated into Greek. It is an act of cultural self-presentation by an Egyptian priest who understood that the new rulers needed to be taught Egypt's history in their own language. Manetho is not serving Ptolemy. Manetho is educating him. The distinction is everything.

**What I'd cut:**
- The mention of "Jewish merchants" in the opening (already flagged in PANEL-1). If they return in the chapter with substance, fine. If not, remove them from the opening.

**Verdict on the notes:** The structural engine is correct. The section plan addresses my concerns if executed well. The test will be whether Section 2 gives the Egyptian priests the same narrative depth and agency that Section 1 gives Ptolemy.
