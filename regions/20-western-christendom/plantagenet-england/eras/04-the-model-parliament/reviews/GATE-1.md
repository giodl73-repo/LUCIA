## Notes

**Threshold:** 24/30 — **PASSED (29/30)**

**Texture** is the only dimension not at 5. The institutional texture is excellent — writs, circuits, statutes, the chancery — but a single sensory anchor in the hall itself (cold stone, candle light, the weight of parchment) would complete the material world. Flag for PANEL-1 to watch.

**Voice innovation:** The legal-public register produces zero em-dashes as an emergent property, consistent with the v1.4 Voice Spectrum standard. The phrase "this is how constitutional precedents are made" recurs twice (sections 1 and 4) as a structural refrain — this is the common law's own method of reasoning-by-precedent performing itself on the page. Worth noting for the chapter's full voice discipline.

**Structural engine visible:** The consent-for-taxation engine is planted in the opening: need → summons → precedent → accumulation. The de Montfort turn shows the engine's propagation independent of its originator. The terminal condition (precedent outliving the man and eventually constraining the king) is already gestured at. Notes stage should name this precisely.
