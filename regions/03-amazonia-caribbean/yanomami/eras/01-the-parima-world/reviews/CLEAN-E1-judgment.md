## Outside-Lens Violations

**Paragraph-by-paragraph review of at-risk moments:**

1. **"This was not a failure of construction but a feature of the design"** (shabono section) — This sentence is slightly at risk of sounding like the author explaining the culture's rationality to a skeptical reader. However, it reads from inside: the community knows this about the shabono; it is not a reinterpretation. Acceptable.

2. **"Not malice; disruption"** (feast section, upstream spirit diagnosis) — Inside characterization. The hekura world's own categories are "malice" and "disruption" as distinct causes with distinct treatments. This is the inside frame, not an outside qualification.

3. **"This was not cause for panic; it was cause for attention"** (knowledge transmission section) — Slightly at risk. "Not cause for panic" implies a reader who might have read it as crisis. However, the sentence continues with the inside-voice resolution ("cause for attention") and the next paragraph describes what "attention" means in practice. Marginal — acceptable as written. Could be tightened: consider simply "This was cause for attention" without the "not cause for panic" qualifier.

4. **The "outsider" passage was revised out of the opening.** Good. The chapter version in the opening correctly replaces the hypothetical outsider with the inside-voice chain ("The child's fever and the family's meals...").

5. **"This is not a limitation. It was the design."** (knowledge section) — Same structure as point 1. Marginally at risk. Recommend: consider simply "Knowledge held in bodies could be lost when bodies died. Aramo had thought about this." without the "this is not a limitation" framing. Let the next passage show why it is the design without announcing it.

## Romanticization / Demonization Check

The reahu section is the highest-risk section for romanticization. Review:

- "The dead became part of the community's body." — Inside frame. Factually grounded in Yanomami understanding. Not romanticized.
- "The dead did not leave. They were incorporated." — Inside frame. No emotional stage direction.
- "The community drank." — Three words. No emotional instruction. The reader feels it because the scene earned it. This is the correct execution.
- "This was the most intimate possible form of honoring the dead" — This sentence adds an evaluative judgment ("most intimate possible"). The Yanomami understanding is that this is the correct form, not a ranking against alternatives. Recommend revision: "To incorporate the ancestor's substance into the bodies of those who loved them, to refuse the separation of death by making it literal." Remove "most intimate possible."
