# Sources — Luba Empire

## Primary / Archaeological

- Lake Kisale excavations — Nenquin, 1963; the foundational Iron Age burial analysis
- Upemba Depression survey — de Maret, 1970s–1980s; the long archaeological sequence
- Lukasa boards — museum collections (Royal Museum for Central Africa, Tervuren; others); the objects themselves

## Academic

- Roberts, Mary Nooter & Roberts, Allen F. *Memory: Luba Art and the Making of History*. 1996. Museum for African Art, New York. — the definitive study of the lukasa
- Vansina, Jan. *Kingdoms of the Savanna*. 1966. University of Wisconsin Press. — foundational comparative study of Central African kingdoms
- de Maret, Pierre. *Fouilles archéologiques dans la vallée du Haut-Lualaba, Zaïre*. 1985. Musée Royal de l'Afrique Centrale.
- Reefe, Thomas Q. *The Rainbow and the Kings: A History of the Luba Empire to 1891*. 1981. University of California Press.
- Burton, W.F.P. *Luba Religion and Magic in Custom and Belief*. 1961. Musée Royal de l'Afrique Centrale.

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent archaeological surveys of the Upemba Depression (post-1990)
- Studies on bulopwe as a political theology (detailed analysis)
- Scholarship on the bana balute training and transmission of lukasa knowledge
- Analysis of the Katanga copper ingot distribution and what it implies about trade networks
