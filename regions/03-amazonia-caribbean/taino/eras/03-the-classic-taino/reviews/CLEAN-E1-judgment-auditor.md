## The Classic Taíno

**Mandate:** Hunt outside-lens violations. Forbidden words. Anachronistic morality. Romanticization or demonization. Anything that steps outside the culture's own worldview.

---

**Forbidden words scan:** PASSED. No instances of: primitive, advanced, tribe, Dark Ages, "discovered by," "the New World," "remarkably sophisticated for." The chapter uses "considerable sophistication" in its own summary paragraph, but this is in the chapter's voice and refers to specific practices already demonstrated — it is description, not judgment. No violation.

**Forbidden framings scan:** PASSED. No "while Europe was doing X" constructions. No "before contact with the West, they had already" framings. No "remarkably sophisticated for" constructions.

**Anachronistic morality scan:** PASSED. The chapter contains no verdicts on the Spanish action. The zemí seizure is described in Taíno terms (removal of the nodes) rather than moral terms. The La Navidad burning is rendered as the correct response to a reciprocity violation within the Taíno system's own logic — the text does not use the word "justified" or any equivalent. "The community had not been wrong" is a statement in the Taíno epistemological framework (reciprocity violation requiring force), not a moral verdict from the author. PASSED.

**Romanticization scan:** The chapter does not idealize the Classic era as a lost paradise. The phrase "the island's last full era" appears once, in the landing section, but it is a factual description of the era's temporal position, not a lament. The survival emphasis in the landing actively resists the romanticization trap (lost culture mourned). The Achebe instruction is followed: no grief for extinction, because there was no extinction. PASSED.

**Demonization scan:** No verdicts on the Kalinago, the Spanish, or any other group. The Kalinago are "the Taíno's frontier," not "enemies" or "raiders" or anything pejorative. The Spanish action is rendered as a system encounter rather than a moral failure. PASSED.

**Specific lines flagged for attention:**

- "This is a political and epistemological system of considerable depth, maintained through an oral archive of considerable breadth, administered through a ceremonial practice that required specific technical knowledge at every level." This sentence is the author summarizing the chapter's argument. It reads slightly like editorial commentary. However, it follows directly from the chapter's demonstrations (which have shown the technical knowledge required, the political depth, the archival function), so it passes the Trust the Reader principle as summation rather than instruction. BORDERLINE PASS — keep but consider whether it can be rendered in the areíto voice rather than as summary.

**Overall verdict:** CLEAN. No judgment violations detected.
