# Panel Review — R1-P5 — Ryszard Kapuściński
## The Network's Height | Kuikuro Era 2

---

This opening has a body. Iauatá sweats. The vine stems resist the blade. The half-rotted branch is heavy even in decay, and the understory has grown up around it in weeks. The cecropia reaches "its pale arms over the cut edge, feeling for the light that the road's opened corridor provides." This is not description of nature. This is the forest pressing against the human intention of the road, and the road pressing back, and a man caught in the middle of that pressure, doing what needs to be done.

I can feel where I am. The light in the corridor at the end of the cleared section — "pale and inviting." The forest wanting to be curved. The shadow under the fallen branch. The smell is not there yet — that is the one gap — but the weight and the visual texture are strong, and the effort of the work (sweaty, specific) grounds the body sufficiently for an opening.

The sound I want: the rains. The opening tells me they are in a rainy season (the new shoots, the fast forest growth, the manioc cakes — the rains are implied but not heard). Give me the sound of the upper Xingu forest in the wet season. The birds in the canopy. The insects. The sound of the stone blade on green wood. These are not decorative details — the sound of the forest during the rains tells you something about the pace of work, the urgency of maintenance, the sensory environment in which this decision to keep the road open is being continuously made.

**The question I would push on:**

The Quarup itself must be rendered sensorially in the chapter. The opening places us three moons before it. The chapter must put us inside it — the sound of the mourning songs, the smell of the cooking fires burning all night before the wrestling, the feel of the central plaza at dawn when the communities are all present and the egitsü ceremony is at its height. The plaza at Kuhikugu with all the visiting groups arrived — this is a specific physical environment that the chapter must deliver. Not a description of the Quarup as an institution. The Quarup happening, in a body, in a place, with the weight and the heat and the sound of people who have traveled the roads to be there.

Give me the egitsü before the epidemic. Give me the network at its fullest.

— Kapuściński
