# Sources — Bulgars

## Primary Sources

- *Nominalia of the Bulgarian Khans* — 9th century; the list of Bulgar rulers; the only text in the Bulgar language
- *Abecedarium Bulgaricum* (Chernorizets Hrabar, *O Pismenekh*) — c. 893; the defense of the Slavonic alphabet
- *Shestodnev* (Hexameron) of John the Exarch — early 10th century; Preslav school masterpiece
- *Zlatostrui* (Golden Stream) — compiled for Simeon the Great; anthology of Byzantine religious texts
- *Manasses Chronicle* — 14th century Bulgarian translation and continuation of a Byzantine chronicle
- Lives of Clement of Ohrid — the hagiographic account of the Cyrillic alphabet's teacher
- *Synodikon of Tsar Boril* (1211) — the list of heretics condemned; major source on the Bogomil movement
