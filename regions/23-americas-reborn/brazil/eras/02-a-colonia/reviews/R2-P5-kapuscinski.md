# R2-P5 — Ryszard Kapuściński (PANEL-2 — Notes Review)

**Notes reviewed:** Brazil, Era 2 — A Colônia — Production Bible
**Round:** Panel-2

---

The section plan gives me what I asked for: a specific woman in the senzala, the smell of burning cane, the body's decision before the mind has made it. Section 2 is the one I will watch most carefully. The notes promise it; the chapter must deliver.

Two sensory targets I want named in the section plan that are not yet there:

First: the sound of Palmares. The notes describe the drum at dusk; the chapter should give us the full acoustic environment of the quilombo — not just the ritual drum but the ordinary sounds of a community. Children. Work. Dispute. Rain on the palm fronds. A free community has sounds that an enslaved community does not, and the contrast should be felt in the body before it is understood by the mind.

Second: the crossing itself. The notes describe the decision to run. What does the forest feel like at night to someone who has never been in a forest alone? The enslaved people who ran from the coastal engenhos toward Palmares in the interior had to cross a landscape that was dangerous in ways the plantation was not — animals, distance, disorientation. And they ran anyway. This is the bodily argument for freedom: the forest at night was less frightening than the engenho. I want the chapter to make me feel why.

The landing section in the notes ends with "the belly of the land, still holding what it swallowed." This is the right return. I trust the chapter to earn it.
