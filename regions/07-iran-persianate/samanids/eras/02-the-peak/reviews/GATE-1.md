## Assessment

**30/30. Gate passed.**

This is a strong opening. Every dimension achieves the highest score. The structural hook — a blind poet bringing a single Persian word into a secretary's lamp-lit study — is not a device but the actual historical question of the era in miniature. The scene earns its own momentum, and it earns the reader's desire to see what the Persian language will become under Samanid patronage.

**Voice note:** The scholarly-court register is new in this project — distinct from the "urban/scholarly" register attributed to Abbasid Baghdad (which tends toward density and layering), and distinct from the "argumentative/text-centered" register of the Jewish Talmud tradition. The Samanid register might be named **Court-Translational**: precise, bilingual in its reference world, moving between two intellectual traditions as naturally as al-Jayhani moves between the Arabic letter and the Persian word. Flag for Voice Keeper at CLEAN stage.

**Innovation note:** The use of a single untranslated word (*farr*) as the structural anchor for the entire opening — carrying cosmological meaning across a millennium of Iranian kingship while remaining available in the prose without being explained — may be flaggable as a **Conceptual Pivot** technique. The word arrives, is given its definition in context, and immediately becomes the era's central question. Flag for INNOVATIONS.md consideration.

**Em-dash count:** 0. Consistent with highest-scoring chapters.
