# Sources — Rwanda

## Primary / Oral

- Rwanda royal traditions — maintained by court abiru; extensively documented before the genocide
- Alexis Kagame, *La Poésie dynastique du Rwanda* (1951) and other works — the foundational inside documentation by a Rwandan scholar-priest
- Kagame, Alexis. *Un abrégé de l'ethno-histoire du Rwanda*. 2 vols. 1972. Editions Universitaires du Rwanda.

## Academic

- Vansina, Jan. *Antecedents to Modern Rwanda: The Nyiginya Kingdom*. 2004. University of Wisconsin Press. — the most rigorous historical reconstruction
- Maquet, J.J. *The Premise of Inequality in Ruanda*. 1961. Oxford University Press. — important early anthropology; to be read critically
- Prunier, Gérard. *The Rwanda Crisis: History of a Genocide*. 1995. Columbia University Press. — the historical background to 1994
- Des Forges, Alison. *Leave None to Tell the Story: Genocide in Rwanda*. 1999. Human Rights Watch.
- Newbury, Catharine. *The Cohesion of Oppression: Clientship and Ethnicity in Rwanda, 1860–1960*. 1988. Columbia University Press.
- Chrétien, Jean-Pierre. *The Great Lakes of Africa: Two Thousand Years of History*. 2003. Zone Books. — comprehensive regional history

## Needed (ACQUISITION)

- Recent scholarship on the ubuhake system and cattle distribution (economic history)
- Studies on the abiru oral tradition and what was lost in 1994
- Analysis of the Hutu-Tutsi category transformation under Belgian colonial rule
- Post-genocide scholarship on Rwanda's historical self-understanding
