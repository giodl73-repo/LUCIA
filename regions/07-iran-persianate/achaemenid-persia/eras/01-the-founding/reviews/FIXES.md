## Opening revision decisions

**Tuchman — "not tolerance as moderns understand the word" seam:**
APPLY. This is both Tuchman's concern and Achebe's. The sentence will be rewritten from inside: show what the system required (tribute, soldiers, non-rebellion) and what it explicitly did not require (conversion, language change), rendered as practice rather than defined against a modern concept. The sentence will be cut; the distinction will be made through the Babylonian encounter's specifics.

**Tuchman — Cyrus's interior:**
APPLY selectively. The chapter will not invent Cyrus's psychology but will use his death against Tomyris as the moment where the man is most visible — his decision to pursue a campaign on ground where his engine could not operate, and what that cost him. The opening remains correctly impersonal.

**Achebe — priests need human presence:**
APPLY in chapter. The opening will not be revised; the Egyptian priest in the Cambyses section will carry this. In the Babylon section of the chapter, the anonymous high priest of Esagila will be given a moment: the experience of endorsing the cylinder's claims, the institutional act of validating a foreign king before the god.

**Achebe — "not tolerance as moderns understand the word":**
Same fix as Tuchman's seam. See above.

**Ibn Khaldun — fiscal dimension:**
APPLY. The network section will include the tribute-as-operating-cost-of-legitimacy argument. The Persian administration maintained temples as a condition of the sacred-order arrangement — this is the rent the empire paid for the authority it purchased.

**Ibn Khaldun — military reality beneath sacred-order principle:**
APPLY. The Lydia section will make clear that the sacred acknowledgment worked because the army had already won. The principle was applied after military victory, not instead of it. The Massagetae section will make this explicit: where the engine could not enter the sacred order, the military advantage had to operate without the legitimacy supplement, and Cyrus died there.

**Ibn Khaldun — terminal condition gradual, not sudden:**
APPLY in notes update. The terminal condition will be noted as gradual in the chapter's landing: Alexander performed the Akitu; the Seleucid administration that followed began the Hellenization. The engine wound down over decades, not in one burning night.

**Davis — knowledge system at method level (Akitu sequence):**
APPLY. The Babylon section will show the Akitu's twelve-day sequence at method level: what happened on which day, what the king's role was at each stage. The reader will understand the Akitu as a system operating at a specific level, not as "a religious ceremony."

**Davis — cult-statue return logistics:**
APPLY. One specific instance: the statue of Shamash returning to Sippar. The priests who had watched their god stand in a foreign sanctuary. The journey home.

**Kapuściński — relay station specificity:**
APPLY. The chapter will find the body that receives the message: not just the smell of horses but the experience of receiving a command from a distance, the simultaneity of the road.

**Kapuściński — Apadana reliefs specificity:**
APPLY. The delegations will be named, the cypress-tree separations described, the individual carved bodies made specific.

**Kapuściński — Pasargadae garden:**
APPLY. The paradise garden (*pairidaeza*) surrounding Cyrus's tomb will appear in the landing.

## Opening revision status

**Decision:** The opening is not revised. The "not tolerance as moderns understand the word" fix will be made in the chapter's development of the Babylon scene, where the system's actual requirements can be shown in practice rather than defined by contrast. The opening's version of this sentence will remain as written because in the opening's context it is doing necessary orientation work at ~850 words; the chapter will make it unnecessary by showing what the system required.

**Rationale:** Revising the opening to remove one sentence at the cost of possibly disrupting the established voice is a net negative. The opening passes at 30/30. The chapter draft will make the distinction from inside.
