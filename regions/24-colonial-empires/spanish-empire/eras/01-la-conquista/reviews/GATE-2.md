## Innovation Flags

**Flag 1 — Legal-Theological (Reconquista modulation):** The Spanish Legal-Theological register holds a new structural complexity not present in the Hammurabi instance: simultaneously holding sincere believer (Palacios Rubios), sincere critic (Las Casas), sincere opponent of the critic (Sepúlveda), and indigenous respondent-within-the-framework (Guaman Poma) — all operating inside the same Catholic theological framework without the text resolving their disagreement. This is a distinct sub-type: **Internal-Debate Legal-Theological**, where the framework generates its own critique and cannot adjudicate between them. Candidate for INNOVATIONS.md.

**Flag 2 — No-Verdict as Structural Climax:** The Valladolid Debate section renders the panel's failure to verdict as the chapter's climactic moment. This is a specific application of v1.6 Premise Non-Recognition (terminal condition) but at an institutional level: the institution cannot produce a verdict because both possible verdicts undermine the institution's own premise. No prior chapter has done this at this structural level. Candidate for INNOVATIONS.md.

**Flag 3 — Guaman Poma as Persistence Mechanism:** The landing deploys an indigenous figure arguing *within* the colonizer's framework as the culture's persistence mechanism — not the colonizer's documents or debates, but the colonized person who learned the colonizer's form well enough to use it against the colonizer. This is a new instance of a persistence mechanism type not yet in the rubric: **Appropriated Form as Endurance**. Candidate for INNOVATIONS.md.
