# Sources — Armenian Diaspora

## Key Academic Works
- Raymond Kévorkian, *The Armenian Genocide: A Complete History* (2011, I.B. Tauris) — the most comprehensive scholarly account
- Vahakn Dadrian, *The History of the Armenian Genocide* (1995, Berghahn) — foundational
- Ronald Grigor Suny, *"They Can Live in the Desert but Nowhere Else": A History of the Armenian Genocide* (2015, Princeton)
- Taner Akçam, *A Shameful Act: The Armenian Genocide and the Question of Turkish Responsibility* (2006) — a Turkish scholar's account; important for its authorship
- Diana Der Hovanessian — Armenian-American poetry and the diaspora voice
- Peter Balakian, *The Burning Tigris: The Armenian Genocide and America's Response* (2003)

## Primary Sources
- Ambassador Henry Morgenthau Sr.'s diplomatic cables and memoir — *Ambassador Morgenthau's Story* (1918) — US eyewitness documentation from Constantinople
- The Red Cross reports (1915–1916) — available in Swiss Federal Archives
- Survivor testimonies — collected at USC Institute for Armenian Studies; Zoryan Institute
- The Blue Book (Bryce and Toynbee, 1916) — the British parliamentary documentation

## Needed (ACQUISITION)
- Ottoman state archives (Istanbul) — partially opened; the key documents remain contested
- German military archive — German officers witnessed the deportations; records in Bundesarchiv-Militärarchiv
- Zoryan Institute full archive
- Contemporary Nagorno-Karabakh displacement documentation (2020, 2023)
