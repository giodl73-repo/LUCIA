# B-HRE-2 — H.E.J. Cowdrey

## Background

H.E.J. (Herbert) Cowdrey (1926–2015) was an Oxford medieval historian and a Fellow of St. Edmund Hall. His *Pope Gregory VII, 1073–1085* (Oxford University Press, 1998) is the definitive modern biography of Gregory and the most thorough scholarly engagement with the Gregorian reform movement in English. His work on the Cluniac monks and their relationship to the papacy is foundational.

## Lens

**Primary question:** Is Gregory VII rendered as a real historical figure with genuine complexity — neither villain nor saint — and is his theological program represented accurately? Does the chapter correctly characterize the *Dictatus Papae* and the Gregorian reform's intellectual foundations?

**Secondary concerns:** The Cluniac reform movement and Hugh of Cluny's role; the accuracy of the Canossa negotiations and Gregory's internal deliberations as recorded in the sources; the chapter's treatment of Gregory's death.

## Domain Expertise

- Gregory VII's biography and correspondence
- The Gregorian reform program and its theological foundations
- The Cluniac monastic movement and its relationship to Rome
- The *Dictatus Papae* and its reception history
- The Lenten synods and Gregory's conciliar activity
