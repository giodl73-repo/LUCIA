# Sources — Benin Kingdom

## Primary / Archaeological

- Benin bronzes — approximately 3,000 objects currently in European and American museums; the core primary source
- Oba's palace oral traditions — maintained by court historians (ibiwe) in Benin City
- Portuguese chronicles — Pacheco Pereira, *Esmeraldo de Situ Orbis* (c. 1506); João de Barros; accounts of early Benin contact
- British expedition records — 1897; the punitive expedition's documentation

## Academic

- Roth, Henry Ling. *Great Benin: Its Customs, Art and Horrors*. 1903. — early European synthesis
- Dark, Philip J.C. *An Introduction to Benin Art and Technology*. 1973. Oxford University Press.
- Ben-Amos, Paula Girshick. *The Art of Benin*. Rev. ed. 1995. British Museum Press.
- Connah, Graham. *The Archaeology of Benin*. 1975. Oxford University Press. — the Iya earthwork system
- Plankensteiner, Barbara (ed.). *Benin Kings and Rituals: Court Arts from Nigeria*. 2007. Snoeck.
- Coombes, Annie E. *Reinventing Africa: Museums, Material Culture and Popular Imagination*. 1994. Yale University Press. — the bronzes in European museum context
