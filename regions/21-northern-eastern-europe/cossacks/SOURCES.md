# Sources — Cossacks

## Primary Sources

- *Litopys Samovidtsya* (Chronicle of the Eyewitness) — 17th century; Cossack chronicle of the Khmelnytsky period
- *Litopys Hryhoriya Hrabyanky* — early 18th century; Cossack chronicle tradition
- *Istoriya Rusov* — attributed to Hryhoriy Konysky; late 18th century; the foundational text of Ukrainian historical consciousness
- The *Hadiach Agreement* (1658) — Khmelnytsky's successor Ivan Vyhovsky's attempt to incorporate Ukraine into the Commonwealth as a third equal partner
- The *Pereyaslav Agreement* records (1654) — the contested founding document of the Muscovite-Cossack relationship
- Ottoman and Crimean Tatar records on the steppe frontier

## Secondary Sources

- Serhii Plokhy. *The Cossacks and Religion in Early Modern Ukraine*. Oxford University Press, 2001.
- Serhii Plokhy. *The Gates of Europe: A History of Ukraine*. Basic Books, 2015.
- Hrushevsky, Mykhailo. *History of Ukraine-Rus'*. 10 vols. Canadian Institute of Ukrainian Studies Press, 1997–2014.
- Longworth, Philip. *The Cossacks*. Holt, Rinehart and Winston, 1969.
- Sysyn, Frank E. *Between Poland and the Ukraine: The Dilemma of Adam Kysil, 1600–1653*. Harvard Ukrainian Research Institute, 1985.
- Magocsi, Paul Robert. *A History of Ukraine: The Land and Its Peoples*. 2nd ed. University of Toronto Press, 2010.
