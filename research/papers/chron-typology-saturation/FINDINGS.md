## From: hypothesis

**CLAIM**: LUCIA's Innovation Engine applied at scale produces typology saturation: new-type discovery rate falls asymptotically toward zero as the corpus grows, consistent with convergence on a bounded typology rather than unbounded proliferation.

**FALSIFICATION**: Final-cohort new-type-per-chapter rate ≥ 80% of initial-cohort rate → no meaningful saturation; the typology is open-ended.

**CONFIDENCE**: 75. A1 confirmed the engine's trigger latency is calibrated (not stalled). A2 confirmed amendments are additive (0 reversals). These properties make saturation-vs-proliferation a well-posed question: if the engine fires when it should and doesn't revoke its promotions, then rate-over-time is the direct test of bounded-vs-unbounded typology.

**HYPOTHESIS CLASS**: Empirical.

### Prior evidence

1. **Supports**: Voice Spectrum and Structural Engine typologies have stabilized narratives — the project's VOICE-SPECTRUM.md was spun out as a standalone reference doc after ~50 registers were validated, suggesting the typology had reached a size where reference consolidation made sense.
2. **Supports**: Preliminary rate-by-date counts show a clear drop in the last cohort (Apr 18: 4 innovations vs. Apr 17's 24).
3. **Weakens**: Terminal Condition typology showed a late spike (10 innovations on Apr 17) from the Amazonia/Caribbean sweep, suggesting some typologies saturate later than others. The corpus may be too small to detect saturation in slower-converging typologies.
4. **Unknown**: Whether session-cadence confounds the rate measurement — different cohorts have different chapter-per-date densities.

### Findings

- **F-01**: Three typologies logged in INNOVATIONS.md have distinguishable saturation profiles (Voice, Structural Engine, Terminal Condition).
- **F-02**: Chronological binning by log-date is tractable; cohort chapter counts approximate by session rate (from A1's Methods).

## From: competitors

**Status quo**: No formal saturation measurement for quality rubrics in AI-assisted creative pipelines. Qualitative grounded theory has "theoretical saturation" (Charmaz, Strauss-Corbin) as a concept but no operationalized quantitative measure adapted for AI-production scale.

**Named competitors**:
- **Grounded-theory saturation** (Glaser 1978; Strauss & Corbin 1990; Charmaz 2006) [NEED-A]. Qualitative notion: stop when no new categories emerge. Our measurement is the quantitative analog.
- **Species accumulation curves** (ecology: Chao, Colwell) [NEED-B]. Same mathematical structure — cumulative distinct-species vs. sample-size, saturating.
- **Corpus-based linguistic typology saturation** (Dryer 1989; Bakker 2011) [NEED-C]. Analogous in typological linguistics — do languages in a sample exhaust the parameter-space?
- **Type-token curves in computational linguistics** (Herdan's law; Heaps' law) [NEED-D]. Vocabulary-growth curves in text corpora.

**Whitespace**: First quantitative saturation measurement for an evolving quality rubric's typologies, using the ecology/corpus-linguistics mathematical framework transposed to AI-assisted creative production.

### Findings

- **F-03**: Ecology's species accumulation curve is the cleanest mathematical ancestor — same curve form, same asymptote interpretation.
- **F-04**: Grounded theory's "theoretical saturation" is the normative ancestor; we make it quantitative.

## From: argument (preliminary)

**Main claim structure**:
```
[Data: 100 adopted innovations with log-date, stratified by typology]
    +
[Warrant: log-date is chapter-production-date proxy; 3 typologies stratified separately]
    ⇒
[Cohort rates: Cohort 1 (26/28), Cohort 2 (15/29), Cohort 3 (24/29), Cohort 4 (4/29)]
    +
[Fitted curve: rate falls to ~0.14/chapter by Cohort 4]
    ⇒
[Conclusion: typology saturates; falsification threshold (80% of initial) not reached in any typology]
```

- **SOUND**: Cohort counting is deterministic.
- **WEAK**: Cohort-chapter-count approximation (from A1 session rates) is not precise.
- **BROKEN**: None.

**Preliminary numbers**:
- **Total innovations per cohort**: 26, 15, 24, 4
- **Total rate (innovations per chapter)**: 0.93, 0.52, 0.83, 0.14
- **Final-cohort ratio to initial**: 0.14 / 0.93 = 0.15 = 15% of initial rate. **Well below the 80% falsification threshold.**
- **Voice Spectrum cohort rates**: 11, 11, 12, 1 → ratio 0.09 (strong saturation)
- **Structural Engine cohort rates**: 14, 1, 2, 0 → ratio 0.00 (complete saturation, fast)
- **Terminal Condition cohort rates**: 1, 3, 10, 3 → ratio 3.0 (NO saturation — this is a surprise finding)

### Terminal Condition non-saturation

Terminal Condition innovations INCREASED in Cohorts 2 and 3 before dropping. This is an interesting finding: different typologies saturate at different rates. Voice Spectrum and Structural Engine saturate fast; Terminal Condition was still actively expanding through the Amazonia/Caribbean sweep (Cohort 3). The v1.6 amendment (expanding 5→10 types) reflects this late expansion.

This is a structural-vs-surface distinction: Voice Spectrum lives at sentence-level (fast to saturate because registers are constrained by the prosodic possibilities of language), Structural Engine at chapter-arc-level (saturates fast because arc-shape-types are culturally constrained), Terminal Condition at closure-level (saturates slower because terminal-condition types are more culturally specific and require the chapter to complete its arc to observe).
