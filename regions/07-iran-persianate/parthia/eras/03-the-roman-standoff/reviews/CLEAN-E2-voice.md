## Voice-Random-Paragraph Test

**Paragraph 1 (System section):**
> "When a house felt inadequately recognized — when a title was withheld, or a provincial post went to a rival family, or a royal marriage was offered to someone of lower standing — the system flexed. Not immediately, not visibly. A house head who felt slighted did not announce the slight. He returned to his province and managed his military obligations with somewhat less enthusiasm than before. He attended the next assembly with slightly fewer cavalry than his household was capable of providing. He mentioned, in conversations with other house heads, that the king's judgment in this or that matter had seemed hasty. The sentence "Not immediately, not visibly" followed by a long explanatory sequence is the border-state register working correctly: proposition, withdrawal, then the detailed elaboration from behind. **Pass.**

The ridgelines changed hands. The border between Roman Armenia and Parthian Armenia moved by a mountain range in each direction, and then back."

Read in isolation: yes. The enumeration ("The passes changed hands. The ridgelines changed hands.") is the border-state register's short declarative mode — the list that accumulates to demonstrate a system rather than a sequence of events. The voice is consistent. **Pass.**

## Register Drift — Full Scan

Looking for: textbook voice ("The economy was based on..."), modern commentary, any paragraph that sounds like it was written for a Western audience rather than from inside the culture.

**Item 1 — MINOR — "This is not stalemate":**
> "This was not stalemate. This was managed competition, conducted at a level below the threshold of full war, expensive for both powers but less expensive than the alternative."

E-1 flagged this. From a voice perspective: "expensive for both powers" and "less expensive than the alternative" sounds like strategic-studies prose — the register of a 21st-century international relations analyst, not the border-state voice. **Same revision as E-1 recommends.**

**Item 2 — MINOR — "Both understandings were simultaneously correct":**
> "Both understandings were simultaneously correct. The Arsacid court maintained two registers not because it was confused about what it was but because it governed two distinct populations..."

The phrase "Both understandings were simultaneously correct" is the author stepping briefly outside to adjudicate between two perspectives. The border-state voice does not adjudicate — it presents and retreats. **Recommend revision:** cut "Both understandings were simultaneously correct" and go directly to "The Arsacid court maintained two registers..." The reader will understand both were correct without being told.

**Item 3 — CLEAR — all other analytical passages:**
The chapter's analytical passages consistently use the Parthian frame as the frame. "The generous king who could not be generous enough" — not a modern judgment, a system description. "The distance is doing the work" — not a commentary, a statement of the Parthian doctrine as the Arsacids understood it. **Clear throughout except Items 1 and 2.**
