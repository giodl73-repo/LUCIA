You are running /chronicle-chapter for: {{people}}, {{era}}

Write a full chapter (~5000 words) that puts the reader inside this
civilization at this moment in time — from inside that civilization's own
worldview.

---

## Input

- **People**: The culture being written
- **Era**: The era within that culture's periodization

Prerequisites:
- `notes.md` exists and is finalized (PANEL-2 feedback incorporated)
- `opening.md` exists (revised after FIXES stage if needed)

---

## Step 1 — Read the Production Bible

Read `notes.md` completely. Internalize:
- The narrative arc — this is the spine
- The section plan — this is the structure
- The panel pressure points — these are the known risks
- The sensory palette — these are your physical details
- The voice notes — this is your register

Read `opening.md` — this integrates as Section 1.

---

## Step 2 — Write the Chapter

Follow the section plan from `notes.md`. For each section:

**Section 1 — Opening (~800w):** Integrate the opening scene. The chapter
begins in scene, not in context.

**Sections 2-3 — Development (~600-800w each):** The world widens. Second
and third perspectives. Causal depth emerges — not explained but demonstrated.
Each section earns its place by advancing understanding.

**Sections 4-5 — Deepening (~600-800w each):** The era's central tension or
transformation. Forces that have been building arrive at something. Human
texture — figures from `figures.md` in their fullest form.

**Section 6 — Landing (~600w):** A specific moment, image, or voice that
carries the weight. Not a summary. Not "and so this era ended with..." The
reader sits with it. Short sentences. Plain language.

---

## Step 3 — Apply All 13 Style Principles

Throughout writing, enforce:

1. **Inside, Not Above** — reader stands inside the culture
2. **World Before Label** — describe before naming
3. **No Time Travelers** — no modern concepts projected backward
4. **Their Calendar, Their Seasons** — their time markers lead
5. **Names in Their Language** — their names first
6. **Earned Emotion** — no emotional stage directions
7. **No Verdicts** — the reader is the jury
8. **People First, Systems Second** — start with a person
9. **Trust the Reader** — do not re-explain what scenes showed
10. **Simple When It Matters** — plain prose for heavy moments
11. **Sensory Ground** — at least one sensory anchor per page
12. **Rhythm Belongs to the Story** — match prose to the culture
13. **Honest Uncertainty** — when the record is thin, say so

---

## Step 4 — Write figures.md

2-5 key figures of this era. Each gets 2-4 paragraphs:
- Who they were, in their own terms
- What decisions they faced
- The one detail that makes them human, not a title
- If the record is thin, say so

---

## Constraints

- Chapter: 4,500-5,500 words
- Figures: 500-1,500 words
- Em-dashes: **8 or fewer** in the chapter
- No forbidden words or framings
- No textbook voice, no re-explanation, no modern observer cameos
- Sensory ground every page
- Passes the Achebe Test throughout
