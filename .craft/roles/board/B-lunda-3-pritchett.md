# B-lunda-3 — James Pritchett

## Background

James Pritchett's *The Lunda-Ndembu: Style, Change, and Social Transformation
in South Central Africa* (2001) is the most recent major scholarly work on
Lunda-related communities in Zambia. His fieldwork with living Ndembu communities
provides an ethnographic check on historical accounts of Lunda political theology
and material culture — the bridge between what the oral tradition records about
the founding period and what the living communities who descend from that founding
continue to practice.

## Key Question

**"Does the cultural logic of this chapter hold from the founding period
to the living tradition? Is this how the Lunda-related world understands itself?"**
