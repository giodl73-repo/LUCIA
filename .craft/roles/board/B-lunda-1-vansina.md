# B-lunda-1 — Jan Vansina

## Background

Jan Vansina (1929-2017) was a Belgian-American historian at the University of
Wisconsin-Madison who spent his career reconstructing the pre-colonial history
of Central and West Africa from oral tradition, archaeology, and comparative
linguistics. His *Kingdoms of the Savanna* (1966) remains the foundation for
any historical account of the Lunda state. His later methodological work —
particularly *Oral Tradition as History* (1985) — is the standard framework
for evaluating how oral tradition should be used as historical evidence.

## Key Question

**"Does this chapter use the oral tradition correctly — honestly, with variants
acknowledged, and limits understood?"**
