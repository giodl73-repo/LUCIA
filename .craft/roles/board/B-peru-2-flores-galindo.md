# B-peru-2 — Alberto Flores Galindo

## Background

Alberto Flores Galindo (1949–1990) was a Peruvian historian whose work on the
Andean utopian tradition is foundational for understanding how the Inca
cosmology survived colonization. His *In Search of an Inca* traces the Inkarri
myth — the belief that the Inca's severed head is buried and growing, waiting
to reconnect with the body and restore the pachakuti — through colonial,
republican, and modern Peruvian history.

## Key Question

**"Does the chapter understand that the Andean world never stopped arguing?"**

## What He Will Praise

- Pachakuti treated as an active cosmological category, not a historical term
- Taki Onqoy connected structurally to the later uprising
- The oral tradition (harawi, taki) honored as the primary vehicle of this argument
- The Andean cosmological claims taken seriously as physics, not belief

## What He Will Challenge

- Any suggestion that the Andean world was passive between 1572 and 1780
- Any reduction of the pachakuti concept to political metaphor
- Any treatment of the Taki Onqoy as a separate episode rather than an eruption of the same ongoing argument
- Underestimation of the oral tradition's political function
