## Mandate

Hunt re-explanation and filler. "This shows that..." Scenes followed by
explanation of what they showed. Redundancies between sections.

---

## Findings

**Re-explanation check:**

The chapter does not explain its scenes after the fact. The *correctio* logic
is established in the opening through action (Alcuin correcting the text),
not through authorial gloss. The palace school section shows the community
of scholars and then moves immediately to their debates and productions; it
does not then step back and say "this shows us the intellectual vitality of
the Carolingian court." The Theodulf passage in Section III presents his
findings and his poem; it does not then summarize what this reveals about
the reform program.

One mild instance: the phrase "The theory of the reform, stated plainly:"
in Section III is a slight pre-explanation — it announces that it is about
to state plainly, then states plainly. The colon construction is the
Capitulary register's version of this move and is acceptable here because
the "stated plainly" is structural, not apologetic. It is not "This illustrates..."
but rather "Here is the argument." Retained.

**Redundancy check between sections:**

The *correctio* concept is introduced in the opening, developed in Section I
as an administrative problem, and expressed as policy in Section III. Each
occurrence adds something: the opening gives the theological logic; Section I
gives the administrative diagnosis; Section III gives the legislative form.
No redundancy.

The Saxon section (IV) and the palace school section (II) both mention Saxon
children at Aachen. The mention in Section II is brief and contextual (the
diversity of the school); the mention in Section IV is substantive (the
palace school as the alternative to forced baptism). No redundancy; the
second mention advances the argument.

The landing (Section VI) names three things that traveled (minuscule, texts,
idea) and one thing that could not. Each of the three is earned separately in
the chapter: the minuscule was developed in Section II, the texts were being
corrected throughout, the idea was the subject of Section V. The landing is
synthesis, not repetition. Clean.

**Filler check:**

No "In conclusion..." no "As we have seen..." no "It is important to note..."
No scene is repeated in summary form. The chapter earns its word count.

**One cut recommended:**

Section II, fourth paragraph: "The room where they debated was a specific room,
in a specific building, in the palace complex that Charles had spent the 790s
assembling at Aachen around the thermal baths the Romans had valued for a
millennium." The phrase "a specific room, in a specific building" is slightly
redundant to itself. The sentence would be tighter: "The room where they
debated was in the palace complex that Charles had spent the 790s assembling
at Aachen around the thermal baths the Romans had valued for a millennium."
Minor; apply in final revision.

**Verdict: CLEAN. One minor tightening recommended; no significant re-explanation
or redundancy found.**
