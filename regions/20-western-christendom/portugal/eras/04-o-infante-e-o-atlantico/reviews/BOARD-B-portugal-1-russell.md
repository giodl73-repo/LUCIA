## B-portugal-1 — Peter Russell — Board Review of O Infante e o Atlântico

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Author of *Prince Henry 'the Navigator': A Life* (Yale, 2000), the definitive modern biography of Henry. Russell spent decades correcting the legend of Henry the Navigator, demonstrating that the "school of Sagres" was largely myth and that Henry operated through his household patronage organization rather than a formal research institute.

**Historical accuracy:**

- **CORRECT:** The chapter accurately characterizes Henry as a "director of exploration" rather than an explorer, and explicitly notes that he "had not sailed himself on any of these expeditions." This reflects the scholarly consensus my biography established.
- **CORRECT:** The chapter correctly identifies the Order of Christ as the primary financial mechanism for the expeditions, and notes Henry became Grand Master in 1420.
- **CORRECT:** The chapter notes that "the legend of Sagres has romanticized [Henry's operation] into something more formal than it was." This is accurate and reflects the historiographic correction my work made. Henry worked from his household, not a formal institution.
- **CORRECT:** The 1441 raid by Tristão and Gonçalves as the founding moment of the Atlantic slave trade is correctly placed. Henry's instruction to "make every effort to take some of the people of the land" is accurate to the sources.
- **CORRECT:** The 1444 Lagos market: 235 enslaved people, August 6. Zurara's account as the primary source. Correct.
- **REQUIRES CLARIFICATION:** The chapter states Henry "petitioned Pope Eugene IV for a monopoly over the Guinea trade and the right to baptize captives" "by 1443." The actual date of this petition is debated; the papal bull *Romanus Pontifex* that formalized these rights was not issued until 1455 under Nicholas V. The chapter correctly identifies the 1455 date for *Romanus Pontifex* but the 1443 petition claim needs sourcing; it may conflate this with the 1442 petition to Martin V for crusade indulgences. Consider removing the specific 1443 date or clarifying the timeline.
- **MINOR:** Gil Eanes's 1434 rounding of Cape Bojador: correct date, correctly described. The chapter's account of how Eanes succeeded (going further west to avoid the shoaling coast) is accurate to what Zurara reports.
- **CORRECT:** The volta do mar: the chapter correctly identifies this as the return-route technique. The mechanism (going west before turning north to find the westerlies) is accurate.
- **CORRECT:** Fernão Gomes's contract with Afonso V (not Henry) in 1469, requiring 100 leagues per year of coastal advance. Correct.
- **CORRECT:** Bartolomeu Dias 1488, Cabo Tormentoso, Cape of Good Hope. Correct.

**Cultural authenticity:**

The chapter presents Henry's Crusade ideology as genuine rather than cynical, which is correct. My biography emphasizes that Henry was deeply pious and that his religious motivation was sincere, not instrumental. The chapter captures this without sentimentalizing it.

The chapter's treatment of Zurara as both source and figure is historically sophisticated. Zurara was not merely a chronicler; he was a participant in the court culture he was recording, and his divided witness is a real structural feature of his text.

**What the chapter gets right:**

- The synthesis of Arabic astronomy, Genoese cartography, and Portuguese coastal knowledge as the intellectual foundation of the program
- The caravel's lateen rig as the material solution to the return-wind problem
- The economic structure: the franchise model, the royal fifth, the transition from household operation to licensed trade
- The presentation of the slave trade and the exploration program as one integrated system, not separate activities
- Henry's relationship to his nephew Afonso V and the transition of the program after Henry's death

**What needs correction:**

1. The 1443 petition claim: either remove the specific date or add a note clarifying it refers to an earlier petition (Martin V, 1442) distinct from the 1455 bull. The current text is potentially misleading about when the formal legal framework was established.

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (pending clarification of the 1443 petition claim)
