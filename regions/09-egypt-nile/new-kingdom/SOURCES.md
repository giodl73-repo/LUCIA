# Sources — New Kingdom Egypt

## Primary / Archaeological

- Annals of Thutmose III — Karnak inscriptions; detailed military campaign records
- Amarna Letters — Tell el-Amarna archive; cuneiform diplomatic correspondence with Near Eastern kingdoms
- Battle of Qadesh accounts — temple inscriptions at Karnak, Luxor, Abu Simbel, Ramesseum
- Valley of the Kings — tomb complex records, archaeological reports (Theban Mapping Project)
- Papyrus Abbott and Papyrus Leopold-Amherst — tomb robbery trial records
- Great Hymn to the Aten — Akhenaten's theological statement; inscribed in tombs at Akhetaten

## Academic

- Redford, Donald B. *Akhenaten: The Heretic King*. 1984. Princeton University Press.
- James, T.G.H. *Pharaoh's People: Scenes from Life in Imperial Egypt*. 1984. Oxford University Press.
- Aldred, Cyril. *Akhenaten: King of Egypt*. 1988. Thames & Hudson.
- Shaw, Ian (ed.). *The Oxford History of Ancient Egypt*. 2000. — relevant chapters on the New Kingdom
- Kitchen, K.A. *Pharaoh Triumphant: The Life and Times of Ramesses II*. 1982. Aris & Phillips.
- Tyldesley, Joyce. *Hatchepsut: The Female Pharaoh*. 1996. Viking.
- Cline, Eric H. *1177 B.C.: The Year Civilization Collapsed*. 2014. Princeton University Press. — Sea Peoples context
