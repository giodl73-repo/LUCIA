## P-1 — Barbara Tuchman — Review of The Great Expansion (Opening)

**Overall:** This opening does something I have rarely seen in historical writing about navigation: it makes you feel the ocean thinking. The reader is on that canoe from the first sentence, and the pull never breaks.

**What works:**
- The drop is immediate and physical. "Kautai felt the swell change before anyone on deck could see it." The reader is inside a specific competence before they know anything about the culture, the era, or the stakes. This is exactly right.
- The grandfather scene (hand pressed to hull) does double duty: characterization and exposition simultaneously. We learn how the knowledge is transmitted while we watch a boy learn it.
- "The pattern told him the island was there." This one-line paragraph is the hinge, and it works because the preceding paragraph earned it. Short sentence after long wave of prose. The rhythm IS the meaning.
- The cargo paragraph transitions from navigation to politics without a seam. Fine mats, tabua, sennit, bark cloth, and then "it carried the relationship itself." The shift from material to abstract is invisible because the material carries the abstract.

**What doesn't:**
- The knowledge-system paragraph (paragraph 5, "This was the knowledge") pulls back from Kautai's body into a more explanatory mode. "Not a single technique but a system that integrated stars and swells and birds..." This is close to telling. Could the system be shown entirely through Kautai's reading, without the step back? The paragraph works, but it is the weakest in the opening because it trades sensation for explanation.
- "The ocean was not empty" paragraph risks repeating what the opening already showed. The first four paragraphs demonstrated a structured ocean. This paragraph names it. Trust the reader more here.

**The question I'd push on:**
Who is Kautai? I want to know his stakes. Is this a routine voyage or a first command? Is the island ahead friendly or uncertain? The navigation is vivid but the human stakes are thin. A reader will follow the sensation for a chapter, but the full chapter needs Kautai to WANT something beyond confirming a heading.
