**Innovations Flagged:**

1. **Saga voice as no-verdict mechanism (Voice/Honesty).** The saga tradition's understatement is not merely a prose style. It is a built-in Honesty discipline. "The wind had been fair" after describing the destruction of Lindisfarne does the ethical work of the No Verdicts principle without any editorial restraint. The culture's own narrative practice IS the discipline. This parallels the v1.2 amendment on Inside-Voice Honesty (culture's own historiographic practice as the source of honesty) but discovers it in a new tradition.

2. **Composite figure with honest disclosure (Figures).** The bonde is unnamed and composite, but the chapter handles this by making the composite nature a feature, not a bug. The viewpoint is a type (the free farmer) rather than a named individual, and this is disclosed in figures.md. The technique allows inside-voice rendering even when the record provides no named individuals for the specific events described. This parallels the v1.1 amendment on Honest Limits but extends it: the limitation is not just acknowledged but is used as a structural tool.

3. **Ship-as-structural-engine (Progression).** The ship is not a setting or a technology section. It is the organizing principle of the entire chapter: the opening builds a ship, the middle sections follow where ships go, the close puts the ship to rest and promises its return. Every section is connected to the ship. This is a new application of the v1.1 Structural Engine amendment: where previous chapters found their engine in social mechanisms (covenant logic, grief-violence cycle), this chapter finds its engine in a material object that embodies the culture's entire way of life.
