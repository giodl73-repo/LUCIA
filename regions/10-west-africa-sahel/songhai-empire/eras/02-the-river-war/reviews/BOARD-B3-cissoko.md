## B-3 — Sekene Mody Cissoko — Board Review of The River War

**Expertise relevant to this chapter:** Author of Tombouctou et l'Empire Songhay (1975); specialist in Songhai social history and the economies of the inland delta.

**Historical accuracy:**
- The description of the inland delta's geography (islands, channels, seasonal flooding, flood-retreat agriculture) is accurate.
- The description of war-canoe construction (kapok/cailcedrat trunks, hollowed by fire and adze, sides built up with sewn planks, sealed with shea butter and clay) is consistent with what we know from later accounts and from the material culture of the middle Niger's boat-building traditions.
- The depiction of the siege of Djenne as an economic blockade rather than a military assault is consistent with the chronicle accounts and with the geographic reality. Djenne's position in the inland delta made conventional siege warfare impractical.
- The seasonal rhythm of Songhai governance (dry-season campaigns on land, flood-season river patrols) is consistent with the ecological reality of the middle Niger.

**Cultural authenticity:**
- The Sorko are correctly rendered as simultaneously practical and spiritual specialists of the river. The separation of "practical" and "spiritual" knowledge does not apply to Sorko culture, and the chapter correctly refuses to separate them.
- The description of the annual flood as the organizing event of the middle Niger's agricultural calendar is accurate.
- The Niger's physical qualities (brown water, clay banks, dry-season dust, flood-season mosquitoes, the smell of river vegetation) are appropriately rendered for this reach of the river.

**What the chapter gets right:**
- The material culture is specific and grounded. The canoes, the river, the delta, the siege conditions are all physically real.
- The treatment of the Sorko and Bozo as essential rather than marginal to the Songhai state's military capability.
- The environmental dimension of governance (the flood cycle as the agricultural calendar, the river as the tax-collection mechanism).

**What needs correction:**
- Minor: the chapter mentions "papyrus" in the inland delta channels. The dominant tall grass in the Niger inland delta is bourgou (Echinochloa stagnina), not papyrus. Papyrus is more associated with the Nile and East African lakes. Recommend replacing "papyrus" with "bourgou grass" or simply "tall grass."

**Verdict:** APPROVED-WITH-CONDITIONS (one botanical correction needed)
