# Sources

## Primary Sources

- Pagan temple inscriptions (11th–13th century) — in Old Burmese, Pali, Mon, and Sanskrit
- *Glass Palace Chronicle* (*Hmannan Yazawin*, 1829) — Burmese royal chronicle
- Chinese records (Yuan dynasty) — on the Mongol campaigns against Pagan
- Shin Arahan hagiography — on the Theravada reformation under Anawrahta

## Key Academic Works

- G.H. Luce, *Old Burma — Early Pagan* (1969–70) — the definitive art-historical study
- Michael Aung-Thwin, *Pagan: The Origins of Modern Burma* (1985)
- Paul Strachan — Pagan architecture survey
- Bob Hudson — Pagan archaeological surveys and pre-Pagan Burman history
- Donald Stadtner — Pagan's Buddhist art and architecture

## Needed

- Recent archaeological work using LiDAR on the Pagan plain
- Studies on the Mon cultural contribution to Pagan civilization
- Analysis of the post-Pagan period and the successor kingdoms
