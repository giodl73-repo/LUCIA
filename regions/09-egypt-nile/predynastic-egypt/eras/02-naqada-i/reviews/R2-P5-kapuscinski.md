## Specific Sensory Targets

**Section 2 (The Green Stone):** The Eastern Desert must be felt. Sandstone radiating heat. The silence of the wadi. The weight of rock in a pack. The green of the malachite against the red of the sandstone. The specific quality of desert light. Do not describe the desert as "harsh" or "barren." Describe what it IS: the heat coming off the rock in the late morning, the shadow of the wadi wall, the sound of footsteps on gravel.

**Section 3 (The Clay):** The potter's hands must be present. The wet clay between the fingers. The pressure of coiling. The smell of a kiln. What does it sound like when thin pottery is tapped? (A ring, almost metallic.) What does the white lime paint look like on the wet surface of the pot? (A bright line on dark clay, the hippo emerging under the brush.)

**Section 4 (The Flood Calendar):** This is the section I am most concerned about. The flood must arrive through someone's body. The water rising in the morning, the smell of the silt, the sound of the river at a level it has not reached since last year. The transition from field-work to workshop-work should be felt as a change in the body's daily rhythm: from bending over grain to sitting at a crucible.

**Section 5 (The Dead):** The burial scene must be physical. The weight of a body being lowered. The ochre staining the hands of the person applying it. The dryness of the desert sand in the grave pit. The specific heat of the desert edge in the afternoon.
